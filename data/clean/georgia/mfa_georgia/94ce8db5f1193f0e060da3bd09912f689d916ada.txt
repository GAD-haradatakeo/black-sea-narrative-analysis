7 November 2024 On 7 November 2024, within the framework of the European Political Community Summit held in Budapest, Georgian Minister of Foreign Affairs Ilia Darchiashvili attended a working breakfast with foreign colleagues. The discussion at the meeting focused on Georgia's EU membership aspirations. The head of Georgia's Foreign Ministry emphasized that joining the European Union is the choice of the Georgian people and reaffirmed the country's commitment to European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations. He stated that the Georgian government will continue active work and implement the necessary reforms to achieve this goal. Additionally, the minister highlighted the importance of support from international partners in this regard. Ilia Darchiashvili also underscored that Georgia is a loyal and significant partner of the European Union. He spoke about the parliamentary elections held in Georgia on 26 October, noting that they took place in a peaceful and competitive environment and highlighted the positive assessments given by international observers. It is noteworthy that the working breakfast was organized at the initiative of Hungary's Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade, Péter Szijjártó. The foreign ministers of Georgia, Turkey, Armenia, San Marino, North Macedonia, and Monaco attended the event. Ilia Darchiashvili expressed special gratitude to his Hungarian colleague and to Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán for his visit to Georgia, which holds particular importance during Hungary's presidency of the EU Council.
