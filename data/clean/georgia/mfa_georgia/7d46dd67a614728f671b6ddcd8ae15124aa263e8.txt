20 December 2019 20.12.2019, Tbilisi –“ I categorically disagree with the spirit of the article. It is senseless to blame Georgia for changing its course for Russia when actual results point quite to the contrary” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said when commenting on the article published by Wall Street Journal. According to him, the west and the U.S., in particular, has no closer partner than Georgia in this region. “Standing shoulder-to-shoulder with NATO and American partners, Georgia is making its own contribution to the Rapid Response Force. We lost 32 soldiers in this mission and it is an irreparable loss to us. This is our share, our contribution towards overcoming the global challenges facing the west and our partners, Europe and the U.S. Please take a closer look at the results of our strategic partnership with the U.S. Recently we signed a new three-year deal with the U.S. in the areas of defence and security. And you call this a change of course in favour of Russia?” – Zalkaliani told journalists. According to the Minister, the Constitution – the main document of the country, the parliament’s resolution and the foreign policy strategic unanimously identify the European and Euro-Atlantic integration as the key priority of the country. “We are taking concrete steps, we have signed the Association Agreement, including the DCFTA, the European perspective has been declared to be our ultimate goal, and you call this the change of the course? We have progress in developing democratic institutions, free media, doing business, as well as in implementing infrastructural projects that, for their part, are enhancing the strategic function of the country. We have progress in any sphere where the U.S. and Europe have their interests”.
