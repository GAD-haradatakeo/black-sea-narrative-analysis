31 January 2019 31.01.2019 – “European Commission’s Report provides a further proof that the Georgian government is on the right path in terms of implementing economic reforms, strengthening democratic institutions and managing political processes ongoing in the country”- the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani said when commenting on the third Association Implementation Report on Georgia released by the European Commission. According to the Minister, international partners have on more than occasion stressed Georgia ranks higher that other EaP countries. David Zalkaliani said that Georgia has the ambition to further develop its relations with the EU and even go beyond the current scope of the Association Agreement. The European Commission released the third Association Implementation Report on Georgia which sets out the state-of-play of Georgia's implementation of its reforms under the EU-Georgia Association Agreement over the past year, including in such areas as democracy and public administration, human rights protection, justice and the rule of law, fight against corruption and organized crime, trade and economic development, regional development and digital economy, agriculture, environmental protection, climate change, healthcare, education, tourism, transport and energy.
