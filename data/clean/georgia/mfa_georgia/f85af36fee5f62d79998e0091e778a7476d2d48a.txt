4 October 2020 After the end of the National Security Council session the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, assessed the existing military confrontation between Armenia and Azerbaijan, noting that any further escalation of the conflict would inevitably hinder the security environment in the entire region. “This, most recent, military confrontation between Armenia and Azerbaijan has created an extremely difficult situation. Unfortunately, due to the military activity it has become impossible to renew dialogue. We all understand very well that any further escalation of the situation will have extremely negative effects on the security environment in the entire region,” – stated Zalkaliani. The head of the Foreign Ministry called on the international community, the cochairs of the Minsk Group, as well as other international actors to do their utmost in order to take specific steps aimed at deescalating the situation. “We have always acted on our good neighbourly relations and will continue to do so in the future. Georgia has always been and remains loyal to our obligations both in terms of our responsibilities towards our neighbours as well as on the international arena,” – noted the Minister. According to the Minister, Georgia has taken significant tangible steps aimed towards de-escalation of the situation on the ground between the belligerent Armenia and Azerbaijan. “Georgia has temporarily terminated permits for transporting military cargo through Georgian territory as well a aerospace for both of the aforementioned states, informing our partners prior to the decision. We remain in constant contact with them, informing them of any action taken by the Georgian Government. In terms of any other transportation of international cargo, the Georgia side is in full compliance with its international obligations and we will continue to remain loyal to our international responsibilities.” – stated David Zalkaliani.
