24 February 2026 On 24 February 2026, on the occasion of the fourth anniversary of Russia’s ongoing aggression against Ukraine, Georgia’s Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Lasha Darsalia, took part in high-level events held under the auspices of the United Nations and the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE). Deputy Minister Darsalia delivered a statement on Ukraine at the 11th Emergency Special Session of the UN General Assembly. Within the framework of the General Assembly, a resolution supporting Ukraine titled “Support for Lasting Peace in Ukraine” was adopted, which Georgia co-sponsored and voted for. The Deputy Minister also addressed a high-level briefing of the UN Security Council. On the same day, Lasha Darsalia spoke online to participants of the OSCE Permanent Council’s reinforced session, during which the ongoing aggression by the Russian Federation against Ukraine was discussed. In his statements to the UN and OSCE, the Deputy Minister emphasized the severe humanitarian consequences of Russia’s aggression against Ukraine and reiterated Georgia’s steadfast support for Ukraine’s territorial integrity. He also highlighted Georgia’s solidarity with Ukraine, expressed through political and diplomatic channels, as well as significant humanitarian assistance. The Deputy Minister drew attention to human rights violations and security challenges resulting from the occupation of Georgian territories by Russia and stressed the necessity of granting access for international human rights mechanisms to the occupied regions.
