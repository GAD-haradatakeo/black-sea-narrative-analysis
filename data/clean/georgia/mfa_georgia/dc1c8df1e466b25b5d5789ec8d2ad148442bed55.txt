6 May 2019 6.05.2019, Bratislava – “Georgia is the leader of the EU Eastern Partnership format and the goals Georgia has achieved over the course of past 10 years are recognised by partners” - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani stated at the meeting of Visegrad Four and foreign ministers of the Eastern Partnership in Bratislava. According to him, the meeting in Bratislava is an opportunity to discuss the past ten year and future intentions. “The Visegrad countries strongly support Georgia’s European integration and they provide practical support for this. At today’s meeting, which is a kind of preparation for the upcoming EaP Ministerial scheduled to take place in Brussels, we took stock of the progress Georgia has made in terms of functional integration and people-to-people contacts. Georgia is the leader among EaP countries and has shown significant progress on its EU path over the past ten years. However, the country still needs the tangible results which will be felt by each Georgian citizen and which will speed up the integration process.” Zalkaliani said. Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani participated in the meeting of the Ministers of Foreign Affairs of the Visegrad Group (V4) and the Eastern Partnership held in Bratislava. The meeting was attended by the foreign ministers of the Visegrad Group (V4) and the Eastern Partnership countries, the foreign minister of Romania as the current presidency of the EU Council, as well as by European Commissioner for European Neighbourhood Policy & Enlargement Negotiations Johannes Hahn, Secretary General of the European External Action Service (EEAS).
