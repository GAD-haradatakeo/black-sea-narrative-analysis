17 April 2018 17.04.2018, Kachreti – The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze participated in the conference held for high-ranking representatives of various religious confessions in Georgia. Bringing Georgia’s foreign policy priorities to the attention of religious leaders, the Minister highlighted the importance of their support for the European and Euro-Atlantic integration processes. According to Mikheil Janelidze, such engagement contributes to the consolidation of people around the same goal. The Minister focused his attention on the projects recently implemented at the initiative of the Centre for Democracy and Development and through the involvement of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Georgian embassies abroad. He spoke, in particular, about the visits of the Patriarchate’s delegation to brussels and the visit of the multi-religious delegation to the U.S. stirring up great interest both in Georgia and among western partners. The aim of the conference was to provide representatives of various religious confessions with information regarding the ongoing programmes and available opportunities in the country. The conference is the final stage of the project within which the leaders of the Georgian Orthodox Church, the Muslim Community, the Latin-rite Catholic Church and the Armenian Apostolic Church visited the U.S. and held meetings with representatives of political, academic, diplomatic and civil communities.
