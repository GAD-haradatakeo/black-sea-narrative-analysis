13 July 2018 13.07.2018, Tbilisi –According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani the government actively works to ensure that Georgia is kept high on the international agenda. In this context David Zalkaliani singled out for special mention the resolution titled “10 years after the August. The Foreign Minister also paid special importance to the European Parliament’s resolution which is unprecedented in terms of its content. “The resolution deals with such issues as the de-occupation of the Georgian territory, Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration, reforms carried out by the country and most importantly, the Otkhozoria-Tatunashvili list. The Resolution directly calls on the EU Member States to ensure that respective sanctions are imposed on the persons included in the list” – David Zalkaliani said. He also underlined that the resolution contains a direct call to the U.S. Trade Representative to open up the opportunity to develop free trade with Georgia. David Zalkaliani also referred to the resolution of the Seimas of Lithuania on the occasion of the 10th anniversary of the war and to the resolution adopted by the Irish Parliament on the 100th anniversary of the first democratic republic of Georgia which support Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and condemn the actions of the Russian Federation.
