11 September 2018 11.09.2018, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with Assistant Secretary General for Public Diplomacy Tacan Ildem. Discussions focused on the results of the Brussels summit and the progress made by Georgia by applying practical instruments on the path of integration to NATO. The sides positively appraised the firm support for Georgia expressed by the NATO Member States and the Alliance during the Summit in Brussels, which was reflected in the Joint Declaration adopted by the NATO-Georgia Commission. Special attention was paid to the need to deepen NATO-Georgia co-operation in terms of strengthening Black Sea security. Special accent was put on the support of Georgia’s NATO integration by the Alliance, which was reflected in the final document of the Summit. The Assistant Secretary General reaffirmed that the Alliance supports the ongoing reforms in Georgia highlighting the country’s important role in strengthening Euro-Atlantic security. Talking points also included the security situation in the region. Tacan Ildem reaffirmed NATO’s support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its peace policy. Special note, in this context, was taken of the NATO-Georgia Commission meeting in Brussels where the Georgian delegation submitted to the Alliance its new peace initiative “A Step toward a Better Future”. Special attention was paid to the role of public diplomacy and strategic communications in terms of achieving the European and Euro-Atlantic goals and ensuring security and stability in the region.
