5 September 2018 05.09.2018, Tbilisi - The U.S. Department of State has included Syria in a list of countries that recognise the independence of occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, therefore Syria will no longer be eligible to receive U.S. funding. The same sanctions were imposed against Nauru in the current year and against Venezuela and Nicaragua in 2017. The reason behind the ban is a US Appropriations act, which supports Georgia’s territorial integrity. According to the document, “none of the funds appropriated by this Act may be made available for assistance for the central government of a country that the Secretary of State determines and reports to the Committees on Appropriations has recognized the independence of, or has established diplomatic relations with, the Russian occupied Georgian territories of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali Region/South Ossetia.” Such chapters in the U.S. legislative acts and their practical implementation represent a very important step towards strengthening the non-recognition policy internationally and provide yet another proof that the United States of America – a strategic partner of Georgia - firmly and effectively supports the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia. This is a clear message to the world community that territorial integrity and sovereignty is one of the main fundamental norms of international law and should be respected by all.
