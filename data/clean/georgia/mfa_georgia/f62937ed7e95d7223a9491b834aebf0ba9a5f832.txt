5 July 2022 A delegation of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan visited Georgia on 5 July 2022. The delegation was led by theSpecial Representativeof theMinistryofForeign Affairsfor theCaucasusandGUAM, AmbassadorTokuda Shuichi. Within the framework of the visit, the delegation met with the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili. The sides discussed current issues of bilateral relations and prospects for the further development of cooperation. The sides also focused on cooperation within the international organizations. The Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs thanked his Japanese counterpart for his steadfast support of Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for the assistance Japan has provided to Georgia over the years. On his part, Tokuda Shuichi noted with satisfaction the positive dynamics of the relations between the two countries. In this regard, economic cooperation and the importance of its further consolidation was emphasized. The special representative of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan thanked the Georgian side for supporting priority resolutions and candidacies for Japan within international organizations. The sides also reviewed global and regional security issues. Tokuda Shuichi emphasized that Japan strongly supports the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia within its internationally recognized borders. The sides highlighted that 2022 marks the 30th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Japan.
