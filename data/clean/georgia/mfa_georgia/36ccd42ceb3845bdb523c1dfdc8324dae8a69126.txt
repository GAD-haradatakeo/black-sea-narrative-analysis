19 April 2021 On 19 April the Georgian Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani and his deputy, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, held a farewell meeting with the ambassador of Swiss Confederation to Georgia, Patric Franzen. During the meeting the sides noted that Georgia and Switzerland share a lot in common, and there is an upward trend in the relations between the two states, both in bilateral and multilateral formats. The Minister expressed his gratitude towards the Swiss side for its mediation role between Georgia and the Russian Federation, noting that Georgia greatly appreciates this effort. Both the Minister and the Deputy Minister expressed gratitude to the Ambassador for his activity in Georgia in terms of development of bilateral relations. This includes the renewed interest of Swiss companies to invest in Georgia and the implementation of the vocational/dual training exchange programmes. The significant contribution of Switzerland to Georgia’s socio-economic and democratic development was also underscored. The sides highlighted the significance of fully using the potential of bilateral cooperation , including in the economic dimension, tourism, agriculture, vocational education, migration, others.
