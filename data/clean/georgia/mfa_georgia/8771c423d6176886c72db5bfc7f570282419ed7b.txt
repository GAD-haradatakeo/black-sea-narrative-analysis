25 September 2023 During his visit to the Republic of Cyprus, on 25 September 2023, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia, met with the Permanent Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Cyprus, Ambassador Kyriacόs Kouros. The main purpose of the visit was to present Georgia’s progress on the EU path and to strengthen the support of the Cypriot side. Teimuraz Janjalia provided his Cypriot colleague with comprehensive information on the progress achieved by Georgia in the process of fulfilling the European Commission’s 12 recommendations. The deputy minister emphasized the need for the support of EU member states, including Cyprus, in this important period for the country. Discussions focused on current issues relating to Georgia-Cyprus bilateral cooperation. Special attention was paid to the deepening of the political dialogue, in this regard, the activation of high-level bilateral visits and the need to regularly hold bilateral political consultations. The sides exchanged views on regional and global current issues. Special emphasis was put on the ongoing events in the region and the challenges arising from the war waged by Russia in Ukraine. The deputy minister informed his Cypriot colleague in detail about the challenges and the difficult humanitarian situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. Ambassador Kyriacόs Kouros once again confirmed Cyprus' strong support for the territorial integrity and sovereignty of Georgia, as well as for its integration into the European Union.
