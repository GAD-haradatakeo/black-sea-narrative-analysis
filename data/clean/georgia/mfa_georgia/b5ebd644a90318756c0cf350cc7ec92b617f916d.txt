27 October 2023 On 25 October 2023, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Khatuna Totladze delivered a statement at the UN Security Council open debate on “Women`s participation in international peace and security: from theory to practice”. In her speech, the Deputy Minister spoke about the policy of the Government of Georgia for promoting the issues related to the needs of the conflict-affected women, as envisaged in the most recent National Action Plan for the implementation of the UN resolutions on Women, Peace and Security. She pointed out the concrete steps the Government of Georgia has made in this direction. The Deputy Minister also emphasized that Government’s efforts to empower conflict-affected women are impeded by the ongoing occupation of the two Georgian regions by Russia, where fundamental human rights violations are observed regularly.
