23 April 2021 The Georgian Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani, held a meeting with his Ukrainian counterpart, Dmytro Kuleba. The strategic partnership and friendship between Georgia and Ukraine were underscored during the meeting. David Zalkaliani expressed support and solidarity to his Ukrainian colleague, Ukraine’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, along with expressing concern over the developments in the occupied regions. David Zalkaliani also presented Dmytro Kuleba with information regarding the situation on the ground in Georgia’s occupied regions. Coordinated activity regarding consolidation and support of Georgia and Ukraine in terms of occupied territories was also discussed, along with joint actions to be taken in terms of integration into NATO and the EU. Particular emphasis was made on preparatory issues for the NATO and EU’s Eastern Partnership Initiative summits, in order to adequately reflect Georgian and Ukrainian interests. The Minister also discussed the bilateral agenda, underscoring successful cooperation in various fields, as well as prospects of said cooperation, noting the significance of exchanging high-level meetings both planned and future, including the positive dynamics of the highest levels of communication.
