31 July 2019 Tbilisi hosted the 11th round of political consultations between the Foreign Ministries of Georgia and Japan on 31 July 2019, in Tbilisi. Deputy Foreign Minister Alexander Khvtisiashvili headed the Georgian delegation while the Japanese side was led by Deputy Director General of the European Affairs Bureau at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Special Representative for the Caucasus and GUAM, Hideki Uyama. Within the framework of consultations, the sides discussed issues relating to political, economic and cultural cooperation. Special attention was paid to the exchange of high- and highest-level visits. The sides positively appraised the current state of bilateral relations and prospects for future cooperation. Talking points also included issues of regional and global importance. The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister updated the Japanese colleagues on the difficult situation in Georgia’s occupied territories.
