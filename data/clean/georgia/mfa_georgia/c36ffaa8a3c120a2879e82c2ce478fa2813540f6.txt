2 December 2021 Within the framework of OSCE Ministerial Council, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with the NATO Deputy Secretary General, Mircea Geoană. The meeting focused on the Riga Meeting of NATO Foreign Ministers praising Georgia's participation in the North Atlantic Council, highlighting the importance of Georgia as a valuable partner for the Alliance and reiterating its strong support for both the country's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its Euro-Atlantic aspirations. In this regard, the Deputy Secretary General positively assessed the reforms implemented by Georgia and expressed her readiness to continue to actively strengthen her partnership with Georgia and upgrade it to a newer level. The participants of the meeting positively appraised the adoption of the document on aspirant countries within the framework of the NATO Ministerial Meeting. The Georgian side noted with satisfaction the partners’ desire to invite Georgia to the NATO Summit in Madrid. David Zalkaliani once again briefed the OSCE Deputy Secretary General on the package of proposals developed by Georgia on its path to NATO integration and stressed Georgia's active involvement in the process of developing the 2030 strategy. The participants of the meeting with the NATO Deputy Secretary General reaffirmed Georgia's special role in ensuring the security of the Black Sea region, as well as in establishing peace and stability in the South Caucasus. The Deputy Secretary General highlighted Georgia's active involvement in the NATO mission in Afghanistan, both in the immediate aftermath of the mission and in its subsequent repatriation process, and stressed the importance of Georgia's continued participation in discussions and actions to strengthen peace in the region.
