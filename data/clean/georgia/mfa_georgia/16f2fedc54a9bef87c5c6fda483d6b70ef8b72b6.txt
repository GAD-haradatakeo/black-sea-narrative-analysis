21 October 2022 On 21 and 22 October 2022, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia, took part in the conference held in Riga. At the panel - "ROOM FOR MANOEUVER IN THE EASTERN PARTNERSHIP: INCORPORATING VARIOUS DEGREES OF INTEGRATION WITH THE EU", he spoke about the importance of the Eastern Partnership and the future architecture of its format amid the new geopolitical situation in the region. He noted that the Eastern Partnership played a positive role in ensuring political association of the partners and economic integration with the European Union. The Georgian side focused its attention on Georgia's integration into the European Union, including on the ongoing work in accordance Georgia’s application for EU membership Teimuraz Janjalia paid particular attention to the role of the Eastern Partnership economic and investment plan for the partner countries and the economic and political importance of the implementation of the flagship projects developed for Georgia within the plan. The Conference was also attended by high-level representatives of the European Union and Member States, as well as representatives of the North Atlantic Alliance, international and local non-governmental organizations, research and analytical institutes, and experts invited from partner countries.
