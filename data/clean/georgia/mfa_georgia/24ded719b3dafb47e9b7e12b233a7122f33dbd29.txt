20 June 2024 On 20 June 2024, the newly appointed Resident Coordinator of the United Nations in Georgia handed over the letter of credentials to the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia. At the meeting following the letter handover ceremony, the long-term and fruitful cooperation between Georgia and the UN Resident Coordinator's Office, as well as the UN team, was emphasized. The parties discussed key areas of cooperation in sustainable development and future plans. The resident coordinator positively evaluated the government's efforts to implement sustainable development goals in Georgia, highlighting steps taken in universal healthcare and improving conditions for disabled people. The conversation also addressed the situation in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions of Georgia, which are occupied by Russia. At the end of the meeting, the First Deputy Minister wished the UN Resident Coordinator success in his future work. Both parties reaffirmed their commitment to continue their close partnership.
