21 June 2021 Government Commission to elaborate and implement the Georgian State Strategy for De-occupation and Peaceful Conflict Resolution was launched today, on 21 June 2021, by decision of the Prime Minister of Georgia. The Government Commission will comprise a working group, which, through the involvement of the wider community, will develop a comprehensive and inclusive state strategy by the end of the year. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs will chair and coordinate the work of the Commission and the working group. The goal of the government commission is to launch the nationwide process aimed at developing a common state approach to, and a strategic vision on, the de-occupation and unification of the country and peaceful conflict resolution. The process will involve state agencies and institutions, as well as civil society – research centers, non-governmental organizations, experts, conflict-affected community and the wider community. By the end of the year, an increasing number of meetings, dialogues, discussions and consultations will be held at the interagency level, as well as with the involvement of civil society, in order to jointly develop a state vision on a peaceful and unified Georgia in the future. In parallel, an intensive process of cooperation will be conducted with Georgia’s strategic partners to get started the process of de-occupation and peaceful conflict resolution, through the proactive and effective involvement of the international community.
