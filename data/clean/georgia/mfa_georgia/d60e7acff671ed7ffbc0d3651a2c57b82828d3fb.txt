26 June 2019 26.06.19, Vienna – The conflicts in the OSCE area were the focus of discussions at the OSCE Annual Security Review Conference (ASRC). Deputy Foreign Minister Lasha Darsalia made a statement at the working session dedicated to this issue. The Deputy Minister provided the conference participants with detailed information on the difficult security and human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied regions. Lasha Darsalia paid special attention to many cases of violent deaths of Georgian citizens. In particular, he focused on the cases of I. Kvaratskhelia, A. Tatunashvili, G. Otkhozoria and D. Basharuli. He also expressed his concern that internally displaced persons and refugees continue to be deprived of the right to return to their homes in a safe and dignified manner. Accoridng to the Deputy Minister, the occupation regime does not grant international monitoring mechanisms an access to the occupied territories that further aggravates the already difficult situation on the ground. Speaking about the Geneva International Discussions, Lasha Darsalia underlined that Russia does not comply with its obligations under the EU-brokered ceasefire agreement and still continues occupation of Georgian territories. The Head of the Georgian delegation highlighted the importance of confidence building between the communities on both sides of the occupation line, and the government’s peace initiaives. On the second day of the Conference, at the session dedicated to Structured Dialogue, the Georgian side put special accent on the necessity of holding discussions on the conflicts in the OSCE area, transnational threats and the updating of the Vienna document.
