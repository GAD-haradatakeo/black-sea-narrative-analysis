25 January 2020 The U.S.-Georgia Strategic Partnership Commission's Working Group on People-to-People and Cultural Exchanges met on 24 January, in Washington. First Deputy Foreign Minister of Georgia Vakhtang Makharoblishvili and First Deputy Minister of Education, Science, Culture and Sport Irina Abuladze co-chaired the meeting from the Georgian side, and Deputy Assistant Secretary for Policy in the U.S. Department of State's Bureau of Educational andCulturalAffairs AleishaWoodward and USAID Deputy Assistant Administrator Alexander Sokolowski – from the U.S. side. The sides discussed the ongoing co-operation in the areas of education, health, sports, cultural exchanges and strategic communications, as well as the issues relation to engagement of the population in the occupied territories and challenges involved in this process. Discussions focused on the Georgian Government’s initiative “A Step towards a Better Future”. The Georgian side brought to the attention of the U.S. delegation the Georgian government’s planned activities. For its part, the U.S. delegation expressed its support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and highly appraised the Georgian Government’s efforts to increase engagement of the population in Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions and for the possibility of extending to them various services and benefits. The U.S. delegation highlighted Georgia’s reform progress on its Euro-Atlantic integration path. The sides paid special attention to the importance of open communication and people-to-people contacts and underscored the need of further strengthening Georgia-U.S. strategic partnership.
