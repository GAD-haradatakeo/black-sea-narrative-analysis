27 July 2021 On 27 July 2021, the First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia held a meeting with civil society representatives within the scope of the government commission for elaboration and implementation of Strategy for de-occupation and sustainable peaceful conflict resolution. The meeting was attended by experts and representatives of various think-tanks and non-governmental organizations. Darsalia acquainted them with the commission’s aims and objectives. According to him, the main task is to get started a whole-of-nation process to work out a common approach and strategic vision on the de-occupation and peaceful resolution of the conflict. To quote Lasha Darsalia, this will be an inclusive process involving not only executive and legislative branches of the government but also civil society – think tanks, NGOs, experts, internally displaced persons. In parallel, intensive work will be done to ensure the effective implementation of the Strategy through the proactive involvement and effective support of the international community.
