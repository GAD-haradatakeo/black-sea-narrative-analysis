5 July 2018 05.07.2018, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with Sir Simon McDonald, the Permanent Under Secretary and Head of the Diplomatic Service at the Foreign and Commonwealth Office. The sides spoke about the close friendly and partner relations between Georgia and the UK developing dynamically within the framework of the Wardrop Dialogue. During the meeting David Zalkaliani emphasized that Georgia’s full-scale European and Euro-Atlantic integration is atop the list of priorities of the new cabinet of ministers. Talking points included the preparations for forthcoming summit of NATO in Brussels and Georgia’s expectations in this regard. The importance of the support of the Alliance Member States, including the UK, was also highlighted. The Georgian Foreign Minister brought to the attention of the British diplomat the grave human rights and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. He put special accent on the Otkhozoria-Tatunashvili list developed by the Government for preventing the further aggravation of the already difficult humanitarian situation. Discussions also focused on the Geneva International Discussions, relations with the Russian Federation and the Georgian Government’s new peace initiative: “A step toward a better future”. Sir Simon McDonald reaffirmed the UK’s firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its European and Euro-Atlantic integration path.
