26 May 2017 26.05.2017, Tbilisi - An exhibition dedicated to the 850th anniversary of the birth of Shota Rustaveli has opened at the Georgian National Museum, on the occasion of Independence Day of Georgia. The exhibition due to last until 11 June will display Guram Akhalaia’s personal collection of unique editions of Vepkhistkaosani published from 1712 to 2017, including Georgian editions and translations of the poem in 51 languages. The exhibition was officially opened by Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze who congratulated the Georgian people on Independence Day and spoke about Georgia’s path and successes over the past 26 years since the restoration of independence. Mikheil Janelidze also highlighted the importance of the unique collection exhibited at the Georgian National Museum saying that the Ministry will spare no efforts to promote Georgian culture abroad. Minister of Culture and Monument Protection Mikheil Giorgadze, Director of the Georgian National Museum Davit Lortkipanidze and collectioner Guram Akhalaia also appeared before the audience. Foreign diplomats were also among guests invited to the unique exhibition of Vepkhistkaosani. The highlight of the exhibition was “Vepkhistkaosani Handwritten in the 21st Century” – pages handwritten at Guram Akhalaia’s initiative during the period from 2003 to 2016. Writers, scientists, translators of the poem, artists, sportsmen, actors and other outstanding figures handwrote per page of the poem.
