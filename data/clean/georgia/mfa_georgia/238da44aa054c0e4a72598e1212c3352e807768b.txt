14 February 2019 14.02.2019, Warsaw – The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani is on his working visit in Poland where, within the framework of the International Conference „Peace and Security in the Middle East”, he held a brief meeting with the U.S. Secretary of State, Mike Pompeo. The sides highlighted the strategic partnership between the two countries. The U.S. once again referred to Georgia as a strong partner and ally of the United States. David Zalkaliani thanked the Secretary of State for the support of Georgia, including for making a public statement on Georgia and condemning Russia’s aggression against the sovereign states during his visit to the Republic of Poland. The Minister updated the Secretary of State on the difficult situation in Georgia’s occupied regions and on Russia’s illegal activities. Reaffirming the United States unequivocal support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, Mike Pompeo said that the U.S. will keep this issue atop its agenda both in bilateral and multilateral formats. According to Mike Pompeo, he remains committed to support the development of defence, security and trade-economic relations between the two countries. Special note was taken of Georgia’s active engagement on global issues. In this light, the U.S. Secretary of State thanked his Georgian colleague for Georgia’s position regarding Venezuela. Positively appraising the intensive communication between the two countries at all levels, the Georgian Foreign Minister and the U.S. Secretary of State exchanged their views over the high-level visits and meetings already conducted or forthcoming.
