18 December 2023 On 15 December 2023, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, participated the 47th session of the Council of Ministers of Foreign Affairs (CMFA) of the member states of the Black Sea Economic Cooperation (BSEC). In his speech, the deputy minister emphasized the importance of the European Council's historic decision to grant Georgia a candidate status and thanked the ministers of foreign affairs of the participating EU member states: Bulgaria, Romania and Greece for their strong support to Georgia in the process of European integration. Also, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili congratulated his Ukrainian and Moldovan colleagues on the European Council's decision to open negotiations. According to the Georgian delegation, the decisions of the European Council will contribute to further integration of Black Sea countries with Europe and this will become the starting point for the peaceful, stable and sustainable development of BSEC member states. The participants paid special attention to the need of increasing regional security and, in this light, to the importance of peaceful settlement of conflicts in the Black Sea region by observing the principles of sovereignty and territorial integrity of the countries. The Georgian side thanked the Republic of Türkiye for its efforts during the chairmanship of the organization and wished success to the Republic of Albania for the upcoming chairmanship in BSEC (January-June 2024).
