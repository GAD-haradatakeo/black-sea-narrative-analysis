12 September 2018 12.09.2018 Tbilisi - The newly appointed Ambassador of the European Union to Georgia, Carl Hartzell, presented copies of his credentials to the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia Davit Zalkaliani and an introductory meeting was held after that. David Zalkaliani congratulated Carl Hartzell on launching a diplomatic mission in Georgia and wished him success in future activities. The sides discussed priorities on the EU-Georgia cooperation agenda and positively assessed the implementation of the Association Agreement. The Minister of Foreign Affairs introduced a new initiative of the government to the head of the European Union Delegation - the process of development of a roadmap for the EU membership. The parties also discussed the upcoming highest-level meeting between the Government of Georgia and the European Commission in November 2018 and underlined the importance of effective preparatory process. At the meeting a special emphasis was made on the peaceful resolution of the Russian-Georgian conflict. The Minister acquainted the EU Ambassador with the situation in the occupied territories and the heavy consequences resulting from Russia's destructive policy. The Minister drew attention to the special role of the EU in the peaceful resolution of the conflict, as well as to the EU involvement in the implementation of the Georgian government's peace initiative.
