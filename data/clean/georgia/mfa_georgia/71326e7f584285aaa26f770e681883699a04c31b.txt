29 November 2021 On 29 November 2021, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia hosted a delegation of the Latvian Parliament (Saeima) led by the Chairman of the Foreign Affairs Committee at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Richards Kols. Discussing issues of bilateral and multilateral relations between the two countries, sides highlighted the traditionally friendly and partner relations between Georgia and Latvia. Teimuraz Janjalia thanked the Latvian parliamentarians for their strong support of the country's territorial integrity, sovereignty and foreign priorities, as well as for their assistance in the fight against Covid-19 and for the allocation of vaccines against the virus. The meeting focused on Georgia's progress on EU and NATO integration. The sides discussed in detail the current issues of Georgia-EU cooperation. Attention was paid to the prospects for the development of the Eastern Partnership format, the future plans of the Associated Trio and Latvia's support in this process. The sides also discussed the Georgia's expectations regarding the EaP Summit scheduled for December. The sides underlined the importance of Georgia's contribution to strengthening international security and of intensifying the NATO integration process. Georgia's active involvement in the evacuation process from Afghanistan was highly praised. Speaking about the regional context, the sides highlighted the ongoing processes in the South Caucasus, and Georgia’s role in this regard. The Latvian side was provided with detailed information on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. The need for a consolidated position of the partners for keeping the issue on the international agenda was underlined.
