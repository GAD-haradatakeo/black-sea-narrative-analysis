20 September 2022 The Deputy Minister and Belgian legislators expressed their satisfaction with the existing friendly relations between the two countries. Special attention was paid to the importance of deepening economic cooperation and to the 30th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries. The Belgian side was provided with information on Georgia’s progress in implementing the European Commission’s recommendations, on the results of the EU-Georgia Association Council and on specific initiatives. The sides emphasized the importance of the EU’s uniform approach to the associated countries, and expressed the hope that Georgia will receive the status of a candidate country in the near future. The members of the friendship group were provided with detailed information on the priority of Georgia's integration into NATO, which is of particular importance in the light of the geopolitical challenges facing the region. The need to fully use the potential of the Black Sea was noted. Teimuraz Janjalia provided the Belgian Legislature with information about the severe humanitarian situation and human rights violations in the occupied regions of Georgia. He focused on the government's peace policy and efforts to resolve the conflict peacefully.
