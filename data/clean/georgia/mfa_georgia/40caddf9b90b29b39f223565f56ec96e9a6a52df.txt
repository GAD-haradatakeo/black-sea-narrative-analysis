22 May 2017 22.05.2017, Tbilisi - Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister David Dondua met Deputy Chief of Mission at the U.S. Embassy to Georgia Nicholas Berliner. The sides discussed the ways to maintain stability in Georgia’s occupied territories and the need to improve the living conditions of the local population. Discussions also focused on the possible contribution of the United States to the reconciliation and conflict resolution process and on concrete initiatives in this regard. The sides spoke about the next round of the Geneva International Discussions. The Georgian side reaffirmed the readiness for constructive co-operation emphasizing the necessity of maintaining the format and the agenda of discussions. Talking points also included NATO-related issues. The sides paid special attention to Prime Minister Giorgi Kvirikashvili’s successful visit to Washington from 8 to 10 May, highlighting the opportunities, which the strategic partnership between Georgia and the U.S. offers.
