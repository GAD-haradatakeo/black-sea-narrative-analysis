11 May 2022 The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili, met with his Cypriot counterpart, Ioannis Kasoulides, within the framework of the Ministerial Meeting of the Global Coalition against ISIS. At the meeting, the Ministers underlined the 30th anniversary of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Cyprus and the friendly and partnership cooperation established during this time. Speaking about Georgia's European integration process, Ioannis Kasoulides reiterated his support for the country. The sides highlighted the irreversibility of Georgia's European agenda, given the progress in the implementation of the reforms under the Association Agreement, Georgia’s political and legislative rapprochement with the EU, and the population’s strong support for the country's European integration process. The Ministers emphasized the European Union’s further support for the efforts of Georgia and the Associated Trio, which is of particular importance in the light of the current regional and global challenges. Speaking about the security environment in the region and in Georgia, Ioannis Kasoulides emphasized Cyprus support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. The Ministers praised the close and fruitful cooperation between the countries in the field of people-to-people contacts and culture and reaffirmed their readiness to facilitate its further consolidation.
