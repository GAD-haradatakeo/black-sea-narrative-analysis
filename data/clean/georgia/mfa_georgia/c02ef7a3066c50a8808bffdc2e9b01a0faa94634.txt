27 January 2022 The 34th session of the governing council of the intergovernmental coalition - "Commonwealth of Democracies" (virtual format) was held on 26 and 27 January 2022. The meeting was opened by the CoD President – the Romanian Foreign Minister, Bogdan Aurescu and the CoD Secretary General Thomas Garrett. During the meeting, the current priorities of the Coalition were discussed, including the role of technologies in the process of democratic development. During the meeting, the chairmen of the CoD working groups presented reports on the activities carried out during 2021. on behalf of Georgia as the chair of the CoD Working Group on Democracy and Technology, Nino Shekriladze, the Head of the UN Division of the International Organizations Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, briefed members of the Council on the group's activities , special attention was paid to the use of technologies in the electoral processes and the successful experience in this field. During the meeting, members of the Coalition's Governing Council - Japan, Canada, Costa Rica, Nigeria, Romania, Hungary, Uruguay, Finland and Sweden - announced their intention to renew their membership in the Coalition's Governing Council. The meeting also discussed issues related to the protection and promotion of the principles of the Warsaw Declaration (which laid the foundation for the Coalition), the future plans of the member states and the measures due to be carried out in 2022.
