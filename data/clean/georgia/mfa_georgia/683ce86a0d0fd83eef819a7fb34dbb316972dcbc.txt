15 February 2024 On 15 February 2024, the 9th round of political consultations at the level of deputy ministers was held between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and Turkmenistan in Ashgabat. The consultations were co-chaired by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili from Georgia, and by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Serdar Mukhametdurdiev from Turkmenistan. The sides discussed current issues of close cooperation between the two countries, both in bilateral and multilateral formats. When discussing bilateral cooperation, a positive assessment was given to the relations at the political level, the need to activate high-level visits. Prospects for trade-economic, parliamentary, investment, education, tourism cooperation between the two countries were especially emphasized. Special attention was paid to the field of transport logistics and opportunities for development of cooperation in this regard. The diplomats discussed the improvement of the bilateral legal framework and agreed to continue the systematic dialogue for its further development. At the meetings, regional security issues were discussed, the Turkmen side was provided with detailed information about the current situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. The Turkmen side once again expressed its support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia within the internationally recognized borders. Within the framework of the visit, the delegation of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia held a meeting with the Deputy Chairman of the Cabinet of Ministers of Turkmenistan, the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Rashid Meredov.
