28 December 2024 As part of the Ambassadors' Conference, a panel discussion was held on Georgia’s foreign policy priorities. The discussion was moderated by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Maka Botchorishvili, and included participation from Nikoloz Samkharadze, Chair of the Parliamentary Committee on Foreign Relations; Levan Makhashvili, Chair of the European Integration Committee; and Irakli Zarqua, Chair of the Diaspora and Caucasus Affairs Committee. Maka Botchorishvili provided an overview of Georgia’s foreign policy priorities, emphasizing the importance of peace and stability for the country’s future amid the region’s current security challenges. According to the Minister, Georgia’s top foreign policy priority remains European integration, with the current government having laid a solid foundation for the irreversibility of Georgia’s European future. The panelists discussed the government’s efforts to advance the policy of non-recognition and restore territorial integrity through peaceful means. They highlighted the importance of maintaining issues related to the peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict as a high priority on the international agenda. Progress and future plans concerning Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration were also discussed. The discussion underscored the importance of preparing Georgia for EU membership, with panelists noting that if the EU expands by 2030, Georgia will likely be among the most well-prepared candidate countries. Significant progress achieved since the signing of the Association Agreement in 2014 was particularly emphasized. The participants stressed the critical importance of dialogue with international partners during the European integration process and the necessity of objectively evaluating ongoing developments. Optimism was expressed that current challenges will be overcome, fostering cooperation grounded in mutual respect. The discussion also covered the government’s vision for relations with neighboring countries and regional security. The participants noted that Georgia’s foreign policy toward its neighbors is balanced, rooted in principles of protecting territorial integrity, sovereignty, good neighborliness, strengthening security, and promoting peace and stability in the region. These principles will continue to guide the country’s foreign policy. Further topics of discussion included Georgia’s strategic partnerships, particularly its relations with the United States and the People’s Republic of China.
