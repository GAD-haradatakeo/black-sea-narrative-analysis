23 October 2018 23.10.2018, Tbilisi - Within the framework of his visit to Lithuania, the Georgian Foreign Minister, Davit Zalkaliani met with the Prime Minister of Lithuania Ministras Pirmininkas. Zalkaliani expressed his gratitude for Lithuania’s outstanding support of Georgia that makes its impact on the agenda of bilateral relations between the two countries, as well as on people-to-people contacts. Ministras Pirmininkas reaffirmed his staunch support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its European and Euro-Atlantic integration. The sides discussed the increasing dynamics of sectoral co-operation between the two countries. It was highlighted that Lithuania has been selected to implement 6 EUTwinningprojects inGeorgia. The sides agreed to direct their efforts towards strengthening trade-economic co-operation in the future. Talking points also included the Georgian government’s reform policy, investment policy, as well as Georgia’s role in international transport ad energy projects.
