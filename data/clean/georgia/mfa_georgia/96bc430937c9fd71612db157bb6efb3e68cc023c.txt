12 June 2021 On 11 June 2021, Presidents of Georgia and the Republic of Lithuania, Salome Zurabishvili and Gitanas Nauseda, signed the Joint Declaration in support for Georgia’s sovereignty, territorial integrity, European and Euro-Atlantic integration. In the document the Presidents acknowledged the special relationship between Georgia and the Republic of Lithuania, stressed the necessity of de-occupation of the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia and implementation of the EU-mediated 12 August 2008 Ceasefire Agreement by the Russian Federation, emphasized the necessity of peaceful resolution of Russia-Georgia conflict based on the fundamental principles of international law, stressing the necessity of safe and dignified return of IDPs and refugees, expelled as a result of ethnic cleansing to their homes.
