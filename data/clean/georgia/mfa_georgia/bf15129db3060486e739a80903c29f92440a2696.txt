20 May 2022 The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili met with the Minister for Foreign, Trade and European Affairs, Ian Borg within the framework of the Council of Europe Ministerial Meeting in Turin. At the meeting, the ministers noted the exceptionally friendly relations between the two countries, which is manifested by constructive cooperation on various issues in both bilateral and multilateral formats. The meeting highlighted Malta's support for Georgia's European aspirations. The Maltese side noted Georgia’s successful results on the path of European integration. In this regard, the sides especially emphasized the population’s growing support for the foreign policy aspirations of the country. The Ministers also discussed the possibility of sharing Malta's European integration experience. Discussions also focused on the security environment and challenges in the region. Reaffirming Malta's unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity in the face of the Russian occupation, Ian Borg highlighted the importance of the international community’s consolidated support at various international forums, where Malta's position has always been strong. In this context, the Ministers positively assessed Georgia-Malta cooperation within the Council of Europe and underlined the decisions and resolutions on the Russia-Georgia conflict adopted within the organization. Speaking about bilateral relations, the sides outlined potential areas for the development of cooperation and highlighted the excellent cooperation and the established good practice of sharing experience in the maritime field. Ilia Darchiashvili thanked the Maltese side for the long-term fruitful cooperation of the Maltese Diplomatic Academy with Georgia.
