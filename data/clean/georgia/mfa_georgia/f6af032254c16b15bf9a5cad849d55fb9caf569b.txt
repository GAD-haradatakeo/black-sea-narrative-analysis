3 October 2021 On 3 October 2021, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with the Director of the OSCE Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights (ODIHR), Matteo Mecacci, Special Adviser to the Director of the ODIHR, Peter Mossop, the Head of the OSCE/ODIHR Election Observation Mission, Albert Ionson. The ODIHR representatives thanked the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and relevant agencies for their effective support for the full implementation of international observation work. The local self-government elections in Georgia were described as competitive and well-run, with a large range of candidates campaigning freely. The sides pointed to several positive aspects identified by observers. It was highlighted that Georgian voters were able to freely cast their votes. Discussions also focused on the gaps identified by the International Election Observation Mission and related recommendations. Zalkaliani reaffirmed the Georgian government’s full readiness to cooperate closely and take effective steps to address the remaining shortcomings and to carry out the recommendations of the International Election Observation Mission. The Georgian side thanked the representatives of the Democratic Institutions and Human Rights Office (ODIHR) for their effective observation work.
