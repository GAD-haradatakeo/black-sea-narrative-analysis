1 March 2019 1.03.2019 – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani participated in the joint meeting of the Commissions on NATO and EU Integrationchaired by Prime Minister Mamuka Bakhtadze. It was highlighted that Georgia successfully carries out reforms under the Association Agreement and the Association Agenda. Participants of the meeting noted that the successful reforms aimed at legislative as well as institutional harmonization are an essential prerequisite for Georgia’s integration with the EU. The document on EU integration -RoadMap2EU was discussed and approved as one of the key items on the agenda of the meeting. RoadMap2EU represents an important document incorporating all reforms to be carried out under the Association Agreement and identifying measures intended to achieve ambitious integration with the EU. The Commission also approved the Action Plan 2019 for implementation of the Association Agreement and the Association Agenda and the Action Plan Progress Report 2018. Discussions also focused on the preparations for the meeting of the EU-Georgia Association Council scheduled to take place in Brussels, on 5 March, as well as on EU financial assistance and various events to mark the 10th anniversary of Eastern Partnership, as well as the high-level Batumi International Conference. Having discussed in detail the implementation of the visa-free travel with the EU, the members of the Commission highlighted that along with practical importance the visa-free regime has a political meaning as well.
