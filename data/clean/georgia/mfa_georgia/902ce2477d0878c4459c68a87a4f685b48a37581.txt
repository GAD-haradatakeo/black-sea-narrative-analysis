21 October 2021 The Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Republic of Kazakhstan held the ninth round of political consultations on 21 October 2021, in Nur-Sultan, at the level of Deputy Ministers. The Deputy Foreign Minister, Alexander Khvtisiashvili led the Georgian side while the Kazakh side was led by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Marat Sizdikov. The sides discussed a wide range of bilateral and multilateral cooperation, including political, economic, cultural and humanitarian issues, as well as the ways to improve the bilateral legal framework and cooperation between the countries within international organizations. Discussions also focused on the development of cooperation in the fields of trade, economy, investments, transport, logistics, tourism, agriculture, information technologies and tourism. Particular attention was paid to the increasingly higher levels of cooperation within the scope pf international transport-transit corridors. The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Alexander Khvtisiashvili briefed his Kazakh counterpart on the dire humanitarian situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and on the actions taken by the Russian Federation along the occupation line. For his part, the Kazakh side reaffirmed Kazakhstan's respect for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. The sides stressed the need to resume high-level bilateral visits in the post-pandemic period and outlined further steps to this end.
