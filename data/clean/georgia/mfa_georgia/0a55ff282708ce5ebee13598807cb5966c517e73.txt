9 September 2017 09.09.2017, Ankara - As a result of intensce co-operation between the Embassy of Georgia to Turkey and the Çankaya Municipality in the Turkish capital Ankara, and as part of efforts to mark the 25th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Turkey, Tbilisi Park has been officially opened in one of the central parts of the Çankaya district. The opening ceremony was attended by Minister of Culture and Monument Protection Mikheil Giorgadze, Georgian Ambassador to Turkey Irakli Koplatadze and Çankaya Mayor Alper Taşdelen. The Tbilisi Park is 4547 square metres in area and includes a variety of sports equipment, a playground for children and leisure places. The park is decorated with various species of trees and flowers. A monument dedicated to Georgia-Turkey friendship is planned to be erected in the central part of the Tbilisi Park. The opening of the Tbilisi Park, according to Alper Taşdelen, will make its contribution to the further strengthening of friendship between Georgia and Turkey. Mikheil Giorgadze described friendly relations between the two countries as exemplary. The opening of the Tbilisi Park was also attended by diplomats accreited to Ankara, employees of the Çankaya Municipality, officials of the Foreign Ministry and representatives of mass media and Georgian diaspora in Ankara.
