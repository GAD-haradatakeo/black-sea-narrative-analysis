14 January 2026 On 14 January 2026, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Giorgi Zurabashvili, took part in the 66th meeting of the GUAM Council of National Coordinators. During the meeting, the participants reviewed current issues on the organization’s agenda and discussed prospects for strengthening cooperation among the member states. Giorgi Zurabashvili addressed the complex situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, drawing attention to the security challenges in the region and underscoring the importance of ensuring peace and stability in the South Caucasus. In this context, particular emphasis was placed on the historic peace agreement reached between the Republic of Azerbaijan and the Republic of Armenia. The First Deputy Minister also noted that since the outbreak of the war in Ukraine, Georgia has demonstrated full solidarity with Ukraine and the Ukrainian people, as evidenced by relevant political and diplomatic steps taken in both bilateral and multilateral formats. The meeting of the Council was attended by Sergiu Mihov, State Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Moldova; Olexandr Mishchenko, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine; and Yalchin Rafiyev, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
