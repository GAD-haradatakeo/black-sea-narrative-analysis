19 May 2022 On 19 May, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia met with the Senior Advisor to the U.S. Secretary of State, Derek Scholes, who arrived in Georgia on 18 May together with representatives from various U.S. agencies, including USAID, Departments of State and Defence. The sides discussed issues relating to U.S.-Georgia strategic partnership and the difficult security environment in the region in the wake of Russian aggression in Ukraine. Lasha Darsalia thanked Derek Schole for the U.S. support to Georgia and fruitful cooperation over the past 30 years of partnership. Describing the visit of the Senior Advisor to the Secretary of State as a clear demonstration of the United States' unwavering support for Georgia in the light of the challenges facing the region, he highlighted the importance of cooperation between the two countries towards enhancing Georgia's security and defense capabilities, strengthening its democratic institutions and economic development. During the meeting with Derek Scholes, the First Deputy Foreign Minister reiterated Georgia's solidarity with Ukraine and reaffirmed Georgia's commitment to continue its political as well as humanitarian support for Ukraine. During the meeting, the sides also discussed the difficult situation in the regions of Georgia occupied by Russia. Lasha Darsalia thanked the United States for its unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. The sides also highlighted U.S. support for Georgia on its path to European and Euro-Atlantic integration. Derek Scholes expressed his gratitude for Georgia's significant contribution to strengthening international security.
