27 May 2024 On 27 May 2024, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili, met with the delegation of the Community of Portuguese Language Countries (CPLP) led by the Executive Secretary of the Commonwealth, Zakaria da Costa, who is currently visiting Georgia. During the meeting, both parties welcomed the positive development of cooperation between Georgia and CPLP countries. They highlighted that this year marks the 10th anniversary of Georgia becoming an associate observer of the Commonwealth. The discussions also included prospects for deepening relations with the member states of the Commonwealth of Nations, particularly within the framework of the United Nations. Ilia Darchiashvili emphasized that developing global partnerships is a primary priority of Georgia's foreign policy. The conversation addressed the challenging humanitarian situation in Georgia's occupied territories. The Foreign Minister thanked the delegation members for their unwavering support of Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, and their commitment to a policy of non-recognition of the occupation. He stressed the importance of continued international support in this regard. The CPLP delegation is visiting Georgia from 25 to 28 May. During their visit, they are meeting with representatives of the legislative and executive branches of the government. The delegation also visited the village of Khurvaleti to observe the situation at the occupation line and attended the military oath-taking ceremony held in honor of Georgia's Independence Day.
