15 November 2017 15.11.2017, Strasbourg - On 15 November, the European Parliament adopted Resolution on Eastern Partnership by 519 votes to 114, with 47 abstentions, the European Parliament’s press office. The Resolution contains recommendations for the EU Council, the European Commission and European External Action Service (EEAS). In particular, the European Parliament calls upon the EU institutions to reiterate that Association Agreements with Georgia, Moldova and Ukraine do not constitute the final goal in their relations with the EU and that the Summit should set for these countries relevant long-term goals. The European Parliament also urges the EU institutions to acknowledge once again the European aspirations of these countries given that, pursuant to Article 49 of the Treaty of the European Union in line with the Rome Declaration of 25 March 2017, any European state may apply to become a member of the EU. Based on the Report, the European Parliament invites the Member States to agree to an ambitious declaration for the 2017 Summit that sets relevant long-term goals. The European Parliament’s report contains clear messages with respect to the sovereignty and territorial integrity of the EU’s eastern partners underlining the need for the the Russian Federation to comply with its international commitments still remaining outstanding. According to the Report, the EU should play a more active role in the peaceful resolution of all ongoing conflicts in its neighbourhood, taking into consideration the continued occupation of two Georgian regions. The European Parliament asks the Commission, the European Investment Bank and other multilateral financial institutions to work towards the successful implementation of the Investment Plan for Europe and of a dedicated support mechanism for Eastern Partnership countries committed to implementing the AAs; to request the establishment of a trust fund for Ukraine, Georgia and Moldova based on the best practices of multi-donor instruments, which could focus on private and public investments in social and economic infrastructure. The document also deals with such issues as providing education opportunities to citizens of eastern partners, providing support to Small and Medium-sized Enterprisesand and boosting infrastructure projects.
