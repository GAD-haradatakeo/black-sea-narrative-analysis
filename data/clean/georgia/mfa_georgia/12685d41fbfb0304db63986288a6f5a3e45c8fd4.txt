2 November 2023 "Georgia is committed to European integration, we are consistently getting closer to the European Union and we are optimistic that Georgia will succeed on this path", Georgian Foreign Minister Ilia Darchiashvili told reporters in Berlin, where he is participating in the conference at the invitation of his German colleague, Annalena Baerbock. Georgia has been implementing the agenda of ambitious reforms for years, and valuable and important work has been done to fulfil the 12 recommendations of the European Commission. Today we have tangible results and there are all grounds for Georgia to obtain the candidate status - the Georgian Minister of Foreign Affairs is quoted as saying. According to Darchiashvili, Georgia is actively working both with the European Union, in a bilateral format, and with EU members and partners, and is ready to move to a new stage of relations with the European Union. The Minister of Foreign Affairs thanked his German colleague for highlighting Georgia's European future. According to him, it is important that the enlargement package and the role of the European Union in terms of integration of East European countries are being discussed at the Conference.
