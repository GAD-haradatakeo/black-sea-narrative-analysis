16 December 2025 The First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Giorgi Zurabashvili, participated in the Eleventh Session of the Conference of the States Parties to the United Nations Convention against Corruption, held in Doha from 15 to 19 December 2025. On 16 December, during the General Debates, the First Deputy Minister addressed the participants of the Session. In his speech, Giorgi Zurabashvili discussed the anti-corruption reforms undertaken by the Government of Georgia, highlighting future plans and priorities. He also informed the session participants about a new legislative initiative, which is set to enter into force in March 2026 and aims to introduce more effective mechanisms for preventing and combating corruption. It was underscored that Georgia attaches great importance to the United Nations Convention against Corruption and that the declared objective of the Government of Georgia is to ensure that the country’s transparent anti-corruption mechanisms and policies are aligned as closely as possible with the highest international standards. The First Deputy Minister emphasized that Georgia also aims to rank among the world’s three least corrupt countries, noting that the firm anti-corruption policy pursued by the Government of Georgia and the progress achieved through ongoing reforms are reflected in international rankings. Giorgi Zurabashvili further underlined that Georgia attaches great importance to cooperation with the international community working on anti-corruption issues, as such cooperation is essential for strengthening national systems and advancing global anti-corruption efforts. It was also noted that Georgia actively cooperates with the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC), the Council of Europe’s Group of States against Corruption (GRECO), and other partner organizations.
