7 April 2022 The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili met with the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Netherlands, WopkeHoekstraon the sidelines of the NATO Ministerial. The Ministers welcomed the 30th anniversary of diplomatic relations between the Netherlands and Georgia and praised the close and friendly relations between the two countries. The Georgian Foreign Minister thanked the Netherlands for its support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as its European and Euro-Atlantic integration. WopkeHoekstrareaffirmed the desire of the Netherlands to strengthen cooperation with the Eastern Partnership countries, including with Georgia, which was reflected in the Foreign and Security Policy of the Netherlands. Georgia's progress on both European and Euro-Atlantic integration was also noted. Special attention was paid to Georgia’s EU application. The Georgian side expressed the hope that the Netherlands will support Georgia's European aspirations. Discussing the security environment in the region, the Ministers praised Georgia's support for Ukraine, focused on the challenges facing Georgia, the situation in the occupied territories, and thanked the Dutch Foreign Minister for his active involvement in the EU Monitoring Mission, which is an important tool for preventing escalation and ensuring stability on the ground. The Ministers positively apprised the high-level ties between Georgia and the Netherlands, including the high dynamics of communication between the Prime Ministers.
