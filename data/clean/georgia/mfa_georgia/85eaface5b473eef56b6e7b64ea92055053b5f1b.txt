10 February 2026 Within the framework of her visit to Hungary, Minister for Foreign Affairs of Georgia Maka Botchorishvili, participated in the Budapest Global Dialogue and delivered an address to the audience, focusing on contemporary challenges to international security and the growing spectrum of hybrid threats. “The erosion of the global order and the growing spread of hybrid threats have made today’s security environment increasingly complex. I represent a nation that understands the true value of peace, having endured the devastating consequences of war time and again. I represent a government that, amid an exceptionally challenging geopolitical landscape, has placed the defense of national sovereignty at the very heart of its policy,” Maka Botchorishvili noted. The Minister for Foreign Affairs placed particular emphasis on the importance of sovereignty, stating that “Sovereignty is not about isolation or rejecting cooperation. It is about responsibility. It is about the right - and the duty - of nations to make decisions based on their own history, their own interests, and the democratic will of their citizens”. The Minister further addressed the external pressures facing Georgia and the risks of interference in its internal affairs, noting that in recent years the country has become the target of unjust and unfounded attacks. “In recent years, Georgia has been placed under unprecedented international scrutiny. We have endured deliberate and unjust attacks - conducted under the claimed defense of democracy - yet directed against the will of the Georgian people, their elected government, and the legitimate actions of the state. We have clearly seen how international institutions, and even our genuine European aspirations, can at times be misused as instruments to exert pressure on sovereign nations and to challenge their sovereign rights.” In her remarks, the Minister underscored the importance of peace, noting that a country situated at a geopolitical crossroads has consciously chosen stability and dialogue, as “this approach is not a sign of weakness - it is about survival and peace.” Maka Bochorishvili also addressed the country’s economic resilience, noting that despite global crises, Georgia has demonstrated steady economic growth, recorded an increase in foreign direct investment, and advanced key sectors such as tourism, logistics, agriculture, and technology. “Our ports, transport corridors, and trade infrastructure connect the region and beyond, positioning Georgia as a hub for commerce and opportunity. This success is the result of bold policy choices, open markets, and an unwavering commitment to stability,” highlighted Maka Botchorishvili. The Minister drew particular attention to Georgia’s role as a regional connective bridge, underscoring its function as a vital link facilitating cooperation, trade, and dialogue across the broader region. “Our role as a bridge benefits Europe by enhancing connectivity, supporting the diversification of energy supplies, and opening new markets for trade and investment. In this way, Georgia is not only securing its own future - it is strengthening Europe’s stability, prosperity, and resilience, with the Black Sea serving as a gateway to regional cooperation and partnership,” emphasized the Minister.
