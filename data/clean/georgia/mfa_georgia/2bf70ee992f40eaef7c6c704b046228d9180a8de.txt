27 May 2017 27.05.2017, Tbilisi - Karl Lamers, Head of the Bundestag Delegation and Deputy Chairman of the Defence Committee of the German Parliament has spoken about the importance of holding the NATO Parliamentary Assembly in Georgia and bilateral relations between Georgia and Germany: “The holding of the NATO Parliamentary Assembly in Georgia is a fact of paramount importance. We received an invitation from Georgia as early as in 2014 and were happy to accept this invitation since Georgia is an important partner of NATO and the largest troop contributor, among non-member states, to the peacekeeping mission in Afghanistan, and NATO’s Enhanced Opportunities Partner. The bilateral relations between Georgia and Germany are very good. And now our presence in Georgia is a powerful signal that Germany supports Georgia on its Euro-Atlantic integration path. The NATO-Georgia Substantial Package was also developed through the support of Germany. We mutually support each other” – Karl Lamers said. German and French representatives of the NATO Parliamentary Assembly were received by Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze. The Georgian side paid special attention to Georgia’s foreign policy successes and reforms implemented in this area. Discussions focused on the progress made by Georgia on its European and Euro-Atlantic integration path. Mikheil Janelidze thanked the German and French representatives for their support and assistance. Special note was taken of the importance of holding the NATO Parliamentary Assembly in Georgia. Tbilisi hosts the NATO Parliamentary Assembly from 26 to 29 May. The session will be attended by up to 600 guests, including by over 300 parliamentarians from NATO member and partner states.
