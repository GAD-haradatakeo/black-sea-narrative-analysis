28 November 2019 28.11.2019, Strasbourg – The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani met with the President of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe(PACE),Liliane Maury Pasquier. Congratulating Georgia on the Presidency of the CoE Committee of Ministers, the PACE President wished Zalkaliani success in his future activities. The parties highlighted the importance of cooperation with the PACE in tackling the challenges facing the organization and effectively upholding its values and principles. Zalkaliani spoke extensively about the priorities of the Georgian Presidency : 1. Human Rights and Environmental Protection; 2. Civil Participation in Decision-Making; 3. Child Friendly Justice – Converging Experience on Restorative Justice in Europe; 4. Strengthening Democracy through Education, Culture and Youth Engagement. The PACE President pledged support in implementing all the four priorities presented by Georgia. During the meeting Zalkaliani focused on the security situation in Georgia’s occupied regions, including the illegal detention of doctor Vazha Gaprindashvili and the need of the international community’s principled reaction to such incidents.
