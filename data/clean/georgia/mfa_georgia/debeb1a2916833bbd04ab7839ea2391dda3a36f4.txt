6 April 2022 On 5 April 2022, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia met with representatives of the UK Parliament’s All-Party Parliamentary Group (APPG) on Georgia chaired by Jonathan Djanogly. During the meeting, the sides welcomed the strategic partnership established between the two countries and the busy agenda of relations in both bilateral and multilateral formats. During the conversation, the sides condemned Russia’s ongoing military aggression against Ukraine, which poses a direct threat to the European security architecture. The importance of the international community’s consolidated and consistent position on the violation of the fundamental principles of international law was underlined. The participants exchanged information on Georgia’s and the United Kingdom’s political, diplomatic and humanitarian efforts to support Ukraine. At the same time, the sides stressed that Georgian and British political parties are unanimous in their support for Ukraine. The sides focused on the difficult humanitarian situation in the occupied territories of Georgia, which is the result of Russia's intervention in Georgia in 2008. It was highlighted that the Kremlin's aggression against Ukraine has the echoes of the 2008 war and is yet another manifestation of Russia's fight for the spheres of influence.
