16 October 2018 16.10.2018, Brussels - The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Khatuna Totladze held her first meeting with UNICEF Georgia’s new representative, Dr. Ghassan Khalil. Presenting his letter of accreditation to the Deputy Minister, Ghassan Khalilspoke about the UNICEF’s activities in Georgia and its future plans. He highly appraised Georgia’s progress in the protection of child’s rights. For her part, Khatuna Totladze updated the UNICEF representative on the government’s recent reforms focused on the protection of the child’s rights. Khatuna Totladze provided UNICEF Georgia’s new Representative with comprehensive information on the grave humanitarian situation in the Russian-occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, the ban on native language education, additional restrictions on free movement and other gross violations of human rights. In this context, the importance of granting the international human rights monitoring mechanisms and organizations, including UNICEF an access to the occupied territories was highlighted. The sides also took special note of the significance of themother tongue-based multilingual education project. Khatuna Totladze brought to the attention of Ghassan Khalilthe government’s peace initiative “A Step toward a Better Future”, which aims at providing children and young people in the occupied territories with an access to all stages of education.
