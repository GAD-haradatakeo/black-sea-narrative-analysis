8 October 2019 “Naming Tbilisi UNESCO’s 2021 World Book Capital indicates the recognition of Tbilisi as an international cultural centre. The Foreign Ministry has been a strong avocate for this recognition and contributed directly to it” - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said at the joint briefing held at the Mediateka together with the Minister of Education, Science, Culture and Sports of Georgia and the Mayor of Tbilisi. According to the Minister, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs actively cooperates with the international partners in the framework of the cultural diplomacy in order to raise the awareness of Georgia in different countries of the world. “Building up Georgia’s positive image abroad is one of the priorities of the Georgian foreign policy. Therefore, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs actively contributes and participates in the implementation of similar projects,” – said the Minister. According to the Minister, thi decision will allow Tbilisi to host a variety of events during the next year that will be aimed at promotion book reading and book industry in general. Zalkaliani thanked the Tbilisi Mayor Kakhi Kaladze and his team for putting forward the idea and for their special efforts to make this idea realize. Director-General of UNESCO Audrey Azoulay has named the capital of Georgia, Tbilisi, World Book Capital for the year of 2021, per the recommendation of the World Book Capital Advisory Committee, which met in Switzerland in June 2019.
