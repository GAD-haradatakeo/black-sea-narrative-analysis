15 December 2021 Speaking on the air of Night Courier, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani discussed his meeting with the EU High Representative, Joseph Borrell within the framework of the Eastern Partnership Summit in Brussels. "We talked to Joseph Borrell about the situation in the region. First of all, I focused on the dire humanitarian situation in the occupied territories, the illegal detentions of our citizens, who still remain in Tskhinvali prison, and the need for the international community to strengthen its efforts to prevent such actions" - Zalkaliani said. According to the Foreign Minister, Georgia, together with its international partners, continued to take measures to prevent Russia’s aggressive policies and illegal actions in the occupied territories, and is determined to keep up cooperation in this direction. The sides also focused on the developments in the region. "We discussed the situation in the region, in particular, in the South Caucasus. The Georgian Prime Minister’s efforts of mediation between Azerbaijan and Armenia was highly praised. As far as you know, through the mediation of the Georgian Prime Minister, the detainees were released and the maps were provided to Azerbaijan. The EU is also aware of our initiative to turn Tbilisi into a platform for dialogue between the three countries of the South Caucasus. We exchanged views over this issue and Borrell pledged the European Union’s strong support in this regard. Yesterday, at the initiative of the President of the European Council, a meeting was held between the leaders of Azerbaijan and Armenia.
