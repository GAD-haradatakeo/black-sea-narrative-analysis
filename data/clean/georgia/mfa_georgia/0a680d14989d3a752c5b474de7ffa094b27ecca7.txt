31 May 2017 31.05.2017, Japan – “Japan ad Georgia share such common values and principles as democracy, human rights, the rule of law. We are now celebrating the 25th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations and are proud of remarkable relations that have been formed between our two countries. We are grateful for the financial and technical assistance Japan has been rendering to Georgia especially when the country was going through the most difficult period” – Mikheil Janelidze said during his meeting with Japanese media representatives. The Minister particularly focused on the liberal investment environment and transit opportunities offered to Georgia. He also spoke extensively about Georgia’s increasing interest in cooperation in the fields of robotics, High technologies and innovations. Discussions also focused on Georgia’s foreign policy course, the progress made by the country in its European and Euro-Atlantic integration process and strategic partnership with the U.S. Talking points also included the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and relations with the Russian Federation.
