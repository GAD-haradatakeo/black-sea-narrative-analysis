26 June 2024 A forum dedicated to Diaspora Day, "Strong Diaspora in the Service of the Motherland," will take place on 27 and 28 June 2024. Organized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and supported by the International Organization for Migration (IOM), this event will highlight significant contributions made by the Georgian diaspora. During the event, winners of the grant programs implemented by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs will be announced. Additionally, a special award will be presented to compatriots working abroad who have promoted Georgia internationally through their efforts. For several years, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs has successfully managed three notable grant programs: "Be a Young Ambassador of Your Country" (since 2018); "Support of Diasporic Initiatives" (since 2019); and "Support of Georgian Dance and Song Ensembles Operating Abroad" (since 2020). Through these programs, a total of 278 diaspora projects have received grants. Furthermore, since 2017, with support from the International Center for Migration Policy Development (ICMPD), the Ministry has been running the "Awarding a Diaspora with Special Merit" project. This initiative honors compatriots living abroad for their exceptional contributions. The annual forum dedicated to the Day of the Georgian Diaspora aims to strengthen the bonds and cooperation between the state and its diaspora, address current challenges and opportunities, and enhance the role of the diaspora in the country's development. Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili, will inaugurate the event and deliver an address. The forum will be attended by high-ranking officials from the executive and legislative branches, representatives of the Patriarchate, and other distinguished guests. On May 28, participants will engage in three thematic groups to discuss key issues such as education and culture, business and economy, and the role of information as a success mechanism for the diaspora.
