27 May 2017 27.05.2017, Tbilisi – Within the framework of the visit of the NATO Parliamentary Assembly to Georgia, Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze held a meeting with President of the NATO Parliamentary Assembly Paolo Alli. The sides emphasized that the fact of holding the spring session of the NATO Parliamentary Assembly in Georgia is a clear proof of NATO’s support for Georgia’s integration process and a good opportunity for representatives of the Assembly to get information on the reforms and processes ongoing in Georgia. According to Paolo Alli, the latest case of holding the NATO Parliamentary Assembly in a non-member state is reported to have taken place 15 yeas ago. The President of the NATO Parliamentary Assembly reaffirmed his firm support for Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic integration saying that supporting Georgia is in the interests of NATO members. The sides also spoke about the progress made by Georgia on its NATO integration path highlighting Georgia’s particular contribution towards the strengthening of common Euro-Atlantic security.
