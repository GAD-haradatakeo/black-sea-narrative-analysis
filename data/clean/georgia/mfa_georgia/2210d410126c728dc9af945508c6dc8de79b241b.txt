5 December 2022 On 5 December 2022, another round of political consultations between Georgia and Canada was held at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, which was co-chaired by the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, and the Assistant Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Canada for Europe, the Arctic, the Middle East and the Maghreb, Ambassador Sandra McCardell. During the consultations, a wide range of issues concerning the relations between the two countries were discussed, including cooperation in the fields of defense and security, trade and economy, people-to-people contacts and prospects for their further development. Special attention was paid to the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. The sides also discussed global and regional political and security issues and prospects for deepening cooperation in multilateral formats. The sides emphasized the strong relations between the countries and the importance of Canada's support to Georgia, especially in light of the challenges facing the region. Attention was focused on the importance of further deepening the political dialogue and dynamic cooperation between Canada and Georgia in all priority areas. The First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs thanked the Assistant Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Canada for fruitful cooperation with Georgia during the 30 years of diplomatic relations, as well as firm support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity within the internationally recognized borders.
