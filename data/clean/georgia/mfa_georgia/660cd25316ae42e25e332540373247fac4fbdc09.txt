23 September 2019 Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with his Danish counterpart, MinisterforForeign Affairs Jeppe Kofod,to discuss friendly relations between Georgia and the Kingdom of Denmark and prospects for further close cooperation, on the sidelines of the 74th session of the UN General Assembly. Discussing the successful relations between the two countries in many areas, the ministers took a special note of Denmark’s effective assistance in the process of Georgia’s institutional transformation. As far as it is known, by decision of the Danish Government Georgia was prioritized in the Danish Neighborhood Program (DANEP) strategy (for 2013-2017) allocating €27 million assistance to Georgia. The Danish Foreign Minister reaffirmed Denmark’s interests in and commitment to further support Georgia on its path to European integration. Discussions focused on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, including the ongoing illegal borderization, closure of checkpoints, and increasing number of detentions of civilians. According to the Danish Minister, the international community should employ all available instruments to achieve de-escalation of the situation and peaceful resolution of the conflict . Zalkaliani noted with satisfaction his first official visit to Denmark, after a lapse of many years and invited his colleague to Georgia. The two ministers also welcomed the establishment of direct flights between Kutaisi and Copenhagen that will significantly contribute to people-to-people and business-to-business relations.
