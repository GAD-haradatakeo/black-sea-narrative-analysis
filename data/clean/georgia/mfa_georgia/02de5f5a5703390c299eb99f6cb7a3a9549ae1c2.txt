24 April 2023 “The main message we received from our partners is that almost everyone supports Georgia’s European integration, European future, our common coexistence in the European family - and this is especially important for us, and we will continue our efforts to bring our country even closer to the European Union”, Foreign Minister Ilia Darchiashvili said following his participation in the European Union’s Foreign Affairs Council meeting in Luxembourg. According to the minister, support for the European future of Georgia was clearly felt in the positions and remarks of the EU foreign ministers. Speaking to journalists, Ilia Darchiashvili highlighted the issues discussed at the session of the Foreign Affairs Council. According to him, the EU foreign ministers spoke about the effectiveness of the reforms carried out by Georgia. Emphasis was placed on the implementation of the European Commission’s 12 recommendations - one of the main issues in terms of European integration. Special attention was paid to the security risks facing the region and the need for joint efforts to ensure security both in the region and in Europe. “Through such visits and meetings, we further strengthen the path Georgia has chosen to follow in its pursuit of EU integration” - Darchiashvili added. According to him, intensive and dynamic relations with European partners, and the steps taken by the Prime Minister of Georgia, in general, are vitally important and will result in very beneficial outcomes for our country in terms of achieving Georgia’s two foreign policy priorities - European and Euro-Atlantic integration.
