19 April 2022 On 19 April 2022, within the framework of his working visit to Prague, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia met with the Special Envoy for the Eastern Partnership of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Czech Republic, Jaroslav Kurfürst. The main topic of discussions was Georgia-EU relations and, in the second half of 2022, the future Czech Presidency of the EU Council. The Czech side welcomed Georgia's application for EU membership and reaffirmed the country's support for Georgia's European choice. The sides discussed the process of completing the European Commission’s questionnaire and the measures taken by the government to strengthen coordination. Teimuraz Janjalia underlined the full commitment of all agencies of the country to fill in the questionnaire as soon as possible. The sides highlighted the possibilities of the Czech Republic’s active involvement in various stages of Georgia's integration with the European Union. The Special Envoy briefed his Georgian counterpart on the country's priorities during the Czech presidency, and emphasized the importance of promoting security in Europe and strengthening the resilience of EU member and partner countries. The conversation focused on Russia's unprovoked and unjustifiable aggression against Ukraine and its impact on Georgia's security. The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister briefed his Czech counterpart on the situation in the occupied regions of Georgia and stressed the need for international partners’ greater involvement. Jaroslav Kurfürst strongly emphasized the Czech Republic’s strong support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders.
