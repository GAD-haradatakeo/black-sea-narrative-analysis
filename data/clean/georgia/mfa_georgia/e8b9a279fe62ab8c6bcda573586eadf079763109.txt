13 November 2018 “The opening of the Norwegian Embassy in Georgia will considerably contribute to the development of co-operation between our two countries in bilateral and multilateral formats and will also serve as an important signal for the Norwegian businesses to make further investments in Georgia” - the Norwegian Foreign Minister, Ine Eriksen Søreide said commenting on her meeting with her Georgian counterpart. The two ministers highlighted excellent relations between Georgia and Norway. It was underlined that Georgia with its investment climate, the rule of law, human rights protection and easiness of doing business is an attractive place for Norwegian companies. Special note was taken of the Norwegian Clean Energy Group’s investments in Georgia’s energy sector. According to the sides, the development of trade-economic co-operation will contribute to the implementation of the Free Trade Agreement between Georgia and EFTA that came into force on 1 September 2017. Ine Eriksen Søreide reaffirmed Norway’s unwavering support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. Zalkaliani thanked his Norwegian colleague for Norway’s open support 10 years after the Russia-Georgia. The sides positively appraised the practice of making joint statement in support for IDP Resolution on behalf of Baltic and Nordic countries. Special note was taken of Norway’s strong support for Georgia’s NATO integration. Talking points also included ways to develop people-to-people contacts.
