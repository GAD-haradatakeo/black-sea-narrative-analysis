17 May 2018 As a result of co-operation between Georgia’s Embassy to the Republic of Korea and the country’s tourist agency Hanjin Travel, which is affiliated with the country’s top airline company - Korean Air, the first charter flight was operated between Tbilisi and Seoul on 17 May. The Deputy Foreign Minister, Giorgi Shervashidze, the Georgian Ambassador to the Republic of Korea, Otar Berdzenishvili and the Head of the National Tourism Administration, Giorgi Chogovadze took part in the welcome ceremony held at the Tbilisi Shota Rustaveli International Airport. Direct flights will be implemented on 24 and 31 May and will bring over 600 Korean tourists to Georgia. “Direct flights between Georgia and the Republic of Korea may lead to the launch of regular flights, which will promote tourism and will deepen bilateral economic ties between the business communities of the two countries” - Deputy Foreign Minister Giorgi Sharvashidze said following the welcome ceremony at the airport.
