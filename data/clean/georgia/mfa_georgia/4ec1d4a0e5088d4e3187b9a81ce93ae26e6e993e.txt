17 May 2021 On 17 May 2021, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with his Armenian counterpart, GagikGhalachyan, who is on a two day working visit in Georgia. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of issues on the agenda of the two countries. Talking points included bilateral political, economic, cultural and humanitarian cooperation. The importance of peace and stability in terms of developing cooperation was also highlighted. According to Lasha Darsalia, taking into consideration the new realities in the region, the countries should upgrade their cooperation to a qualitatively higher level, in line with the current international agenda. Lasha Darsalia said that Georgia remains committed to the principles of cooperation, good-neighbourly relations and people-to-people contacts. For his part, the Deputy Foreign Minister, GagikGhalachyan spoke about the importance of active cooperation between Georgia and Armenia.
