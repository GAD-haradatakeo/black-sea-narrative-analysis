3 May 2019 3.05.2019, Tbilisi - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with Deputy Assistant Secretary of State for European and Eurasian Affairs George Kent, and the co-chairs of the U.S.-Georgia Strategic Partnership Commission's (SPC) Democracy and Governance Working Group. Along with various issues under the Strategic Partnership Charter, the sides discussed the reforms both already implemented and still ongoing in Georgia. Discussions also focused on all areas of strategic partnership between the two countries. The sides reconfirmed the commitment of effectively using all available formats of co-operation. Conversations also included ways to deepen bilateral economic co-operation both in trade and investment areas. The importance of the participation of US companies in the Anaklia Deep-Sea port project was also highlighted. The sides also exchanged views over the processes taking place in the region, including security challenges. Special attention was paid to the difficult security and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. The sides agreed on the need to keep the issue relating Georgia’s occupied territories high on the international agenda and the importance of the support of the U.S. as a key strategic partner of Georgia.
