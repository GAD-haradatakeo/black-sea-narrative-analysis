11 November 2019 11.11.2019, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with Secretary General of the Council of Europe Marija Pejčinović Burić who paid her first official visit to Georgia. Following the meeting David Zalkaliani made a statement for the press stating that “after 20 years of becoming a member of the CoE, Georgia will for the first time take over the presidency of the committee of Ministers on 27 November for a six-month term. Taking over the presidency is a huge responsibility for Georgia, and the country will remain committedto further promote the principles of democracy, human rights and the rule of law, which are the core values of ⁦the CoE.” Georgia worked out priorities for presidency and a 6-month calendar envisaging various meetings and conferences during the presidency. Both the priorities and the calendar will be unveiled a few days before Georgia officially assumes the presidency. At the meeting the partiesspoke about bilateralcooperation between Georgia and the CoE, grave human rights and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, Russian occupation of the Georgian territories and “illegal borderization,” as well as violation of human rights in the region. Within the framework of the visit, the Ministry hosted the the ceremony of launching the Council of Europe Action Plan for Georgia 2020-2023. The plan was developed as a result of constructive cooperation between the Council of Europe and Georgia and serves to bring Georgia closer to European standards in the areas of human rights, rule of law and democracy. The presentation of special commemorative coin dedicated to the 70th anniversary of the CoE and the 20th anniversary of Georgia’s CoE membership was also held within the scope of the CoE Secretary General’s visit to Georgia.
