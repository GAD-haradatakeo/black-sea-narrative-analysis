2 February 2022 On 2 February 2022, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Lasha Darsalia, met with the European Parliament's Standing Rapporteur on Georgia, MEP Sven Mikser, who paid a visit to Georgia in the process of preparing the European Parliament’s third Report on the Implementation of the EU Association Agreement. At the meeting, the sides focused on the security challenges facing the country and the region in general. The First Deputy Minister briefed the MEP on the deteriorating security, humanitarian and human rights situation in the Russian-occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali. The First Deputy Minister paid particular attention to the extremely tense security situation, which impacts human rights in the occupied regions and exacerbates the humanitarian crisis on the ground. The European Parliament's Standing Rapporteur on Georgia underscored the EU's unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders, and reiterated strong support for the country's sovereign right to choose its own foreign policy course. The sides discussed Georgia's progress on European and Euro-Atlantic integration course. Lasha Darsalia spoke about the irreversible process of Georgia's EU integration and the progress made by Georgia in the implementation of the Association Agreement, including the Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area. It was noted that this process will actively continue to prepare the country to apply for EU membership in 2024. The sides positively assessed the recent Eastern Partnership summit and the political declaration adopted, which includes a new EaP agenda.
