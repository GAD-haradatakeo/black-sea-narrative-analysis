14 November 2018 Within the framework of his visit to the Kingdom of Denmark, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani held a meeting with his Danish counterpart, Foreign Minister AndersSamuelsen. The Ministers discussed the successful relations between Georgia and Denmark both in bilateral and multilateral formats, to which attests the fact that Georgia and Ukraine were selected as priority countries to receive Danish support under the Danish Neighbourhood Programme. Highlighting Denmark’s unwavering support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, the Georgian Foreign Minister Denmark’s special role in the EUMM. Discussions focused on the situation in Georgia’s occupied regions, the Danish side expressed its concern over the occupation regime’s installation of artificial barriers in the Kareli District and the Russian Federation’s activities on the ground. Talking points also included Georgia’s success on the European integration path and the country’s future agenda. The Minister highlighted the unprecedented format of the EU-Georgia High Dialogue, the first meeting of which will be held in Brussels in the near future. The sides highly appraised Georgia’s important contribution to NATO’s peace operations. Discussions focused on the significance of Denmark’s practical assistance in Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic integration process and successful co-operation in the areas of defence and security. The Ministers expressed their close interest in facilitating trade-economic co-operation between the two countries, and for this purpose, the Free Trade Agreement with the EU creates a good opportunity.
