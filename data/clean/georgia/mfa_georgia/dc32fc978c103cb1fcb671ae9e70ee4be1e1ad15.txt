14 September 2024 From 9 to 11September 2024, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, participated in the international conference "Yerevan Dialogue 2024," held in Yerevan. During the forum, Darsalia delivered a speech at the panel titled "South Caucasus at the Crossroads: Between Fragmentation and Cooperation." In his remarks, he discussed the region's transit potential and its growing importance amid the current complex geopolitical situation, emphasizing Georgia's role and its readiness to foster regional cooperation. In addition to participating in the conference, Darsalia held bilateral meetings focused on energy and sustainability with high-ranking officials, including the UAE’s Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Abdulla Balaala; Serbia's State Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Nikola Stojanović; Kazakhstan's Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Albek Kuantirov; and North Macedonia’s Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade, Zoran Dimitrovski. These meetings covered bilateral relations, cooperation within international organizations, and prospects for further collaboration. The "Yerevan Dialogue" conference, held for the first time this year, was co-organized by Armenia’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs and India’s prestigious research organization, the Observer Research Foundation (ORF). The conference addressed five key thematic areas: the new international order, connectivity, green transit and the future of energy, digital communities as drivers of a stable and secure economy, and the future of work.
