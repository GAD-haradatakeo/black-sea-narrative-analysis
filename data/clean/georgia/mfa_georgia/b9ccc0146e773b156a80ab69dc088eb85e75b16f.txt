19 November 2021 "The goal for Georgia is to ensure that we have a strong diaspora, with strong cultural and educational centers abroad, which actively participates in the development of the country and pursues Georgia's interests" - the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani is quoted as saying at the Parliament’s plenary session “Minister’s Hour”. The Foreign Minister briefed MPs on the projects already implemented and ongoing in 2021 with international partners and organizations. "The implementation of the first stage of the construction-rehabilitation works for the leuville Georgian-French cultural centre and Georgian Academy – the so-called orangery project was completed as scheduled, in 2021” - said David Zalkaliani, adding that from July 2021 the leuville estate was transferred for management to the Ministry of Culture, Sport and Youth of Georgia. According to him, the existing diaspora programs have been successfully implemented this year, in particular, the "Sunday School Support Program", the "Diaspora Initiatives Support Program", the "Be a Young Ambassador of Your Country" program. In 2021, a new grant program was launched - "Support for Georgian Dancing and Singing Groups Abroad". "A pilot program for teaching Georgian language specifically designed for Greek public schools is being successfully implemented. A similar program may be introduced in other European cities as well. Despite the difficult situation in the world, 11 new Sunday schools were opened abroad. Four of them were launched within the framework of the 'Diaspora Initiatives Support Program' implemented by the Ministry," the Minister explained. David Zalkaliani focused on the establishment of new Georgian cultural centers in the future and on the importance of engaging diaspora representatives in the development of the country.
