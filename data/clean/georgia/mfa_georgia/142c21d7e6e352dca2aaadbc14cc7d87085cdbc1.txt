6 April 2021 On 6 April 2021, the newly appointed Ambassador of Romania to Georgia, Răzvan Rotundu presented copies of his credentials to the Vice Prime Minsiter and Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani. At the meeting following the presentation ceremony, the sides discussed a broad spectrum of cooperation between Georgia and Romania and welcomed the commitment of the two countries to upgrade their partnership up to the strategic level. Discussions also focused on close economic cooperation between Georgia and Romania. In this light, the sides emphasized the need to expand transport and communications cooperation and carry out joint projects in these two sectors, in order to put the Black Sea potential to its fullest possible use. Speaking about Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration process, Zalkaliani paid special attention to the forthcoming Eastern Partnership summit scheduled for autumn and Georgia’s expectations in this regard. Talking points also included the need of Black Sea security cooperation within the framework of NATO, active interaction within the international organizations and the challenges facing the region. For his part, the Romanian Ambassador reaffirmed Romania’s full commitment to Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its European and Euro-Atlantic integration.
