29 December 2020 During the presentation of the annual report of the Ministry, David Zalkaliani discussed steps taken in terms of supporting Georgian candidates nominated to various international organisation. According to the Minister, in this regard, efforts were actively exerted. Zalkaliani made particular emphasis on the fact that for the first time in history a Georgian candidate was selected to the International Criminal Court, which is a huge success for Georgia. Georgian candidates were also selected to the Children’s Rights Committee (CRC) and to the position of legal advisor to the permanent secretariat of BSEC. The GoG (government of Georgia) has re-nominated Zurab Pololikashivili to the position of a candidate for Secretary General of UN’s World Tourism Organisation (UNWTO) for the 2022-2025 term. Georgia will also assume the chairmanship of GUAM for the duration of 1 year on the 1st of January of 2021. With the aim to conduct democratic reforms, economic development, protection of human rights, de-occupation of Georgia’s territories and peaceful resolution of the Russo-Georgian conflict, Georgia pays particular attention to cooperation with international organisation.
