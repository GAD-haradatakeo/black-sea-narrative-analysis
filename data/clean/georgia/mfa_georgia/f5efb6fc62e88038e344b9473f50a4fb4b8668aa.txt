17 October 2022 "The Norwegian Foreign Minister’s visit is a clear proof of the increasing level of bilateral relations between Georgia and Norway and of Norway's responsible engagement in and solidarity with the East European region in this extremely difficult geopolitical context for us" - the Georgian Foreign Minister, Ilia Darchiashvili said evaluating the visit of his Norwegian counterpart, Anniken Huitfeldt to Tbilisi. According to Darchiashvili, Georgia considers Norway as a close friend and a reliable partner, sharing with it common European values ​​and interests in building a more peaceful, sustainable and equal world. "This year we are celebrating the 30th anniversary of the re-establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries. It is with pride I’d like to note that over the past three decades, the close friendly ties and close cooperation between Georgia and Norway have brought concrete tangible results in a number of directions. Special note should be taken of our fruitful cooperation within the framework of defense and security, economy, energy, "international development cooperation", the Minister said. Darchiashvili drew special attention to the long-term activities of Norway as one of the largest donors in Georgia, which has contributed to the country's democratic modernization and socio-economic development. The sides also highlighted Norway’s role in the implementation of the inclusive education model in Georgia and the preservation and rehabilitation of our cultural heritage monuments. The Georgian Minister of Foreign Affairs explained that the sides discussed the security challenges facing the region and Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations. He thanked his Norwegian colleague for her strong support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, for her significant contribution to the process of peaceful settlement of the Russia-Georgia conflict, as well as to the confidence-building process.
