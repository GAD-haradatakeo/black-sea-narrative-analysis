10 September 2021 On 10 September, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani held a meeting with the UN Resident Coordinator in Georgia, Sabine Makhl. The sides discussed long-term and fruitful cooperation between the UN team and Georgia. Emphasis was placed on the UN team’s efforts towards the sustainable development and improvement of the economic and social situation in Georgia, as well as on the UN’s important role in promoting the implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals in Georgia. The sides discussed the 2021-2025 United Nations Sustainable Development Cooperation Framework between Georgia and the UN. Signed on 2 October 2020, the framework document sets out a plan of cooperation between Georgia and the UN for the next 5 years and aims to support the implementation of the 2030 Sustainable Development Agenda in Georgia. The sides paid special attention to Georgia’s national development priorities presented in the document, including the need to strengthen the security of the conflict-affected population, which is especially important given the dire humanitarian and human rights situation in the Russian-occupied territories of Georgia.
