5 December 2024 “Using this opportunity, I would like, once again, clearly state that the current government of Georgia is the one who created the solid foundation for the irreversibility of Georgia’s European future and remains firmly faithful to the commitments related to European integration. Since the European Union has not yet made a decision on opening accession negotiations with Georgia, claims that the Georgian government suspended negotiations with the EU are not true” - the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili said during her speech at the 31st OSCE Ministerial Council meeting in Malta. According to the Minister, Georgia’s key foreign policy priority is European integration, and political speculations on this vital issue have become the source for polarization and form an additional threat to the country's stability and security. As the Minister noted, despite the most difficult security environment, Georgia has managed to achieve significant transformation and visible progress in terms of democratic and economic development, and this fact is duly attested by reputable international rankings and reports. In her speech, the Minister of Foreign Affairs expressed gratitude to the OSCE/ODIHR for monitoring the parliamentary elections. According to the Minister, despite the fact that more than 2 million Georgian citizens have made their choice in the presence of an unprecedentedly large number of international and local observers, speculations related to the elections do not stop, and these actions are used to create an artificial political crisis in Georgia that leads to destabilization. “We believe that the final report of the OSCE/ODIHR based on objective assessments will allow us to continue cooperation on implementation of its recommendations” – the Minister said.
