15 November 2018 15.11.2018, Tbilisi – The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili participated in the event to mark the 100th anniversary of independence of Latvia. On behalf of the Government of Georgia, he congratulated the Latvian people on the Day of Independence. According to him, 2018 is the important milestone in the history of both countries as like Latvia, the First Democratic Republic of Georgia was also established in 2018. Speaking about the friendly relations between Georgia and Latvia, the Deputy Minister said that 2018 marks the 25th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries. “Over these 25 years the bilateral political dialogue, economic cooperation, interactions across variety of fields, people-to-people and regional ties between our countries have flourished. At the same time Latvia has been one of the strongest supporters of Georgia on the way of building a strong, democratic and prosperous state, Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity as well as our European and Euro-Atlantic Integration processes. I am confident that our excellent relations and close friendship will further advance to produce the mutual benefit for our people” - Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili said. The event dedicated to the 100th anniversary of independence of Latvia was held at the Tbilisi State Conservatoire. The exhibition “Century of Latvia” and the concert “Three Latvian Tenors” were conducted on the sidelines of the event.
