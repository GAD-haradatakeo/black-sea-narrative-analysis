24 September 2021 In the framework of the 76th session of the UN General Assembly, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Estonia, Eva-Maria Liimets. The Ministers highly appraised the bilateral relations and fruitful cooperation between the two countries in various fields, including economy and sustainable development, trade and tourism, education and culture, e-government and communications. The sides expressed special interest in deepening economic cooperation, which has a great potential in terms of overcoming post-pandemic challenges. Zalkaliani thanked his Estonian counterpart for the projects implemented in Georgia within the framework of development cooperation aimed at helping Georgia implement fundamental reforms. According to Zalkaliani, the strategy of cooperation between Estonia and Georgia is in full compliance with the priority areas for Georgia, such as education, environmental protection, information technologies, institutional development, entrepreneurship, etc. The meeting focused on the process of Georgia's integration into NATO, as well as on prospects for the development and deepening of cooperation with the European Union. The progress achieved by Georgia on the path of European and Euro-Atlantic integration was also highlighted. The Georgian Foreign Minister briefed his Estonian counterpart on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and stressed the importance of Estonia's support within the framework of international organizations. In particular, Zalkaliani thanked his Estonian counterpart for supporting Georgia-related resolutions adopted by the UN General Assembly and the Human Rights Council, the Council of Europe and the OSCE on Georgia.
