16 November 2020 Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani participated in the 2020 Ministerial to Advance Freedom of Religion or Belief held virtually, on 16 November. The event was hosted by Poland, in cooperation with the U.S. This ministerial is part of an international effort to defeat religious persecution and protect religious freedom and human rights. Polish Minister of Foreign Affairs Zbigniew Rau and U.S. Secretary of State Mike Pompeo will deliver keynote remarks delieved opening remarks. Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani also spoke before the audience. Zalkaliani spoke on the ways to protect and preserve religious freedom and human rights in the face of the COVID-19 pandemic. He said that Georgia is proud of its centuries-long tolerance to various religious minorities living in the country, stating that Russia harshly violates religious rights and freedom of the Georgian citizens living in the currently occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. According to him, They are unable to carry out religious rituals and conduct services. People are not allowed to visit the graves of their ancestors due to the barbed-wire-fences illegally erected by occupying forces.
