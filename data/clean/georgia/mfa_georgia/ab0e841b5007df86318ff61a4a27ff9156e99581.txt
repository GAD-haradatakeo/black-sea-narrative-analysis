23 February 2021 The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, held a telephone conversation with his Estonian counterpart, Eva-Maria Liimets. The Ministers underscored the outstanding, friendly relations and partnership between the two states. The Estonian party, once again, confirmed that it remains a significant partner of Georgia, staunchly supporting Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, along with European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations. The existing geopolitical situation and the significance of coordinated cooperation in terms of achieving interests of both nations in the face of the challenges were underscored. The head of Georgia’s Foreign Ministry expressed his gratitude towards his Estonian colleague for keeping the Georgian issue high on Estonia’s political agenda, including in terms of Estonia’s bilateral relations with the Russian Federation. The Ministers also touched upon the issue of the political environment in Georgia, underscoring the importance of constructive mode of operation. The Ministers expressed readiness to continue active cooperation both in terms of bilateral as well as multilateral relations on all relevant issues of international nature, exchanging high-level visits.
