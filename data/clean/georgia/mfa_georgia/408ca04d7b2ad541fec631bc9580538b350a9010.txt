6 February 2019 06.02.2019, Washington – Prospects for further enhancement of Georgia-U.S. co-operation and strategic partnership were discussed at the meeting between the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani and the Co-chairman of theSub-committee on Europe of the CommitteeonForeign Relations, Senator Ron Johnson(R-Wisc.) The Minister thanked the Senator for his unequivocal support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its Euro-Atlantic integration. According to the U.S. Senator, Georgia Support Act initiated by the U.S. Congress, as well as many other resolutions and statements issued in support for Georgia clearly indicate that Georgia is high on the U.S. political agenda. Ron Johnson praised Georgia for its progress in democratic reforms, economic development and Euro-Atlantic integration. He also expressed gratitude for Georgia’s important contribution to the international peacekeeping operations including the Resolute Support Mission.
