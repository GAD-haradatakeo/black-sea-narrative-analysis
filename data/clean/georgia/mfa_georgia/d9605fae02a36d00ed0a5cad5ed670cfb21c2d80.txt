22 June 2020 The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, responds to the resolution on “cooperation with Georgia” adopted by the UN Human Rights Council, which outlines the existing situation on the ground in the occupied territories of Georgia in terms of human rights, underscoring the necessity for the members of the global, international community to have free access to said regions, obligating the High Commissioner of UN on Human Rights to annually provide a written report, along with an oral presentation, regarding the state of Human Rights environment in Georgia’s occupied regions. According to the statement of the Minister, for the duration of 4 years Georgia has been actively engaged within the Human Rights Council, in order to ensure the adoption of such important resolutions. As explained by Zalkaliani, the mere fact, that Georgia manages to receive an increasing amount of votes in support of the resolution in question year after year, shows the extremely positive dynamic, which confirms the support of the international community towards our country, further solidifying the place of the Georgian issue on the agenda of this international arena.
