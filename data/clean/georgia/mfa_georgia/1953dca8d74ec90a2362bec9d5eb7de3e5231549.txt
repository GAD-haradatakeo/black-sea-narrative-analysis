25 November 2019 The Georgian Foreign Ministry has joined the Violence against Women 16-Day Government Campaign. Sexual harassment has been determined as the main focus of the Campaign for 2019. The aim of the campaign is to raise public awareness about sexual harassment and the laws regulating the offence. Sexual harassment is a violation of human rights and has been made a punishable offence since making a legislative amendment in May 2019. Within the scope of the campaign, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs plans to conduct various awareness-raising events. Besides, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, together with Georgia’s diplomatic representations abroad will support and promote the Georgian government’s activities directed against gender violence. Within the 16-day campaign, the Ministry will pay special attention to women’s human rights situation in Georgia’s conflict zones. The Georgian government-led campaign involves all state agencies that are members of the Inter-Agency Commission on Gender Equality, Violence against Women and Domestic Violence. Sexual harassment in public places is now punishable by a fine of 300 GEL, or, in case of aggravating circumstances - by a fine ranging from 500 to 1000 GEL, or by corrective work for up to 1 month or administrative detention for up to 10 days. Those affected by sexual harassment in public places must immediately call the police (by dialing 112) in order to ensure that adequate evidence is detected and an alleged offender is detained.
