14 November 2019 While reviewing the CoE Secretary General’s 20th Consolidated Report on the Conflict in Georgia at the Committee of Ministers Deputies of the Council of Europe, a statement on behalf of the EU was made where the EU reaffirms its support for peaceful resolution of the conflicts and confidence building projects. It is stated that the EU will continue to support the human rights and humanitarian projects in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, including within the projects to be carried out under the Council of Europe Action Plan for Georgia 2020-2023. According to the Statement, the EU does not recognize the legitimacy of the so-called presidential elections in Abkhazia and the so-called parliamentary elections in South Ossetia conducted during the reporting period. The EU expresses its concern over the deteriorating human rights and humanitarian situation on the ground. Unprecedented restrictions on the freedom of movement have made their adverse effects on the local population. Reference is made to the death of Margo Martiashvili as the recent tragic example of restriction of free movement. The EU also highlights the necessity of immediately opening all so-called crossing points on the ABL with the Tskhinvali region. The EU expresses its concern over the human rights violations continuing with impunity in the occupied regions and points to the necessity of investigating and serving justice in cases concerning tragic loss of life of Georgian citizens. The EU expresses its concern that illegal detentions, unprecedented restrictions on freedom of movement, erection of barbed wire fences and other barriers lead to the separation of families and communities, increases the number of IDPs and affects the right to education in the native language. In its statement the EU calls on the Russian Federation to comply with the six-point ceasefire agreement of 12 August 2008 and grant the EUMM access to Georgia’s occupied territories.
