17 October 2019 17.10.2019, Tbilisi – According to David Zalkaliani, Georgia-U.S. relationship is today at an all-time high and Georgia enjoys the bipartisan support of the U.S. executive and legislative authorities. He highlighted the importance of the Joint Declaration marking the 10th anniversary of the U.S. –Georgia Strategic Partnership Charter, which defines all priority areas of cooperation between the two countries. The Minister attached paramount importance to submission of the Georgia Support Act to the Congress. The Act reaffirms U.S. support for Georgia’s sovereignty, independence and territorial integrity as well as for the strengthening of Georgia’s security and defence capabilities. According to him, the Consolidated Appropriations Act contains important messages regarding the de-occupation of Georgia’s occupied regions. To achieve the goals of the Act, USD 127 mln was allocated to Georgia exceeding by USD 22 mln the allocation for the previous year. The increased funding highlights the importance of Georgia to the U.S. as the region’s leader and a strong ally of the United States. According to Zalkaliani, strategic partnership with the U.S. will further develop, including within U.S.-Georgia Strategic Partnership Commission.
