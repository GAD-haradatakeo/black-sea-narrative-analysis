20 September 2024 On 20 September 2024, the OSCE Permanent Council held a session to discuss the 16th anniversary of Russia's large-scale military aggression against Georgia in 2008. Lasha Darsalia, Georgia's First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, addressed the Council, highlighting the severe security and human rights situation in Georgia's occupied territories. He also underscored Georgia’s peace policy, which focuses both on de-occupation and on rebuilding trust and reconciliation between communities divided by the war. During the session, Georgia’s Friends Group in the OSCE, comprising the USA, Bulgaria, United Kingdom, Denmark, Estonia, Ireland, Iceland, Canada, Latvia, Lithuania, Norway, Poland, Romania, Slovakia, Ukraine, Finland, Sweden, and the Czech Republic, issued a supportive statement. The statement reiterated the Group's unwavering support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders. It also expressed concern over the continued occupation of Abkhazia and South Ossetia and stressed the need for a peaceful resolution in line with international law, including the UN Charter and the OSCE Helsinki Final Act. The Friends Group called on Russia to fulfill its obligations under the August 12, 2008 ceasefire agreement, withdraw its forces to pre-conflict positions, reverse the illegal recognition of the occupied territories' independence, and ensure the safe, dignified return of all internally displaced persons (IDPs) and refugees. The statement also referenced the January 2021 European Court of Human Rights ruling on the 2008 conflict, which found that after the August 12 ceasefire, Russia maintained effective control over Abkhazia and South Ossetia and was responsible for severe human rights violations in those regions. Concern was expressed regarding the ongoing humanitarian, human rights, and security challenges in the occupied territories. The Friends Group reiterated support for the Geneva International Discussions and emphasized the importance of progress on key issues like the non-use of force, the establishment of international security arrangements on the ground, and the safe, dignified return of IDPs and refugees, in line with international law. They also supported the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanisms (IPRM) in Ergneti and Gali and called for the unconditional resumption of the IPRM’s work in Gali. The Group further endorsed the full implementation of the EU Monitoring Mission's mandate and expressed support for the mission’s continued efforts. They also commended Georgia’s peace initiatives, including the “Step to a Better Future” and “Peace Fund for a Better Future” programs, aimed at improving socio-economic conditions and building trust between communities in Abkhazia and South Ossetia.
