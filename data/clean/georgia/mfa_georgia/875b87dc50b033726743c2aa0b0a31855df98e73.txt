20 January 2023 On 20 January 2023, presentation of the results of European Union’s innovative project for the competitiveness of the private sector in Georgia was held at the Radisson Blu Iveria hotel. In his welcome speech, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Vladimir Konstantinidi, emphasized the growing interest of Georgian compatriots living abroad in contributing to the development of the country's economy. At the closing session of the project, the IOM Georgia Chief of Mission, Sanja CelebicLukovac, the Thematic Coordinator for Economic Development and Market opportunities at the Delegation of the European Union to Georgia, Dominik Papenheim, and representatives of the project's partner organizations, local governments and diaspora addressed the public. The project's migration component, among other things, was devoted to sharing the knowledge and experience of compatriots living abroad in the process of strengthening and increasing the competitiveness of the private sector of Georgia. Within the framework of the project, the participation of Georgian companies in the foreign exhibition, contact with potential partners, search for export opportunities in Europe took place with the active participation of the representatives of the Georgian diaspora. The team of the United Nations Migration Agency (IOM), involved in the implementation of the innovative project of the European Union, invited several highly qualified representatives of the diaspora to share their experience in Georgia. The innovative project of the European Union is a joint initiative of four agencies of the European Union and the United Nations - the United Nations Development Program (UNDP), the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), the Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) and the International Organization for Migration (IOM).
