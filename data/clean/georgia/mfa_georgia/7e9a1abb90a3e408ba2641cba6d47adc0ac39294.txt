1 December 2021 The First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia took part in a public discussion organized by the Alliance and Latvian Institute of International Affairs within the framework of the NATO Ministers of Foreign Affairs held in Riga, dedicated to the Alliance's 2030 Strategy. The event was opened by NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg and the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Latvia, Edgars Rinkēvičs. the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, the former Foreign Minister of Lithuania, LinasLinkevičius, the Co-Chair of the Warsaw Security Forum, and the Director of the Global Europe Program at the Wilson Center Katarzyna Pisarska, Daniel Hamilton Director delivered speeches at the session devoted to NATO and its Partners. In his speech, the First Deputy Minister gave a detailed overview of the process of Georgia's integration into NATO and the progress achieved in this direction. He stressed Georgia’s important role in ensuring security in the region and its successful involvement in NATO-led operations and missions. He also highlighted Georgia’s growing role in NATO' s efforts to strengthen security in the Black Sea region, and stressed successful cooperation with the Alliance. Darsalia stressed that the Alliance should maintain its strong position regarding the Open Door Policy in its future strategy. The First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs reaffirmed Georgia's firm choice to join the Alliance and the commitment to take appropriate steps in this direction.
