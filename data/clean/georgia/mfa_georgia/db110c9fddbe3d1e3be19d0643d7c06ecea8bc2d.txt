28 February 2021 Within the framework of the official ceremony that took place at the Tbilisi International Airport the Georgian Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani, met with the Preside4nt of the European Council, Charles Michele. The Vice Prime Minister expressed gratitude towards the President of the European Council for holding a visit to Georgia, especially considering the complex international environment, further exacerbated by the global pandemic. The importance of cooperation was underscored in the framework of the Eastern Partnership Initiative, along with stable and sustainable development of the region. In this regard, particular emphasis was made on Georgia’s case, as an associated state of the EU. The necessity for all sides to exert efforts in terms of conducting political processes in Georgia in a constructive manner, via the means of dialogue, was also noted, especially considering the difficulties associated with the current global pandemic and general security issues faced by Georgia. Taking into consideration the existing security environment of the region, the significance of EU’s active and tangible engagement was underscored, in order to ensure peaceful resolution of the conflict, along with security in the region.
