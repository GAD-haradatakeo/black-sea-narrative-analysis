29 December 2020 During the final meeting held on December 29th of 2020 the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, talked about the importance of the strategic partnership between Georgia and the US, underscoring the achievements accomplished during the year of 2020. “This year was of particular significance in terms of our cooperation with the US. Georgia was visited by the US State Secretary, Mike Pompeo; the visit, in terms of timing and content came as a clear demonstration of the support of Georgia on behalf of the US,” – explained the Minister. According to Zalkaliani, said visit was being organised for a prolonged period of time and represented the culmination of the fruitful and intensive cooperation that Georgia has had with this administration. The head of the Georgian diplomatic institution noted that intensive work with the new US administration will be important, in order to ensure the engagement of the US in terms of the process of developing Georgia’s defence and security capabilities as well as the Georgian economy. “Work will continue with the US in terms of the free trade agreement, in order to bring our trading/economic relations to a high mark, corresponding to the high levels of our cooperation in terms of political relations, defence and security, etc,” - stated the Minister. David Zalkaliani also discussed Georgia’s relations with Europe, pointing out that, despite the difficulties associated with the pandemic, throughout the year of 2020, a rather dynamic relationship continued with Europe. “The process of elevating our bilateral partnership to strategic levels must continue, with consequent deepening of relations with the traditionally friendly allies of Georgia in Europe,” – noted the Minister.
