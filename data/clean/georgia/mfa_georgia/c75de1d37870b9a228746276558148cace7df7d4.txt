5 May 2020 The Georgian government continues the orderly and gradual repatriation process. Today the charter flight will bring home Georgians from Athens. Among passengers are persons with special needs, chronic, oncological or other grave health conditions, pregnant women, minors and elderly people. Aboard the flight will also be the Georgians who had been transiting through Greece and are now unable to leave the country due to coronavirus-related restrictions, as well as our fellow citizens who have been left without shelter because of the pandemic. Persons with grave health conditions have been transported by specialmobile resuscitationvehicle to the airport, as organized by the embassy. The Consulate General of Georgia in Thessalonica also arranged for the transportation of Georgian citizens to Athens Under the Coordination Council’s schedule, flights have been performed from various cities of the world, including from Berlin, Vienna, London, Athens, U.S., Larnaca, Amsterdam, Beijing, Rome, Paris, Tel-Aviv, Barcelona, Warsaw, Madrid, Prague, etc. Georgia’s diplomatic representations abroad have assisted 18,857 citizens of Georgia in the host countries. The Government has organized the repatriation 9,630 citizens to Georgia, as of 5 May The Georgian MFA website is updated every day to provide the latest information regarding support measures undertaken by the Georgian government with respect to Georgian citizens abroad.
