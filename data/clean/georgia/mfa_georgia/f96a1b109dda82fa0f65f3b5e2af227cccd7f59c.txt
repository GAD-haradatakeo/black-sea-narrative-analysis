29 October 2018 29.10.2018, Istanbul –As part of his official visit to Istanbul, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani met his Azerbaijani counterpart, foreign Minister Elmar Mammadyarov. The two ministers reaffirmed the strategic partnership and close friendship between Georgia and Azerbaijan. They gave high appraisal to the the Georgian Prime Minister Mamuka Bakhtadze’s recent visit to Azerbaijan and high-level meetings held in Baku. Discussing the issues relating to regional co-operation including the trilateral Azerbaijan-Turkey-Georgia meeting, the sides highlighted the great potential of this format for activating people-to-people contacts, as well as relations in the areas of culture and tourism. Talking points included the involvement of the two countries in international transit and energy projects. The international importance of the Baku-Tbilisi-Kars (BTK) railway participation, especially in terms of realizing One belt One road Initiative, was also highlighted. The Ministers welcomed the launch of the Trans-Anatolian Pipeline and agreed to further work towards popularizing the transit potential of Azerbaijan and Georgia. The Ministers discussed the security situation in the region and reaffirmed their support for the territorial integrity of each other’s countries and their commitment to resolution of the conflicts in compliance with the norms and principles of international law.
