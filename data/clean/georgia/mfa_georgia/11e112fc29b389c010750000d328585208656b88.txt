26 September 2025 On 26 September 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, participated in and delivered remarks at the Sixth Annual Ministerial Meeting of the Group of Friends of Universal Health Coverage (UHC) and Global Health, of which Georgia serves as a co-chair alongside Japan and Thailand. The theme of this year’s meeting was “Strategic Investments for Sustainable Health Systems: Strengthening Health Systems and Promoting Universal Health Coverage in a Changing World.” The event was also addressed by the Director-General of the World Health Organization (WHO), Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus. In his statement, the Deputy Minister highlighted Georgia’s efforts and progress since the introduction of the Universal Health Coverage system in 2013. He noted that in 2025 Georgia launched a comprehensive reform of primary healthcare and outlined its key directions. Darsalia emphasized that these reforms are grounded in strong cooperation with the WHO, partners, and professional organizations, thereby ensuring that Georgia’s progress aligns with global commitments, including the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. In conclusion, the Deputy Minister stressed that in a rapidly changing world, health systems must be resilient, inclusive, and future-oriented. He underlined that Georgia’s experience demonstrates how strategic investments in primary healthcare, digital health, and financial protection not only strengthen resilience but also bring the country closer to the vision of universal health coverage.
