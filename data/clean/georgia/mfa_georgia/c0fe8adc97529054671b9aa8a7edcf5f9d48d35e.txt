21 January 2022 On 21 January 2022, the First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with the newly appointed Special Representative of the OSCE Chairperson-in-Office for the South Caucasus, Co-Chair of the Geneva International Discussions, Viorel Moşanu. The sides discussed the difficult security, human rights and humanitarian situation in the occupied territories getting increasingly worse due to the Russian Federation’s provocative steps amid the pandemic. Discussions also focused on the process of de-facto annexation of the occupied territories of Georgia by Russia, increasing militarization, the so-called Borderization, closure of the occupation line in the Tskhinvali region, restriction of free movement, which isolates the population living in the occupied regions and further aggravates the humanitarian crisis on the ground. Special attention was paid to the illegal detention of Georgian citizens as a hybrid tool used by the Russian Federation, as well as to the need of immediate release of Irakli Bebua, Mamuka Chkhikvadze and other illegally detained persons. The sides discussed the dynamics of the Geneva International Discussions, Russia's destructive steps and the need to make progress on key issues on the negotiating agenda, such as Russia's implementation of the ceasefire agreement, implementation of the non-use of force, establishment of international security mechanisms and return of internally displaced persons. The sides raised the issue of resuming the Incident and Response Mechanism (IPRM) meetings in Gali without any preconditions, as well as the smooth and efficient functioning of the Ergneti IPRM. The sides also discussed the current developments in the region and stressed that Russian-led occupation and de facto annexation is part of Russia's aggressive policy in the Black Sea and South Caucasus region. Speaking about the situation in the South Caucasus, the need to strengthen the international community’s involvement in the region was stressed.
