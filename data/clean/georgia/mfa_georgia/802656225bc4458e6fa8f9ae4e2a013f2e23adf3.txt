1 June 2018 On 1 June 2018, the Holy See’s new Apostolic Nuncio to Georgia Msgr. Jose Bettencourt presented copies of his credentials to the Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze. Following the official ceremony, the sides discussed a broad spectrum of bilateral and multilateral relations between Georgia and the Holy See. The Vice Prime Minister congratulated Msgr. Jose Bettencourt on his appointment as the Nuncio of the Holy See. According to the Minister, the close relations between Georgia and the Holy See are based on the latter’s support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and non-recognition policy. Mikheil Janelidze also spoke about the importance of the Holy See’s various cultural and educational projects in Georgia that bring the two states closer to each other and expressed his gratitude for scholarships awarded by the Holy See to Georgian students. According to the new Nuncio, the Holy See highly appreciates the bilateral relations with Georgia that are characterized by mutual respect and are manifested in high-level visits and various initiatives. Msgr. Bettencourt highlighted Pope Francis’ special attitude to Georgia and the importance of his successful visit to the country. Msgr. Jose Bettencourt reaffirmed that the Holy See continues to support the initiatives that are directed at ensuring full respect of the sovereign rights of any country within the framework of international law.
