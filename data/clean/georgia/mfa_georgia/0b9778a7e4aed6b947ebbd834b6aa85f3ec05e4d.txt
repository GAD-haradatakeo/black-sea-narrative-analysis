14 November 2018 Tbilisi - On November 14-15, 2018 The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, together with the Ministry of Internally Displaced Persons from the Occupied Territories, Labour, Health and Social Affairs are carrying out the Biological and Toxin Weapons Convention Peer Review Compliance Exercise at the Richard Lugar Center for Public Health Research. Georgia plays its essential role in supporting current global security architecture by demonstrating its commitment to the international instruments and implementing their requirements at a national and International level. Georgia is a party to the all major arms control and disarmament treaties and regimes, which play important role in improving biosafety and biosecurity in the region. By convening the Exercise, Georgia is demonstrating that the Richard Lugar Center is an open institution under the auspices of the National Center for Disease Control and Public Health of Georgia, which fully complies with the provisions and obligations of the Biological and Toxin Weapons Convention and completely meets international standards for biosafety and biosecurity.
