10 September 2021 The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia received the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Slovak Republic to Georgia, Pavel Wizdal. The sides discussed in detail the current state of close cooperation between the two countries, including the high-level visits planned for the near future. Special note was taken of the importance of the format of bilateral consultations between the Foreign Ministries of two countries. It was highlighted that the projects implemented in Georgia with Slovakia’s support contribute to the development of our country, as well as to the successful implementation of reforms. The Deputy Foreign Minister and the Ambassador focused on deepening sectoral cooperation, including in trade, economic and tourism fields. During the meeting, the conversation focused on Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration process. In this regard, the Deputy Foreign Minister highlighted Georgia's priorities, with special focus on the upcoming Eastern Partnership summit and Georgia’s expectations in this regard. Discussing the situation in the occupied regions of Georgia, the sides put a special accent on the importance of strengthening international partners’ support for Georgia. The Slovak side reaffirmed its unequivocal support for Georgia's priorities. The Ambassador expressed his gratitude for Georgia’s assistance in the evacuation process from Afghanistan. The Deputy Foreign Minister and the Ambassador exchanged information on future plans for Georgia-Slovakia cooperation and stressed the commitment to further deepen close relations.
