24 November 2022 On 24 November 2022, within the framework of his working visit to the Republic of Estonia, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Teimuraz Janjalia met with his Estonian counterpart with the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Estonia, KyllikeSillaste-Elling. The sides discussed the bilateral cooperation agenda between Georgia and Estonia. They highlighted the importance of maintaining and further deepening the active political dialogue with the Estonian side. Special attention was paid to the need for regular high-level bilateral visits. Speaking about European integration, Teimuraz Janjalia provided his Estonian colleague with detailed information about the implementation by Georgia of the European Commission’s recommendations and the progress Georgia has achieved on the path of integration. In the context of joining NATO, Teimuraz Janjalia noted Georgia's expectations regarding the NATO summit scheduled for the next year, including the importance of implementing the Bucharest summit decision. The sides discussed the security challenges in the region against the backdrop of Russia's ongoing war against Ukraine. The Estonian colleague was provided with detailed information about the difficult situation created in the occupied territories of Georgia and the threats facing Georgia. The importance of Estonia's strong support in today's most difficult geopolitical situation was emphasized, as well as the need for European partners to keep the issues related to Georgia’s occupied regions high on the agenda. The Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Estonia once again confirmed Estonia's firm support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its foreign policy priorities.
