19 February 2024 The First Foreign Deputy Minister, Lasha Darsalia and the Deputy Foreign Minister, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili held meetings with the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Lithuania, Jonas Survila, who is visiting Georgia. During the meetings, the sides discussed the issues on the agenda of bilateral relations between Georgia and Lithuania. They underscored the dynamic of their relationship and the significance of enhancing collaboration, particularly in trade and economics. The importance of maintaining a consistent political dialogue, which includes high-level visits, was emphasized. Special attention was focused on Georgia's journey towards European Union membership and the country's efforts in this direction. The Lithuanian side welcomed the progress of Georgia on the path of European integration and affirmed their willingness to support Georgia's aspirations for both EU and NATO membership. The meetings focused on issues of regional and global importance. The situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and the security challenges facing the region were discussed. The Lithuanian side emphasized Georgia’s constructive role in ensuring peace in the region, including in the implementation of peace initiatives. 2024 marks the 30th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Lithuania. To mark the mentioned date, on 20 February 2024, the NATO and EU Information Center will host a public panel discussion: "30 Years of Diplomatic Relations between Georgia and the Republic of Lithuania: from Consolidation of Independence towards Common Future at the EU and NATO" in which Deputy Ministers of Foreign Affairs of the two countries will participate.
