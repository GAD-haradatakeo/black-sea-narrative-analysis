3 May 2019 3.05.2019, Tbilisi - The U.S.-Georgia Strategic Partnership Commission's (SPC) Democracy and Governance Working Group met at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia in Tbilisi to review progresson ensuring political pluralism, media freedom and independence of the justice system. The meeting from Georgia was attended by representatives of the legislative, executive and judicial branches of the Georgian government. The sides also exchanged views over the issues relating supervision over security agencies, local self-government bodies, de-centralization, law-enforcement reforms, independence of judicial authorities, improvement of the election system and human rights protection. The importance of strong institutions and transparent and accountable governance, as well as the particular role of civil society was also highlighted at the meeting. Participants of the meeting paid special attention to the importance of the U.S. support in further strengthening the independent justice system checks and balances between different branches of the government. The sides agreed that additional reforms will further strengthen Georgian democracy and will contribute to the country’s integration with European and Euro-Atlantic structures. The Georgian delegation was led by co-chairs Deputy Foreign Minister Vakhtang Makharoblishvili and Deputy Minister of Justice Gocha Lortkipanidze. The U.S. delegation was led by Deputy Assistant Secretary of State for European and Eurasian Affairs George Kent, USAID Deputy Assistant Administratorfor the Bureau ofEurope and Eurasia Alexander Sokolowski, Deputy Assistant Secretary of State for Democracy, Human Rights and Labor MichaelKelleher.
