26 April 2018 26.04.2018, Tbilisi – Within the framework of the 100th anniversary since the foundation of the first democratic republic of Georgia, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia continues a cycle of cultural and informational meetings. Today, on 26 April, at 18:00, the Information Centre on NATO and the European Union under the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia will host Professor Davit Kolbaia, who will read the lecture on “Georgian emigration to Poland from 1918 to 1939”. Davit Kolbaia will speak about the foundation of the first democratic republic of Georgia, the recognition of its independence and Georgian emigration to Poland. Dr David Kolbaia, Kartvelologist is the head of the Caucasian Seminar of the Eastern European Studies Department at the University of Warsaw. He is the editor-in-chief of "Pro-Georgia Journal of Kartvelological Studies". He is the founder of the international conference of the Caucasiological studies named after St. George Peradze. In 2005 he established the Centre of Caucasiological Studies of the Warsaw University at the Tbilisi State University. He has been awarded Ivane Javakhishvili Medal and Order of Merit. As part of the celebrations of the anniversary date, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, along with other agencies is actively involved in carrying out various cultural, informational and public activities. With the goal of raising awareness about this important period in the history of the first democratic republic of Georgia, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs plans to organize public lectures, historical photo exhibitions, Open Door days and student competitions.
