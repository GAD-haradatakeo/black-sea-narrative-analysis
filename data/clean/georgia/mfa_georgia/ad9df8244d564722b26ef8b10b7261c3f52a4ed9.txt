19 October 2018 19.10.2018, Tbilisi - The Georgian Foreign Minister, Davit Zalkaliani met members of EUGBC Board of Directors and representatives of council's member business companies. Davit Zalkaliani emphasized the priority role of economic diplomacy in the activities of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. He noted that cooperation and coordination with business sector is crucial for economic development and growth of investment opportunities of Georgia. In this regard, he highlighted example of EU-Georgia Business Council, which, together with Georgia's diplomatic missions abroad, carries out many activities to popularize the country's economic and investment opportunities. Speaking extensively about Georgia’s European integration agenda, the Minister paid special attention to the EU integration roadmap - ROADMAP2E and highlighted the role of civil society, as well as of the business community in its further development and implementation. Discussions also focused on the successful implementation of the Association Agreement, including the DCFTA that provided a background for making the EU a key trade partner of Georgia. While on the theme of European integration, special mention was taken of the upcoming high-level dialogue with the European Union to be chaired by the President of the European Commission and the Prime Minister of Georgia that will be conducted for the first time in Brussels, on 21 November. Discussing the issues relating to co-operation between the EUGBC and the Georgian Foreign Ministry, the sides focused on the employment of Georgian citizens abroad. It was highlighted that both the EUGBC and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs are working hard to ensure the legal employment of citizens within the framework of circular migration. Meeting with representatives of the EU-Georgian Business Council served the goal of strengthening co-operation with the EU Member States towards attracting a greater volume of investments to Georgia.
