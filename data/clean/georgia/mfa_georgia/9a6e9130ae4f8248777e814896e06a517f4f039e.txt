7 April 2022 On April 7, 2022, First Deputy Foreign Minister Lasha Darsalia met with Marek Schiegel, Head of the EU Monitoring Mission. The sides discussed Russia's recent illegal, provocative actions in the occupied regions of Georgia, which further aggravate the already difficult security and humanitarian situation on the ground, as well as the highly exacerbated security situation in the region in the wake of Russia's large-scale military aggression against Ukraine. Discussions focused on the intensified process of de facto annexation, military build-up, erection of barbed wire fences and other artificial barriers along the occupation line, illegal detentions of Georgian citizens and restrictions on free movement. The need for the immediate release of Georgian citizens detained by the Russian occupation forces was highlighted. The First Deputy Minister stressed the critical need for Russia to implement the 12 August 2008 ceasefire agreement. The sides also stressed the effective use of the Geneva International Discussions, the need to restore without preconditions the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanism (IPRM) in Gali, and the regular implementation of this mechanisms in Ergneti. At the same time, Lasha Darsalia expressed his gratitude for the EU support and engagement in the peaceful settlement of the Russian-Georgian conflict.
