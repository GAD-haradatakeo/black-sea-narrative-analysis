22 August 2018 21.08.2018 - The U.S. Senate Foreign Relations Committee held a hearing on Georgia-Russian relations where Assistant Secretaryof the Bureau of European and Eurasian Affairs Wess Mitchell and Assistant Secretary of the Treasury for Terrorism Financing Marshall Billingslea delivered their remarks. While on the theme of relations with Russia, Wess Mitchellmade reference to co-operation with Georgia. He paid special attention to the removal of restrictions on the acquisition of lethal defensive weapons. “We have placed particular emphasis on bolstering the states of frontline Europe. In Ukraine and Georgia, we lifted restrictions on the acquisition of defensive weapons” - Wess Mitchellsaid. According to him, penalties imposed on Russia make the Russian government and Putin, in particular, to revise his strategy and actions.
