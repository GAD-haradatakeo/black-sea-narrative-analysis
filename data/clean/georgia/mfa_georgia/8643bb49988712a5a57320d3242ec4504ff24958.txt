22 May 2019 22.05.2019, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with Vice President of the Federal Office for Migration and Refugees of Germany Andrea Schumacher. The sides positively appraised the dynamic bilateral co-operation between Georgia and Germany. Highlighting Germany’s special role in the European integration process. Speaking about Georgian citizens’ visa-free travel with the EU/Schengen zone, Zalkaliani said that it is one of the most important achievements on the EU integration path. Therefore, the government spares no efforts to ensure its unhindered functioning. Discussions focused on the difficulties arising out of the abuse of visa-free travel rules, including the increasing number of Georgian asylum seekers in Germany. It was highlighted that the great majority of such applications are not granted since Georgia is considered to be a safe country. David Zalkaliani drew Andrea Schumacher’s attention to the Georgian government’s planned and implemented measures both within the executive and legislative branches of the government that are aimed at avoiding the abuse of asylum system. According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, Georgia cooperates with the EU and the European Commission to plan joint actions.
