22 February 2021 The fifth round of bilateral political consultations was held online on 22 February between the foreign ministries of Georgia and the Arab Republic of Egypt at the level of deputy ministers. Deputy Foreign Minister Aleksandre Khvtisiahvili headed the Georgian delegation, while the Egyptian delegation was led by Assistant Foreign Minister for European Affairs Badr Abdel-Aty. The parties discussed important issues on the agenda of cooperation between Georgia and the Arab Republic of Egypt in the political, trade-economic, investment, culture, education, tourism dimensions. Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili updated the Egyptian delegation on the Geneva International Discussions, the difficult humanitarian and human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied regions, and violations of human rights and international treaties by Russia. For its part, the Egyptian party reaffirmed unwavering support for sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia. The Parties agreed to cooperate closely on a range of issues.
