19 March 2025 “We firmly believe that by ensuring peace and security, we can achieve significant progress and fully unlock the region’s potential,” stated Georgia’s Minister of Foreign Affairs, Maka Botchorishvili, during the panel session "A Vision from the Caucasus" at the Raisina Dialogue. In her speech, the Minister highlighted the current state of affairs in the South Caucasus and Georgia’s role in fostering peace and dialogue in the region. "Georgia strongly supports the peace agreement between Armenia and Azerbaijan and is committed to achieving lasting peace in our region. We believe that peace and security are essential to unlocking the full potential of the region. This potential can only be realized through effective cooperation," she noted. The Minister also addressed the challenges facing Georgia, emphasizing that territorial integrity and sovereignty remain the country’s primary concerns. She reaffirmed that the peaceful resolution of conflicts is one of the top priorities of Georgia’s foreign policy. "Georgia is a small country in the South Caucasus that continues to face challenges related to territorial integrity. Two regions of Georgia remain under Russian occupation, with a continued military presence. Upholding international law and its principles is crucial not only for us but for the global community as well. Therefore, territorial integrity and sovereignty remain fundamental principles that underpin the international order. We continue to actively engage in all available international formats to discuss these issues and work toward resolving conflicts in the South Caucasus, which pose significant risks to regional security. At the same time, we clearly and unequivocally emphasize that the path to conflict resolution must be exclusively peaceful," Minister Bochorishvili asserted. She further stressed that the peaceful settlement of conflicts is not only vital for the South Caucasus but also for the broader region and European security, as Georgia’s security cannot be viewed in isolation from the European security framework. Discussing Georgia’s relations with the European Union, the Minister underscored the longstanding cooperation that has evolved over decades. "We have had and continue to maintain strong cooperation with NATO and the European Union. Georgia has actively contributed to peacekeeping missions, including in Afghanistan, where we were the largest non-NATO troop contributor. Since 2014, we have been an EU-associated partner and have a Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Agreement, which provides opportunities for integration into the European market. Georgia has positioned itself as a leading country among EU partner states, achieving substantial progress in implementing reforms that benefit our society and enhance our standing in international rankings related to anti-corruption efforts, open governance, and the business environment. All these advancements stem from a structured reform agenda that we have consistently pursued over the years," she explained. The Minister further emphasized that the European Union remains a crucial security actor for Georgia. "The EU is the only international presence on the ground in Georgia, which we perceive as a security guarantee. The European Union Monitoring Mission along the occupation line remains of utmost importance to us," she stated. Maka Botchorishvili also addressed Georgia’s attainment of EU candidate status in 2023 and the government’s efforts in this regard. "It was this government that made the strategic decision to enshrine Georgia’s European integration aspirations in our Constitution in 2018. This remains our key foreign policy objective. However, we must acknowledge that both Georgia and the EU need to explore pathways to achieving this goal together, as the current geopolitical climate presents challenges for both sides," she noted. She further asserted that Georgia and the European Union must engage in substantive dialogue on the country’s accession process. Turning to regional economic development, the Minister highlighted Georgia’s critical role in the development of the Middle Corridor and emphasized that maximizing the country’s potential in this regard is a top priority. "We have substantial opportunities to contribute to this initiative, but there is still much work ahead. That is why we are making significant investments in infrastructure projects, including the Anaklia deep-sea port. Georgia presents an attractive environment for investment, and we remain open to collaboration with all interested partners," she stated. In her speech, the Minister placed particular emphasis on Georgia’s cooperation with India. "India is a major regional actor and an important partner for Georgia. Our bilateral relations are strong, yet there remains significant untapped potential for deepening our cooperation in various fields. The scope of our collaboration is broad, and one of our key priorities is concluding negotiations on a Free Trade Agreement, which will further strengthen our economic ties. Notably, Georgia already benefits from 16 free trade agreements with various partners, including the EU, China, and the United Arab Emirates. Our unique position enhances our strategic value and provides opportunities for regional and global actors to engage in mutually beneficial economic partnerships with Georgia," she stated. The Minister reaffirmed Georgia’s readiness to actively work toward strengthening bilateral ties.
