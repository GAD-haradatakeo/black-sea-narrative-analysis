28 December 2024 As part of the Ambassadors' Conference, a series of panel discussions were held on various thematic issues. The panel discussion on defense and security was moderated by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Maka Botchorishvili. Participants in the discussion included Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Defense Irakli Chikovani, State Minister for Reconciliation and Civic Equality Tea Akhvlediani, and Vice Speaker of Parliament Gia Volski. In her opening remarks, the Minister of Foreign Affairs emphasized the importance of maintaining peace and stability for Georgia’s future development, particularly in light of the existing security challenges in the region. She thanked her colleagues for their participation in the Ambassadors' Conference and discussed the priorities of the governing team in addressing security challenges. State Minister Tea Akhvlediani spoke about the achievements made within the framework of the panel discussion and the future plans. She highlighted that the Georgian government is unwavering in its commitment to restoring the country’s territorial integrity through peaceful means. According to Tea Akhvlediani, the policy of reconciliation and engagement is an integral part of the state’s approach to the peaceful resolution of conflicts, particularly in relation to the populations residing in the occupied regions of Georgia. She also noted that the implementation of Georgia’s State Strategy for Civic Equality and Integration for 2021-2030 has been ongoing for the fourth consecutive year with positive dynamics. Vice Speaker Gia Volski addressed the security challenges facing the country and underscored the importance of reconciliation policies in ensuring the peaceful restoration of Georgia’s territorial integrity.
