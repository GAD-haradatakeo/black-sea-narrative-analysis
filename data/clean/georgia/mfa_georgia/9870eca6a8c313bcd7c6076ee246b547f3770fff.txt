20 October 2025 On 21 October 2025, Georgia’s Minister of Foreign Affairs, Maka Botchorishvili, held a meeting with Rashid Meredov, Deputy Chairman of the Cabinet of Ministers and Minister of Foreign Affairs of Turkmenistan, who is on an official visit to Tbilisi. During the meeting, the foreign ministers of both countries emphasized the traditionally friendly relations between Georgia and Turkmenistan and noted recent high-level visits, which have contributed to further strengthening bilateral cooperation. Particular attention was given to enhancing trade and economic ties. The ministers highlighted joint cooperation between Georgia and Turkmenistan in the development of the Middle Corridor, which connects East and West. The meeting also underscored the productive collaboration between the foreign ministries of both countries. Emphasis was placed on mutual support within international organizations, with the expectation that this mutually beneficial cooperation will continue in the future. The parties highlighted the special role of parliamentary cooperation and stressed the importance of deepening relations in culture, education, science, tourism, and sports. The discussion also touched upon the significance of upholding international norms and principles. Rashid Meredov reaffirmed Turkmenistan’s respect for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. The ministers additionally discussed the security environment in the region and emphasized the necessity of ensuring peace and stability. During his visit to Georgia, the Turkmen Foreign Minister is also scheduled to meet with the Chairman of the Georgian Parliament, Shalva Papuashvili, President Mikheil Kavelashvili, and Prime Minister Irakli Kobakhidze.
