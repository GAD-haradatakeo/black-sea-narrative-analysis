9 August 2021 While presenting the ten-year action plan of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs: "Foreign Policy focused on Western Values and the Country’s Interests. 10 years' vision, 10 goals", the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, spoke in detail about the need to strengthen Georgian diaspora and develop cultural diplomacy. "Georgia must empower its diaspora with strong cultural and educational centres abroad, which will make an active contribution to the development of the country and upholding of Georgia’s interests.," Zalkaliani said. According to him, to achieve the aforesaid goals, the government will make efforts during the next 10 years to ensure effective communication between the fellow citizens living in Georgia and abroad; set up new Georgian cultural centres; ensure effective protection of the rights and legal interests of Georgian citizens abroad by further strengthening the consular service; improve the consular service quality and accessibility; develop the Georgian-French memorial centre of Georgia's First Democratic Republic - the Leuville Georgian Academy, in cooperation with the Ministry of Culture, Sport and Youth Affairs; Speaking on the cultural diplomacy, Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of improving international awareness of unique Georgian culture and establishing the image of Georgia as a country with European identity In this light, the Ministry for the next 10 years will encourage active cooperation with international organizations, including with the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) and will contribute to Georgia’s participation in high-level international forums and other events.
