30 January 2019 30.01.2019 - The Defence Committee of the Canadian Parliament adopted a Resolution Responding to Russian Aggression against Ukraine, Moldova and Georgia in The Black Sea Region. The Resolution reads that Russia’s war in Georgia, as well as its illegal occupation of the two Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali (South Ossetia) since 2008, its illegal annexation of Crimea in 2014, its involvement in the conflict in eastern Ukraine since 2014 and its continued military activities along NATO’s eastern flank “have given rise to concerns about its intentions in Central and Eastern Europe, but also elsewhere around the world.” Of particular importance is a list of recommendations, which the Defence Committee refers for consideration of the Government. That Canada facilitate and strengthen parliamentary dialogue between Canada and Georgia, Moldova and Ukraine; That the Government of Canada increase sanctions under the Justice for Victims of Corrupt Foreign Officials Act (Sergei Magnitsky Law) against those connected to the ongoing aggression against Ukraine, Moldova (It automatically refers to Otkhozoria-Tatunashvili List); That the Government of Canada negotiate defence cooperation agreements with Moldova and Georgia; That the Government of Canada advocate for an increased NATO presence to respond to Russian escalation in the Black Sea; That the Government of Canada support the aspirations of Ukraine, Moldova and Georgia to join the NATO alliance in due course; That the Government of Canada commit to supporting Ukraine, Moldova, and Georgia in their resistance to hybrid warfare attacks, specifically with regard to cyber-attacks on government systems and critical infrastructure, and resistance to the dissemination of foreign propaganda and disinformation through the media. The report of the Defence Committee is a demonstration of the Canadian Parliament’s unequivocal support for Georgia. Of particular importance is the fact that along with expressing support for Georgia’s integration with NATO and reinforcement of bilateral co-operation, the Resolution calls for extending Magnitsky Law to the persons accused of committing aggressive actions against Georgia. The Georgian Embassy in Ottawa remains engaged with the Government and Parliament of Canada to ensure practical implementation of the issues envisaged in the Resolution. The approval of the document was preceded by the special hearing on 4 December 2018, where the Georgian Ambassador to Canada Konstantine Kavtaradze was invited as a keynote speaker.
