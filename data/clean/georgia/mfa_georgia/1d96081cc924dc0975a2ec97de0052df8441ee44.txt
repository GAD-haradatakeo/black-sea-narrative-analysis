7 December 2021 On 7 December 2021, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Teimuraz Janjalia met with the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Romania to Georgia, Răzvan Rotundu. The sides discussed issues of bilateral and multilateral relations between the two countries, emphasizing the traditionally friendly and partner relations between Georgia and Romania. Teimuraz Janjalia thanked his Romanian counterpart for his strong support for the country's territorial integrity, sovereignty and foreign priorities. Discussing Georgia's progress on EU and NATO integration, the sides exchanged views over the development of the Eastern Partnership format, the plans relating to the Associated Trio, and Romania's support in this process. The sides also focused on the Georgian side's expectations regarding the Eastern Partnership Summit scheduled for December. They reaffirmed their support for the strong and effective EaP. According to them, the Brussels Summit should send a strong political message to the EaP countries, especially to those whose main foreign policy priority is full integration into the EU, and should promote sectoral cooperation within the EaP and set important short- and medium-term goals. Discussing the ongoing processes in the South Caucasus, the sides paid special attention to the regional energy and transport projects. The Romanian side was briefed on the Russian Federation’s destructive activities in the occupied territories of Georgia, including on the dire humanitarian situation and the gross human rights violations on the ground.
