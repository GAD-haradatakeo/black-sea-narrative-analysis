16 July 2021 On 16 July 2021, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia hosted the inaugural meeting of the government commission for elaboration and implementation of Strategy for de-occupation and sustainable peaceful conflict resolution. The First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia chaired the Commission meeting. The meeting was attended by the chair of the commission for elaboration and implementation of Strategy for de-occupation and sustainable peaceful conflict resolution, the Deputy Justice Minister, the Deputy Defence Minister, the Deputy Minister of Internal Affairs, theDeputy State Ministerof Georgia for Reconciliationand Civic Equality, the First Deputy Head of the State Security Service, representatives of the Ministry of Internally Displaced Persons from the Occupied Territories, Labour, Health and Social Affairs of Georgia and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. The inaugural meeting focused on the goals and objectives of the Government Commission. According to the chair of the Commission, the main task is to get started a nationwide process to work out a common approach and strategic vision on the de-occupation and peaceful resolution of the conflict. To quote Lasha Darsalia, this will be an inclusive process involving not only executive and legislative branches of the government but also civil society – think tanks, NGOs, experts, internally displaced persons. In parallel, intensive work will be done to ensure the effective implementation of the Strategy through the proactive involvement and effective support of the international community. The Government Commission announced the creation of an inter-agency working group which will elaborate a draft Strategy for de-occupation and sustainable peaceful conflict resolution by the end of the year.
