20 March 2019 20.03.2019, Buenos Aires – The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani met with his Bangladeshi counterpart, Abul Hassan Mahmood Ali. The Georgian Foreign Minister brought to the attention of his Bangladeshi colleague the Georgian reform agenda and the international recognition the country has gained due to its progress in terms of economic and democratic development. Special note was taken of the geopolitical location of Georgia acting as a link between Europe and Asia and of the large-scale infrastructure projects being carried out in Georgia. The sides spoke about the need to co-operate more actively towards bringing the business communities of the two countries closer, as well as towards intensifying people-to-people contacts. Zalkaliani informed the Bangladeshi side on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and the Georgian Government’s peace policy. Zalkaliai introduced to his Bangladeshi colleague Georgia’s candidate to the World Food Organization, Davit Kirvalidze. Highlighting his professionalism and Georgia’s programme aimed at increasing the FAO’s effectiveness, the further development of agricultural sector and achievement of UN goals in all priority areas.
