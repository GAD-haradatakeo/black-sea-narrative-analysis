19 November 2025 Today, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Khatuna Totladze presented the draft of Georgia’s 2025 National Report on the human rights situation in the country within the framework of the 4th cycle of the United Nations Universal Periodic Review (UPR). The presentation was delivered at a session of the Parliament’s Committee on Human Rights and Civil Integration. The report aims to provide the UN Human Rights Council with information on the measures implemented by the Government of Georgia during 2021-2025 to advance the protection of human rights in the country. The draft report was prepared under the coordination of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, with the involvement of all relevant state institutions, including the executive, legislative, and judicial branches of government. In her remarks, the Deputy Minister focused on the actions undertaken and the key documents adopted during the reporting period in the area of human rights protection and promotion. These include the National Human Rights Strategy of Georgia for 2022–2030 and the Action Plan for Human Rights Protection of Georgia for 2024–2026, approved to ensure its effective implementation. The Deputy Minister emphasized that the report places particular attention on the grave human rights situation in Georgia’s Russian-occupied regions. It was noted that the occupying power continues to commit violations of fundamental rights, including the right to life, torture and ill-treatment, arbitrary detention, discrimination on ethnic grounds, violations of the right to education in one’s native language, property rights, the right to health, freedom of movement, and others. It was further underscored that full responsibility for these violations lies with the Russian Federation, as the occupying power and the state exercising effective control. It is noteworthy that the committee hearing was attended by representatives of the state institutions that participated in the preparation of the draft report. In conclusion, Khatuna Totladze stated that following the parliamentary discussions, the document will be finalized and, through the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, submitted to the UN Human Rights Council. At the end of the session, the Committee Chair expressed gratitude to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for its fruitful cooperation and for preparing and presenting the draft report.
