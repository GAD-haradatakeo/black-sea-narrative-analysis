21 March 2018 21.03.2018, Tbilisi – Our goal is to ensure that Georgia is presented as a successful aspirant for NATO membership at the upcoming NATO Summit in Brussels and its success in the area of reforms is duly reflected in the context of the Open Door Policy” - the Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze following the meeting of the State Commission on NATO Integration chaired by the Prime Minister of Georgia. According to the Minister, the State Commission is a format, which can coordinate domestic reforms necessary for NATO integration. “Georgia successfully pursues the reforms in the areas of democracy, economy and defence that can lead the country to NATO membership. Georgia successfully uses all tools to prepare for the NATO membership and this success is recognized by NATO Member States. Our goal is to ensure that Georgia is presented as a successful aspirant for NATO membership at the upcoming NATO Summit in Brussels and its success in the area of reforms is duly reflected in the context of the Open Door Policy. NATO and NATO Member States provide Georgia with important practical assistance in increasing our defence capabilities and improving our compatibility with NATO standards” – Mikheil Janelidze said. As a result of the recent changes in the government structure, the Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister assumed the office of chairman of the State Commission on NATO Integration.
