7 July 2025 On 7 July 2025, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Giorgi Zurabashvili and Head of the Anti-Corruption Bureau Razhden Kuprashvili held an informational meeting with members of the diplomatic corps and representatives of international organizations accredited in Georgia. The meeting focused on the Law on Foreign Agents Registration and the Law on Grants, as well as related matters. During the meeting, representatives of the Anti-Corruption Bureau briefed the diplomatic corps on the Bureau’s activities in relation to these legislative acts. Diplomatic representatives were once again assured that the laws are grounded in democratic principles and aim to promote transparency and trust-based cooperation. As noted by Razhden Kuprashvili, there are no circumstances under which the enactment of these laws would hinder the operations of international or non-governmental organizations in Georgia. The Head of the Anti-Corruption Bureau addressed the diplomats' questions in detail. The Georgian side reaffirmed its openness to cooperation with international partners and expressed its readiness to continue dialogue on these issues through various formats. “At the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, in cooperation with the Anti-Corruption Bureau, we held a meeting with the diplomatic corps and representatives of international organizations. During the meeting, the Head of the Anti-Corruption Bureau provided the diplomatic corps with comprehensive information on the contents of the law, the regulations it introduces for organizations operating in Georgia, and related issues. It was a very informative session for the diplomatic community. A number of questions were raised and addressed,” said First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Giorgi Zurabashvili following the meeting. According to Razhden Kuprashvili, diplomats and representatives of international organizations were informed about the process of the implementation and enforcement of the law. “We discussed issues related to the enforcement of the Foreign Agents Registration Law and the Law on Grants. We provided details on the implementation process and the current enforcement mechanisms. We also addressed the fact that there are no circumstances today that pose any threat to the activities of international organizations or specific non-governmental organizations. It was once again emphasized that the law will contribute to greater transparency in the activities of all organizations receiving donor funding. The meeting was valuable in that it provided an opportunity for representatives of every embassy to raise questions directly with me and receive answers.
