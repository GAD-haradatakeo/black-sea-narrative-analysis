8 April 2021 The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with the Head of the Delegation of the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) in Georgia, Severin Chappaz and the ICRC Envoy on Missing Persons in the Caucasus, Daniel Mac Sweeney, on 8 April 2021. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of cooperation issues between Georgia and the ICRC. Lasha Darsalia thanked the ICRC representatives for the Organization’s humanitarian efforts in Georgia, especially for its support to the population in Georgia’s occupied territories, including to the people affected by the Russia-Georgia conflict. Discussions also focused on the difficult humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, and in this light, on the necessity of ensuring unhindered access for the international humanitarian and human rights organizations, including for the ICRC, to Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. The sides highlighted the effective cooperation in finding and identifying missing persons, as a result of which, and with the ICRC's support, over 200 missing persons have been identified and reunited with their families. In this view, the sides paid special attention to the appointment of the ICRC Envoy on Missing Persons in the Caucasus, Daniel Mac Sweeney, as well as to the ICRC’s activities in the three countries (Azerbaijan, Georgia, Armenia) of South Caucasus. The Georgian side emphasized the significance of the ICRC's further engagement in efforts to find missing persons in Georgia. The sides reaffirmed their commitment to deepen cooperation in providing humanitarian assistance to the conflict-affected population.
