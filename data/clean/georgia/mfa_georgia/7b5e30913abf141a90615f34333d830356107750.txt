10 November 2017 10.11.2017, Tbilisi – “I welcome the very positive report released by the European Commission regarding Georgia's implementation of its Association Agenda in 2017 and the clear progress in Georgia's reform agenda. This report is indeed the recognition of our government’s great success including in terms of strengthening of Georgia’s institutional capacities and the progressive approximation of technical regulations and standards with those of the EU. The year 2016 was really eventful and successful on Georgia’s European integration path: Georgian citizens were granted visa-free travel with the EU, Georgia joined the Energy Community (EC), we signed an agreement with Europol allowing us to participate in a wide range of programmes on education, culture and innovations. This year our businessmen exported their products to the EU market. The EU has become one of our largest trade partners. High appraisal was given to our reforms, including sectoral ones that are fully in line with the EU priorities. The report highlights the fundamental constitutional reform positively appraised by the Venice Commission and the democratically conducted elections of self-government bodies” - Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze said when commenting on the European Commission’s Report regarding Georgia’s implementation of the Association Agreement. Mikheil Janelidze welcomed the assessments by the EU High Representative for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy, Vice-President of the European Commission, Federica Mogherini and the Commissioner for European Neighbourhood Policy and Enlargement Negotiations, Johannes Hahn, highlighting Georgia's progress in the implementation of the Association Agreement.
