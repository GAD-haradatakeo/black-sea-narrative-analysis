5 October 2020 According to the Deputy Foreign Minister Lasha Darsalia, social media reports that arms shipments are allegedly carried into Azerbaijan via the territory of Georgia are disinformation and a lie. Lasha Darsalia said that Georgia temporarily suspended issuing permits for transiting military cargoes via its territory. “With respect to cargo carriages, Georgia’s position is clear, and this position was expressed during yesterday’s meeting of the National Security Council. Georgia will suspend issuing permits for military cargo carriages by both countries. The restriction will in no case apply to civilian cargoes. Georgia will continue to fully comply with its obligations in this regard, towards both third countries and our neighbours. As we observe, however, social media sources continue to share various types of disinformation and fake news on alleged shipment of arms through Georgia. The recent news story, for example, alleges that there were arms deliveries to Azerbaijan, in transit of the Georgian territory, which is definitely a lie and a fake news” – Lasha Darsalia said. The Deputy Foreign Minister urged all parties, including Armenia, not to be misled by disinformation “I’d like to call on Armenia and the Armenian people not to be manipulated by this disinformation. Georgia, for its part, will continue to fully comply with its international obligations. Let me also urge everyone to rely only on credible and reliable sources for sharing the information” – Lasha Darsalia added. Documents Similar News
