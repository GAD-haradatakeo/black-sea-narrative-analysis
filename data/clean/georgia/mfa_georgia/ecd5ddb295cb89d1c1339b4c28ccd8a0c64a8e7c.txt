26 November 2019 26.11.2019, Berlin – Germany will further continue to support and assist Georgia as Georgia is Germany’s valuable and trustworthy partner in Europe” – German Foreign Minister Heiko Maas said during the joint press conference with his Georgian counterpart, David Zalkaliani. ‘Germany’s relations with Georgia are close and trustworthy. Georgia makes a tremendous contribution to the peace operation in Afghanistan. Georgia is our reliable, stable and valuable partner in Europe. We therefore support Georgia’s European aspirations and want the Georgian people to benefit from the outcomes of European integration. First of all, we would like to strengthen cooperation within the framework of Free Trade Agreement to ensure that its benefits have tangible effects on Georgian citizens” – Maas said. He also paid special attention to the inadmissibility of human rights violations in Georgia’s occupied territories. “We strongly support Georgia’s independence and territorial integrity. The sovereignty of Georgia is non-negotiable. Georgians travel visa-free to Germany for already two years but in their own country, they are barred from access to South Ossetia and Abkhazia. Many Georgians are illegally detained and this must be stopped” - he added. The German Foreign Minister called on the parties to the conflict for a dialogue. “We are closely following the developments taking place in Georgia and express the hope that the parties will achieve peaceful resolution of the conflict through a dialogue. If there is any way we can contribute to this process, we will willgly do that” – Heiko Maas said. Documents Similar News
