26 February 2026 The parties discussed current issues of cooperation in the field of migration, as well as preparatory work for the summit planned this year in Georgia (GDS) under the framework of the Global Diaspora Policy Alliance (GDPA). The Deputy Minister emphasized the role of the diaspora in the development of both countries of origin and residence, highlighting the significance of the GDPA as an international platform for cooperation. She noted that the Alliance aims to increase diaspora participation in sustainable development processes, strengthen collaboration with compatriots living abroad and facilitate the sharing of experiences among member states of the Alliance. The parties highlighted the significance of the upcoming summit, its objective of moving from political dialogue to tangible outcomes, and achieving specific, measurable results. Arthur Erken, Regional Director for Europe and Central Asia at the International Organization for Migration, expressed his gratitude to the Georgian side for the fruitful cooperation and the successful organization of the summit, and conveyed his readiness to support organizational matters.
