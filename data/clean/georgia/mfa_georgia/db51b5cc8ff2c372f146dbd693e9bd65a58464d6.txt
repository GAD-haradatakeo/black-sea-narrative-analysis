26 November 2021 On 26 November 2021, the 57th meeting of the GUAM Council of National Coordinators was held via video conference, within the framework of Georgia's Chairmanship of the Organization for Democracy and Economic Development (GUAM). GUAM National Coordinator, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia Teimuraz Janjalia took part in the online meeting. The meeting was also attended by Deputy Foreign Minister of the Republic of Azerbaijan Mahmud Mammad-Guliyev, the State Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and European Integration of the Republic of Moldova, Vladimir Cook, the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine, Igor Kharchenko and Secretary General of GUAM, Altai Efendiev. The sides discussed current issues on the agenda of the Organization, including in political, inter-parliamentary, sectoral and other areas. Positively assessing the existing cooperation with partners within the GUAM+ format, the sides focused on prospects for further expanding these formats. The National Coordinators highlighted the importance of holding a meeting of the GUAM Council of Foreign Ministers, scheduled for Stockholm within the framework of the OSCE Ministerial Council. The sides reviewed security challenges facing the region. The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister provided the participants with detailed information on the grave humanitarian situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. Teimuraz Janjalia paid special attention to the need to strengthen coordinated cooperation and interaction between the GUAM Member States within international organizations.
