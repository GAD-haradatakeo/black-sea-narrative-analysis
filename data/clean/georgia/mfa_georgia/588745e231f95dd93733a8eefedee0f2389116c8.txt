10 November 2021 On 10 November 2021, the annual conference "Day of Kartvelology" was organized in a virtual format, by the University of Westminster and the Embassy of Georgia. The 34th conference was dedicated to the 30th anniversary of the restoration of Georgia's independence. The conference was opened by the President of Georgia - Salome Zourabichvili, who spoke about the successful relations between Georgia and Great Britain. The keynote speakers at the event were Alexander Khvtisiashvili, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Sophio Katsarava, Ambassador of Georgia to the United Kingdom, and Mark Clayton, Ambassador of the United Kingdom to Georgia. In his speech, the Deputy Foreign Minister, Alexander Khvtisiashvili, highlighted the Georgian-British bilateral cooperation, as well as the progress made in economic development during the years of independence. The Deputy Minister briefed the conference participants on the government’s reforms to promote economic liberalization and economic growth in Georgia. He also provided information on Georgia's leading position in international rankings and its investment potential in economic sectors. Alexander Khvtisiashvili underlined Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations and noted that further development of trade and economic relations with Great Britain is of priority importance to the country. In his speech, the Deputy Minister noted that the Georgian side attaches special importance to the further development of the region's transport and energy potential and actively participates in important projects implemented in this direction.
