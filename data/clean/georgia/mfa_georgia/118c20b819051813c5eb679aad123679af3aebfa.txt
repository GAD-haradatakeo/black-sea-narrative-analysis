23 April 2021 Coinciding on World Book and Copyright Day, 23 April marked the launch of the Tbilisi - World Book Capital programme. The programme launch ceremony was opened by Tbilisi Mayor Kakhi Kaladze. The President of Georgia Salome Zourabichvili and the Deputy Foreign Minister and the Vice President of the Georgian National Commission for UNESCO, Khatuna Totladze spoke before the audience. Thanking the UNESCO Director General for his decision, the Deputy Foreign Minister highlighted the importance of the programme in terms of promoting the book and the publishing sector, in general. She also put special accent on UNESCO’s role in raising literacy across the world. World Book Capital was launched by a UNESCO General Conference in 2001. The project promotes the book culture worldwide and takes place in different cities every year.
