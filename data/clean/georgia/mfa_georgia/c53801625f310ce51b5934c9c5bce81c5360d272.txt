6 July 2017 06.07.2017 – Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze attended the reception to mark the Independence Day of the United States of America. President of Georgia Giorgi Margvelashvili, Prime Minister Giorgi Kvirikashvili and U.S. Ambassador to Georgia Ian Kelly. “I congratulate the American people on the Day of Independence. It is an exceedingly important day not only for the American people but for the entire free world as this day is a symbol of democracy and freedom. These are the shared values of Georgia and the U.S. enjoying the ties of strong partnership for the last 25 years. I’d like to take special note of the active co-operation launched with the new U.S. administration. Following the Georgian Prime Minister’s meeting with the U.S. Vice President in Washington, we are now looking forward to Mike Pence’s visit to Georgia. This visit is yet another proof of strategic co-operation in defence, economy, democracy and all other important areas, which is oriented not only towards strengthening friendship between the two countries but also towards maintaining stabilist in the region” – Mikheil Janelidze said. The reception was attended by representatives of the executive and legislative branches of the government and members of the diplomatic corps accredited to Georgia and other invited guests.
