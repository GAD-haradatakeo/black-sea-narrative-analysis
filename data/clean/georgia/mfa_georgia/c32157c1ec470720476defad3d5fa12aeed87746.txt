26 May 2017 26.05.2017, Poti – As part of the activities dedicated to Independence Day of Georgia, Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister Vakhtang Makharoblishvili visited Poti and took part in the official opening of the Exhibition titled “European Pages of Georgian History”. The exhibition organized under the aegis of the Georgian Foreign Ministry puts on display a set of letters, manuscripts and photos preserved at the Georgian National Museum. All the exhibited materials serve as an ample proof of Georgia’s centuries-old aspiration to Europe. The exposition was launched at the Georgian Foreign Ministry on 30 March, on the occasion of visa-liberalization granted to Georgia, after which the exposition began to travel around Georgia. Deputy Foreign Minister Vakhtang Makharoblishvili, Minister of Internally Displaced Persons from the Occupied Territories, Accommodation and Refugees of Georgia Sozar Subari, Deputy Minister of Education Teimuraz Murgulia and Mayor of the Poti Municipality Irakli Kakulia spoke before the attending audience. Vakhtang Makharoblishvili participated in various activities to mark Georgia’s Independence Day.
