9 August 2021 On 9 August 2021, the Vice Prime Minister/ Minister of Foreign Affairs David Zalkaliani presented the 10-year Plan of Action: “Foreign Policy Focused on Western Values and the Country’s Interests: 10-year Vision, 10 Goal”. The event which was opened by the Prime Minister of Georgia, Irakli Garibashvili was attended by representatives of the executive and legislative branches of the governments, members of NGOs and diplomatic corps. Speaking before the audience, Davit Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of foreign policy for the development of the country and for ensuring its national security. He also put heavy focus on the need to set well-thought-out and clear goals and objectives in this regard. “The paramount goal before us is to restore Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and to achieve membership of the EU and the North Atlantic Alliance - the historic choice of the Georgian people enshrined in the Constitution of Georgia” – the Minister said. According to him, it is for the first time in the history of the Ministry that a 10-year plan was developed to determine where Georgia should be 10 years ahead, in terms of state-building and international positioning. 10 key goals of the 10-year plan are as follows: By 2030, the process of de-occupation of the country must begin and its course should be irreversible. Substantial progress needs to be made towards strengthening Georgia’s sovereignty and restoring its territorial integrity. Second – an application for membership in the EU must be made by 2024, the status of candidate for membership should be obtained by 2030, and membership talks should be opened to further approximate the Georgian legislation to that of the EU; Third - the plan developed together with the Alliance to achieve Georgia’s eventual membership of NATO needs to be implemented, Georgia should be fully prepared for the political decision of NATO and bring the process to its logical outcome by making Georgia a member of the North-Atlantic Alliance; Fourth - tangible results need to be achieved in terms of upgrading the high-level formats of bilateral relations to a new strategic level. This will considerably contribute to lobbying the country’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration process, ensuring security, opening up and using new opportunities for the creation of the politically stable, democratically sound and economically successful state in the East European region; Fifth – the establishment of a comprehensive alliance based on the firm U.S.-Georgia strategic partnership - reinforced by military-political and trade-economic components; Sixth - by pursuing a well-balanced and mutually beneficial regional policy based on partnerships, the South Caucasus should be established as an area of cooperation, good neighbourhood and dynamic development overcoming existing differences; Seventh – Emergence of the possibility of normalization of political relations with Russia in accordance with the principle of de-occupation and the territorial integrity of Georgia - the beginning of the real process of conflict resolution. Eighth - launching an EU-Georgia connectivity dialogue and after 10 years establishing Georgia as a regional leader and assuming the important function of a bridge connecting the East and the West; Ninth - Through active economic diplomacy, we are aiming at: enhanced investment capacities and foreign investment capital; national, regional and international transport, energy and investment projects effectively put into practice; preferential treatment for Georgian export goods and diversified export markets; Tenth – by 2030, Georgia must have further strengthened diaspora with successful cultural and educational centres abroad, which will make an active contribution to the development of the country and upholding of Georgia’s interests.
