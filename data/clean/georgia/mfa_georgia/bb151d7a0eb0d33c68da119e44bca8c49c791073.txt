18 January 2019 18.01.19, Tbilisi – Speaking at the briefing following the government meeting, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani commented on the introduction of the Georgia Support Act to the House of Representatives. “The fact that the Georgia Support Act was re-introduced to the newly-elected House of Representatives points once again to the priority importance of Georgia. We are grateful to the United States of America, and in particular, to CongressmenGerry Connolly andAdam Kinzinger for their continuous attention and strong support to Georgia” – David Zalkaliani said. The Georgia Support Act was first introduced by Co-Chairmen of the Georgia Caucus Congressmen Ted Poe (R-Texas) and Gerald Connolly (D-Virginia) on 26 June 2018. The Act supports Georgia’s sovereignty, independence, and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders;The Act calls for imposing sanctions on Russian actors for the commission of serious human rights abuses in the occupied Georgian territories of Abkhazia and South Ossetia; pays special attention to security and defence co-operation between Georgia and the U.S. and requires the Secretary of State to develop a five-year strategy of co-operation with Georgia.
