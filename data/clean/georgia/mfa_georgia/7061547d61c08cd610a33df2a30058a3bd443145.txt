22 April 2024 "Our cooperation is based on common European values. In this spirit, I would like to confirm once again that Croatia fully supports Georgia in its efforts to become a member of the European Union and NATO" - Gordan Grlić-Radman, Croatia's Minister of Foreign and European Affairs, reaffirmed Croatia's backing for Georgia's aspirations to join the European Union and NATO during a meeting with Georgia's Foreign Affairs Minister, Ilia Darchiashvili. The two ministers attended the opening ceremony of the Honorary Consulate of Georgia in Pula, emphasizing the shared European values that underpin their cooperation. They discussed the significance of the consulate's opening in strengthening cultural, economic, and tourism ties between the two nations. During the meeting, they highlighted the fruitful collaboration between Croatia and Georgia, particularly emphasizing the importance of the Georgian embassy's establishment in Zagreb as a pivotal step in deepening their relations. They also celebrated the 30th anniversary of diplomatic relations between the two countries, highlighting the positive momentum in various areas of cooperation. Ilia Darchiashvili expressed gratitude to his Croatian counterpart for Croatia's steadfast support in Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration journey, especially following the European Council's decision to grant Georgia candidate country status.
