13 April 2021 On 13 April 2021, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Alexander Khvtisiashvili held a meeting with the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Republic of Tajikistan to Georgia Rustam Soliev (residing in Baku). The sides discussed a broad spectrum of bilateral and multilateral cooperation, including in the political, economic, transport/transit, agriculture, energy, tourism, healthcare and banking areas. Special attention was paid to the need to share experience in implementing reforms in Georgia, to improve the legislative framework of the two countries and issues relating to cooperation within the international organizations. The sides highlighted the importance of bilateral contacts and the need to conduct high-level bilateral visits. The Tajik side welcomed the increased trade turnover between Georgia and Tajikistan and expressed its interest in the full use of Georgia’s transit potential, which will be further facilitated by the International Automobile Traffic Agreement to be signed within the scope of the Tajik Transport Minister’s visit to Georgia on 14 and 15 April. Alexander Khvtisiashvili briefed the Tajik Ambassador on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories.
