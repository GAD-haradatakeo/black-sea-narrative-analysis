8 July 2021 The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkalaini held a meeting with the Japanese Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to Georgia, Akira Imamura. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of Georgia-Japan bilateral cooperation and the situation in the region. The sides referred to the Prime Minister’s successful mediation between Azerbaijan and Armenia as an important step towards peace and stability. Highlighting the need to further deepen political and economic relations between the two countries, the sides discussed in detail prospects for future cooperation in regional formats, including in a Japan-Georgia-EU format. Special mention was made of the necessity of further strengthening connectivity in the areas of energy, infrastructure and transport. The sides discussed the pandemic situation and the vaccination process. David Zalkaliani thanked the Japanese Ambassador for the assistance rendered to Georgia in the fight against the pandemic, as well as for financial support to Georgia. The sides also noted the importance of works performed by the Japanese International Cooperation Agency (JICA). The Japanese Ambassador reaffirmed his country’s firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. According to him, Japane objects to any attempt to redraw the internationally recognized borders of Georgia. David Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of the Tokyo Olympic Games as a way to bring together various nations, especially amid the global pandemic, and wished Japan the successful conduction of the Olympiad.
