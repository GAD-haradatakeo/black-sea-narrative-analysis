12 October 2018 12.10.2018, Tbilisi – “Ambassador John Bolton’s upcoming visit to Georgia serves the goal of deepening strategic partnership between the U.S. and Georgia in the areas of defence and security, as well as in the economic dimension” -The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani said when commenting on the upcoming visit of the U.S. national-security adviser,John Bolton to Georgia. According to the Minister, discussions during the visit will focus on the regional challenges and greater involvement of the United States in these processes. Special attention will be paid to Georgia’s role in the region. The visit provides yet another proof that Georgia and the South Caucasus region as a whole figure prominently on the U.S. Administration’s agenda. “Our partnership is unprecedentedly high, which demonstrates how high the U.S. government places its relations with Georgia on its political agenda” – David Zalkaliani said. The Foreign Minister also spoke about the Prime Minister, Mamuka Bakhtadze’s visit to Washington where the Georgian delegation held a meeting with the U.S. national-security adviser,John Bolton. It is interesting to note that discussions during the visit are concentrated on the priorities in the defence and security areas that strengthen the role of Georgia as a regional ally of the U.S. Talking points also include the possibilities of enhancing economic relations through the prism of free trade prospects.
