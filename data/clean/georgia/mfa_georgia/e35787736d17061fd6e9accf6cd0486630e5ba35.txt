22 June 2018 Permanent Representatives of the OSCE Member States paid a visit to Georgia from 19 to 21 June 2018. The OSCE Ambassadors held meetings with the President of Georgia Giorgi Margvelashvili, First Deputy Chairman of the Parliament, Tamar Chugoshvili, Deputy Foreign Minister Vakhtang Makharoblishvili, President of the OSCE Parliamentary Assembly Giorgi Tsereteli and representatives of the Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Internal Affairs and the office of the State Minister for Reconciliation and Civil Equality. Within the framework of the visit, the OSCE Ambassadors visited the village of Sobisi where the OSCE-funded drinking water project was implemented and the village of Odzisi where they saw on the ground the difficult situation of the local population living near the occupation line. During the meetings with Georgian government representatives, discussions focused on the need to achieve peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict and the importance of keeping the conflict-related issues high on the OSCE agenda. The Ambassadors were updated on the difficult human rights and humanitarian situation in the occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. It was underlined that the occupation forces are barring the international monitoring mechanisms from accessing the occupied territories in order to carry out monitoring on the ground. Talking points also included democratic reforms implemented in the country and successful co-operation with the OSCE independent institutions in this process. Within the framework of the visit, the OSCE Ambassadors held meetings with members of civil society, EUMM representatives and beneficiaries of the national programmes of the OSCE High Commissioner on National Minorities.
