17 February 2019 17.02.2019, Munich – According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, the Georgian Prime Minister, Mamuka Bakhtadze presented a full picture of Georgia’s successes, as well as the challenges facing the country at the session dealing with the issues relating to East European security, within the framework of the Munich Security Conference. In the words of David Zalkaliani, the Prime Minister highlighted all major issues, making accents on the security of the country, the situation in the occupied territories and Russia’s aggression, as well as on Georgia’s peace initiatives and the necessity of consolidating the international community towards ensuring the peaceful resolution of the conflict. „The Prime Minister paid special attention to the progress and tangible results Georgia has made on the European and Euro-Atlantic integration path. He also spoke about the economic dimension, including Georgia’s role in the geo-political environment and the important economic potential that allows the country to show economic growth despite the challenges facing it. Accent was put on Georgia’s progress on NATO integration path and on the fact that, in terms of integration, Georgia is a leader among all other aspirant countries and this requires adequate steps from NATO” – the Minister said. According to him, it is highly important to ensure that Georgia is widely represented at all panels of the Munich Securty Confernece “Despite persisting geo-political challenges, Georgia continues to be atop the international agenda. Three events were dedicated to Georgia, including two paralle session of which one was organized by Gworgia and the other – by the Munich Security Confernce and the German Atlantic Council where Georgia was invited as a keynote speaker. One more session that was dedicated to East European security also dealt with Georgia. It all indicates that Georgia’s participation in the Munich Conference was successful in general” – David Zalkaliani said. Documents Similar News
