2 June 2022 On 2 June 2022, the newly appointed Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to Georgia presented copies of his credentials to the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Alexander Khvtisiashvili. Following the presentation ceremony, the Deputy Foreign Minister congratulated the Chinese Ambassador on his appointment and wished him success in his future work. The sides discussed a wide range of issues on the agenda of bilateral and multilateral cooperation. In this context, the sides reaffirmed their unwavering and consistent support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of each other’s countries. Discussions focused on trade and economic cooperation between Georgia and the People's Republic of China. It was noted that China has become the number one export and trade partner of Georgia, based on data for January-April 2022. Stressing the importance of exchanging high-level visits, the sides expressed the hope that Georgia and China will further intensify cooperation in this direction. The sides discussed the possibilities of celebrating the 30th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and China.
