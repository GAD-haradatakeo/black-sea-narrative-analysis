15 February 2022 On 15 February 2022, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani met with the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of France to Georgia, Diego Colas. The sides focused on the security environment in the region and the recent developments around Ukraine. They reiterated their solidarity with Ukraine and full support for its sovereignty and territorial integrity. The French Ambassador spoke in detail about the French President Emmanuel Macron’s visits to Moscow and Kiev to resolve the crisis. It was noted that France, as the country holding the Presidency of the Council of the European Union, spares no effort to de-escalate tensions. David Zalkaliani thanked the Ambassador for France's support to Georgia in the framework of the dialogue with Russia. He stressed the impact of the ongoing processes on Georgia. The Minister drew special attention to the difficult security and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions as a result of Russia's destructive steps, and noted that Russia's provocative actions in Georgia’s occupied territories are part of its policy towards the entire region. The Minister underlined Georgia's efforts to effectively use the Geneva International Discussions and noted the need for Russia to implement the EU-brokered ceasefire agreement of 12 August 2008. The meeting also focused on the security environment and challenges facing the South Caucasus region. David Zalkaliani noted Georgia's commitment to promote peace and cooperation in the region and stressed the need to strengthen the involvement of Western partners in the region.
