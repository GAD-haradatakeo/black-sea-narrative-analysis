12 July 2024 On July 12, 2024, in New York, Lasha Darsalia, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, presented the third Voluntary National Review (VNR) on the implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in Georgia at the High-Level Political Forum on Sustainable Development (HLPF). It is noteworthy that Georgia has previously submitted VNR reports at the HLPF in 2016 and 2020. The HLPF, held annually under the auspices of the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC), assesses the implementation of sustainable development goals on a global scale. In his speech, the deputy minister highlighted the efforts made over the past four years to advance the 2030 agenda, as detailed in the report. He emphasized that the document deals with best practices, challenges, initiatives, and solutions implemented by the government to accelerate the achievement of the SDGs. The report focuses on three main areas: economic growth, human capital and social welfare, and democratic governance. During the presentation, Darsalia also mentioned the steps taken by the Government of Georgia to improve the humanitarian and human rights situation for people forcibly displaced from the occupied territories of Georgia and those living beyond the occupation line, through the implementation of sustainable development goals.
