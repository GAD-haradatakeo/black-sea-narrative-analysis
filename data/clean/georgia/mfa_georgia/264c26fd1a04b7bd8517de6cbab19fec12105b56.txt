8 June 2018 08.06.2018, Tbilisi - The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze met with students of the International Relations Department of the University of Westminster. Mikheil Janelidze spoke about Georgia’s achievements and foreign policy priorities and integration processes with the European Union and NATO, the economic development of the country and the situation in the region. The Minister paid special attention to Georgia-NATO co-operation, which is aimed at enhancing the country’s defence capabilities. Extensively reviewing the process of Georgia’s integration with the EU, Mikheil Janelidze said that the ultimate goal of the Government of Georgia is to achieve full membership of the EU. Mikheil Janelidze highlighted the difficult situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and the government’s efforts to overcome the challenges facing the country. Students of the University of Westminster arrived in Georgia from 6 to 8 June, at the invitation of the Saint Andrew the first-called Georgian University of the Patriarchate of Georgia, under the auspices of the Democratic Education Network at the University of Westminster. Georgia has close relations with the University of Westminster hosting Georgian Studies Day every year.
