6 May 2019 .05.2019, Bratislava – Within the framework of his visit to the Slovak Republic, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani attended the Georgian wine testing reception. The Polish Foreign Minister and the Ukrainian Deputy Foreign Minister were also present at the event. “A Georgian, rising toast says “Gaumarjos”, which means “be victorious”, such wishing to others victory and prosperity. Let’s make our partnership and cooperation effective, result oriented and gain concrete benefits to our mutual prosperity and development” – David Zalkaliani said speaking before the attending audience. Taking stock of the meeting of the Ministers of Foreign Affairs of the Visegrad Group (V4) and the Eastern Partnership in Bratislava, the Georgian Foreign Minister highlighted Georgia’s tangible results on the EU integration path. A documentary on Georgian wine was released during the event. According to the film, wine is an inseparable part of Georgian cultural identity and inheritance. Archeological findings suggest that wine making is dating back to 8000 years. Qvevri (a clay vessel) an egg-shaped earthenware vessel, is used for making, ageing and storing the wine in Georgia. As proof of its cultural significance, and in accordance with Principles of Convention on Protection of Intangible Cultural Heritage of UNESCO, the status of National Monument of Intangible Cultural Heritage has been assigned to “the ancient Georgian tradition of Qvevri winemaking”.
