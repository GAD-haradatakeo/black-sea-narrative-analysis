22 February 2018 22.02.2018, Tbilisi – Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze held a meetng with his Danish counterpart, Anders Samuelsen who paid his first official visit to Georgia. The two ministers spoke about Georgia-Denmark close partnership in the political, trade-economic, defence, humanitarian and other areas, the process of Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration process, ongoing reforms in the country, the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. According to the ministers, the potential of economic co-operation between the two countries must be used to the maximum that will increase the Danish business community’s interest in Georgia and contribute to the further attraction of investments to Georgia. Anders Samuelsen reaffirmed Denmark’s unequivocal support for Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration, as well as for its sovereignty and territorial integrity. Mikheil Janelidze and Anders Samuelsen held a joint press conference, during which the Danish Foreign Minister presented Danish Neighbourhood Programme– a scheme envisaging considerable financial support to Georgia. Anders Samuelsen’s visit to Denmark was preceded by the statement of the Danish Foreign Ministry stating that this visit is first of all an expression of Denmark’s firm support to Georgia and an attempt to help Georgia stop Russian aggression and independently choose its own path of development.
