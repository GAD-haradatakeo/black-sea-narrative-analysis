13 January 2021 The Foreign Ministers of Germany, Korea, Iran, Jordan and Czechia (formerly known as the Czech Republic) congratulate David Zalkaliani upon his re-appointment. “For many years our two countries have been establishing close ties, both in terms of bilateral relations, as well as within the framework of the Eastern Partnership Initiative. Germany will continue to support the process of democratic reformation in Georgia, along with its territorial integrity” – stated Heiko Maas in his letter, where the German Foreign Minister also underscores the importance of constructive dialogue in the process of overcoming challenges existing between political powers. According to the statement of the Czech Foreign Minister, Tomáš Petříček, Czechia will continue its staunch support to Georgia in terms of both reforms as well as on the path of European and Euro-Atlantic integration. He also goes on to note that the global pandemic has had an impact on the level of intensiveness of communication between Czechia and Georgia, however, this is nothing that cannot be overcome with joint efforts. In her congratulatory letter, the Foreign Minister of the Republic of Korea, Kang Kyung-Wha, expresses hope that cooperation between the two states will only continue to increase considering mutual interests of Georgia and Korea, especially in terms of the unforeseen challenges associated with the spread of the COVID-19 pandemic. The head of the Iranian Foreign Ministry, Mohammad Javad Zarif, expresses hope that considering the efforts of the leadership of our two countries bilateral relations will only continue to improve between Georgia and the Islamic Republic of Iran.
