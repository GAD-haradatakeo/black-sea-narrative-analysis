15 December 2021 According to the Minister of Foreign Affairs, today's meeting between the Georgian Prime Minister and the NATO Secretary General was extremely important and fruitful. Discussions focused in detail on the steps that need to be taken to bring Georgia closer to NATO membership, especially in the light of Russia’s recent acute rhetoric and its preconditions regarding the decision of the Bucharest Summit. "Today we received a very clear message from our NATO partners. The NATO Secretary General was quite specific and straight to the point saying that NATO integration is a matter of decision exclusively by NATO and those aspiring to join the Organization. The reference was directly to the NATO Charter, and in particular, to Article 10 of the Washington Treaty explicitly stating that any decision to invite a country meeting the NATO criteria to join the Alliance is taken by NATO and no third country has a say in such deliberations. This was a very clearly articulated message today” – Zalkaliani said. "We also heard clear messages about the practical cooperation we have with NATO, including in the context of Black Sea security. We heard the NATO Secretary General voicing his support for Georgia to implement the practical instruments bringing us closer to NATO. And there was unanimous recognition that Georgia is already acting as a member of the Alliance" – Zalkaliani added. Documents Similar News
