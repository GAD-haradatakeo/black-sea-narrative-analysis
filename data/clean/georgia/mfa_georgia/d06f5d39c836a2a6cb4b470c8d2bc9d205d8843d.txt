22 October 2025 On 22 October 2025, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, held a meeting with the Secretary General of the Organization of the Black Sea Economic Cooperation (BSEC), Lazăr Comănescu. Taking into account the strategic importance of the Black Sea, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili emphasized the necessity of maintaining peace, stability, and cooperation in the region. The Deputy Minister highlighted that Georgia attaches particular importance to the further development of the Black Sea region’s transit potential. In this context, he underlined the need to strengthen security in the region, which serves as a prerequisite for the sustainable and stable economic development of BSEC member states. For his part, the BSEC Secretary General noted that despite the existing challenges in the region, the organization continues to operate successfully. Lazăr Comănescu expressed his gratitude to the Georgian side for its active engagement within the organization and reaffirmed his readiness to pursue close cooperation during Georgia’s Chairmanship of BSEC in 2026.
