23 December 2025 On 23 December 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, held a meeting with members of the House of Representatives of Japan, Karen Makishima, and Japan’s Parliamentary Vice-Minister for Digital Transformation, Hideto Kawazaki, who were on an official visit to Tbilisi. During the meeting, the parties discussed the positive momentum in the development of bilateral cooperation between Georgia and Japan. Particular emphasis was placed on the importance of exchanging high-level visits, deepening cooperation in the field of digital transformation, and exploring opportunities for benefiting from and sharing Japan’s experience in this area. It was noted that the two countries already enjoy successful cooperation in the field of emergency assistance. The discussion also underscored the need to increase tourist flows between the two countries. In this context, it was highlighted that there is strong interest in visiting Japan, and that simplifying the existing visa regime for Georgian citizens would be essential to further promote tourism. The meeting also addressed Georgia’s prospective participation in the high-level international event International Horticultural EXPO 2027, to be held in Yokohama in 2027. At the conclusion of the meeting, the parties expressed confidence that the results-oriented cooperation between Georgia and Japan would continue to develop successfully in the future.
