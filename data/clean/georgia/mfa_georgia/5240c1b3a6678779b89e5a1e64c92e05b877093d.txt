21 June 2022 The Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Kingdom of Belgium to Georgia, Michel Petermans, the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Luxembourg, Pierre Franck, the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Republic of Croatia to Georgia, Branko Zebić and the Ambaassador designate of the Republic of Bulgaria to Georgia, Vesselin Valkanov presented copies of their credentials to the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, on 20 June 2022. At the meeting, the Deputy Foreign Minister stressed the irreversibility of Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration and noted that the country will continue to pursue an ambitious reform agenda. The participants of the meeting highlighted the role of Belgium, Luxembourg, Croatia and Bulgaria in supporting our country on the path of European and Euro-Atlantic integration. The Ambassadors pledged to continue to actively support our country's foreign policy priorities as Georgia has reached the turning-point on the path to European integration,. The sides discussed current issues relating to bilateral and multilateral relations between the two countries and prospects for intensifying cooperation in various fields. Emphasis was placed on the importance of exchanging high-level visits. Talking points included the need to deepen trade and economic cooperation between the two countries and to make full use of the existing potential.
