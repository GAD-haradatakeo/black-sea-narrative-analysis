26 February 2026 On 26 February 2026, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Alexander Khvtisiashvili, participated in an event dedicated to the birthday of His Majesty Emperor Naruhito of Japan. In his address, Alexander Khvtisiashvili highlighted the longstanding successful cooperation and friendly relations between Georgia and Japan. “Japan was among the first countries to recognize Georgia’s independence in 1918 and, since the establishment of diplomatic relations in 1992, has been a steadfast and strong supporter of our country’s sovereignty and territorial integrity,” stated the Deputy Minister. He further noted that, over the years, Japan has contributed significantly to supporting Georgia’s economic progress, technological advancement, and democratic development. He also highlighted the activities of the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) in Georgia, both in the areas of infrastructure and education. In addition, the growing dynamics of trade and economic relations between the two countries was highlighted, with particular emphasis on the increase in Japanese visitors to Georgia last year and the exchange of high-level official visits. In conclusion, the Deputy Minister expressed his gratitude to the Embassy of Japan in Georgia, and personally to Ambassador Ishizuka, for their contributions to strengthening Japan-Georgia relations.
