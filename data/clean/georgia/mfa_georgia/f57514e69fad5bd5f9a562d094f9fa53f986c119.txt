25 October 2021 On 21 October, the Deputy Foreign Minister, Vladimer Konstantinidi together with representatives of the International Center for Migration Policy Development (ICMPD) met with Georgian Diaspora representatives and members of the Georgian diaspora organizations in Barcelona, ​​Madrid (Alcobendas), Burgos, Valencia and Guadalajara. During the meeting, the Deputy Minister provided them with detailed information on the diaspora programs implemented or planned by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia to help Georgian citizens living abroad solve various problems, and at the same time, contribute to strengthening diaspora organizations and building the image of Georgia. Ketevan Gorgoshidze, Project Manager of the International Center for Migration Policy Development (ICMPD) and David Moia, a Spanish expert on migration law, presented a guide on Spanish migration legislation developed through cooperation with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, under the ENIGMA 2 program. After the presentation of the guide, the meeting continued in a question and answer mode. During the visit, Deputy Foreign Minister Vladimer Konstantinidi visited the St. Antimos Iverian Georgian Sunday School in Barcelona and handed over to them textbooks created within the framework of the Sunday School Support Program.
