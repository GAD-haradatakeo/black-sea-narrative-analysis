15 February 2021 First political consultations at the level of deputy foreign ministers were held between the Foreign Ministries of Georgia and the Republic of Tajikistan. The Georgian delegation was headed by Deputy Minister Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, while Deputy Minister Muzapar Khuseinzoda led the Tajik delegation. The parties discussed the bilateral and multilateral cooperation in the political, parliamentary, economic, humanitarian, healthcare dimensions, as well as issues relating to the improvement of bilateral legislative framework and to cooperation within the international organizations. The parties noted the increasing trade turnover between Georgia and Tajikistan. Georgia’s important role in transit carriages and Tajikistan’s enhanced interest in the use of Georgia’s potential was also highlighted. Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili acquainted his Tajik colleague with the difficult humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied regions. For his part, Muzapar Khuseinzoda reaffirmed his government’s support for sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia. The parties exchanged their experience in fighting against the COVID-19 pandemic. Following the consultations, Foreign Minister of Georgia David Zalkaliani and Foreign Minister of the Republic of Tajikistan Sirojiddin Muhriddin signed Cooperation Programme between the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Tajikistan for 2021-2022.
