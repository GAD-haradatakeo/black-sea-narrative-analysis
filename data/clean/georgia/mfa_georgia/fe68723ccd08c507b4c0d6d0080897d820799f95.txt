22 January 2020 21.01.2020, Tbilisi – “Georgia is an important country in terms of security, stable factor in volatile region and in terms of values like rule of law, human rights and democracy. Due to hard work and reforms Georgia has truly become an example for the region. Of course, this brings with it high expectations of keeping those standards” - the Foreign Minister of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, Stef Blok said following his meeting with the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani. According to him, the Netherlands will continue to support Georgia’s reform process. “I look forward to free and fair parliamentary elections later this year. The Netherlands is and will continue to be an ally in Georgia's reform process for many projects on the ground. Your reforms efforts are especially impressive, considering the difficult security situation the country is in” - Stef Blok said. Stef Blok expressed his concern over the occupation of the Georgian territories and human rights situation there. “The Netherlands remains deeply concerned about the situation and recent developments in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and South Ossetia, especially with regards to the human rights' situation. I will be visiting the EUMM at the ABL later today. Let me underline once more, our full support for the independence, sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia within its internationally recognised borders” – the Dutch Foreign Minister said. According to him, Georgia and the Netherlands enjoy long-term excellent relations, to which attests the Georgian Prime Minister, Giorgi Gakharia’s recent visit to the Kingdom of the Netherlands. “The Netherlands is Georgia’s 12th largest trade partner and one of the biggest investors that strengthens our confidence in Georgia’s economic growth and potential” – Stef Block said.
