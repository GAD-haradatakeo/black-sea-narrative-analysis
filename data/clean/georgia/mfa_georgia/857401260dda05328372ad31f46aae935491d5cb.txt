6 March 2020 The UN Security Council discussed on 5 March 2020 the large-scale cyberattack launched by Russia against Georgia. At the session held behind closed doors, Estonia, the United Kingdom and the United States,as well as other members of the Security Council unanimously condemned the cyberattacks conducted by Russia’s military intelligence service against the Georgian Government and media websites on 28 October. “We are clear that Russia’s military intelligence service – the GRU – conducted these cyberattacks in attempt to sow discord and disrupt the lives of ordinary Georgian people” - reads the statement made at a press availability. “These cyber-attacks are part of Russia’s long-running campaign of hostile and destabilizing activity against Georgia”, according to the statement. Moreover, the statement further goes on to highlight that "these actions clearly contradict Russia’s attempts to claim it is a responsible actor in cyberspace and demonstrate a continuing pattern of reckless GRU cyberoperations against a number of countries".
