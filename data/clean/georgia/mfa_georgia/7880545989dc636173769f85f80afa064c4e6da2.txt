24 June 2022 "The path to EU membership is long and difficult. It is, however, our firm determination to go through this path together with our European partners. Your experience and our partners’ support and assistance are of vital importance to us," the Georgian Foreign Minister, Ilia Darchiashvili said at a joint press conference following his meeting with the Hungarian Foreign and Trade Minister, Péter Szijjártó. Responding to the European Council’s decision on granting Georgia a European perspective, Ilia Darchiashvili stressed that Georgia's future lies within the European Union. According to the Minister, by recognizing Georgia’s EU membership perspective, Georgia has received a guaranteed promise that it will definitely become a member of the European Union. "This is the recognition of the progress made over the years, as well as of the special level of relations and integration that currently exists between Georgia and the European Union," he added. He thanked the EU leaders for recognizing the historic and civilizational choice of the Georgian people and stated unequivocally that the Georgian government will continue to pursue the ambitious EU integration agenda, including by implementing relevant reforms. "Georgia will definitely make progress in this direction too and will prove this time, like many times before, that Georgia deserves a candidate status already today," the Foreign Minister said. The Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade of Hungary, Péter Szijjártó visited Georgia on 24 June 2022. Ilia Darchiashvili hosted his Hungarian counterpart at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
