20 October 2017 20.10.2017, Tbilisi – Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze received Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Poland, Witold Waszczykowski, after which the two ministers held a joint press conference. The Georgian Vice Prime Minister made an extensive review of Georgia-Poland partner relations having developed over the centuries thanking his Polish counterpart for Poland’s firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and its European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations. According to Mikheil Janelidze, Georgia is consistently moving closer to the EU, through the support of its partner countries, including Poland. Mikheil Janelidze highlighted the successful co-operation between the two countries in the areas of security, defence, security, culture and other areas. Singling out trade-economic relations for special mention, he said that trade turnover between the two countires increased by 34% in 2017. According to the Minister, there is a huge potential of further deepening business relations, trade-economic links and people-to-people contacts between the two countries. The introduction of direct flights will further contribute to the development of these relations, to which attests 20-percent growth in the number of Polish visitors to Georgia. Mikheil Janelidze positively appraised the format of Tbilisi Conference, which serves the goal of further deepening sectoral co-operation and exchange of information.
