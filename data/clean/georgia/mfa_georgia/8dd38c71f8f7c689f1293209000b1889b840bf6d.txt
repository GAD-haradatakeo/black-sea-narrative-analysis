14 April 2020 The Georgian Foreign Ministry has been entrusted two main tasks by the Prime Minister-led Coordination Council: providing care and support to Georgian citizens abroad and ensuring their safe and gradual repatriation to Georgia - Mikheil Ninua said speaking before the Diaspora and Caucasus Issues Committee of the Parliamentary of Georgia. According to the Deputy Foreign Minister, priority importance is given to people with disabilities and grave health conditions; pregnant women and infants, minors and elderly people (at 65 and older), as well as students left without funding. “The diplomatic representations abroad do their utmost to assist our fellow-citizens including by providing them with accommodation, food, and health and hygiene supplies. We have created an online platform to offer medical consultations to our citizens in the US, Great Britain, Spain, France, Italy, Germany, France and Greece” – Mikheil Ninua said. According to Mikheil Ninua, 5238 of Georgian citizens have been assisted as of today. Georgia has put in place a food voucher programme for Georgian citizens abroad, involving SPAR company.
