20 October 2022 "Georgia pays special attention to the development of relations and mutually beneficial cooperation with our compatriots living abroad. It is important for Georgia to have a strong diaspora, which will actively pursue the country's interests abroad," the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili is quoted as saying during the Minister’s Hour at the Parliament. The Minister informed the representatives of the Georgian Legislature about the important steps taken in 2022 together with international partners and organizations (IOM, ICMPD, GIZ). It was noted that the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia participated in the Global Diaspora Summit and joined the Dublin Declaration, which is a global diaspora action plan. The Ministry also hosted a session entitled "Georgian Diaspora and Science Beyond Borders" within the framework of the conference organized under the aegis of IOM and UNESCO. According to Darchiashvili, the existing diaspora programmes were successfully implemented this year, namely the Sunday school support program and Be a young ambassador of your country program. On 27 May of this year, we held a traditional forum - Georgian Diaspora for Georgia's European Future. According to the minister, special attention was paid to the establishment of new educational and cultural centers abroad - in particular, 5 new Sunday schools were set up. In the public schools of Greece and Turkey, the specially designed Georgian language teaching pilot program continues successfully. Speaking about future plans, Ilia Darchiashvili focused on the importance of establishing new Georgian cultural centers.
