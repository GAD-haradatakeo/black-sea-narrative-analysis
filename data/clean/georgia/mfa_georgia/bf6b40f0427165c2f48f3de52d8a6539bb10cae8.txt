19 February 2022 Within the framework of the Munich Security Conference, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with his Slovenian counterpart, Minister of Foreign Affairs, Anže Logar. David Zalkaliani thanked Slovenia for its active and effective support of Georgia's European integration goals during the EU’s Slovenian Presidency. The Ministers also reviewed the Georgian Foreign Minister’s first official visit to Slovenia last year and the implementation of its results, including the strengthening of trade and economic ties between the two countries. Speaking about the security environment in the region, he reiterated the inadmissibility of forced escalation of the process and stressed the need to address the situation around Ukraine, as well as regional and global security issues in general through the maximum involvement of the international community. The sides reiterated support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Ukraine and Georgia, as well as the need to respect the sovereign Euro-Atlantic choice of both countries. The Minister also briefed his Slovenian counterpart on Georgia’s peace policy and on the provocative steps taken by the occupation regimes to deteriorate the security environment on the ground, and noted the importance of the international community's response to the occupation regimes’ provocations.
