7 February 2019 07.02.2019, U.S. - Within the framework of his visit to the United States, the Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani met Senator David Perdue (R-GE). The Minister thanked the Senator for his strong support of Georgia for many years and for many legislative initiatives on Georgia introduced by him to the U.S. Congress The sides highlighted the increasing co-operation with the U.S. Congress over the past few years, and the high level of bipartisan political support as evidenced by the Georgia Support Act. The Senator was briefed on Georgia’s ongoing democratic reforms and on the government’s vision of the country’s future development. Conversations included NATO integration process and U.S. support in this regard. Georgia’s leading position in international ratings was also highlighted. Special attention was paid to Georgia’s transit function as a regional hub and its economic reforms.
