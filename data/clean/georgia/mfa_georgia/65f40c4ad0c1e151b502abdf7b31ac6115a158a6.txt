14 January 2022 On 14 January 2022, the Ambassador-designate of the Hellenic Republic to Georgia, Maria Theodoros presented copies of her credentials to the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani. Following the ceremony, the sides held a meeting where the Minister congratulated the Ambassador on her new position and expressed the hope that she will greatly contribute to further deepening the close and friendly relations between the two countries. The Minister and the Ambassador-designate noted that the traditionally friendly relations between the two countries provide a good basis for further deepening cooperation in both bilateral and multilateral formats. The sides also emphasized that there is a great potential for deepening trade and economic, tourism, connectivity and maritime relations. The meeting focused on Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration. In this regard, the Minister highlighted Georgia's priorities. He also spoke in detail about the situation in the occupied regions of Georgia and the importance of further consolidating the partners’ support. For her part, Maria Theodorou confirmed that Greece will continue to actively support Georgia. Speaking about future plans, it was noted that 2022 marks the 30th anniversary of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Greece and this will be used by the countries to advance Georgia-Greece historical and cultural ties.
