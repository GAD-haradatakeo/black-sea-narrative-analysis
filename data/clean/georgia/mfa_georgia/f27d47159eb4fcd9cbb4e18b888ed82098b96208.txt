15 November 2017 15.11.2017, Ashgabat -The Agreement on Lapis Lazuli Corridor connecting Afghanistan with Europe through Turkmenistan and South Caucasus was signed in Ashgabat. The Agreement was signed by representatives of Afghanistan, Turkey, Turkmenistan, Azerbaijan and Georgia. The Agreement is designed to contribute to transport relations between the five nations by facilitating movement of cargoes and passengers, as well as by resolving administrational issues and harmonizing transit-related procedures. According to Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze, by signing the Lapis Lazuli Corridor Agreement Georgia became part of the exceedingly important international transit corridor. “The signing of the agreement will significantly contribute to the development of cooperation between the members of the project in the areas of trade and transport and beyond. This is an important step taken towards the implementation of our policy to ensure that Georgia is important part of the region’s new corridors and transit” – Mikheil Janelidze said. He also highly appraised bilateral co-operation between Georgia and Turkmenistan which is based on a wide range of common interests.
