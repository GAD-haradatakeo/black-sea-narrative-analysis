17 September 2018 17.09.2018, Washington – Commenting on the meeting between the Georgian Prime Minister and the National Security Advisor of the United States, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani said that the meeting with the National Security Advisor of the United States, John Bolton will bring about important results in terms of further consolidation of U.S.-Georgia strategic partnership. According to David Zalkaliani, the sides held substantial talks on the ongoing developments in Georgia, as well as on the challenges facing the region. “The National Security Advisor of the United States is a vastly experienced diplomat – a person who is perfectly aware of the situation in the region. And it was very important for him to hear our opinion about how we view the challenges we are confronted with” – Zalkaliani said. According to him, discussions during the meeting focused on the situation in the occupied territories and in the region as a whole, including the U.S. strategic interests. “As far as you know the United States is involved in important geo-strategic infrastructure projects that are based in the Georgian territory. Talking points also included co-operation with the U.S. in the areas of defence and security. Special attention was paid to the development of economic relations that need to be enhanced to match the high level of political relations between the two countries. According to David Zalkaliani, the issue of Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic integration process was also highlighted during the meeting. The U.S. firmly supports Georgia’s integration with NATO that was confirmed at the Summit in Brussels. John Bolton spoke about his position regarding the ongoing processes in Russia. “Our American partners continue to place Georgia atop the agenda of their bilateral relations with Russia, saying that Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity is the red line that cannot be crossed” – Zalkaliani said.
