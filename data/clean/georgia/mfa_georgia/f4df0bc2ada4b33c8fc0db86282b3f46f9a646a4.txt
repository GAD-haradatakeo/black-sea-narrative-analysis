6 September 2022 Key priorities of Georgia-EU cooperation, the progress achieved on the road to European integration and the current situation in the occupied regions were main issues of discussions at the meeting between the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili and MEP Michael Gahler, in Brussels, within the 7th session of the Georgia-EU Association Council. The sides highlighted the historical decision to grant Georgia a European perspective. It was noted that Georgia will actively work to fulfill the recommendations of the European Commission. The sides noted with satisfaction the fruitful and close cooperation between Georgia and the European Parliament. Speaking about the security environment in the region, attention was focused on the difficult situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. Special mention was made of the Georgian government’s peace policy in the conflict settlement process and the European Parliament’s strong support for the country's territorial integrity and sovereignty.
