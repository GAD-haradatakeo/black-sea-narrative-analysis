25 March 2021 The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with the vice-president of the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC), Gilles Carbonnier, in Geneva, on 25 March 2021. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of cooperation issues between Georgia and the ICRC. LashaDarsalia thanked Gilles Carbonnier for the ICRC’s humanitarian efforts in Georgia, as well as for the Organization’s regular assistance to the population in Georgia’s occupied territories, including to the people affected by the Russia-Georgia conflict. In this light, Lasha Darsalia highlighted the importance of the ICRC'sunhindered access to Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, as well as the need of visiting illegally detained citizens of Georgia. The sides spoke about the appointment of ICRC Envoy on Missing Persons in the Caucasus, Daniel Mac Sweeney and ICRC activities in the three countries (Azerbaijan, Georgia, Armenia) of South Caucasus. The Georgian side emphasized the significance of the ICRC's further engagement in efforts to find missing persons in Georgia, as well as the ICRC'sneutral mediation in the existing coordination mechanisms. According to Lasha Darsalia, over 200 missing persons have been identified and reunited with their families with the support of ICRC. The Deputy Foreign Minister reaffirmed Georgia's commitment to continue work in this regard, with the support ofthe Interagency commission for the identification and transfer of bodies of missing persons, which was set up in 2019. For his part, the ICRC Vice-president expressed his commitment to continue close cooperation with the Government of Georgia, as well as further humanitarian support of the Georgian population.
