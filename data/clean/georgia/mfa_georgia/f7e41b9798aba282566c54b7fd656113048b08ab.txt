19 October 2022 On 19 October 2022, within the framework of his working visit to the Republic of Lithuania, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Teimuraz Janjalia met with the Adviser to the Prime Minister on Eastern Partnership and Democracy, Galina Vaščenkaitė. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of Georgia-Lithuanian cooperation. Attention was focused on the importance of exchanging high-level visits and maintaining a close communication at the governmental level in order to further intensify the political dialogue between the two countries. Discussions focused on the situation and security challenges in the region, as well as on the recent developments around Ukraine. The Lithuanian side was provided with detailed information about the current situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. The sides emphasized the need for the partners’ consolidated position for maintain the issue of Georgia high on the international agenda. Georgia’s progress on the path of European and Euro-Atlantic integration was also highlighted at the meeting. The adviser to the Prime Minister of Lithuania confirmed her strong support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia, as well as for the foreign policy priorities.
