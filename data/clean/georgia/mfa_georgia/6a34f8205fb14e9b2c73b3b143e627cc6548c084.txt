16 September 2021 The European Parliament’s document pays considerable attention to Russia-Georgia putting a heavy accent on the illegal occupation of the Georgian territories by Russia and violations of the Ceasefire Agreement. The document highlights that Russia’s actions to destabilize and violate the territorial integrity of Georgia, and failure to fulfill its commitments under the 12 August 2008 EU-mediated ceasefire treaty is threatening peace and security in Europe The European Parliament makes clear that “the failure to respond adequately to the various Russian aggressions since the one against Georgia in 2008 prompted Russia to continue aggressive military and political campaigns, both in its neighborhood and beyond thus weakening and undermining the rules-based international order and stability in Europe and elsewhere’. The document maintains that Russia uses illegal passportization policy in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions (as well as in Donbass and Crimea) to strengthen its control over the occupied territories. The European Parliament underscores that in attempting to engage with the Kremlin, the EU must have clearly defined red lines including full respect for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of partner countries. The European Parliament maintains that the EU should demand – including before EU and other international organisations that Russia commits to the resolution of the ongoing conflicts and to the prevention of any future conflicts, starting by returning the occupied and illegally annexed territories in the EaP region according to their internationally recognised borders and by respecting countries’ EU, Euro-Atlantic and democratic choices. The EU should make it clear that a return to ‘business as usual’ cannot be envisaged until Russia halts its aggressive policy and hybrid warfare against the EU, its Member States and the EaP countries, and the territorial integrity of Georgia, Moldova and Ukraine is restored within their internationally recognised borders. The European Parliament recommends that the Council, the Commission and the High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy offer ambitious EU integration policy to the EaP countries.
