11 April 2023 On 11 April, a conference titled "Three Seas Initiative: Perspectives and Challenges ahead of Bucharest Summit" was held in cooperation with the Georgian Foundation for Strategic and International Studies and the Romanian Embassy to Georgia. The conference was attended by representatives of the corps, local and international research centers. The Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia addressed the participants of the conference with a welcome speech. The Deputy Minister spoke about the Three Seas Initiative and the opportunities created by the mentioned platform. Teimuraz Janjalia focused on the importance of Georgia's participation in the initiative. In this context, the Deputy Minister emphasized the need to fully exploit the potential of the Black Sea and strengthen regional cooperation. Taking into account the existing geopolitical situation, Teimuraz Janjalia noted the relevance of the mentioned format and spoke about Georgia’s leading role in promoting the energy security of both the region and Europe. The deputy minister emphasized the importance of implementing the flagship projects, especially in terms of Black Sea connectivity, in cooperation with the European Union. Attention was focused on the construction of the Black Sea submarine cable and the supply of electricity from the South Caucasus to Europe through Georgia. The Deputy Minister expressed the hope that by supporting the member countries of the Three Seas Initiative, Georgia will make its own contribution to deepening the mentioned regional cooperation.
