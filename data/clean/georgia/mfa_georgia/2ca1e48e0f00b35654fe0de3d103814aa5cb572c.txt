25 July 2018 25.07. 2018, Washington - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani stated in Washington that Georgia has the firm support of the US Senate Foreign Affairs Committee and this support has already been revealed many times. Zalkaliani’s statement came after his meeting with the Senate’s Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman Bob Corker. “We spoke about the Georgia-US strategic partnership and the further enhancement of relations, challenges in the region and the situation in the Georgian occupied regions. I briefed him about human rights violations cases in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali occupied regions of Georgia,” Zalkaliani said. Zalkaliani stated that he also informed Corker about the Georgian government’s peace initiative “A step toward a better future” to the population in the occupied territories. According to him, discussions focused on the strategic partnership issues between Georgia and the U.S., persisting challenges in the region, the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and grave human rights violations there. The Georgian Foreign Minister also brought to the attention of the Senate’s Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman that Georgia’s full-scale integration with the European and Euro-Atlantic structures is a key priority of the Georgian government’s programme. Zalkaliani was invited to Washington by the US State Secretary Mike Pompeo to participate in an international conference on religious freedom. “The Georgian delegation will have the possibility to speak about cases of restriction of freedom of religion in the occupied territories of Georgia during the international gathering” – Zalkaliani said. ​ Documents Similar News
