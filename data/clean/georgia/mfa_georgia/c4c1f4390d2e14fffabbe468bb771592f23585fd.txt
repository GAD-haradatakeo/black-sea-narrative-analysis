1 June 2017 1.06.2017, Japan – Independence Day of Georgia and the 25th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Japan have been celebrated in Tokyo. Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze, Japanese Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs Motome Takisawa and Secretary General of the Parliamentary Group of Firendship with Georgia Yasutoshi Nishimura spoke before the invited guests. They highlighted the friendly relations and partnership between Georgia and Japan. According to the Japanese Vice Minister, Georgia is an important friend of Japan sharing the commitment to basic values such as democracy and the rule of law. He said that the relationship between our countries extends beyond politics to embrace economy, people-to-people contatcs, culture and tourism as well. According to the Georgian Foreign Minister Since the first days of independence Japan has been rendering enormous support to Georgia. Mikheil Janelidze once more conveyed sincere gratitude to the Japanese Government and Japanese people for their political support and technical and financial assistance to Georgia. The reception was attended by members of the legislative and executive branches of the Japanese government, as well as by representatives of the Japanese cultural and business communities.
