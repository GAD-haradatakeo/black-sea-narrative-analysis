9 November 2023 On 8 November 2023, within the framework of the general policy debate of the 42nd session of the UNESCO General Conference, the head of the Georgian delegation, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Khatuna Totladze, delivered a speech where she referred to the European Commission’s historical recommendation regarding the granting of EU candidate status to Georgia and expressed the hope that the leaders of the European Union will make a positive decision in December of this year. The head of the Georgian delegation spoke about the successful cooperation between Georgia and UNESCO, positively assessed the activities of the organization in terms of taking urgent measures against the background of the current challenges, and once again emphasized the readiness of the country to contribute to this effort. Khatuna Totladze spoke about the country’s progress on fulfilling the sustainable development goals, as well as about the ongoing education reforms and cooperation with UNESCO in this regard . The Deputy Minister paid special attention to the humanitarian crisis and gross violation of human rights in the occupied territories of Georgia. She condemned the killing of Tamaz Gintur, who was abducted from the village of Kirbal by the Russian military on 6 November. Khatuna Totladze spoke extensively about the creeping occupation of Russia, the deliberate destruction of the monuments of cultural heritage and the violation of the right to receive education in one's native language. It was noted that Georgia welcomes the return of the United States of America to UNESCO. The mentioned event was evaluated as an important step towards ensuring the universality of the organization. The deputy minister once again reaffirmed Georgia's support for Ukraine and welcomed UNESCO's efforts in providing emergency aid to Ukraine.
