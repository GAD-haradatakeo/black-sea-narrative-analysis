16 February 2021 On 16 February, the Georgian Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani, met with the acting chair of OSCE, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Kingdom of Sweden, Ann Linde, in the State Ceremony Palace. During the meeting of the heads of the foreign ministries of Georgia and Sweden relevant issues associated with international cooperation with the state currently acting as the chair of OSCE were discussed, with the Georgian party expressing full readiness to cooperate with Sweden in all, three, dimensions of OSCE. During the meeting it was noted that active engagement in the process of peaceful resolution of the Russo-Georgian conflict on behalf of Sweden, as the chair of OSCE, represents one of central priorities. Emphasis was also made on serious challenges and threats associated with the illicit occupation of Georgia’s regions by the Russian Federation, both for Georgia as well as the entire Black Sea region and Europe in general. The Georgian Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs presented his Swedish counterpart with detailed information regarding the illicit occupation of Georgian territories of Abkhazia and the Tskhinvali Region, especially in terms of steps taken towards de facto annexation, which cause dire results, in terms of prolonged closure of the occupation line and the humanitarian crisis resulting from said closures, along with constant violations of human rights, illegal arrests and kidnappings of Georgian nationals.
