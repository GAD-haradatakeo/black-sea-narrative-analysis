27 November 2019 Commenting of the Georgian Presidency of the Committee of Ministers of the Council of Europe, CoE Secretary General, Marija Pejčinović Burić expressed her belief that Georgia will successfully run its term for the next six months. She also highly appraised the priorities of the Georgian Presidency. “Indeed it is a great day for Georgia and also for the council of Europe. This year the Council of Europe and Georgia celebrate anniversaries, the Council of Europe –the 17th of its creation and Georgia – 20th anniversary of its accession to and participation in the work of the CoE. I think the very fact that Georgia will assume the presidency is great in this historically important year for the Council of Europe and for all member states. But also Georgia will be able to promote its culture and profile itself on international scene. For Georgia this will be big opportunity for the next six months really to work to the benefit of the Council of Europe and to all the citizens of 47 members of the CoE. We are very thankful to Georgia for having its special contribution to four very important areas. I will mention two particularly important areas: one: for the first time among the presidencies of the Council of Europe the relations between human rights and environmental protection, the theme that is very important, very topical, and second, how to improve justice system when children are in question. It is very important to find ways how to protect children even in the situation when they are faced with the judiciary. So from today I believe Georgia will be in a position to really run a very successful presidency. Being in Georgia a few weeks ago I was reassured by Minister Zalkaliani, and also by all others I spoke with, from the President to the Prime Minister and the chairman of the Parliament that Georgia is ready to assume this very important presidency and it will be a successful one.
