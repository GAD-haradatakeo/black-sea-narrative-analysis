14 July 2021 The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia held a meeting with the UK Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to Georgia, Mark Clayton. The Ambassador congratulated Teimuraz Janjalaia on his appointment to the position of Deputy Foreign Minister and wished him success in his new role. Discussions focused on the agenda of bilateral strategic relations and active interaction within the international organizations. Special focus was put on defence and security cooperation. In this context, the Deputy Foreign Minister welcomed the arrival of British Royal Navy air defence destroyerHMS Defenderat the Batumi Port, which points to Great Britain;’s increasing role in ensuring the Black Sea security. The sides highlighed the importance of the Wardrop Strategic Dialogue in terms of further deepening the political dialogue and sectoral cooperation. Talking points also included the ways to strengthen economic cooperation. Discussions also focused on the security challenges in the region. In this context, Teimuraz Janjalia paid specia attention to the importance of the international community’s consolidated position with respect to Georgia’s occupied territories. Special note, in this light, was taken of the 30 June Joint Communique of the UK and German Foreign Ministers reaffirming their unequivocal support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for the implementation of the Ceasefire Agreement. Referring to violent attacks on civil activists and journalists on 5 July, the sides agreed on the need to protect the highest standards of freedom of expression and assemply, as well as the right to peaceful protest.
