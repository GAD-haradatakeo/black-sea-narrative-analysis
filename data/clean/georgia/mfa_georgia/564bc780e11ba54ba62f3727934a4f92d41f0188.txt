30 September 2021 The 8th round of bilateral political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Republic of Korea was held at the level of Deputy Ministers, on 30 September, in Seoul. The Georgian delegation was led by Alexander Khvtisiashvili, and the Korean side - by Seung-Bae Yeo. The sides discussed important issues on the agenda between Georgia and the Republic of Korea. Notably, Talking points included the growing dynamics of bilateral and multilateral relations, the importance of exchanging high-level visits between the two countries, the need to deepen the parliamentary dimension and sectoral cooperation, to show mutual support within international organizations and to share experience in the areas of innovation, technology and education. Particular attention was paid to the intensification of trade and economic relations. Special note, in this light, was taken of the completion of the feasibility study on the Free Trade Agreement (FTA) between Georgia and Korea, as well as of the ongoing negotiations on the Agreement on Mutual Promotion and Protection of Investments. The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister thanked the Korean side for providing humanitarian assistance to Georgia to deal with the pandemic and for timely transfer of diagnostic tools at an early stage of the spread of the virus. The Georgian side welcomed the decision to open Embassy of the Republic of Korea in Georgia in 2022, which will deepen the existing partnership and friendly ties between the two countries. The sides discussed the possibilities of holding joint cultural and public diplomacy events to mark the 30th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Korea. Alexander Khvtisiashvili thanked the Korean side for the progress made in this direction and expressed the hope that the two countries will move to the next stage of negotiations in the near future.
