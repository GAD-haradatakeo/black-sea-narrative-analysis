19 September 2025 On 18 September 2025, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, addressed the joint statement delivered last week by 36 OSCE participating States at the meeting of the OSCE Permanent Council, which criticized the human rights situation in Georgia. In his remarks, the Deputy Minister underlined that Georgia remains fully committed to its obligations under the OSCE human dimension. He emphasized that the Georgian Government’s consistent approach and unwavering commitment to the protection of fundamental human rights, democracy, and the rule of law ensures that these values are safeguarded for all citizens of Georgia. “It must be stressed that Georgia’s democratically elected government is taking all necessary steps to guarantee strict adherence to the principles of the rule of law, transparency, and accountability within the country. Any actions that run counter to national interests and the sovereignty of the state are addressed in accordance with national legislation,” stated Lasha Darsalia. When speaking about the upcoming local self-government elections, the Deputy Minister noted that, as in the past, the Government of Georgia reaffirms its firm commitment to holding these elections in a free, fair, and transparent environment, in line with best practices and international standards. “We believe that the premature and unfounded assessments and assumptions reflected in the statement have a negative impact on the pre-election environment. In this regard, although the OSCE/ODIHR report on the 2024 parliamentary elections became the target of attacks and speculation by certain political actors, both domestically and internationally, the Government of Georgia has officially invited the OSCE/ODIHR to conduct monitoring of the upcoming local self-government elections. In accordance with our legislation, the accreditation and registration process is open, and both international and domestic observers are duly registered,” stated the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs. When commenting on the ongoing investigative processes within law enforcement agencies, Lasha Darsalia noted that the relevant institutions, in line with national legislation, are carrying out all necessary legal procedures in the course of investigations and criminal prosecutions. “The same applies to the judiciary, which considers all cases brought before it, as confirmed by the judgments of the European Court of Human Rights,” Darsalia emphasized. The Deputy Minister also addressed the situation concerning opposition leaders who failed to appear before the parliamentary investigative commission and disregarded their legal obligations, which consequently led to their criminal liability. “The President of Georgia has recently pardoned the leaders of that party who are participating in the local self-government elections. This decision was taken with the aim of dispelling any claims that the elections are being held under conditions of restricted political competition,” Darsalia remarked. At the session, the Deputy Minister also cited statements by the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights and the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom: “In conclusion, allow me to refer to the recent remarks of the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights, Volker Türk, who stated: “Freedom of expression is fundamental – but it does not protect those who use speech to spur violence and hatred. A growing number of leaders are now instrumentalizing freedom of expression to cover their own speech while silencing those who disagree. This must be called out, each and every time”. Similarly, the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, Keir Starmer, emphasized that: “People have a right to peaceful protest. It is core to our country’s values. But we will not stand for assaults on police officers doing their job,” remarked Lasha Darsalia. “The Government of Georgia remains fully open and committed to working with its partners on all issues of shared interest, with the clear objective of further strengthening friendly relations and constructive cooperation. At the same time, I wish to reaffirm our commitment to OSCE obligations, as well as our irreversible path toward European integration, which remains steadfast and defines our future course,” stated Lasha Darsalia.
