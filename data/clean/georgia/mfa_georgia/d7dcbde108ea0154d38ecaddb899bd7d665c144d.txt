1 November 2018 17.02.2017, Munich – TheMunich Security ConferenceCoreGroupMeeting was held in Belarus from 30October to 1 November 2018. Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia spoke at the panel dedicated to regional conflicts. Along with Lasha Darsalia, the panel was attended by Armenian President Armen Sarkissianand Moldovan Prime Minister Pavel Filip. Lasha Darsalia highlighted Georgia’s peace policy with respect to resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict. He provided the participants of the meeting with detailed information on the difficult security and human rights situation in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. According to him, even 10 years after the Russia-Georgia War, Russia still continues to disregard the 12 August 2008 Ceasefire Agreement thus destabilizing the situation not only in Georgia but also in the region and posing a threat to European Security. In this light, Lasha Darsalia highlighted the necessity of the international community’s firm and principled position with respect to Russia whose illegal and destructive actions infringe the territorial integrity of the sovereign states and undermine the international order. On the sidelines of the event, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister held bilateral meetings with the President of the UN General Assembly, the Minister of Foreign and European Affairs of Slovakia, State Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affair of Moldova, Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Belarus. TheMunich Security ConferenceCoreGroupMeeting brought together high- and highest level delegations. Heads of international organizations, representatives of academic and research centres.
