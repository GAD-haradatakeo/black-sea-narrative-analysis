22 September 2025 On 22 September 2025, Giorgi Zurabashvili, First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, attended a festive event dedicated to the Independence Day of the Republic of Armenia. In his address, Giorgi Zurabashvili emphasized that the restoration of Armenia’s independence stands as a powerful symbol of commitment to freedom, democracy, and justice. He noted that Armenia’s contemporary achievements attest to the steadfast pursuit of these values by the Armenian people - an aspiration that found its clear expression in the referendum of 21 September 1991. Deputy Minister Zurabashvili highlighted that the active political dialogue, expanding economic cooperation, and cultural exchanges between Georgia and Armenia further strengthen the already close, multidimensional cooperation and mutual support between the two nations. He underlined the significance of the historic friendship and strategic partnership between Georgia and Armenia, grounded in a shared vision of ensuring peace, stability, and enhanced cooperation in the region. Concluding his remarks, Giorgi Zurabashvili extended his congratulations to the Republic of Armenia on its Independence Day and wished the Armenian people peace, prosperity, and success.
