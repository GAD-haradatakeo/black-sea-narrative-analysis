4 January 2021 On 4 January 2021 the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, held a telephone conversation with his Indian counterpart, Subrahmanyam Jaishankar. During the conversation the parties underscored the established friendly relations between Georgia and the Republic of India in terms of bilateral relations, including positive dynamics in the dimensions of politics, economics, investments, culture and education. Additionally, emphasis was made on multilateral formats of cooperation and the positive dynamics of said efforts within the framework of international organisations. During the discussion the Ministers also deliberated the situation surrounding the COVID-19 pandemic both in Georgia as well as in India. It was underscored that the governments of the two states have taken steps to tackle the aforementioned pandemic. The importance of coordinated efforts and mutual assistance during these trying times was also noted. The discussion also touched upon the issue of starting the vaccination process, as well as future avenues of cooperation in this regard.
