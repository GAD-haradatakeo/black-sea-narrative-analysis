16 March 2019 On 14 March, the U.S. House of Representatives Committee on Foreign Affairs adopted H.R. 1616 European Energy Security and Diversification Act of 2019. Speaking before the Foreign Affairs Committee, Congressmen Michael McCaul (R-TX) highlighted the need to support Georgia. According to him, the Act aims at assisting the countries that are under Russia’s direct attack, including Georgia and Ukraine. Co-sponsored by Adam Kinzinger, William Keating, Brian Fitzpatrick and Vicente Gonzalez, the Act is aimed to assist European and Eurasian countries to reduce their dependence on energy resources from countries that use energy dependence for undue political influence, such as the Russian Federation; to promote the energy security of European and Eurasian allies and partners of the United States by encouraging the development of competitive energy markets that provide diversified sources, types, and routes of energy. The Act is yet another step taken by the United States to reduce Russian influence on the energy sector.
