19 March 2020 19.03.2020 Tbilisi – “We remain in contact with the European Commission, as well as with the EU Member States, individually. We are ready to assist, on a case-to-case basis, those citizens of Georgia, who are faced with problems relating to their 90-day legal stay in the EU” – the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani said at a press briefing following the Inter-Agency Coordination Council led by the Prime Minister. According to the Minister, the EU Member States have developed a temporary regulation, according to which, foreign nationals present on their territories, who are unable to freely move for reasons ofthe existing coronavirus-related situation, including force majeure, will have their visas extended, for which they should apply to competent authorities of the Member State where they are present. “Our consulates and embassies are ready to assist our citizens in this regard, based on the host countries they are in” – the Minister said. Georgia’s diplomatic missions abroad regularly update information on their websites and social networks regarding rules applying in their host countries and continue to assist Georgian citizens in their communication with respective services of the countries concerned.
