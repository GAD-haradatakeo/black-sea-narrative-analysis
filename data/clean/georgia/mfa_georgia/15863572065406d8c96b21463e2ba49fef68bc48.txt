22 November 2025 On 21 November 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, participated in the event dedicated to the 34th anniversary of Turkmenistan’s Independence Day and the 30th anniversary of the declaration of its permanent neutrality. On behalf of the Government and the people of Georgia, the Deputy Minister congratulated the friendly Turkmen people on these significant milestones and wished them continued progress and success. Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili spoke about the friendly and partnership-based relations between the two countries, noting that Georgia-Turkmenistan cooperation is grounded in the principles of mutual respect and equality. The Deputy Minister highlighted the positive dynamics of high-level exchanges. Particular emphasis was placed on economic cooperation between the two countries, including growing trade turnover and the increasing number of visitors arriving in Georgia from Turkmenistan. “According to data for the first nine months of 2025, trade turnover has increased by 10.3 percent. A total of 268 companies with Turkmen capital are registered in Georgia with active status. Close cooperation continues in the areas of transport, transit, and port infrastructure. We are successfully collaborating within the framework of the Middle Corridor development,” noted Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili. In conclusion, the Deputy Minister expressed his gratitude to the Ambassador of Turkmenistan, Dovletmyrat Seyitmamedov, and the entire staff of the Embassy for their efforts in strengthening the friendship between the two countries.
