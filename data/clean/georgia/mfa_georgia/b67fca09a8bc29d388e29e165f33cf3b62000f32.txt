3 March 2020 3.03.2020,Tbilisi – Within the framework of the Armenian Prime Minister Nikol Pashinyan’s visit to Georgia, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a bilateral meeting with his Armenian counterpart, Foreign Minister ZohrabMnatsakanyan. Discussions focused on a broad spectrum of issues on the agenda of Georgia-Armenia relations, including in the political, trade and economic areas and people-to-people contacts. The two ministers positively appraised the Armenian Prime Minister’s official visit to Georgia to outline various plans for the development of bilateral partnership. Paying special attention to co-operation between Georgia and Armenia in multilateral formats, the sides agreed to work closely to develop people-to-people contacts and signed a memorandum of cooperation in this area. Discussions also focused on efforts to prevent the outbreak of coronavirus.
