6 February 2026 Within the framework of a working visit to Washington, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia participated in the 6th International Religious Freedom Summit (2026). The Summit, initiated by the President of the United States, Donald Trump, aims to address global challenges to freedom of religion and belief and to mobilize international efforts to strengthen these freedoms. On the sidelines of the Summit, the Deputy Minister met with Vicky Hartzler, Chair of the United States Commission on International Religious Freedom (USCIRF). During the meeting, Lasha Darsalia briefed Chair Hartzler on the situation in Georgia’s occupied regions, including restrictions imposed on the local population with regard to freedom of religion and the conduct of church services in the Georgian language. The Deputy Minister also held a meeting with the Co-Chair of the International Religious Freedom Summit, Sam Brownback. Furthermore, Lasha Darsalia attended the National Prayer Breakfast, an event established in 1953 that brings together individuals of different faiths and religious denominations under the leadership of the United States Congress.
