13 June 2020 Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani spoke over the phone with his Danish counterpart, Jeppe Kofod. Conversations largely focused on current and future cooperation within the Eastern Partnership. The Ministers discussed the informal video-conference on 11 June involving the members states of the EU and EaP. They highlighted the strategic importance of EaP and the need of close coordination in developing ambitious goals of partnership. David Zalkaliani and Jeppe Kofod also exchanged views over the agenda of the video-conference of the heads of state of EU and EaP Member States scheduled for 18 June. In this view, David Zalkaliani updated his Danish colleague in detail on the Georgian side’s priorities regarding Eastern Partnership. Zalkaliani provided his colleague with information on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. He said that the occupation troops continue illegal activities on the ground by installing artificial barriers and violating human rights. The Danish Foreign Minister congratulated Georgia on the success in containing COVID-19. Highlighting the dynamics of bilateral relations between Georgia and Denmark, Zalkaliani thanked his Danish colleague for the important assistance provided to Georgia through the Danish Investment Fund.
