12 January 2022 On 12 January 2022, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Khatuna Totladze received the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Republic of Lithuania to Georgia Andrius Kalindra. The sides underlined the traditionally friendly and partners relations between Georgia and Lithuania. Khatuna Totladze thanked the Lithuanian Ambassador for her strong support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for the foreign policy priorities of Georgia. The meeting focused on Georgia's progress on EU and NATO integration path, including on prospects for the development of the Eastern Partnership format, on future plans for the Associated Trio and on the importance of intensifying the NATO integration process. The sides reviewed the ongoing developments in the region and issues relating to global security. The Lithuanian Ambassador of was informed once again focused on the actions taken by the Russian Federation in the occupied territories, including on the dire humanitarian situation and gross human rights violations. The sides underlined the need for the partners’ consolidated position in relation to the occupied territories of Georgia. Andrew Kalindra reaffirmed Lithuania's unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration process.
