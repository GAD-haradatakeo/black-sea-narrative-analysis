27 February 2020 26.02.2020, Tbilisi - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani opened Austria’s regional ambassadorial meeting in Tbilisi. “This fact stands as a manifestation of the special role currently played by Georgia in regional developments. We are glad to observe that Tbilisi is under close focus of our European partners already established as a host city for regional diplomatic meetings. A couple of years ago, our German and French colleagues held a joint ambassadors’ conference in Georgia and so we hope to see such practice continues, serving as an example for other countries” - Zalkaliani said. He also welcomed the conduction of another round of bilateral political consultations between the Georgian and Austraian Foreign Ministries. He also highlighted that Georgia’s partnership with Austria is based on shared values and mutual mutual trust and that Austria stands among Georgia’s most reliable partners demonstrating support in terms of vital issues for Georgia. Besides, Austria has been providing substantial assistance to Georgia’s socio-economic and institutional development, and EU approximation. The Minister also spoke about Georgia’s role in strengthening Europe’s energy security as well as the grave consequences of the Kremlin’s aggressive policy in the region. According to Zalkaliani, despite Russia’s unprecedented scale of provocations that further aggravates the already volatile security, humanitarian and human rights environment on the round, Georgia remains the leader of democratic transformations in the region. The Minister also highlighted the importance of peaceful resolution of the conflicts and the role of the EU in this regard.”European integration and NATO membership is the only natural path of our development and the best way to guarantee democracy, security peace and prosperity in our country” – Zalkaliani said.
