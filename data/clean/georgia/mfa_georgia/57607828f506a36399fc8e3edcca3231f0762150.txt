5 February 2021 On 4 February 2021, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with the Head of the EU Monitoring Mission in Georgia (EUMM), Ambassador Marek Szczygieł. The sides discussed the security and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions and in the villages across the occupation line. The sides discussed in detail the difficult security, humanitarian and human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. The Deputy Minister updated Ambassador Marek Szczygieł on Russia’s recent provocations. In this regard, special emphasis was put on the illegal detention of Georgian citizens, the so called borderisation process and free movement restrictions, ethnic discrimination and banning of medical evacuations. Lasha Darsalia underscored the need The Deputy Minister underscored that illegally arrested Georgian citizens must be immediately released. The sides stressed the need of unconditional restoration of the Gali Incident prevention and response mechanism (IPRM), as well as Ergneti meetings. Lasha Darsalia highlighted the role of the EUMM as the only international monitoring mission in Georgia in deescalating the situation on the ground.
