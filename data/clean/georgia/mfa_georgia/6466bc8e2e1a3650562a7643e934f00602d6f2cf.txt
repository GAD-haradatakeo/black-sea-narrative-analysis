2 September 2021 A telephone conversation took place between the Vice Prime Minister/Foreign Minister of Georgia, David Zalkaliani, and the Foreign Minister/Alternate Prime Minister of the State of Israel. The Foreign Ministers of the two countries reaffirmed the importance of the centuries-old friendly relations between the Georgian and Jewish nations and the prospects for their further development. Talking points included current issues on the agenda of bilateral and multilateral cooperation and the great economic potential, including in the areas of bilateral trade, investment and tourism. The sides discussed ways to combat the global pandemic and the dynamics in the development of partnership between the two countries. Yair Lapid invited his Georgian counterpart to pay an official visit to Israel and expressed the hope for an opportunity to discuss all important issues in greater detail during the upcoming visit. David Zalkaliani congratulated Yair Lapid on his appointment, wished him success in his future endeavors and confirmed the readiness to host his Israeli counterpart’s official visit to Georgia at any convenient time.
