18 January 2022 The Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia met with the State Advisor to the Prime Minister of Romania, Iulian Chifu. Noting the traditionally friendly relations between Georgia and Romania the sides stressed the importance of further intensifying the political dialogue. Discussions focused on regional and Black Sea security issues. The sides highlighted the importance of strengthening trade and economic ties, including in the areas of transport, energy and communications, in order to fully exploit the resources of the Black Sea. Speaking about Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration, The Deputy Minister informed the State Advisor on Georgia's plans to submit an application for EU membership in 2024, as well as on Georgia’s priorities, including physical rapprochement with the EU and enhancement of sectoral cooperation. Aspects of cooperation in the format of the Eastern Partnership and the Associated Trio were also discussed. While on the theme of NATO-Georgia relations, the sides stressed the importance of cooperation with NATO on Black Sea security and the tangible progress made by Georgia as an aspirant on the path of Euro-Atlantic integration, Focusing on the situation in the occupied territories, the Deputy Minister briefed the State Advisor on the dire humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied regions and the facts of human rights violations. The sides highlighted the importance of international partners’ support for bringing these issues to the international agenda and for implementing the Georgian government's peace initiatives. The State Adviser to the Prime Minister of Romania reaffirmed Romania's unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its European and Euro-Atlantic integration.
