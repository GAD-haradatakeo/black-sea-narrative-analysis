24 November 2023 On 24 November 2023, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, took part in the event dedicated to the National Day organized by the Embassy of the United Emirates. On behalf of the Government of Georgia, the Deputy Minister congratulated the United Arab Emirates on their National Day and wished them prosperity and success. Alexander Khvtisiashvili talked about the growing diplomatic relations between Georgia and the United Arab Emirates. According to the deputy minister, 2022 was a special year for the bilateral relations of the two countries as they celebrated 30th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and the United Arab Emirates - a reliable friend and partner in the Gulf region; Strengthening relations with the UAE as one of the leading countries is a very important priority to us. The UAE has always stood at the helm of innovation and progress, which is an important promise of hope for the future and optimism. Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili also emphasized the importance of the Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA) recently signed in Dubai, which laid the solid foundation for further deepening trade and investment cooperation between the two countries and offers the two countries many opportunities in the future to promote more intense and simplified trade and economic relations. Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili highlighted the exchange of high level visits this year, including the visit of the Prime Minister of Georgia to Dubai, attending World Government Summit 2023; visit of the Deputy Prime-Minister of Georgia to Abu Dhabi (10 October) and visits of the Minister of State for Foreign Trade of the UAE to Georgia (in March and in October in the framework of “Tbilisi Silk Road Forum”). According to Khvtisiashvili, these visit have greatly contributed to the enhancement of bilateral relations between Georgia and the United Arab Emirates.
