13 December 2018 Speaking at the NATO-Georgia Commission meeting, the NATO Secretary General said that Georgia is moving forward using all the mechanisms needed for NATO membership. Georgia has made an impressive progress in the implementation of the comprehensive reforms and is a role model of democratic development. According to Stoltenberg, the year 2018 was very dynamic in terms of NATO-Georgia co-operation to which attests the North Atlantic Council meeting with Georgia and Ukraine within the framework of the NATO Ministerial in July. The NATO-Georgia Commission meeting was attended by Deputy Foreign Minister Vakhtang Makharoblishvili from the Georgian side. Discussions during the meeting focused on the Annual National Programme review document. The Allies positively assessed Georgia’s implementation of the Annual National Programme and the democratic, economic and defence reforms implemented in the country. Talking points included issues relating to Black Sea security. The Allies expressed their concern over Russia’s aggressive actions both with respect to Georgia and Ukraine and reaffirmed their commitment to deepen co-operation with Georgia towards strengthening Black Sea security. Discussions also focused on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. The Deputy Foreign Minister updated the allies on Russia’s increased military presence in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions and the difficult humanitarian situation on the ground. Along with political support, the Alliance expressed its readiness to continue providing practical assistance to Georgia in terms of implementing projects within the Substantial NATO-Georgia Package.
