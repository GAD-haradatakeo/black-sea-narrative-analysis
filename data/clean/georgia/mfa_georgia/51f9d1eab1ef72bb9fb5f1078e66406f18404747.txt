14 September 2018 14.09.2017, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with European Union Special Representative for the South Caucasus and the Crisis in Georgia Toivo Klaar. Discussions focused on the security and human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied regions. The sides highlighted the need to effectively use the Geneva International Discussions and the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanisms as effective formats of negotiations. The Foreign Minister expressed his concern over Russia’s destructive steps affecting the entire peace process underlining the importance of constructive participation of all parties to the Geneva International Discussions and the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanism. Special attention during the meeting was paid to the need to deliver justice on the murder cases of Georgian citizens Archil Tatunashvili, Giga Otkhozoria, and Davit Basharuli. The Georgian Foreign Minister thanked the European Union Special Representative for the South Caucasus and the Crisis in Georgia for active involvement in the peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict, as well as in bringing closer the communities torn apart by the war.
