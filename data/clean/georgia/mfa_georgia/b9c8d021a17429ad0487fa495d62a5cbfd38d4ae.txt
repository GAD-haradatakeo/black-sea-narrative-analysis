30 July 2024 On 30 July 2024, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, met with Zhou Qian, the Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to Georgia. During the meeting, they discussed a broad range of bilateral and multilateral issues between Georgia and China. The parties highlighted the need to advance political dialogue and explored opportunities to enhance cooperation in trade, economics, culture, education, and other areas. They also considered ways to maximize the potential in tourism and trade, including the possibility of establishing direct flights from various Chinese cities to Georgia. The strategic importance of Georgia for the region and the potential to strengthen its role as a transit hub were emphasized.
