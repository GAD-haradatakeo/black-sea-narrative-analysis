24 March 2018 24.03.2018 Tbilisi -​ Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze has described the United States 2018 Spending Bill increasing US assistance for Georgia as yet another important step taken towards deepening Georgia-U.S. startegic partnership. According to Mikheil Janelidze the Bill gives a legal assessment to Russia’s aggressive actions that is highly important in terms of the non-recognition policy. “I would like to thank the US Congress, the Administration and President Donald Trump for the support of Georgia. This is first of all the demonstration of firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. The document bans financing of countries recognizing independence of Geo's occupied regions. bans financing of countries recognizing independence of Georgia’s occupied regions that is a very important lever in terms of the non-recognition policy” – Mikheil Janelidze said condemns any form of recognition of the independence of or the establishment of diplomatic relations with the Russian-occupied Georgian territories of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali (South Ossetia), as well as the support of the Russian occupation of Georgian territories. According to the document, none of the funds appropriated by this Act may be made available for assistance for the central government of a country that the Secretary of State determines and reports to the Committees on Appropriations has recognized the independence of, or has established diplomatic relations with, the Russian occupied Georgian territories of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali Region/South Ossetia.
