22 August 2018 UK Foreign Secretary Jeremy Hunt delivered his remarks at the United State Institute for Peace. He outlined several developments that should give cause for concern, of which the most alarming is the Kremlin’s policy. “Firstly the established rules of international conduct are repeatedly being flouted by Russia. The seizure of Crimea in 2014 was the first time that territory has been annexed in Europe by force of arms since 1945. But in fact it was not the Kremlin’s first territorial incursion in this century, which was the invasion of Georgia in 2008” – Jeremy Hunt said. While on the theme of the August war, the British Foreign Secretary underlined that “a limp response to Russia’s intervention in Georgia in 2008 can only have made the 2014 annexation of Crimea more likely”. According to him, credibility for a state provides a guarantee that its assurances can be relied upon by friends and its threats taken seriously by adversaries. “But instead of building up our credibility, we have been weakening it” – he said.. Jeremy Hunt believes that Russia’s foreign policy undermines the international order and makes the world a more dangerous place. “Those who do not share our values need to know that there will always be a serious price to pay if red lines are crossed – whether territorial incursions, the use of banned weapons or, increasingly, cyber attacks”- the UK Foreign Secretary said.
