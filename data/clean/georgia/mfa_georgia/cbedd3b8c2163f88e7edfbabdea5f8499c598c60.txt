11 February 2021 On 11 February, at the request of Georgia, the OSCE Permanent Councilheld an exchange of views regarding the prolongation of illegal detention of Georgian citizen Zaza Gakheladze for more than 12 years. Strictly condemning this illegal decision aimed at destabilizing the situation on the ground, the Georgian side urged the Russian Federation to ensure immediate release of Zaza Gakheladze and all other illegally detained Georgian citizens. According to the Georgian side, the prolongation of Zaza Gakheladze’s illegal detention is all the more alarming against the background of the judgement delivered by the European Court of Human Rights on 21 January – the judgement, which confirms the occupation of, and exercising effective control over the Georgian territories by the Russian Federation and holds Russia responsible for the flagrant violations of human rights on the ground. Statements in support for Georgia were made by the U.S. UK, EU and Ukrainian delegations, as well as by the Canadian delegation on behalf of Iceland, Lichtenstein and Norway. Expressing their concern over the prolongation of Zaza Gakheladze’s illegal detention, all speakers demanded his immediate release. They also called on Russia to comply with the Strasbourg Court’s judgment on the Russia-Georgia conflict. The delegations reaffirmed their strong support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity within its international recognized borders and underscored the necessity for Russia to comply the EU-brokered Ceasefire Agreement signed in 2008.
