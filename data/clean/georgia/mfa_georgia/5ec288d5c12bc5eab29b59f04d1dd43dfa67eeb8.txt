16 February 2018 16.02.2018, Munich - Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze met with Japanese Foreign Minister Tarō Kōno on the sidelines of the 54th Munich Security Conference in Munich. The two ministers highly appraised the historically friendly relations and successful co-operation. The Japanese Foreign Minister congratulated Mikheil Janelidze and Georgian people on the victory of Georgian sumoist Levan Gorgadze, also known as Toshinochin, who won the Emperor's Cup in Japan. The Georgian Foreign Minister thanked Tarō Kōno for the Japanese Government’s support to Georgia since the day of its independence. The sides highlighted the high potential of trade-economic co-operation between Georgia and Japan. They welcomed the ongoing negotiations over the investment protection agreement. The Japanese Foreign Minister expressed its interest in the promotion of Georgian wine in the Japanese market. Welcoming Japan’s decision to simplify visa procedures for Georgian citizens, the Ministers expressed their interest in the development of tourism co-operation. The Georgian side expressed its gratitude to the Japanese government for handing over hybrid and electric cars to Georgia's Ministry of Internal Affairs. Georgia’s economic and transit potential and the Japanese companies’ growing interest in Georgia was also highlighted. Discussing the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, the Japanese Foreign Minister reaffirmed his government’s firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. Special note was taken of the successful co-operation within the framework of international organizations, including the effective functioning of GUAM-Japan format.
