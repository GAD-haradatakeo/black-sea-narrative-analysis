6 November 2018 06.10.2018, Tbilisi - The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili held a meeting with the deputy minister of foreign affairs of the Republic of Poland Szymon Szynkowski vel Sęk. The sides discussed main issues relating to co-operation in the areas of public and cultural diplomacy. Key priorities of the newly-founded Polish Institute were also highlighted. Speaking about Poland as an active supporter of Georgia’s full-scale integration with European and Euro-Atlantic structures, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili thanked his Polish counterpart for his contribution to the promotion of Georgian culture in the European space and underlined that the Polish Institute will create an additional platform for the development of new forms of co-operation. According to Szymon Szynkowski vel Sęk, Poland considers Georgia as a leading country of the Eastern Partnership with tremendous cultural and intellectual potential and pledges its full support for co-operation in both bilateral and multilateral formats.
