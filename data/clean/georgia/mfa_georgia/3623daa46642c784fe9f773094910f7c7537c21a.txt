27 February 2018 27.02.2018, Geneva – Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze held bilateral meetings with his colleagues and heads of various state delegations within the framework of the UN High Level Segment of the Human Rights Council. Within the framework of the visit, Mikheil Janelidze met with the Chairman of the Council of Ministers of Afghanistan, Abdullah Abdullah. The sides spoke about excellent partner relations and co-operation between between the two countries. The head of the Afghan Government expressed his gratitude for Georgia’s important contribution to NATO’s peacekeeping operation. Mikheil Janelidze, for his part, reaffirmed Georgia’s readiness to continue its contribution towards strengthening peace and stability in the region, and to share Georgia’s experience in the institutional development of Georgia. The sides discussed the opportunities in the area of economy, highlighting the potential of co-operation in the process of transit of Caspian energy resources. While in Geneva Mikheil Janelidze met with his Hungarian counterpart, Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade Péter Szijjártó. Discussing important issues relating to bilateral co-operation, the sides expressed mutual support for each other’s countries in international formats. The Georgian Foreign Minister also met with Foreign Minister of Senegal Sidiki Kaba State Minister in charge ofHuman Rightsof Morocco Mustafa Ramid, African Union Commissioner for Political Affairs Minata Samate Cessouma. Discussions during the meetings focused on the need to develop relations in different fields, including in education and public health.
