10 July 2019 “The Russian President’s decision not to impose sanctions on Georgia was the only right decision in the given situation” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani told journalists. “The situation in which the country was thrusted due to certain persons’ irresponsible, destructive and provocative statements may have brought dire consequences for the country” – Zalkaliani said. According to him, given the Georgian government doing its utmost to ensure inclusive economic development and to improve the welfare of people and with the 4.5% growth of GDP in the face of the current challenges, such provocative statements served no other goal but to cause the reaction similar to that given by the Russian Duma. However, the government showed a high sense of responsibility to prevent it. “We were very close to the decision taken in 2006 when Georgians were massively deported from Russia. In addition to flight bans, Georgia faced the risk of economic embargo, and thousands of Georgian citizens living in Russia - the threat of deport, according to the Minister. “Who would have taken responsibility if something bad had happened to thousands of our citizens living in Russia?
