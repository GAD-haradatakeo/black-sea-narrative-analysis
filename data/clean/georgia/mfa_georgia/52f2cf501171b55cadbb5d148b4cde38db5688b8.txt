20 February 2022 In the framework of the Munich Security Conference, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with his Ukrainian counterpart, Minister of Foreign Affairs, Dmytro Kuleba. The Georgian Vice Prime Minister expressed his full solidarity with and support to Ukraine in the difficult period Ukraine is going through. The sides stressed that the international community’s support and consolidation is essential to de-escalate the situation and avoid intervention. In this light, the Foreign Ministers recalled the harsh lessons Ukraine and Georgia have learned from armed confrontation and occupation of territories by Russia. The Ministers stressed the importance of the international partners’ unanimous support, as well as of their united position within the scope of the ongoing security dialogue regarding the inadmissibility of dividing the world into spheres of influence and the inviolability of the principle of the sovereign choice of countries. The sides agreed to continue close coordination, both in bilateral and various international formats, to support each other and cope with the challenges facing them.
