10 March 2025 On 10 March 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, participated in the opening ceremony of the Third International Consumer Protection Week. The event was organized by the Competition and Consumer Protection Agency, the Georgian National Energy and Water Supply Regulatory Commission (GNERC), the State Insurance Supervision Service, the National Bank of Georgia, the Communications Commission, and the National Food Agency. During the event, the Deputy Minister addressed the audience. In his speech, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili discussed the ongoing reforms in Georgia, highlighting the successful implementation of the Association Agreement, particularly in areas such as legislative harmonization, institutional strengthening, and economic development. The Deputy Minister emphasized that the protection of consumer rights is a key commitment under the Association Agenda, which Georgia has been effectively fulfilling. He also welcomed the efforts of the Competition and Consumer Protection Agency in raising public awareness regarding new consumer protection standards in the country. In conclusion, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili reaffirmed the Ministry of Foreign Affairs’ commitment to continuing its active cooperation with the Competition and Consumer Protection Agency, particularly within the framework of the Association Agreement, to further advance consumer protection in Georgia.
