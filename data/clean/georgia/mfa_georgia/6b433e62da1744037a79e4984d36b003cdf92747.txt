25 May 2018 “Gaining the independence established a beacon for the the Georgian statehood for the ensuing decades and the ideals of freedom and independence that drove the architects of independence 100 years ago resonate with us today as both Republic of Moldova and Georgia continue to struggle for liberty and unhindered independence” – reads the letter of the Minister of Foreign Affairs and European Integration of the Republic of Moldova, Tudor Ulianovschi to the Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze, on the occasion of the 100th anniversary since the foundation of the first democratic republic of Georgia. According to Tudor Ulianovschi, Moldova highly values the partnership and dynamic relations between the two countries. Tudor Ulianovschi expresses his willingness to further enhance the dialogue and co-operation between the foreign ministries of Georgia and Moldova. “I strongly believe that our shared values and interests will be fulfilled through materializing the common commitment towards European integration.
