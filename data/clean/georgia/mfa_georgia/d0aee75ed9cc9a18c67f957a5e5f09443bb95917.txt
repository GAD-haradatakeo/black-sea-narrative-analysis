15 October 2021 The Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with the President of the Republic of Slovenia, Borut Pahor. The meeting focused on Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration process and the steps taken by Georgia in this direction. The sides highlighted Georgia's plans to apply for EU membership in 2024. Discussions focused on the forthcoming Eastern Partnership summit and preparations in this regard. the Minister underlined the importance of the support of the Slovenian EU Presidency for Georgia. The conversation focused on the difficult humanitarian and security situation in the Russian-occupied territories of Georgia. The Minister thanked the President of Slovenia for supporting Georgia's territorial integrity and sovereignty, and the country's European and Euro-Atlantic integration. Zalkaliani briefed the President of Slovenia on Georgia's peace policy and stressed the Georgian government’s commitment to share with the citizens of the occupied territories all the benefits of European integration.
