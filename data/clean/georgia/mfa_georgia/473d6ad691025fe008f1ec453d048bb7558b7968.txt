19 October 2022 The Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia, participated in the Eastern Partnership Connectivity Seminar organized by the Ministries of Foreign Affairs and Transport and Communications of Lithuania together with the European Commission, on 19 October 2022 in Vilnius, Lithuania. In addition to the member states of the European Union and the Eastern Partnership, the strategic partners of the European Union - the USA and Japan - were invited to the event. The meeting was devoted to present the opportunities provided by the EU Global Gateway program and other regional connectivity frameworks, such as the TEN-T Network, the Three Seas Initiative, as well as other regional connectivity priorities for the Eastern Partnership countries. Deputy Minister Teimuraz Janjalia spoke about the growing importance of Georgia as a strategic link connecting Europe and Asia against the background of the current geopolitical developments, and about the country's plan to ensure the maximum use of its transit potential. The deputy minister emphasized the priority of the development of connectivity on the Black Sea, which will contribute to the development of alternative energy supply routes, cargo transportation and digital connections. Janjalia introduced the reforms implemented in Georgia, which are aimed at the modernization of the infrastructure, in accordance with European and international norms. In this context, he particularly emphasized the importance of the implementation of strategic projects agreed within the framework of the Trans-European transport network, as well as the economic and investment plan of the Eastern Partnership, including the development of the country's port infrastructure, modernization of railways and highways, the Black Sea underwater power transmission and optical fiber cable, Intensification of ferry shipments.
