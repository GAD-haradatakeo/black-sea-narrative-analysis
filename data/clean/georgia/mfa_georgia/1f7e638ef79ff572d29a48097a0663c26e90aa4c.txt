26 May 2020 The Georgian and Israeli Foreign Ministers talked over the phone on 26 May. The Israeli Foreign Minister, Gabriel Ashkenazi congratulated his Georgian counterpart and the Georgian people on the national holiday. For his part, David Zalkaliani wished his colleague success in performing his new role as foreign minister. Highlighting the historically close and warm relations between Georgia and Israel, the Ministers reaffirmed their commitment to resume the mutually beneficial cooperation, including in the areas of trade, economy and tourism. The parties expressed their satisfaction at the Israeli Government’s decision to resume air traffic with Georgia as a COVID-19-safe country. Special note was taken of Georgia’s impressive progress in the fight against the pandemic and Georgia’s readiness to host tourists from Israel. Conversations also included issues relating to the need to deepen people-to-people contacts. The parties agreed to continue active communication to further strengthen bilateral relations.
