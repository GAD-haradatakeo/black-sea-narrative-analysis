4 November 2019 4.11.2019, Tbilisi – “The main task before us to put into action international human rights mechanisms in the occupied territories” - Lasha Darsalia. Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister Lasha Darsalia commented on the incident that took place on 4 November in Akhalgori when a 60-year-old woman was injured and was not carried to the Tbilisi-controlled territory for medical treatment. According to the Deputy Minister, the actions of the occupation regime have led to the extremely difficult humanitarian situation on the ground. Local residents are restricted from free movement and therefore, are often barred from getting qualified medical aid in the Tbilisi-controlled territory. Darsalia said that the Russian Federation and the occupation regimes are politicizing human rights and humanitarian issues. “Georgian side uses all international formats to underscore the necessity of adopting neutral approaches to discussing humanitarian issues. The main task before us is to put into action international human rights mechanisms in the occupied territories. We continue our engagement with our partners, first of all, with the co-chairs of the Geneva International Discussions in order to ensure the opening of the occupation line that remains closed for already several months and to give the local population the opportunities of free travel" - Lasha Darsalia said.
