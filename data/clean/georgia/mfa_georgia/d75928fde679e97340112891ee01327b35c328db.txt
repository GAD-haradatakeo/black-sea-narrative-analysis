10 September 2021 On 9 September 2021, in the framework of his working visit to the Republic of Estonia, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister of Affairs of Georgia Teimuraz Janjalia held a meeting with the Chairman of the National Defence Committee of the Riigikogu (Parliament of Estonia) Enn Eesmaa. Highlighting the exceptionally friendly relations between the two countries, the sides discussed issues of cooperation in bilateral and multilateral formats. The importance of active communication between the legislatures of the two countries was also noted at the meeting. Exchanging views over Georgia's progress in terms of EU and NATO integration, the sides emphasized the largest contribution of Georgia as a non-member of the North Atlantic Alliance to the Resolute Support Mission and its active participation in NATO-led operations. In the light of the current crisis in Afghanistan, the Estonian side once again thanked Georgia for its contribution to the military and civilian evacuation process. Teimuraz Janjalia informed the Estonian side about the difficult human rights and humanitarian situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. He underlined the need for the international partners’ consolidated position to maintain the issue high on the international agenda.
