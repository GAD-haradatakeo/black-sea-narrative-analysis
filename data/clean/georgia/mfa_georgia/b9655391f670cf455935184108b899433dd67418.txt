27 April 2021 On 27 April, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Alexander Khvtisiashvili participated in the 77th session of the United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific (ESCAP). The high-level session was held under the theme „Building back better from crises through regional cooperation in Asia and the Pacific“. In his address, Alexander Khvtisiashvili paid special attention to the importance of cooperation and solidarity in response to the coronavirus disease (COVID-19) pandemic and took stock of Georgia’s actions against the spread of the virus. The Deputy Foreign Minister also highlighted Georgia’s commitment to further deepen digital and transport cooperation with ESCAP Member States. According to Khvtisiashvili, Georgia did not close borders to transit cargoes during the pandemic and fulfilled all obligations to ensure the unhindered movement of cargoes across the borders. He also noted that the Government of Georgia developed a range of online services during the pandemic considerably facilitating the provision of cervices to citizens of Georgia. According to the Deputy Minister, Georgia is involved in important regional transport and energy projects providing for the traffic of cargoes and energy resources from east to west and vice versa. He also expressed the hope that Georgia will position itself as part of a digital bridge between Europe and Asia. Khvtisiashvili reaffirmed that Georgia remains committed to the Sustainable Development Goals for 2030 and called on the partner states to cooperate closely to ensure their implementation.
