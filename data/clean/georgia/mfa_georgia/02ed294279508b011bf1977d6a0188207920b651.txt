7 June 2021 The first stage of the grant competitions under the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia’s programs—“Be Your Country’s Young Ambassador,” “Support for Diaspora Initiatives,” and “Support for Georgian Dance and Song Ensembles Abroad”—has been completed. This initial stage involved verifying candidates’ compliance with the basic formal requirements of the application, including the submission of complete documents as specified by the grant programs and adherence to the deadlines established for their submission. Members of the competition commission, composed of representatives from the Parliament’s Committee on Diaspora and Caucasus Issues, the Administration of the President of Georgia, the Government of the Autonomous Republic of Abkhazia, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Ministry of Education and Science, the Ministry of Culture, Sport and Youth, the Youth Agency (a legal entity under public law), and the International Centre for Migration Policy Development (ICMPD), reviewed various organizational matters, agreed on the schedule for interviews with candidates, and discussed the criteria to be applied during the selection process. In particular, they evaluated candidates’ motivation, experience in diaspora-related activities, and knowledge of the programs, Georgia, and their host countries. All participants in the grant competitions are notified in writing of the commission’s decisions. It is noteworthy that these programs aim to strengthen the connection between the Georgian diaspora and their homeland, promote Georgia and Georgian culture abroad, support initiatives aligned with European integration and national interests, preserve national identity and cultural heritage among compatriots, and expand Georgia’s circle of young international friends. Accordingly, the winners selected to advance to the next stage of the competitions will implement a wide range of cultural, creative, sports, educational, and other activities in their countries of residence by 31 December of the current year. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia’s grant project “Be Your Country’s Young Ambassador” is implemented with the support of the International Centre for Migration Policy Development (ICMPD) under the EU-funded ENIGMMA 2 project.
