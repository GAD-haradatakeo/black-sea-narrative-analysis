16 November 2021 Within the framework of the Eastern Partnership Ministerial, the Vice Prime Minister of Georgia, Minister of Foreign Affairs David Zalkaliani held a meeting with the Secretary General of the EU External Action Service, Stefano Sannino. During the meeting, the sides discussed the process of Georgia's integration into the EU, the issues of Georgia-EU cooperation, both in the bilateral context and within the framework of the Eastern Partnership. In this regard, the sides focused on the expectations of the forthcoming Partnership Summit, as well as on Georgia's vision and initiatives in the process of economic, sectoral and physical rapprochement with the EU. Talks also focused on the goals of the Association Trio and the prospects for cooperation with the EU in a trio format. The sides also discussed the security situation in the region, the difficult humanitarian and security situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. The importance of EU involvement in the peaceful conflict settlement process was also underlined. Speaking about the current political processes in Georgia, the Georgian side highlighted the steps taken by the government to de-escalate the situation and its readiness to contribute to ensuring peace and stability in the country.
