30 April 2019 30.04.2019, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with Managing Director for Europe and Central Asia in the European External Action Service (EEAS) Thomas Mayr Harting. The sides discussed priorities of Georgia-EU bilateral co-operation, including the implementation of the Association Agreement, the democratic reforms implemented in the country, unhindered visa-free travel of Georgian citizens throughout the EU/Schengen area, the tenth anniversary of Eastern Partnership and the Ministerial Meeting scheduled to take place in Brussels on 13 and 14 May. Conversations also included European Neighbourhood Policy, security and stability-related issues, the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and the EU’s role in ensuring stability. Special accent was put on peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict. Zalkaliani highlighted the EU’s special role in implementing the Georgian Government’s peace initiative.
