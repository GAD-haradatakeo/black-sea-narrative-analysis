4 October 2021 On 4 October 2021, at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with the Apostolic Nuncios of the Holy See to Georgia, Monsignor Jose Avelino Bettencourt, the EU Ambassador to Georgia, Carl Hartzell, the U.S. Ambassador to Georgia, Kelly Degnan and the Japanese Ambassador to Georgia, Imamura Akira. The sides discussed the leaked surveillance recordings spread by media. The Georgian Foreign Minister reaffirmed the government's interest in establishing the truth around the leaked files, over which the Georgian Prosecutor's Office immediately launched investigative proceedings. Zalkaliani provided the foreign diplomats with information on the fact-finding process undertaken by the Prosecutor's Office of Georgia to establish the authenticity and source of files disseminated by the media. It was highlighted that the investigation agency appealed to the United States and other countries for legal assistance in the course of investigation. According to Zalkaliani, Georgia is ready to receive additional legal aid from interested international partners. Zalkaliani reaffirmed Georgia’s commitment to the principles of international obligations, including under the 1961 and 1963 Vienna Conventions on Diplomatic and Consular Relations.
