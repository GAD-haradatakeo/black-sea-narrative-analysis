25 September 2025 On 25 September 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, delivered a statement at the United Nations Security Council high-level open debate entitled: “Artificial Intelligence and International Peace and Security: Challenges, Multidimensional Impact, and Responsible Use.” In his remarks, Deputy Minister Darsalia emphasized the growing significance of artificial intelligence (AI) in the context of international peace and security. He provided a detailed overview of both the positive contributions of AI and the threats and risks associated with its harmful misuse - particularly its militarization, the conduct of cyberattacks, and the dissemination of false information and disinformation. “The development of artificial intelligence must take place within the framework of the UN Charter, international humanitarian law, and human rights law. Only an approach rooted in international law and the protection of human rights will enable the establishment of safe, secure, and trustworthy AI systems,” Darsalia underscored. At the same time, he highlighted that the effective implementation of the Global Digital Compact will strengthen collective efforts to harness AI for the benefit of humanity. “It is noteworthy that Georgia has taken a significant step forward by signing the Council of Europe’s Framework Convention on Artificial Intelligence, Human Rights, Democracy and the Rule of Law - the first legally binding international instrument in this field,” he noted. In conclusion, the Deputy Minister reaffirmed Georgia’s readiness to continue close cooperation with the international community to ensure that the development of artificial intelligence contributes to the protection of human rights and the strengthening of international peace and security.
