29 January 2026 „We firmly believe that the security, stability and development of the Black Sea region constitute a pillar of Europe’s prosperity“ - This was stated by the Minister for Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, at a joint press conference with the Secretary General of the Organization of the Black Sea Economic Cooperation (BSEC), Lazar Comanescu. Maka Botchorishvili noted that, as the Chair country of the Organization, Georgia will, over the next six months, make every effort to promote the effective functioning of BSEC and to implement activities and initiatives in line with the defined priority areas. According to the Minister, these priorities include strengthening regional connectivity and advancing the development of the Middle Corridor, ensuring energy security and enhancing the region’s tourism potential as well as cultural ties. Maka Botchorishvili also highlighted the challenges and opportunities present in the region, underscoring Georgia’s strategic location and its significant role in this context. “In an increasingly complex and rapidly evolving global environment, Georgia remains a constructive and reliable partner, playing a decisive role in the shaping of global supply chains, energy diversification and the deepening of economic ties” – The Minister emphasized. Emphasis was placed on the importance of strengthening connectivity and on Georgia’s vision of the Middle Corridor not merely as a transport route, but as a secure, rapid, and efficient economic bridge. In this context, Georgia’s strategic location and open economic policy were highlighted, positioning the country as a key hub linking Europe and Asia. The Minister drew attention to ongoing infrastructure initiatives, including the Black Sea submarine electricity cable, the development of liquefied natural gas production in the Black Sea, and the construction of the East-West Highway, all of which are expected to make a significant contribution to Europe’s overall prosperity. Maka Botchorishvili also referred to the successful cooperation between Georgia and BSEC, noting that, as a founding member of the Organization, Georgia has actively promoted regional dialogue and economic sustainability since its establishment.
