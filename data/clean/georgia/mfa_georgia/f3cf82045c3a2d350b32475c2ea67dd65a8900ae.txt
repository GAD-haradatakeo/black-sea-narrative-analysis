16 April 2021 On 15 April 2021, within the framework of the OSCE Permanent Council, a group of OSCE Member States (Bulgaria, Czech Republic, Estonia, Finland, Ireland, Canada, Latvia, Lithuania, Norway, Poland, Romania, Finland, Sweden and Ukraine) delivered a joint statement on the recent judgment by the European Court of Human Rights in the case concerning the armed conflict between Georgia and the Russian Federation in August 2008 and its consequences. The Statement refers to the judgment by the Court as a landmark decision in establishing the facts, in defence of the rules-based international order and the interests of those, whose rights and fundamental freedoms have been violated, and in many aspects continue to be violated up to the present moment, by acts of military aggression by the Russian Federation and subsequent occupation of a significant part of the Georgian territory. According to the Joint Statement, the Court ruled that the Russian Federation exercises effective control over the Georgian Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. The Court holds Russia responsible for the fact that around 23 000 Georgian nationals who fled their homes in the Georgian Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, continue to be deprived of a possibility to return to their homes. The Court underlined the duty of the Russian Federation to enable the inhabitants of Georgian origin to return to their respective homes. Based on the Joint Statement, the Court ruled that the Russian Federation was responsible for a violation of Articles 3 and 5 of the Convention due to the arbitrary detention of the 160 civilians and their inhuman and degrading treatment. The Court also came to a conclusion that the Georgian prisoners of war were victims of treatment contrary to Article 3 of the Convention. The Court attested that the Russian Federation maintained its strong military presence in both Georgian Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions instead of withdrawing its forces to the pre-conflict positions as stipulated by the ceasefire agreement. In the Joint Statement, the OSCE Member States call on the Russian Federation to fully comply with the judgment, including by putting an end to human rights violations in the Georgian Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, enabling the IDPs to return to their homes in safety and dignity, and ensuring an adequate and effective investigation of the events as indicated in the ECHR’s ruling.
