31 October 2025 Within the framework of the 43rd session of the UNESCO General Conference, the Georgian Pavilion was launched in the city of Samarkand, under the aegis of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. The pavilion is dedicated to Georgia’s rich cultural heritage. The opening ceremony was accompanied by a reception featuring performances of Georgian polyphonic music. The event was officially opened by the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili. In her address, Minister Botchorishvili highlighted Georgia’s ancient and diverse cultural heritage, its 8,000-year-old winemaking tradition, and the unique character of Georgian polyphonic singing. As the Minister underlined, both Georgian wine and polyphonic singing have been recognized by UNESCO as part of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, and Georgia takes great pride in being the cradle of wine. Minister Botchorishvili expressed her gratitude to her Uzbek counterpart for his support and warm hospitality, emphasizing the close and friendly relations between Georgia and Uzbekistan. The reception was attended by the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Uzbekistan, Bakhtiyor Saidov, representatives of the Uzbek government, senior UNESCO officials, as well as delegations and diplomats from various countries.
