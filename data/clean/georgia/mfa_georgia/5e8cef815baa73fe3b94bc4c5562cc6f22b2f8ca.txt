11 November 2021 The 41st session of the UNESCO General Conference is held in Paris, from 9 November to 24 November 2021. The Georgian delegation led by the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Khatuna Totladze takes part in the work of the Conference. On 10 November, in the framework of the General Policy Debates, the Georgian Deputy Minister spoke before the participants of the General Conference. She underlined the successful cooperation between Georgia and UNESCO and the country's efforts to achieve the goals of sustainable development. The Deputy Minister spoke about the ongoing reforms in the education system of Georgia. In addition, she expressed full support for the ongoing reforms in UNESCO, which is important in terms of increasing the efficiency and flexibility of the organization. The Deputy Minister thanked the Director-General of UNESCO for the decision to grant Tbilisi and Batumi the status of Creative Cities and to merge them into the Creative Cities Network (UCCN). Khatuna Totladze focused on the problems in the occupied territories of Georgia and noted the need to increase the involvement of the organization in the processes, especially in solving problems related to cultural heritage.
