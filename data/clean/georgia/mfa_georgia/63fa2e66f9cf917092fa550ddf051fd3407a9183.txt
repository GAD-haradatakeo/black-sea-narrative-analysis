11 June 2022 Within the framework of his visit to Dublin, on 10 June 2022, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia met with Robert Troy, Minister of State for Trade, Digital and Company Regulation of Ireland. The sides reviewed the current agenda of bilateral relations, with a special focus on deepening trade and economic cooperation and making full use of existing potential. The role of the Georgian-Irish Business Council was highlighted in terms of identifying new, promising areas of cooperation and intensifying contacts between the business communities of the two countries. The sides discussed the possibilities of exchanging business delegation visits and the importance of direct flights, which will give a positive impetus to the intensification of both trade-economic and people-to-people contacts. The Deputy Foreign Minister spoke about the implementation of the Association Agreement by Georgia, including the Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area. Teimuraz Janjalia highlighted the growing trends of trade with the EU, paying special attention to the importance of popularizing Georgian products in the EU market and strengthening the connectivity with the EU. In addition, the Deputy Minister briefed the host side on Georgia's geostrategic location, attractive business environment and investment opportunities.
