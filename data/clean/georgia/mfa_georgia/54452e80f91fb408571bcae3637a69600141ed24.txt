11 May 2022 Within the framework of the Ministerial Meeting of the Global Coalition against ISIS, the Georgian Foreign Minister, Ilia Darchiashvili met with his Croatian counterpart, Gordan Grlić-Radman. Discussing issues of bilateral relations, the Ministers paid special attention to the process of Georgia's European integration and shared Croatia’s experience in this regard. Gordan Grlić-Radman reaffirmed Croatia's support for Georgia on its path to EU integration. Making special mention of Georgia's progress in terms of both sectoral reforms and political rapprochement with the European Union, the Ministers underlined that the Georgian people fully support the country’s European integration. Gordan Grlić-Radman reaffirmed Croatia's readiness to share its EU integration experience with Georgia. Speaking on regional security issues, the ministers highlighted the common challenges facing the countries of the Associated Trio and noted that the EU's strong support for all three countries in responding to these challenges should be reflected in the relevant decision in the membership application review process. The ministers noted Croatia’s support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. They also spoke about the great potential that exists for deepening trade-economic and political relations between the two countries. The ministers agreed to exchange high-level visits.
