22 February 2019 22 February 2019 marks one year since the brutal murder of Archil Tatunashvili by Russia’s occupying forces in Tskhinvali. The illegal detention, torture and murder of Archil Tatunashvili is a clear example of the difficult human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and ethnic persecution. The Government of Georgia and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs spare no efforts to take effective measures, jointly with the international community, to ensure that justice is delivered. Georgia continues to place the murder cases of Archil Tatunashvili, as well as Giga Otkhozoria and Davit Basharuli high on the international agenda. The deprivation of Tatunashvili’s life drew an unprecedented international response. Through the dedicated efforts of Georgia/ Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Lithuanian Government imposed restrictive measures on persons on the Otkhozoria-Tatunashvili List; The European Parliament adopted a resolution calling on the Member States and the EU Council to apply sanctions to persons on the List; The Irish Parliament passed a resolution calling on the Irish Government to take respective restrictive measures; The U.S. adopted Georgia Support Act sanctioning individuals who violate human rights in the occupied regions;the Canadian Parliament adopted a resolutionmaking a recommendation to apply sanctions against those connected to the ongoing aggression against Georgia, including to human rights violations. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs continues to work with our partners to ensure that restrictive measures are imposed on persons accused or convicted of murder, kidnapping, torture or inhumane treatment, and serious damage to the health of Georgian citizens in the occupied territories that aims at the prevention of impunity and further deterioration of the human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied regions.
