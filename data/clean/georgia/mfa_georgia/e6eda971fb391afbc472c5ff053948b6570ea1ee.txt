24 September 2025 On 24 September 2025, within the framework of the 80th Session of the United Nations General Assembly in New York, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, delivered a statement at the Biennial Summit on A Sustainable, Inclusive, and Resilient Economy: Implementing Commitments on Financing the Sustainable Development Goals. The Minister reaffirmed Georgia’s readiness to contribute meaningfully to collective global efforts aimed at fostering development. She emphasized that Georgia remains firmly committed to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and continues to pursue their effective implementation. According to the Minister, despite significant challenges, the country has preserved resilience and macroeconomic stability. “Despite regional uncertainty and external shocks, we have maintained macroeconomic stability through prudent fiscal and monetary policies. Over the past four years, Georgia’s economy has grown by an average of 9.7 percent,” noted Minister Botchorishvili. In her address, she further underlined that such progress has been achieved even under the difficult conditions of ongoing security challenges and the illegal occupation of Georgia’s territories by the Russian Federation. Minister Botchorishvili highlighted that Georgia is actively implementing strategic projects in renewable energy development, digital transformation, and the enhancement of regional infrastructure. She singled out the Black Sea submarine cable and the Anaklia deep-sea port projects as pivotal initiatives aimed at strengthening connectivity between Europe and Asia. These projects, she stressed, will not only reinforce Georgia’s economic foundations but will also contribute to transforming the Middle Corridor into a broader, competitive, secure, and sustainable trade route. The Minister concluded that the Summit should serve as a turning point in reinforcing the implementation of the “Pact for the Future,” ensuring the mobilization of resources, political will, and cooperation necessary to advance fair and sustainable development. Finally, Minister Botchorishvili expressed her gratitude to the Secretary-General of the United Nations for convening the Summit and underscored its significance in light of the global challenges facing the international community.
