28 November 2022 On 28 November 2022, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia held a meeting with the EU expert mission visiting Georgia. At the meeting, the sides reviewed the issue of security sector reform, the priority areas of Georgia-EU cooperation in the field of security, and the formats of bilateral dialogue. The First Deputy Minister informed the representatives of the EU institutions about the security situation in Georgia and focused on the EU’s active involvement in the peaceful resolution process of the Russia-Georgia conflict. Discussions at the meeting focused on such issues as: Georgia's readiness to continue its involvement in the EU crisis management operations; more active sharing of expertise on the common security and defense policy of the European Union; Continuation of EU assistance programs, including in the field of cyber security; Enhancing cooperation with EU agencies, etc. At the meeting, the sides positively assessed the ongoing cooperation with the European Union in terms of security and highlighted the opportunities for further expansion of this cooperation. The First Deputy Minister once again confirmed Georgia's commitment to reforms to bring Georgia closer to EU norms and standards, and emphasized the importance of Georgia's involvement in the EU enlargement policy.
