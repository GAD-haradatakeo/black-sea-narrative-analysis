26 September 2020 On 25 September2020 within the framework of the high-level week of the 75th annual assembly of the UNSC (Security Council of the United Nations) the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, participated in an event organised by Germany and France, “Alliance for Multilateralism – Our shared responsibility in the rehabilitation process after the Pandemic”. In his address David Zalkaliani discussed the global threats stemming from the Coronavirus Pandemic. In this regard particular emphasis was made on the necessity for unwavering adherence to the principles outlined in the UN charter via the means of multilateral diplomacy. The Minister Noted that today the world faces new challenges, which cannot be overcome by individual states separately. Simultaneously, David Zalkaliani underscored the global challenges that existed prior to the Pandemic, which were only further exacerbated by this global health crisis. In this context, the Minister also discussed the occupation of Georgian territory on behalf of the Russian Federation, along with the hybrid threats emanating from Russia, which have also only been intensifying. Towards the end of his address, David Zalkaliani called upon the international community not to neglect cases of violations of norms of international relations. The foundation for the initiative “Alliance for Multilateralism” was built in 2019 by The Foreign Ministers of Germany and France, respectively, Heiko Maas and Jean-Yves Le Drian. The aim of the aforementioned initiative is to strengthen the multilateral system based on respecting the norms of international law, allowing to maintain peace and stability on a global scale.
