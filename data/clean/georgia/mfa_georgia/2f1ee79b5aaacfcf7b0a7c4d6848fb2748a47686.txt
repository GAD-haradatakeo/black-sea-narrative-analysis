20 September 2022 On 19 September of this year, Ilia Darchiashvili met with the foreign ministers of Peru, Saint Lucia, East Timor, Botswana, Ghana and Gambia. Following the meeting with Saint Lucia's Minister of Foreign Affairs, International Trade and Diaspora, Alva Romanus Baptiste, the Ministers signed an agreement on visa-free travel between the two countries. According to the ministers, the signing of this agreement is an important step, which will contribute to making stronger the relations between the two countries. Ilia Darchiashvili emphasized the special importance of cooperation with Latin American and Caribbean countries and expressed his gratitude for Saint Lucia for supporting Georgia in international formats. Ilia Darchiashvili and the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Peru, César Landa discussed Georgia's successful relations with Latin American countries and the existing potential in this direction. The ministers expressed their readiness to actively work for the development of bilateral cooperation in various fields, especially in terms of sharing the experience of Georgia’s successful reforms in public services. At the meeting with the Ministers of Foreign Affairs of Ghana, Gambia and Botswana, Shirley Ayorkor Botchwey, Mamadou Tangara and Lemogang Kwepi, the successful diplomatic relations of Georgia with African states, the growing potential of bilateral relations in education, sharing experience of reforms in the field of public administration and other directions were emphasized. At the meeting with the Minister of Foreign Affairs of East Timor, Adaljiza Magno, the sides emphasized Georgia's cooperation with South Asian countries, which provides for a good opportunity to further strengthen the friendly ties between Georgia and East Timor.
