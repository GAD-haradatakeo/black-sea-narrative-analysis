6 September 2018 6.09.2018, Tbilisi – “I would like to express my special gratitude to our key strategic partner - the United States of America for its decision to include Syria in a list of countries that will no longer be eligible to receive U.S. funding because of their recognition of the so-called independence of occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. It is a follow-on to the U.S. Appropriations Act where support for Georgia is registered both at the legislative and executive levels” - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said adding that the same sanctions were imposed against Nauru, Venezuela and Nicaragua. According to David Zalkaliani, Syria is not a beneficiary of U.S. financial support, however, yet another confirmation of this fact by Georgia’s strategic partner is an important political message to the international community and yet another proof that the United States of America firmly and effectively supports Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. “It is a very strong from the U.S. government that it will never tolerate the violation of such fundamental principle of international law as sovereignty and territorial integrity” – the Minister said. According to the Appropriations Acts for 2017 and 2018, none of the funds appropriated by these Acts may be made available for assistance for the central government of a country that the Secretary of State determines and reports to the Committees on Appropriations has recognized the independence of, or has established diplomatic relations with, the Russian occupied Georgian territories of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali Region/South Ossetia.” Documents Similar News
