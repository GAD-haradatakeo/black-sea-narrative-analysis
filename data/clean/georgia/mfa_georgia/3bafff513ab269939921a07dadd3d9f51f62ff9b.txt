17 October 2019 17.10.2019, Tbilisi – Integration into the European single market, maximum use of the potential of the Black Sea’s economic infrastructure, creation of the investment platform of Eastern Partnership are the key priority areas, on which, according to David Zalkaliani, the Georgian Foreign Ministry actively works to achieve goals beyond 2020 within the framework of the Eastern Partnership. “As far as the expectations and concrete initiatives for the period beyond 2020 are concerned, one of the main directions will be Georgia’s integration into theEuropeanSingle Market, which will provide us with the free movement of goods, capital, services, and labour – the 'four freedoms' within theEuropeanUnion. Another key area we will focus on will be the maximum use of the potential of economic infrastructure, i.e. implementation of joint transport projects and creation of EaP investment platform that wll connect the region with the EU businesses and will promote investments” – Zalkaliani said. According to the Minister, Georgia has already put forward a new strategic vision for the Eastern Partnership and proposals for the determination of goals beyond 2020. Documents Similar News
