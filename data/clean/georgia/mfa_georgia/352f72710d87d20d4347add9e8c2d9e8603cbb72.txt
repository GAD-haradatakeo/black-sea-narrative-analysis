15 December 2021 The 2nd Senate of the Highest Criminal Court of the Federal Land of Berlin has sentenced Vadim K., a Russian citizen, to life imprisonment for the murder of Zelimkhan Khangoshvili, a citizen of Georgia, on August 15, 2019 in Berlin, and for illegal possession of a semi-automatic firearm. The Senate ruling states (quote :) "The accused had received an order from a state body within the Government of the Russian Federation for the assassination of a former Chechen fighter, Tornike K., in Berlin for his negative sentiment towards the Russian central government and his role in the Second Chechen War." The Georgian side has been cooperating with the relevant German authorities regarding the case of the murder of the Georgian citizen and publicly responded to the fact of the murder from the very beginning. Moreover, after the German Federal Prosecutor's Office charged the accused Russian citizen for the murder and named the state authorities of the Russian Federation the mastermind behind the murder, it strongly condemned the Russian Federation's violent actions and called on the international community to respond. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia condemns and expresses its concern over the murder of the Georgian citizen and calls on the world community to properly assess the incident.
