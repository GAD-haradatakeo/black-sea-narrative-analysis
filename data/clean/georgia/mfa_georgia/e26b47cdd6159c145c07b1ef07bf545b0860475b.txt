5 June 2018 05.06.2018, Tbilisi - On 29 May 2018, the Syrian regime made an illegal decision and, as a result of the pressure from the Russian Federation, established the so-called diplomatic relations with Russian occupation regimes in Sokhumi and Tskhinvali in violation of the fundamental norms and principles of international law. This decision represents the direct support for Russia’s military aggression, ethnic cleansing and occupation of Georgia’s indivisible regions – Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia. In this regard, the Georgian side, based on the Decree of 31 May 2018 by the Government of Georgia, terminated the Agreement between the Republic of Georgia and Syrian Arab Republic on Diplomatic Relations of 18 May 1993. The Note Verbale of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia on the break-off of the diplomatic relations between the two countries on June 1 2018 was handed over to the diplomatic representations of the Syrian Arab Republic to the United Nations and in the Arab Republic of Egypt. The Permanent Representation of Georgia to the United Nations also informed the UN Secretary General about the break-off of the diplomatic relations between Georgia and Syrian Arab republic.
