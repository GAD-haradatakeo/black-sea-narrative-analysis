4 April 2019 4.04.2019, Washington – Within the framework of his visit to Washington, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani and Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Meleşcanu assessed the relations between the two countries and Georgia’s progress on the path of integration with the Alliance. Georgia being a valuable partner of NATO continues to make an essential contribution to the global security, as evidenced by Georgia’s successful participation in the NATO-led peacekeeping missions, according to the Ministers. Speaking about co-operation between the two countries in the context of Black Sea Security, the sides highlighted the military-political, as well as economic and practical importance of the Black Sea and highly appraised the role of Georgia and Romania in terms of effectively realizing the transit function of the Black Sea. Special note was taken of the quadrilateral meeting of the foreign ministers of Georgia, Azerbaijan, Turkmenistan, Romania. The Georgian delegation thanked Romania for the support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as of its European integration path.
