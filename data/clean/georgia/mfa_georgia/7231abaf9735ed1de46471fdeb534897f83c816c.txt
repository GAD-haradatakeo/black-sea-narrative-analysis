12 December 2017 12.12.2017, Tbilisi - Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze has participated in the conference “Leaving No One Behind” aiming to increase political participation of women in Georgia. In his keynote address to the conference participants, Mikheil Janelidze highlighted the country’s progress pointing out that the efforts exerted in team with the international partners will make it possible for Georgia to make a breakthrough in achieving gender equality in all aspects of political, economic and social life. Speaking about the efforts made by Georgia in implementing the UN Security Council Resolution N 1325 and the related national plan, Mikheil Janelidze underlined the importance of the Inter-agency Commission on Gender Equality, Violence against Women and Domestic Violence. Irakli Kobakhidze, Speaker of the Parliament of Georgia, Kakha Kaladze, Mayor of Tbilisi; and Tamar Chugoshvili, First Vice Speaker of the Parliament of Georgia and Chairperson of the Gender Equality Council; Martina Quick, Ambassador of Sweden to Georgia and Armenia; Ian Kelly, Ambassador of the United States to Georgia; and Shombi Sharp, UNDP Resident Representative in Georgia, addressed the participants of this high-level forum.
