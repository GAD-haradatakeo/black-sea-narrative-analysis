2 July 2021 Georgia-Slovenia bilateral consultations on EU integration were held by video-conference on 2 July 2021. The Director-GeneralforEuropean Integrationfromthe MinistryofForeign Affairs, Salome Shapakidze participated in the meeting from the Georgian side, whereas from the Slovenian side themeeting was attended by the Director-Generalat theDirectoratefor CommonForeignandSecurity Policyof the Ministry ofForeignAffairs, JernejMüller. The sides discussed the priorities of the Slovenian Presidency of the EU Council, Eastern Partnership and the summit scheduled for December 2021, as well as issues of priority importance in the relations between Georgia and the EU. The Slovenian side identified the further deepening of cooperation with the EU neighbourhood, especially, with the EaP countries as one of the priorities of the Slovenian Presidency. According to the representative of the Slovenian Foreign Ministry, cyber security, digitalization, connectivity and green transformation are also the key items atop the agenda of the Slovenian Presidency. Highlighting the strategic importance of the Eastern Partnership, both sides set new and ambitious goals for the future, based on the principles of differentiation and inclusiveness. Special attention was paid to prospects for the deepening of cooperation between the associated partners and the EU, especially based on the memorandum signed by the three countries on 17 May. Talking points also included COVID pandemic and its consequences, as well as the importance of greater cooperation with Slovenia, as well as with the EU in the process of economic recovery.
