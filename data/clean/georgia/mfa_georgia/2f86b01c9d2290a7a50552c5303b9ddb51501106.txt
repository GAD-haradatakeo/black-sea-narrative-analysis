27 February 2025 On 27 February, 2025, the first round of bilateral political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and El Salvador took place in San Salvador. The Georgian delegation was led by Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Lasha Darsalia, while the Salvadoran delegation was headed by Deputy Minister Adriana Mira. During the consultations, representatives of both foreign ministries conducted a comprehensive review of key issues on the bilateral and multilateral agenda. Particular emphasis was placed on the importance of enhancing cooperation across various sectors. The discussions underscored the necessity of fully leveraging the existing potential for strengthening trade and economic ties between the two countries. In this regard, both parties highlighted the crucial role of engagement from relevant government institutions as well as representatives of the business community. The significance of high-level bilateral visits was also emphasized. The deputy ministers of foreign affairs explored prospects for deepening cooperation within international organizations, with a special focus on mutual support for resolutions and candidacies. Additionally, the parties exchanged information on current developments in the South Caucasus and Latin American regions. At the conclusion of the meeting, the deputy ministers reaffirmed their commitment to maintaining close cooperation in both bilateral and multilateral formats and agreed to institutionalize the practice of holding regular bilateral consultations. Notably, within the framework of the consultations, the deputy ministers signed Framework Agreement on Cooperation between the Government of Georgia and the Government of the Republic of El Salvador.
