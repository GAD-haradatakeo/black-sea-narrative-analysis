17 November 2018 17.11.2018, Tbilisi – The Ministry of Foreign Affairs hosted a presentation of Sunday School Book, which consists of three levels and along with the Georgian language classes, provides lessons in the history, geography and culture of Georgia. The book was created at the initiative of the Georgian Foreign Ministry and through the support of the Ministry of Education and Science. Speaking before the attending audience, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani referred to the Georgian Diaspora as one of the key priorities of the Foreign Ministry’s activities. Zalkaliani paid special attention to the importance of Sunday Schools in terms of maintaining the national and cultural identity. Keynote Speakers also included the Deputy Minister of education, Science, Culture and Sport, Irine Abuladze, the Director General of the National Library under the Georgian Parliament, Giorgi Kekelidze and the Director of the Diaspora Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Rati Bregadze. The book is designed according to the modern principles of teaching and provides Georgian compatriots living abroad with proper language and communication skills. There is currently a total of 84 Georgian Sunday Schools functioning abroad and 11 Georgian language courses attended by up to 3000 children aged 6 to 14. In 2017, the Georgian Foreign Ministry developed a policy document “United and Strong Diaspora with Close Ties to Homeland”, which is aimed at ensuring the unity and strength of Diaspora and its close links with the homeland.
