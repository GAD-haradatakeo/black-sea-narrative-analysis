29 March 2024 On 29 March 2024, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Khatuna Totladze, participated and gave a speech at the conference - "Women’s role in economic growth and reforms." The deputy minister highlighted one of Georgia's key foreign policy priorities during the annual conference. She discussed the country's progress toward joining the European Union (EU), emphasizing the significant strides made and outlining future steps. Khatuna Totladze emphasized that attaining EU membership status marks a historic milestone for Georgia, providing a substantial boost to its integration into the European democratic community. This move tightly aligns Georgia's future with that of European nations. Furthermore, Totladze stressed the importance of promoting women's representation in public life and business, citing gender equality as a crucial foundation for Georgia's democratic and economic advancement. She pointed out that Georgia is actively collaborating with the EU to enhance women's positions within the business sector. The conference, organized by the USAID Economic Management Program, Business Development Program, and Gazelle Finance, also featured the participation of women entrepreneur associations such as the Council of Women Entrepreneurs in Georgia and the Business Federation "Women for the Future."
