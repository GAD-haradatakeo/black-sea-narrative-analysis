25 April 2023 On 25 April 2023, the third round of political consultations at the level of deputy foreign of Georgia and the Kyrgyz Republic were held in Bishkek. The Georgian delegation was led by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, and the Kyrgyz delegation - by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Aibek Moldogaziev. During the consultations, the sides discussed a wide range of bilateral and multilateral cooperation, including in political, economic, cultural-humanitarian fields. Discussions also focused on the issues of cooperation within international organizations. The sides spoke in detail about the possibilities of developing partnership in the fields of trade, economy, investments, transport, information technologies and tourism; plans were made to exchange high-level visits between the two countries and to activate trade-economic relations. The deputy ministers shared information and views on the current developments in the regions of the South Caucasus and Central Asia. Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili informed his colleague about the difficult situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and the actions taken by Russia along the occupation line. For its part, the Kyrgyz side confirmed once again its respect for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia within its internationally recognized borders. At the end of the consultations, the representatives of the international legal departments signed the protocol on preliminary consultations on the inventory of the contractual and legal framework of Georgia and Kyrgyzstan. Within the framework of the same visit, a meeting was held in the Parliament of the Kyrgyz Republic (Zhogorku Kenesh) with the head of the friendship group with Georgia, Baktibek Sidikov.
