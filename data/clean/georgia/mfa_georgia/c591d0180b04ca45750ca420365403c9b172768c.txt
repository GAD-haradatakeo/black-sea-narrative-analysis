23 May 2020 “Georgia suspended the Treaty on Open Skies toRussia in 2012 as, following the Russia-Georgia 2008 war,Russia began to make wrong interpretations of all international agreements, including the treaty, with respect to Georgia’s occupied territories as well. In 2010, Russia imposed restrictions on flights in a 10-kilometer corridor off the boundaries of occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali Region/South Ossetia, claiming these were now independent countries and not parties to the Treaty. This constitutes a violation of the norms of international law, wrong interpretation of the treaty and non-compliance with its obligations” - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said regarding the Treaty on Open Skies. According to Zalkaliani, Georgia and its strategic partners made an increasing number of condemning statements. This issue was posed and Georgia’s position in this regard was reiterated on more than occasion in Vienna, as well as within the consultative commission. “The U.S. Statement on the Treaty on Open Skies names the Russian-occupied Georgian regions as one of the reasons. This once again indicates that issues relating to Georgia is of paramount importance to Georgia’s key strategic partner and that we have deep and fundamental cooperation on all important issues of international security” – Zalkaliani said. According to him, Georgia has been in close communication with its strategic partner, the US, regarding the issue and continues consultations with other signatories of the treaty on how the country might be involved in the treaty in the future.
