11 October 2017 11.10.2017, Tbilisi - The EU-GeorgiaStrategic Security Dialogue was launched today at the Georgian Foreign Ministry. The first meeting of the dialogue was opened by the Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Minister of Foreign Affairs Mikheil Janelidze: “Georgia remains one of the strongest allies of the EU in the region and a key security partner as reflected in the EU's Global Security and Foreign Policy document. We are engaged in the EU crisis management operations and contribute to stability in Europe, as well as elsewhere. We are confident that through closer interaction, we shall be able to better assess our place in common European security architecture and contribute more effectively to the joint efforts. This initiative clearly indicates the EU’s interest to engage with Georgia further on issues related to foreign and security policy. That the regular EU-GeorgiaStrategic Security Dialogue in this format and within the Association council will provide a forum for closer intergration in the security dimension” – the Minister said. Mikheil Janelidze highlighted the importance of close co-operation with the EU, against the backdrop of the challenges facing the international community “Today, when we live in a more connected, contested and complex world, both conventional and hybrid warfare,extremism and radicalization, cyber-crime, terrorism and organized crime – challenges, which are becoming overwhelmingly sophisticated and complex, cannot besolved without shared responsibility and cooperation” – Mikheil Janelidze said. The Foreign Minister thanked the EU representatives for the EU’s active contribution to the peaceful conflict resolution in Georgia, saying that Georgia highly values the EU’s instruments EUSR, EUMM, GID co-chairmanship, the policy of engagementwithout recognition, financial assistance, which are essential to succeed in the peace process.
