22 September 2021 The Council of Ministers of Foreign Affairs of the Organization for Democracy and Economic Development – GUAM met on 22 September 2021, in New York, as part of the 76th session of the UN General Assembly. The meeting was chaired by the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani and was attended by the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Azerbaijan, Jeyhun Bayramov, the Minister of Foreign Affairs and European Integration of the Republic of Moldova, Nicu Popescu, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine, Dmytro Kuleba and the Secretary General of GUAM, Altai Efendiev. At the ministerial meeting, the sides discussed the main issues of cooperation on the agenda of the organization, including political, sectoral and inter-parliamentary aspects. David Zalkaliani provided the participants with detailed information on the difficult security, humanitarian and human rights situation in the occupied regions of Georgia and the provocative actions of the occupation forces. The Minister stressed the fact that Russia does not comply with the 2008 ceasefire agreement. In this light, he stressed the importance of the international community’s consolidated involvement. The Minister also spoke about the local self-government elections scheduled for 2 October 2021 in Georgia and related issues. The participants of the ministerial meeting positively assessed the cooperation within the GUAM + format with the partner countries and other international and regional organizations. The Ministers, at the initiative of the Georgian side, took a decision to set up GUAM Strategic Communications Working Group.
