12 November 2025 On 12 November 2025, the third round of political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the State of Kuwait was held. The Georgian delegation was led by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, and the Kuwaiti delegation - by the Assistant Foreign Minister for European Affairs, Sadiq Mohammed Marafi. The parties discussed the current agenda and cooperation plans between Georgia and Kuwait, including political, economic, investment, agricultural, tourism, and cultural areas. Particular emphasis was placed on exploring opportunities to deepen trade and sectoral cooperation, as well as prospects for concluding a Free Trade Agreement between Georgia and the Gulf countries. The growing flow of visitors from Kuwait to Georgia was noted with satisfaction. The importance of high-level visits was emphasized, as they are expected to further strengthen bilateral and multilateral ties. The parties also exchanged views on ongoing regional and global developments, as well as the security environment. Georgia’s Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, briefed his Kuwaiti counterpart on the dire situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. The Kuwaiti side once again reaffirmed its support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. The parties agreed that the next, 4th round of political consultations will take place in 2026 in Tbilisi. During the visit, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili met with Sheikh Hamad Jaber Al-Ali Al-Sabah, Chief of the Emir’s Administration and Minister of Kuwait. Additionally, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, together with Giorgi Pertaia, President of the Georgian Chamber of Commerce and Industry, met with officials from the Kuwait Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Bukhamseen Holding, and Al-Mutawa & Real Estate Investments to discuss ways to enhance bilateral trade and potential investment projects.
