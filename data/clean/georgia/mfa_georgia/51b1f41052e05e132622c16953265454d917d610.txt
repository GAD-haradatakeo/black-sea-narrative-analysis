23 February 2026 The third round of political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Republic of Tajikistan was held on 23 February 2025. The Georgian delegation was headed by Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Alexander Khvtisiashvili, while the Tajik delegation was led by Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Nigina Alizoda. During the consultations, the parties reviewed the existing relations between Georgia and the Republic of Tajikistan. Discussions covered bilateral and multilateral cooperation, including in political, economic and cultural-humanitarian areas, the further development of the bilateral legal framework, cooperation within international organizations, as well as ongoing developments in the region and globally. The parties noted with satisfaction the significant increase in trade turnover between Georgia and the Republic of Tajikistan over the past year. They underscored the growing importance of the Middle Corridor in enhancing connectivity between Asia and Europe and emphasized Georgia’s important transit role. The parties also exchanged views on developments in various regions of the world and on the broader security environment. Alexander Khvtisiashvili focused on the grave humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and the actions undertaken by the Russian Federation. For her part, the head of the Tajik delegation once again reaffirmed the Republic of Tajikistan’s unwavering support and respect toward Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity.
