7 May 2020 The Riyadh-Amman-Tbilisi charter flight performed yesterday has brought home 48 Georgian citizens. Among passengers were Georgian students and other citizens who have been stranded in Jordan and Saudi Arabia due to restrictions relating to COVID-19. The Georgian embassy kept providing them with food, drink and sanitary goods during the period preceding the repatriation. Under the Coordination Council’s schedule, flights have been performed from various cities of the world, including from Berlin, Vienna, London, Athens, U.S., Larnaca, Amsterdam, Beijing, Rome, Paris, Tel-Aviv, Barcelona, Warsaw, Madrid, Prague, etc. Georgia’s diplomatic representations abroad have assisted 19,215 citizens of Georgia in the host countries. The Government has organized the repatriation 9,815 citizens to Georgia, as of 7 May. The Georgian MFA website is updated every day to provide the latest information regarding support measures undertaken by the Georgian government with respect to Georgian citizens abroad.
