21 April 2021 On 20-21 April 2021, Member States of the Community of Democracies Governing Council (GC) convened for 33rd virtual meeting. Nino Shekiladze, the Head of the UN Division of the International Organizations Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs attended the meeting from Georgia as a member of the Governing Council (GC). The meeting was opened by Bogdan Aurescu, the Foreign Minister of Romania - the CoD Presidency and the CoD Secretary General, Thomas Garrett. The Parliamentary Secretary of the Canadian Minister of Foreign Affairs, Robert Oliphant presented Canada-initiated Declaration Against Arbitrary Detention in State-to-State Relations. The participants of the meeting discussed the activities carried out by the Community of Democracies and the issues relating to adherence to common democratic values and standards outlined in the Warsaw Declaration. Co-chairs of the Coalition’s working groups presented respective reports. Georgia as the chair of the Community of Democracies Working Group on Democracy and Technology delivered a report on its activities and future plans. The Governing Council meeting successfully adopted the Outcome Statement, in which Member States expressed their support for increased participation and representation of all women and girls in democratic governance and sustainable development processes. The Governing Council currently unites 29 member states – Argentina, USA, Guatemala, UK, El Salvador, Estonia, India, Italy, Japan, Canada, Republic of Korea, Costa Rica, Lithuania, Mali, Mexico, Mongolia, Morocco, Norway, Nigeria, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Georgia, Hungary, Uruguay, Finland, Sweden, Chile and Northern Macedonia.
