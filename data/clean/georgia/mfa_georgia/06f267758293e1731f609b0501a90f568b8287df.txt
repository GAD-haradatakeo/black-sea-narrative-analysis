1 March 2022 On 1 March 2022, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani, participated in a high-level side event within the framework of the 49th session of the UN Human Rights Council - "Russian attack on Ukraine and its human rights consequences", co-sponsored, among other countries, by Georgia as well. The event was dedicated to the dire human rights situation against the background of the ongoing war in Ukraine. Speaking before the participants of the event, the Foreign Minister condemned Russia's military aggression against Ukraine, expressed his solidarity with the heroic Ukrainian nation fighting for freedom, and reaffirmed his firm and unwavering support for Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders. According to the Minister, at stake is the rules-based international order, the fundamental principles of which are repeatedly and grossly violated by Russia. In this context, he spoke about the steps taken by Russia against Georgia, including the military intervention into Georgia in 2008 and the grave consequences of the occupation, and stressed the inadmissibility of forcibly redrawing the borders of the sovereign state and dividing the world into spheres of influence.
