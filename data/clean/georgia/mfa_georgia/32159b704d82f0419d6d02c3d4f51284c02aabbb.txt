27 August 2017 25.08.2017, Bad Hersfeld, Germany – “I am very much delighted to host the foreign minister of Georgia tonight hear in Bad Hersfeld - in the heart of Europe and in the heart of Germany. The Minister’s visit to Germany underlines how close and reliable the relationship between Germany and Georgia is. And we would like to send a positive signal to the future. The relationship between two countries is not based just on political dialogue, it is also an invitation to civil society, and to representatives from culture, media, science, and business to improve the cooperation” - Michael Roth, Minister of State for Europe at the Federal Foreign Office said following his meeting with the Georgian Vice Premier, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze. Mikheil Janelidze arrived in Frankfurt and Bad Hersfeld at the invitation of Michael Roth. The sides spoke extensively about Georgia-Germany relations, Georgia’s European integration process and the importance of Germany’s political, as well as practival support on this path. The Georgian Vice Premier updated Michael Roth on the ongoing reforms in Georgia, the process of implementation of the Association Agreement and other important steps taken on the path of European integration. Issues relating to the preparations for the Eastern Partnership Summit and Georgia’s expectations with respect to it were also discussed. The Ministers noted with satisfaction the effective implementation of the the activities as part of the year of friendship of Germany and Georgia. The sides also underlined the importance of Georgia’s status as the Guest of Honour at the 2018 Frankfurt Book Fair that will raise awareness of Georgia and its culture in Germany and in Europe as a whole.
