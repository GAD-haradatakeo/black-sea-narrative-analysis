7 June 2021 Latvia will provide Georgia with 15 thousand doses of COVID-19 vaccines - the Latvian Foreign Minister, Edgars Rinkēvičs said after the meeting with his Georgian counterpart, David Zalkaliani. “I am delighted for us to have kicked off the process of provision of 15 thousand doses of COVID-19 Vaccines to Georgia, with logistical processes already underway” – noted the Minister. Edgars Rinkēvičs underscored the significance of joint efforts in the fight against the pandemic. The Latvian Foreign Minister also discussed the reintroduction of direct flights, noting that “Air Baltic” conducts flights Tbilisi-Riga three times a week, expressing hope that such connections will return to the pre-pandemic levels, supporting tourism in both our countries. On 7-8 June the Latvian Foreign Minister, Edgars Rinkēvičs, is holding an official visit to Georgia, within the framework of which he will also meet with the Georgian PM, Irakli Gharibashvili, and the Speaker of Parliament, Kakha Kuchava. During his stay in Georgia the Latvian Foreign Minister will visit the occupation line in the village of Odzisi, acquainting himself with the situation on the ground. Additionally, Edgars Rinkēvičs will honour the fallen heroes who fought for Georgia’s unity, laying wreath to the Heroes’ Memorial, making a note in the book of most dignified guests.
