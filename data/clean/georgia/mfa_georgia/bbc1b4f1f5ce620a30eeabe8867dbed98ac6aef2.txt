17 September 2025 On 17 September 2025, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, hosted representatives of the diplomatic corps accredited in Georgia at the grape harvest. Alongside the Minister of Foreign Affairs and members of the diplomatic corps, the event was also attended by the Minister of Environmental Protection and Agriculture of Georgia, Davit Songhulashvili, and the State Representative in the Kakheti region, Giorgi Aladashvili. In Kakheti, at Château Zegaani, the diplomats were introduced to both traditional Georgian methods of winemaking and contemporary technologies. The program included a Georgian wine tasting and culinary masterclasses. Guests had the opportunity to visit vineyards featuring unique grape varieties cultivated on the estate and to participate in the traditional grape harvest. Representatives of the diplomatic corps were welcomed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili; the Minister of Environmental Protection and Agriculture, Davit Songhulashvili; and the State Representative in the Kakheti region, Giorgi Aladashvili. Minister Botchorishvili expressed her gratitude to the guests for their participation in the harvest. She emphasized that such events provide members of the diplomatic corps accredited in Georgia with an opportunity to become acquainted with the country’s rich culture, traditions and centuries-old history of viticulture and winemaking. Levan Mekhuzla, Chairman of the National Wine Agency, briefed the foreign diplomats on the significance of viticulture and winemaking for Georgia, provided information about the progress of the 2025 harvest, and presented the tradition of the grape harvest.
