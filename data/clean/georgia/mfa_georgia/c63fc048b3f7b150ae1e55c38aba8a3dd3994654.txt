8 June 2017 The congresman said that he is proud to be the co-chair of the Congressional Caucus on Georgia. He highlighted the importance of Georgia-U.S. partnership underlining that “the United States is going to stay and stand with Georgia in the future”. Ted Poe recalled the Georgia-Russia war in 2008. According to him, “the reason Russia decided to pick Georgia as the first former soviet country to invade was that Georgia is really a beacon of hope and democracy in that region”. He said that the world’s reaction to Putin’s aggression was not strong enough in 2008 that prompted Russia to enter into Crimea and East Ukraine. The congressman spoke about the government’s role in Georgia’s transformation into a European and Euro-Atlantic country. He took special note of Georgia’s successful democratic development expressing his support for the free trade agreement between Georgia and the U.S. He thanked Georgian soldiers for fight on the battlefield alongside Americans that creates a greater bond between the two nations. On closing, Ted Poe read out a passage from the inaugural address of the 35th President of the United States, John Kennedy: “Let every nation know, whether it wishes us well or ill, that we shall pay any price, bear any burden, meet any hardship, support any friend, oppose any foe, in order to assure the survival and the success of liberty.” According to the American congressman, these are the words that iunderpin the relationship of the United States with Georgia.
