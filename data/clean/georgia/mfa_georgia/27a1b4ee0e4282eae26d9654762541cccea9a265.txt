12 April 2021 The Georgian First Deputy Foreign Minister, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili held his first meeting with the U.S. Deputy Chief of Mission in Georgia, Rian Harris. Discussing various issues on the agenda of bilateral cooperation, the sides highlighted the importance of the U.S. engagement in and support for Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration process. While exchanging views on strategic partnership between the two countries, the sides paid special attention to the issues relating to deepening trade-economic, defence and security cooperation. Talking points also included the progress made by Georgia in implementing the Association Agreement. In the light of Georgia’s NATO membership, special note was taken of the ongoing preparations for high-level meetings and formats scheduled for the coming days and the importance of coordinating planning with the key strategic partner. The First Deputy Foreign Minister and the Deputy Chief of Mission also discussed the tense situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, as well as the security environment in the region, in general.
