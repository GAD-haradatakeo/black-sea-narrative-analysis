4 May 2023 On 4 May 2023, a delegation of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan, led by the Parliamentary Vice Minister for Foreign Affairs, Yuumi Yoshikawa, visited Georgia. Within the framework of the visit, the delegation met with the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia. The sides discussed the current issues relating to relations between Georgia and Japan, both in bilateral and multilateral formats. Special attention was paid to the positive dynamics of high-level visits over the recent period. The sides emphasized the need to further strengthen the partnership in all possible directions. თhe importance of mutual cooperation within the international organizations was also emphasized. Discussions focused on the global and regional security environment. Lasha Darsalia thanked his Japanese colleague for his steadfast support of Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. At the end of the meeting, the sides reaffirmed their readiness to continue close interaction, which will take the partnership between the two countries to an even higher in such areas as economy, connectivity, sustainable development, energy security, climate change, cultural and people-to-people contacts.
