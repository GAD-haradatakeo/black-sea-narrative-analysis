29 January 2026 The Minister for Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, met with Lazar Comanescu, Secretary General of the Organization of the Black Sea Economic Cooperation (BSEC). The meeting focused on Georgia’s assumption of the organization’s chairmanship, effective 1 January 2026, and on reviewing the cooperation agenda for this period. The meeting addressed the priorities of Georgia’s six-month chairmanship, highlighting the country’s role in the region and the achievements made through the consistent policy. According to the Minister, Georgia, during its chairmanship, aims to deepen regional dialogue and to strengthen the strategic role of the Black Sea as an integral component of European and global security. The meeting also focused on the challenges facing the region, with particular emphasis placed on the importance of upholding the international order based on the norms and principles of international law. The Minister reaffirmed to the Secretary General of the BSEC Georgia’s readiness to make maximum efforts, within the framework of its chairmanship, to enhance the effectiveness of the organization. Within the framework of his visit to Georgia, the Secretary General of the BSEC will hold meetings with senior representatives of Georgia’s executive and legislative authorities.
