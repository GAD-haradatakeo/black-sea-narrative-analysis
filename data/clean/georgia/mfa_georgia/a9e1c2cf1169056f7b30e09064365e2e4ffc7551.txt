26 September 2024 On 25 September 2024, the Ambassador of the People's Republic of China to Georgia, Zhou Qian officially delivered a note to Georgia's Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, informing him of the decision by the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. As of 30 September, Georgian citizens will be able to travel to Hong Kong visa-free and stay for up to 30 days. During the meeting at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, both sides discussed key points of bilateral cooperation between Georgia and China. Special focus was placed on leveraging the visa-free travel opportunity for Georgian citizens in Hong Kong, especially in terms of promoting tourism, enhancing people-to-people connections, and strengthening business and trade-economic relations. It is worth noting that since May 28, 2024, Georgian citizens have enjoyed visa-free travel to the People's Republic of China. Following the signing of the visa-free agreement between Georgia and China, the Georgian government worked actively with Hong Kong authorities to extend this privilege to Hong Kong, which maintains its own immigration and visa policies due to its special administrative status. In 2018, Georgia signed a free trade agreement with Hong Kong, facilitating greater access to the Hong Kong consumer market for Georgian products. As one of the world’s largest international financial, transport, and trade hubs, Hong Kong is home to some of the most important commercial ports globally. The decision to allow Georgian citizens visa-free entry to Hong Kong is yet another significant outcome of the strategic partnership established between Georgia and China last year.
