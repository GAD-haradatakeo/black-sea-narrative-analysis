9 September 2019 9.09.2019, Tbilisi – “McCain was a true friend and champion of Geogia and he demonstrated this friendship from the very outset of Georgia’s independence. During my office at the Embassy in Washington, I met him several times. He had very interesting views on Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic perspectives” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani told Senator John McCain’s wife – Cindy McCain as he spoke at the Tbilisi International Conference organised by the McCain Institute for International Leadership and the Economic Policy Research Center. According to the Minister, thanks to Senator John McCain’s efforts and due to the support of Georgia’s key strategic partner – the United States, Georgia continues to move ahead. It is considered as the most successful aspirant country which has all practical instruments to attain its ultimate goal – membership of NATO, including European Neighbourhood Policy, NATO-Georgia Commission, Annual National Programme. Highlighting Georgia’s impressive security and defence reforms that were carried out in close keeping with the Alliance’s recommendations, Zalkaliani said that as an aspirant country and a reliable strategic partner, Georgia continues to make its own contribution towards strengthening global peace and security. “Georgia is the largest non-NATO contributor to the the Resolute Support Mission operation in Afghanistan where Georgian troops have fought shoulder to shoulder with US troops”. In the words of Zalkaliani, membership of NATO is one of the top priorities of Georgia’s foreign and security policy. “The Government of Georgia remains committed to take effective steps to advance on the road to NATO membership” - Zalkaliani said.
