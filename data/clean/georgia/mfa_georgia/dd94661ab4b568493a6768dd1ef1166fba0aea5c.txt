27 October 2022 "The official visit of the Prime Minister of Georgia to Hungary has ended today. Hungary is an outstanding supporter of our country's European and Euro-Atlantic integration. Accordingly, this visit bears a special significance" - the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili is quoted as saying while evaluating the visit of the Head of the Government of Georgia, Irakli Gharibashvili to Hungary. According to him, the visit will yield concrete tangible results in terms of Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration. Further, the minister noted that the existing successful cooperation between the two countries will move up to a newer level. "New impulses in the bilateral relations that we have pursued over the years will bring our two countries even closer, and our cooperation will get even stronger," Darchiashvili explained, emphasizing that trade and economic relations, as well as cooperation in the field of education, will be of priority interest to Georgia and Hungary. "We had very interesting and in-depth discussions on how to strengthen and deepen our relations. I am confident that this visit will lead to an even more dynamic, more result-oriented cooperation. We count on Hungary as a strong supporter of our country. In our pursuit of foreign priorities, Hungary, together with other partners, will be one of the countries to support and help our country effectively," Ilia Darchiashvili said. Documents Similar News
