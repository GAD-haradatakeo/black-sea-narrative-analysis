24 September 2018 According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, during her meeting with the Georgian Prime Minister, Mamuka Bakhtadze, the High Representative of the European Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy Federica Mogherini gave a high appraisal to the reforms being implemented by the Government of Georgia. “At the meeting with the High Representative of the European Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy, Federica Mogherini, we discussed all important aspects of bilateral co-operation between Georgia and the European Union. Mogherini gave a high appraisal to the reforms being implemented by the Government of Georgia towards the strengthening of economic and democratic institutions. It was emphasized that Georgia will further move ahead with the successful implementation of the Association Agreement that will contribute to Georgia’s further integration with the European Union. Georgia has a very ambitious plan to take steps beyond the association Agreement that, quite naturally, was met with approval by the European Union” – David Zalkaliani said. According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, discussions also focused on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. It was reaffirmed that the EUMM, as the only international mechanism acting as a deterrent factor, makes a serious contribution to the efforts of avoiding the further escalation of the conflict. According to Zalkaliani, the European Union will continue to engage actively in the Geneva International Discussions.
