1 November 2019 „After a long-term pause, the Georgian Prime Minister paid an important official visit to the Republic of Turkey. Meetings were conducted in a friendly and warm atmosphere. We are returning to Georgia with concrete and tangible results” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said commenting on the visit of the Georgian governmental delegation’s visit to Turkey. According to him, main areas of strategic partnership between the two countries were identified during the Georgian Prime Minister’s meeting with the President of Turkey. “As a result of the visit, the sides agreed to conduct a meeting of the Strategic Partnership Council in Turkey which will deal with all issues of topical importance in our bilateral relations” – the Minister said. In the words of the Minister, the visit once again made clear that Turkey firmly supports Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. According to Zalkaliani, discussions focused on important geostrategic projects that are carried out through the involvement of Georgia and Turkey. Special attention was focused on the importance of further enhancing transport and infrastructure projects that will bring important economic benefits to Georgia and Turkey, as well as to the entire region. “The South Caspian Corridor and the Baku-Tbilisi-Karsi Railway are the main projects on which we will place more meaningful focus in the future” – Zalkaliani said. According to him, the leaders of the two countries reaffirmed the necessity of further deepening trade-economic relations. “”The potential of our countries is not used to the full and I believe that this meeting will give a new impetus to our efforts to double the trade turnover between our two countries” the Minister said adding that the Turkish President will arrive in Georgia soon that is a further proof of solid strategic partnership between Georgia and Turkey.
