7 November 2018 7.11.2018, Tbilisi – “The previous government committed a crime not only against people but also against the country by betraying the principles, which Levan Mikeladze adhered to” – the Georgian Foreign Minister, Davit Zalkaliani said when signing a memorandum between the Levan Mikeladze Institute and the Foundation. „Levan took this step first of all for the sake of his country and for the sake of the ideals he was so deeply committed to. He had an army of friends both in Europe and America. He was held in high esteem internationally and that’s what was so frantically feared then. But we have nothing to fear about. We are loyal to those principles and values. Levan wrote in his letter that his decision was fully in line with his values and principles. Now we will take over from him his relentless fight and efforts to ensure that Georgia regains its rightful place in the European and Euro-Atlantic family. He also wrote about his feeling of danger saying that Georgia would still have to fight its way ahead and many ordeals awaited us. Indeed, all his apprehensions came true. Problems and obstacles will always continue to hinder our path but if we get united around such noble personalities we will succeed to overcome with dignity the problems facing us” - David Zalkaliani said. According to David Zalkaliani, Levan Mikeladze took the important step, at the cost of his life, to save the reputation of his country. He established a high standard of diplomacy. He dreamed about a European-style state – an ideal to which the Foreign Ministry is now aspiring. The event held to commemorate the outstanding Georgian diplomat Levan Mikeladze was attended by representatives of the Levan Mikeladze Research Institute, members of the Board of the Levan Mikeladze Foundation, as well as the diplomat’s friends and family members.
