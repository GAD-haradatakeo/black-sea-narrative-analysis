10 May 2018 10.05.2018, Tbilisi - Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze attended the reception on the occasion of the 70th anniversary of independence of the State of Israel. Mikheil Janelidze congratulated the Israeli people on their national day. He spoke about the centuries-old friendship between the two countries that becomes increasingly stronger day by day. “A clear example of this friendship is the fact that on 28 March Georgia recognized the Georgian-Jewish relations as intangible cultural heritage. Georgia has already addressed UNESCO to give international recognition to these unique relations” – Janelidze said. According to the Foreign Minister, Israel is an example of how “the nation may get united and build an exemplary state within a short period of only 70 years, despite the persisiting challenges”. “What sets Israel apart is its democracy, aspiration to novelty and a remarkable sense of patriotism” – the Minister added. Highlighting the high level of political and economic partnership between the two countries, the minister appraised as yet another step towards the strengthening of economic co-operation the signature of a memorandum between the ministers of economy of Georgia and Israel, which is a step to start “studying the opportunity of free trade between the two countries”. Mikheil Janelidze said that the relations based on common values, trust and mutual respect provides a solid basis for the further development of the political dialogue between the two countries and serves the strengthening of partnership in the areas of foreign relations, defence, internal affairs, economy, investments, agriculture, tourism and healthcare.
