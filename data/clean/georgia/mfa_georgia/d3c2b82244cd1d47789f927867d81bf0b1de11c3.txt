21 February 2024 A meeting of the Association Council between Georgia and the European Union chaired by the Prime Minister of Georgia, Irakli Kobakhidze and the EU High Representative for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy, Joseph Borrell took place in Brussels on 20 February 2024. During the meeting, the agenda of the Association Council, encompassing key aspects of Georgia-EU cooperation, was addressed. Both sides welcomed the European Commission's historic decision to grant Georgia candidate status, acknowledging Georgia's advancements toward European integration. Furthermore, the sides assessed the progress on the European Commission’s nine recommendations, reaffirming the European Union's unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. Additionally, attention was drawn to Georgia's progress in implementing the Association Agreement and the Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Agreement, as well as its increasing significance in the context of EU connectivity policy.
