7 July 2022 The 2nd thematic group on industrial and enterprise policy and mining activity; tourism; corporate law and corporate governance; consumer policy; tax policy of the EU–Georgia Economic and Sectoral Cooperation Sub-committee held its 7th regular session on 7 July. The participants of the meeting positively assessed Georgia’s efforts towards integration with the European Union, and significant progress on implementation of the Association Agreement. The adoption of new laws on "Entrepreneurs" and "Consumer Protection" was positively evaluated as a step forward bringing the Georgian legislation closer to that of the EU in the above-mentioned areas. The participants of the meeting highlighted Georgia’s progress in taxation and customs sectors achieved within the framework of the Association Agreement, as well as the decision taken in 2022 regarding the inclusion of Georgia in the Fiscal Program (Fiscalis Programme), which represents an additional opportunity to share EU experience. A positive assessment was also given to measures carried out for the promotion of tourism, innovations, technologies, small and medium enterprises. The session was chaired by Davit Bujiashvili, the Director of the EU Aid Coordination and Sectoral Integration Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, from the Georgian side, and Daniela Tramachere, the Head of the Economic Affairs and Global Health Division of the EU External Action Service, from the EU side. From the Georgian side, representatives of the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Justice, the National Tourism Administration, Produce in Georgia agency, the Innovation and Technology Agency, the ServiceforAccounting,Reporting and Auditing Supervision, the National Minerals Agency and the Competition Agency took part in the session, and from the European Union, the European Commission, Representatives of the European External Action Service and the EU Delegation to Georgia.
