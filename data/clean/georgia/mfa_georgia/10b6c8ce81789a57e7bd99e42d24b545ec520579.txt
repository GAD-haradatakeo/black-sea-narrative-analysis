25 October 2022 On 24 and 25 October 2022, the OSCE Mediterranean Conference was held in the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan. The Georgian delegation led by the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, participated in the conference. Within the framework of the conference, a high-level political panel was held on the strengthening of security and cooperation in the Mediterranean region and the role of youth in this regard. The First Deputy Minister made a statement on behalf of the Georgian delegation. In the statement, Lasha Darsalia spoke about the role of young people in building a safe and peaceful society and the efforts made by the Georgian government in this direction. The First Deputy Minister highlighted Georgia's efforts to introduce the best international practices. Lasha Darsalia also focused on Russia's aggression in Ukraine, Russia's occupation of Georgian territories and the difficult security situation in the region. In this context, the First Deputy Minister spoke about the importance of peaceful conflict resolution and reconciliation between war-torn societies and noted that the Georgian government pays special attention to increasing the role and involvement of young people in the peace process, which is one of the government's priorities. Within the framework of the Conference, the First Deputy Minister held bilateral meetings with the Secretary General of the OSCE, Helga Maria Schmid, and the Secretary-Generalof theMinistryofForeign AffairsandExpatriatesLeenaAl-HadidofJordan.
