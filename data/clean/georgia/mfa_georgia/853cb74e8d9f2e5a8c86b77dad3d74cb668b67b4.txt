18 December 2018 18.12.2018, Tbilisi –Within the framework of the Ambassadors’ Conference – 2018, the Georgian Ambassadors abroad met with government representatives to discuss issues relating to defence and security. The panel moderated by Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani was attended by Defence Minister Levan Izoria, Head of the State Security Service of GeorgiaVakhtang Gomelauri, Head of the Georgian Intelligence Service Davit Sujashvili and representative the Information-Analytical Department of the Ministry of Internal Affairs,Kakha Kemoklidze. Discussions during the meeting focused on the security and defence challenges faced by Georgia. Special attention paid to the importance of inter-agency co-operation. In his speech Vakhtang Gomelauri highlighted Georgia’s role in international anti-terrorism efforts and the measures taken by the State Security Service to counter corrupt practices, smuggling of radioactive, chemical and biological substances. The Head of the State Security Service spoke about Georgia’s co-operation on the exchange of classified information with the EU Member States. He also paid special attention to issues relating to illegal migration flows to Georgia and to the entire region. Special accent was placed on attempts of external forces to create turbulent environment in the country. According to Defence Minister Levan Izoria, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs plays a paramount role in deepening co-operation with NATO as well as in strengthening interoperability of Georgian armed forces with NATO standards. He emphasized that by implementing the Substantial NATO-Georgia Package, Georgia has become a role model of relations with partners.
