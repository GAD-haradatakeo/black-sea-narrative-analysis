30 January 2024 On 30 January 2024, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Teimuraz Janjalia, met with the newly appointed director of the European Commission's Directorate General for European Neighborhood Policy and Enlargement Negotiations, the direction of cooperation with Eastern Partnership countries, Adrian Kirali. The meeting was attended by the head of the EU delegation to Georgia, Pawel Herczynski, the new Head of the European Union Delegation to Georgia, representatives of the European Commission and the EU delegation, as well as Georgian state agencies. The main topic of discussions was Georgia's implementation of the European Commission’s recommendations. The sides discussed in detail the steps taken by the country's authorities and the progress achieved in this regard. Both sides underscored the commencement of a new phase in the relationship between Georgia and the European Union, following the country's attainment of candidate status. Both sides acknowledged the significance of continuing the reforms to pave the way for accession negotiations.
