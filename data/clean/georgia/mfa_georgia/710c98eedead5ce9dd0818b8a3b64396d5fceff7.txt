17 July 2020 On 17 July of this year, the first meeting of the competition commission for the grant program “Support for Diaspora Initiatives” was held at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, chaired by Deputy Minister Vladimer Konstantinidi. During the meeting, commission members reviewed the results of the first stage of applications and agreed on the principles for subsequent work. A total of 87 applications were submitted from 29 countries. Due to non-compliance with the basic formal requirements, 13 applicants did not pass the first stage, and 4 applicants withdrew their applications. The grant program was open to Georgian citizens residing abroad. Its objective is to provide effective assistance to compatriots and to fund projects aimed at supporting Georgian citizens and their families abroad in the context of the ongoing challenges posed by the spread of the novel coronavirus (COVID-19). The program also seeks to support the functioning of existing diaspora organizations and associations established by compatriots.
