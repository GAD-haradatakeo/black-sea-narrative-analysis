1 October 2021 The Georgian First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia spoke before the participants of the virtual side-event titled “Developing safeguards to prevent the abuse of new technologies and growing influence of digital authoritarianism” which was held on the margins of the 10th Ministerial Conference of the Community of Democracies on 30 September 2021. The event was organized by The Working Group on Democracy and Technology chaired by Georgia. The First Deputy Foreign Minister spoke about the special role of digital technologies in ensuring effective and accountable democratic governance, including by ensuring that citizens are involved in democratic processes and in public life. The important role of digital technologies in promoting gender equality, decision-making and in contributing to women's effective participation in economic, political and cultural processes was also emphasized. The First Deputy Foreign Minister focused on the risks of using new technologies to the detriment of democracy. A case in point is the cyber-attacks against democracies and the hybrid war, which Georgia has repeatedly experienced. According to Lasha Darsalia, such actions can impede the proper exercise of human rights, endanger democratic institutions and governance, and undermine the rules-based international order. Lasha Darsalia highlighted the importance of multilateral cooperation and the need for coordinated efforts in responding to this challenge. In this regard, he put a special accent on the important role of Working Group on Democracy and Technology.
