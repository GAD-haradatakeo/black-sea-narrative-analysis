17 September 2018 17.09.2018, Tbilisi – “Lamberto Zannier often visits Georgia but it is his first visit in the capacity as OSCE High Commissioner on National Minorities, . For over many years we have been maintaining close relations with the OSCE High Commissioner’s Office which carries out several projects in Georgia and helps us tensure that national minorities are fully integrated with Georgia’s political, as well as economic, social and cultural life, and that their rights are fully protected” - Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister David Dondua said. According to David Dondua, he provided Lamberto Zannier with detailed information on the difficult humanitarian and human rights situation in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. “It is interesting that Zannier plans a visit to the Abkhazia region to see for himself how difficult is the human rights and humanitarian situation on the ground” Dondua said. Within the framework of his visit, Zannier will hold meetings with the President of Georgia, Giorgi Margvelashvili, the Chairman of Parliament, Irakli Kobakhidze and other representatives of the executive and legislative authorities of Georgia. The OSCE High Commissioner will also hold meetings with the Public Defender, Nino Lomjaria and representatives of national minorities. It is Lamberto Zannier’s first visit in his capacity as High Commissioner. The OSCE High Commissioner on National Minorities, Lamberto Zannier will leave Georgia on 21 September.
