14 March 2019 14.03.2019, Tbilisi – Speaking before the Parliament’s Foreign Relations Committee, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani spoke about the important efforts made by the Foreign Ministry towards strengthening Georgian sovereignty and peacefully restoring Georgia’s territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders. According to him, the steps taken by the Foreign Ministry clearly indicate that Georgia is atop the international community’s agenda. David Zalkaliani singled out for special mention a series of international events held in this regard: The situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and the resolution of the conflict were discussed many times in the format of the UN Security Council, namely: at a closed meeting held under the chairmanship of Great Britain, on 8 August. The situation in Georgia’s occupied territories was discussed at the UN Security Council Open Debateheld at the initiative of the President of Poland, on 17 May, on “the role of the UN Security Council in upholding international law within the context of the maintenance of international peace and security“, as well as at the UN Security Council Open Debateheld under the chairmanship of the Foreign Minister of Poland, on 22 May on “Protection of civilians in armed conflicts”; the number of supporters of the UNResolution on the Statusof Internally Displaced Persons and Refugees from Abkhazia, Georgia and theTskhinvaliRegion/South Ossetia, Georgia increased up to 81; Georgia organized two high-level parallel events at the UN Headquarters to discuss the difficult security and human rights situation in the occupied regions; Within the framework of the 37th session, the UN Human Rights Council adopted Resolution on Co-operation with Georgia, which condemns the occupation of the Georgian territories and the grave human rights situation on the ground; The OSCE Permanent Council discussed the 10th anniversary of the Russia-Georgia war. The OSCE Group of Friends of Georgia released a statment and recorded a joint video of the foreign ministers of the Group Member States;within the framework of the 25th OSCE Ministerial Council, the OSCE Group of Friends of Georgia made a joint statement in support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia; theOSCE Parliamentary Assemblyat its 27th Annual Session in 2018adopted Resolution “Ten Yearssince the 2008 war inGeorgia”; The Council of Europe Secretary General’s 17th and 18th Consolidated Reports on the Conflict in Georgia were discussed in 2018; The Committee of Ministers’ Deputies of the Council of Europe adopted a decision on the agenda item: “The Council of Europe and the Conflict in Georgia” holding the Russian Federation accountable for the occupation of Georgia’s territories; The issue relating to Georgia was discussed at the 128th Session of the Council of Europe; On the occasion of the 10th anniversary of Russia-Georgia war, the Committee ofMinistersof the Council of Europe made a statement in support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia; The Council of Europe Parliamentary Assembly adopted Resolution on „Humanitarian needs and rights of internally displaced persons in Europe“, which holds Russia accountable for the grave situation in the occupied regions; Four rounds of the Geneva International Discussions were held in the course of 2018; On the occasion of the 10th anniversary since Russia’s large-scale military aggression against Georgia, the international community reaffirmed its unanimous position with respect to Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. The Foreign Ministers of Latvia, Lithuania and Poland and the Vice Prime Minister of Ukraine jointly arrived in Georgia; The European Parliament, the Senate of the Republic of Ireland, the Seimas of Lithuania and the Sejm of Poland adopted resolutions in support for Georgia 10 years after Russia’s large-scale military aggression against Georgia. Intensive efforts were made for ensuring the imposition by the international community of restrictive measures on persons in the Otkhozoria-Tatunashvili List; The U.S. enacted Consolidated Appropriations Act imposing financial sanctions on Syria and Nauru due to their recognition of Georgia’s occupied territories as “independent states”; The Asad Regime’s illegal decision on establishment of so-called diplomatic relations with occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region was unanimously condemned by the international community, including by the Syrian opposition forces reaffirming their unwavering support for Georgia’s territorial integrity. According to Zalkaliani, number one priority of the Foreign Policy Strategy 2019-2022 is the strengthening of Georgian sovereignty and peaceful restoration of Georgia’s territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders. In this light, he put special accent on efforts to deter Russia’s annexation of Georgia’s occupied territories and to prevent further escalation of the conflict; to ensure the strengthening of the non-recognition policy, the improvement of human rights and humanitarian situation in the occupied regions and return of IDPs and refugees to the places of their origin.
