31 October 2025 The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, delivered a statement at the 43rd session of the UNESCO General Conference. In her address, the Minister emphasized the vital role of culture in shaping national identity, as well as in fostering understanding and unity among nations. She drew attention to the importance of protecting cultural heritage - not only for Georgia, but for humanity as a whole. According to Minister Botchorishvili, over the centuries, Georgia has created an invaluable cultural heritage that continues to inspire humanity. At the same time, she noted that Georgia’s strategic location has contributed both to the diversity and uniqueness of its culture. “Our geographical position between Europe and Asia has served not only as a corridor for transport and economic exchange, but also as a bridge for ideas, traditions, and human connection. The cultural heritage of Georgia reflects both our distinct identity and the diversity that lies at the heart of our nation,” Botchorishvili stated. The Minister further highlighted that the recognition of the Georgian traditional method of winemaking in qvevri and of Georgian polyphonic singing as elements of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity has once again firmly established Georgia’s place within the global cultural landscape. In her address, the Minister also highlighted the importance of UNESCO’s mission in promoting peace. She noted that Georgia understands the true value of peace, having personally experienced the consequences of conflict. “Even today, parts of Georgia remain under Russian occupation, and the people living in those regions are deprived of access to education in their native language, of freedom of movement, and of the ability to preserve and celebrate their cultural identity. These realities remind us all of the importance of UNESCO’s mission - to ensure that education, culture, and heritage are protected everywhere, for every community, without exception," stated Maka Botchorishvili. The Minister reaffirmed that Georgia remains firmly committed to peace and to the principles of international law - sovereignty, territorial integrity, and the protection of human rights. At the same time, she commended UNESCO’s efforts aimed at safeguarding cultural heritage in the occupied regions of Georgia. According to Maka Botchorishvili, along with culture and science, education serves as the foundation of sustainable peace. “ For Georgia, education is the cornerstone of our progress and resilience. It empowers individuals, strengthens communities, and ensures that the next generation inherits not only knowledge but also the values of tolerance, equality, and mutual respect,” the Minister of Foreign Affairs highlighted. The Minister also drew attention to the planned reform of Georgia’s higher education system. „We highly appreciate UNESCO’s leadership in advancing the Education 2030 Agenda and in promoting international cooperation in this vital field. Georgia stands ready to further strengthen its partnership with UNESCO and with all Member States to ensure that education remains a force for opportunity, dialogue, and peace," Maka Botchorishvili said.
