30 November 2022 It is of paramount importance to Georgia to participate in the Meeting of NATO Ministers of Foreign Affairs in Bucharest. The Bucharest Meeting is a continuation of the ongoing high-level political and strategic dialogue between NATO and Georgia, which takes on a particularly meaningful focus in the light of the current security environment. Georgia welcomes the decisions adopted by the NATO Ministerial Meeting. It is particularly important that in the Statement of the NATO Foreign Ministers, the Alliance pays due attention to the reflection of the Open Door Policy and, in this light, reaffirms the decision taken at the 2008 Bucharest Summit that Georgia will become a member of NATO. Georgia highly appreciates the Alliance's strong support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. It is also important that the Ministerial Meeting in Bucharest took decisions regarding the implementation of a set oftailored supportmeasures forGeorgia. The main goal of these measures is to enhance Georgia’s interoperability with NATO, and build its defence capabilities and resilience. Georgia is glad that Finland and Sweden participate in the Ministerial Meeting as states invited to join the Alliance. Their accession will make the Euro-Atlantic area more secure. Georgia welcomes the decisions adopted by the Meeting of NATO Ministers of Foreign Affairs - the decisions that are aimed to strengthen the security of the Member States and provide continued support to the partners.
