13 December 2017 12.12.2017, Tbilisi - Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze participated in the conference highlighting first phase of the European neighborhood programme for agriculture and rural development (ENPARD), covering 2014-2017. The conference was held under the title "For a Better Rural Life.” Speaking before the audience, Georgia’s Prime Minister, together with the EU Ambassador to Georgia Janos Herman and Minister of Agriculture Levan Davitashvili summarized the achievements of the ENPARD. According to Mikheil Janelidze: “This is unprecedented grant aid to Georgia from the EU, already benefitting over 250 thousand Georgians. EU support to Georgian agriculture and rural development has exceeded 500 million resulting in new agriproducts and new EU market access. This is one of the EU programmes delivering tangible results to each citizen of Georgia and ensuring the development of our country and ensuring its European future” – Mikheil Janelidze said. The first phase of ENPARD ran from 2014-2017 with a budget of 150 million GEL. The programme was based on three key pillars: helping modernise and professionalise the Ministry of Agriculture and related institutions so they could better assist farmers; promoting and developing the cooperative model of farming in Georgia; and introducing the notion of rural development in Georgia, which means moving beyond only agriculture to create other employment opportunities in rural areas.
