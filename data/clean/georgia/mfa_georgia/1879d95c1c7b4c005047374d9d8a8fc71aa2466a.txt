31 August 2020 On August 31st of 2020 the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, held a meeting with the representative of the UN High Commissionaire for Refugees (UNHCR) to Georgia, Kemlin Furley. During the meeting the Georgian Foreign Minister expressed gratitude towards the representative of the UNHCR for the humanitarian efforts exerted by the local office, which entails supporting those displaced from Abkhazia and the Tskhinvali Region, as well as those still residing in the aforementioned occupied territories. Additionally. David Zalkaliani provided Kemlin Furley with information regarding the challenging humanitarian situation on the ground in the occupied territories of Georgia. The Minister placed particular emphasis on the recently activated provocations coming from the occupied Tskhinvali Region, which, as the pandemic continues to rage, creates specifically challenging circumstances for the peaceful population residing on the ground. In this regard, the significance of the involvement and engagement of the international community, including the UN, David Zalkaliani once again underscored the significance of the involvement and engagement of the UNHCR, along with the local office, in the 2nd working group of the Geneva International Discussions. Towards the end of the conversation Kemlin Furley presented the Georgian Foreign Minister with a letter from the UN High Commissionaire for Refugess, inviting him to participate in the 71st session of the Executive Committee of UNHCR to be held on 5-9th October of 2020, expressing her hope that Georgia will be represented in this event on the highest level.
