5 November 2025 On 5 November 2025, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Giorgi Zurabashvili, met with representatives of Die Junge Volkspartei, the youth wing of the Austrian People’s Party, which is part of Austria’s governing coalition. The delegation is currently visiting Georgia as part of a study trip. Die Junge Volkspartei is the largest youth political organization in Austria, uniting around 100,000 members. Its primary objective is to promote youth participation and engagement in public life at both the municipal and federal levels. During the meeting, the First Deputy Minister briefed the politically engaged youth on the key areas of bilateral relations between Georgia and Austria. He highlighted the fruitful cooperation between the two countries and the dynamic exchange of high-level visits that has intensified in recent years. Mr. Zurabashvili also outlined Georgia’s foreign policy priorities, emphasizing that integration into Euro-Atlantic structures remains the foremost goal of the Georgian government. He underlined that this aspiration reflects the steadfast will of the Georgian people and is enshrined in the Constitution of Georgia. The Deputy Minister provided the delegation with a detailed overview of the steps Georgia has taken in recent years to advance its rapprochement with the European Union, noting that Georgia continues to faithfully implement the commitments undertaken under the Association Agreement. The discussion also addressed the challenges facing Georgia, with particular attention to the grave humanitarian and human rights situation in the occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali/South Ossetia. The First Deputy Minister reaffirmed the Georgian government’s commitment to the peaceful resolution of the conflict and underscored the crucial role of the international community and the importance of close coordination with Georgia’s partners in this process.
