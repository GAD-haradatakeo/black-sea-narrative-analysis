19 February 2018 “The progress Georgia has achieved in terms of improving the business climate is exemplary both regionally and internationally” – Mikheil Janelidze said when commenting on the steps taken by the Foreign Ministry in the area of economic diplomacy. The Minister spoke about the tarde agreements between Georgia and its partners. “Georgia is an open economy ranking 8th and 9th in the world in economic freedom and ease of doing business, respectively. We signed a free trade agreement with China in 2017 and are also probing into similar opportunities with India. We enjoy free trade with all of our neighbors, the European Union, the European Free Trade Association, Turkey, Belorus, Ukraine, Russia and Central Asian states” – Mikheil Janelidze said. According to the Foreign Minister, the transformation of Georgia into the region’s trade and transit hub is one of the important priorities of the Georgian Government’s concept. “In parallel to strengthening co-operation with the EU and traditional partners, we explore new opportunities to develop relations south-east Asian countries”.
