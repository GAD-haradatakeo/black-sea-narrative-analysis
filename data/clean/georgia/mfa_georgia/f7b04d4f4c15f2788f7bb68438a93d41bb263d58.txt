2 May 2019 The 1344th meeting of the Committee of Ministers Deputies of the Council of Europe adopted its 6th decision within the scope of the agenda issue “the Council of Europe and the Conflict in Georgia” in Strasbourg, on May 2, 2019. Like the previous decisions, the document reiterates the unequivocal support of the Council of Europe Member States for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia within its internationally recognised borders; special attention was paid to the international obligations of Russia as the country exercising exercising effective control over the occupied territories of Georgia. The decision represents an important legal and political document whereby the international community assesses the grave situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and underlines that the Russian Federation’s military presence in Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions poses a threat to regional peace and security and violates international obligations including those under the 12 August 2008 Ceasefire Agreement. The decision states that more than ten years after the conflict between the Russian Federation and Georgia, Georgia is still prevented from exercising the legitimate jurisdiction over its own territories due to the fact that the Russian Federation continues to impede the peaceful conflict resolution process and undermines the security and stability in the region through its continuing military presence in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia. It is important to highlight the like the previous decisions, this document too reiterates that the conflict is between the two members states of the Council of Europe – Russia and Georgia. The document enlists the Russian Federation’s illegal activities in Georgia, including increased military exercises and infrastructure reinforcements, the implementation of the so-called treaties on alliance and strategic partnership/integration, incorporation of military units of the Tskhinvali region of Georgia into the armed forces of the Russian Federation, the creation of a so-called “joint group of armed forces” in the Abkhazia region, establishment of so-called “joint information and coordination centres of law enforcement agencies”, as well as the functioning of so-called customs points in both Georgian regions, aimed at the integration of these regions respectively into the customs sphere of the Russian Federation; installation of razor and barbed wire fences and other artificial obstacles along the administrative boundary lines (ABLs) that impede the peaceful conflict resolution process and undermines the security and stability. The document reiterates that any illegal act by the Russian Federation aimed at changing the status of the Georgian regions, including through issuing Russian passports and so-called residents’ permits, thus establishing a so-called status of foreign residents, have no legal effect and further complicate the situation on the ground. The Committee of Ministers Deputies expresses grave concern over impunity around the deaths of Georgian IDPs, David Basharuli, Giga Otkhozoria and Archil Tatunashvili, and over the recent case of arbitrary detention and death of another Georgian citizen - Irakli Kvaratskhelia under unclear circumstances. In this connection, the document takes note of the Georgian Parliament’s Resolution on the “Otkhozaria-Tatunashvili Act” and of Georgia’s Government’s decision to impose national restrictive measures against those responsible for the deaths of Archil Tatunashvili and Giga Otkhozoria; The decision expresses concern that the human rights situation in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region has been further deteriorating, taking note of the intensified discrimination of Georgians on the grounds of ethnicity, through additional restrictions to freedom of movement, residence, work, and property rights in case of forced registration as foreign residents or demands to change surnames and ethnic identity. The document expresses grave concern about the restriction on crossings in Abkhazia region for almost one month, and closure of crossing points in Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia for more than two months in the winter of 2019, which lead to severe humanitarian consequences for the local population. The Committee of Ministers Deputies highlights that IDPs and refugees continue to be deprived of their fundamental right to return to their places of origin in a safe and dignified manner. The document strongly condemns a decision in Sokhumi on 4 April 2019 that provides for the punishment by death. Reconfirming the high priority of an effective use of the Geneva International Discussions (GID) the committee expresses concern about the suspension since June 2018 of the functioning of the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanism (IPRM) in Gali. Reiterating their call to the authorities of Russia exercising effective control over the occupied territories, the Committee of Ministers Deputies urges the Russian Federation to create conditions for the voluntary, safe and dignified return of all IDPs and refugees; to cease any form of ethnic discrimination towards the residents of the Georgian regions, first and foremost, the ethnic discriminatory measures against the Georgian population of Gali and Akhalgori districts, including demands to register as foreign residents or to change the surnames; to remove any obstacles to the investigation into deaths of the ethnic Georgians and to bring the perpetrators to justice; to immediately cease policies leading to human rights violations in both regions of Georgia; to remove any impediment, restriction or limitation to the right to freedom of movement across the administrative boundary lines (ABLs), including for medical and education purposes; to cease arbitrary detentions of persons, including in the context of so-called “illegal border crossing” and to re-open the “crossing points”; to cease violations of the right to education in schools and preschools, including education in the native Georgian language in both Georgian regions; to prevent further deterioration of monuments belonging to the cultural heritage throughout Georgia’s regions. The Committee of Ministers Deputies welcomes the peace initiative of the Government of Georgia entitled “A Step to a Better Future”, as a sign of Georgia’s firm commitment to promote reconciliation and engagement between the societies on both sides of the administrative boundary lines; as well as the recently launched program of the Government of Georgia – “Produce for a Better Future”. The Committee of Ministers Deputies calls on the Russian Federation to secure immediate and unrestricted access to the territories beyond the control of the Government of Georgia to the Council of Europe monitoring bodies. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia welcomes yet another decision on the conflict in Georgia adopted the Committee of Ministers Deputies of the Council of Europe and expresses its gratitude to the Council of Europe Member States for their firm support and co-operation.
