23 May 2022 The First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia participated in the NATO-Georgia Commission at the level of Deputy Permanent Representatives to NATO, on 23 May 2022. The Georgian delegation presented the 2022 Annual National Program of Georgia at the meeting. The Allies positively assessed Georgia’s progress on the reforms and reiterated their support for the country’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration. The sides also discussed the ways to develop NATO-Georgia cooperation, the security environment in the region in the wake of Russian military aggression in Ukraine, and the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. In addition to political support, the Alliance pledged to further provide Georgia with its practical assistance, including for the implementation of the ongoing projects under the NATO-Georgia Substantial Package, which will strengthen the country's defence capabilities. The Allies unanimously reaffirmed their support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity and expressed their gratitude for the country's valuable contribution to Euro-Atlantic security. During the visit of the First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia to the NATO Headquarters, bilateral meetings were also held with the Assistant Secretary General for Defence Policy and Planning, Patrick Turner, and with the NATO Secretary General's Special Representative for the Caucasus and Central Asia, Javier Colomina.
