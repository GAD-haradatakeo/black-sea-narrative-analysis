23 April 2021 After the end of the trilateral ministerial between Romania-Poland-Turkey, the Foreign Minister of said states issued a joint statement. “We agreed that further strengthening of the political dimension of the alliance as well as its deterrence and defence capabilities, solidifying its sustainability and the “Open Doors” policy, along with supporting our Balkan partners, remains significant. We underscored that it is NATO’s constant interest to continue dialogue with our partners, considering that said relations are to remain beneficial to all side involved,” – read the statement. The significance of inviting Georgian and Ukrainian Foreign Ministers to the Ministerial was also noted in the address issued by the Foreign Ministers. “Considering the context, today, Foreign Ministers of Ukraine and Georgia were invited to attend informal meetings here in Bucharest, as well as to exchange positions. We managed to convince our colleagues from Georgia and Ukraine concerning the unwavering obligations of our states in terms of supporting them and providing them with assistance. We reconfirm our support for independence, sovereignty and territorial integrity of both Georgia as well as Ukraine, within their internationally recognised borders, along with their Euro-Atlantic aspirations. We managed to come to an agreement regarding the continuation of coordinated trilateral efforts in this format, in order to support further development of NATO and support our partners, in terms of implementing projects in this dimension, along with other initiatives,” – read the statement.
