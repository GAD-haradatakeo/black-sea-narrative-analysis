22 October 2020 The Council of Europe Committee of Ministers adopted its seventh decision on the agenda item “Council of Europe and Conflict in Georgia” on 21 October 2020. Like in previous decisions, the document adopted today reiterates the unequivocal support of the Council of Europe member States for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia within its internationally recognised borders. The documents points to the legal obligation of Russia as a country exercising effective control in Georgia’s occupied territories. It needs to be highlighted that the decision represents an important legal and political document by which the international community duly assesses the grave situation in Georgia’s occupied regions and underlines that the Russian Federation’s continuing military presence in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region poses a threat to peace and security in the entire region, violates its international obligations and commitments, including under the EU mediated 12 August 2008 Ceasefire Agreement. The decision adopted by the Committee of Ministers focuses on the 12-year period in the aftermath of the Russian aggression and describes the Russian Federation’s illegal activities as leading to severe human rights and humanitarian consequences on the ground. According to the document, Georgia is still prevented from exercising the legitimate jurisdiction over its regions due to the continuous impediments put up by the Russian Federation, including its continuing military presence therein. Like previous decisions, this decision also highlights that the conflict is between two Member States of the CoE: Russia and Georgia. The document further states that the Russian Federation continues to impede the peaceful conflict resolution process and to undermine the security and stability in the wider region through its continuing military presence in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia, including increased military exercises and infrastructure reinforcements, the implementation of the so-called treaties on alliance and strategic partnership/integration, incorporation of military units of the Tskhinvali region of Georgia into the armed forces of the Russian Federation, the creation of a so-called “joint group of armed forces” in the Abkhazia region, establishment of so-called “joint information and co-ordination centres of law enforcement agencies”, as well as the functioning of so-called “customs points” in both Georgian regions, the erection of fences by the authorities exercising effective control in South Ossetia, in the Gugutiantkari and Chorchana/Tsnelisi areas. Based on the Document, any illegal act by the Russian Federation aimed at changing the status of the Georgian regions, including through issuing Russian passports and so-called residents’ permits, thus establishing a so-called status of foreign residents, have no legal effect. In its decision the Committee of Ministers expresses grave concern over impunity around the deaths of Georgian IDPs – David Basharuli, Giga Otkhozoria and Archil Tatunashvili – and expresses concern over the death of another Georgian citizen – Irakli Kvaratskhelia – under unclear circumstances at a military base of Russian FSB forces in the Abkhazia region; recalls the “Otkhozaria-Tatunashvili List” adopted by the Parliament of Georgia, and the national restrictive measures decided on by the Georgian Government against those responsible for grave human rights abuses in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia. The decision expresses particular concern over the intensified discrimination of Georgians on the grounds of ethnicity in both Georgian regions, through further restrictions to freedom of movement, residence rights, the right to work and property rights in case of forced registration as foreign residents or demands to change surnames and ethnic identity. The document expresses grave concern about the continuous restrictions on crossing into the Abkhazia region, and lengthy closure of “crossing points” in Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia, which lead to severe humanitarian consequences for the local population. According to the document, IDPs and refugees continue to be deprived of their fundamental right to voluntary return to their places of origin in a safe and dignified manner; deep concern is expressed over the demolition of houses of Georgian IDPs in the Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia, in violation of property rights of IDPs. The document strongly condemns a decision in the region of Abkhazia that provides for the punishment by death. The Committee of Ministers reconfirms the high priority of achieving tangible results in the Geneva International Discussions (GID); expresses concern about the suspension of the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanisms (IPRMs) in Gali since June 2018 and welcomes the resumption of the Ergneti IPRM on 30 July 2020. Bearing in mind that human rights and fundamental freedoms shall be protected by all relevant States Parties to the European Convention on Human Rights in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia, the deputies reiterate their call to the authorities exercising effective control: to create conditions for the voluntary, safe and dignified return of all IDPs and refugees; to cease any form of ethnic discrimination towards the residents of the Georgian regions, first and foremost, the ethnic discriminatory measures against the Georgian population of the Gali and Akhalgori districts, including demands to register as foreign residents or to change their surnames in order to be granted access to full civil rights; to remove any obstacles to the objective investigations into the deaths of ethnic Georgians in the Georgian regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali region/South Ossetia and to bring the perpetrators to justice; to immediately cease policies leading to human rights violations in both regions of Georgia; to remove any impediment, restriction or limitation to the right to freedom of movement across the administrative boundary lines (ABLs), including for medical and education purposes; to cease the denial and/or delay of medical evacuations especially amid the COVID-19 pandemic; to cease arbitrary detentions of persons, including in the context of so-called “illegal border crossing” and to re-open “crossing points”; to cease violations of the right to education in schools and preschools, including education in the native Georgian language in both Georgian regions; to prevent further deterioration of monuments belonging to the cultural heritage throughout Georgia’s regions. The Council of Europe Committee of Ministersexpresses support for the peace initiative of the Government of Georgia entitled “A Step to a Better Future”, as a sign of Georgia’s firm commitment to promote reconciliation and engagement between the societies on both sides of the administrative boundary lines as well as the programme “Peace Fund for a Better Future”; The Council of Europe Committee of Ministers calls on the Russian Federation to ensure that the Commissioner for Human Rights and the Council of Europe monitoring bodies are granted access to the territories beyond the control of the Government of Georgia.
