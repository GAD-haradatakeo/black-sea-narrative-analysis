24 March 2018 24.03.2018 -​ Australia supported the Resolution on Co-operation with Georgia at the United Nations (UN) Human Rights Council. “The resolution seeks immediate access by the Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights and international and regional human rights mechanisms to Abkhazia and South Ossetia. It is appropriate for Georgia to put forward such a resolution, since these regions are integral parts of Georgia. It is appropriate for the international community to continue to investigate alleged human rights violations in these regions, including reports of arbitrary detention, torture and restricted freedom of movement. It is not appropriate that another country has opened polling stations for its own Presidential elections in these regions during the last week” – the representative of Australia said. The Resolution of the UN Human Rights Council recognizes the efforts of the Government of Georgia to strengthen democracy, the rule of law and the promotion and protection of human rights. The Resolution expresses serious concern over the human rights and humanitarian situation in the occupied regions of Georgia.
