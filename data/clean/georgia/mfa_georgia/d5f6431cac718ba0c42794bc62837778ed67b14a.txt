12 November 2025 On 12 November 2025, a commemorative plaque dedicated to Akaki Chkhenkeli was unveiled on the building of Tbilisi Public School No. 49. The ceremony was organized jointly by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Ministry of Education, Science and Youth, and the Tbilisi City Assembly. The unveiling was carried out by First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Giorgi Zurabashvili, Deputy Minister of Education, Science and Youth Tamar Makharashvili, and First Deputy Chair of the Tbilisi City Assembly Beka Davituliani. In August 2025, Tbilisi Public School No. 49 was officially named after Akaki Chkhenkeli. In his remarks, Giorgi Zurabashvili addressed the audience and highlighted Akaki Chkhenkeli’s exceptional contributions to the nation, emphasizing his pivotal role in the formation of modern Georgian diplomacy. Akaki Chkhenkeli was one of the most distinguished political figures, diplomats, and public servants in twentieth-century Georgian history. His name is closely associated with the establishment of the First Democratic Republic of Georgia.
