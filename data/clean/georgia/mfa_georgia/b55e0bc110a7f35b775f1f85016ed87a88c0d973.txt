19 September 2018 19.09.2018, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with Deputy Assistant Secretary of State for European and EurasianAffairs, George Kent. The sides highly appraised to Georgian Prime Minister’s visit to the U.S. and meetings held in Washington that, according to the sides, will contribute to the further strengthening of partnership between the two countries. The sides discussed the issues relating to bilateral co-operation paying special attention to the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and the peace policy for conflict resolution; special note was taken of the role of the United States in the peaceful resolution of the conflict, including in the Geneva International Discussions; high appraisal was given to the Georgian Government’s peace initiative “A Step toward a Better Future”. The U.S. side reaffirmed support for Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integartion. Georgia’s contribution towards ensuring Euro-Atlantic security was also highlighted. The sides placed great value on U.S.-Georgia co-operation in the areas of defence and security reaffirming their readiness to promote the development bilateral trade-economic relations. Special attention was paid to the successful democratic transformation process ongoing in Georgia.
