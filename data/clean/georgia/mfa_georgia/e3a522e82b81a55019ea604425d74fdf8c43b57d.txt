28 June 2018 27.06.2018, Tbilisi – on 27 June 2018, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with media represtatives of the EU Member States who arrived in Georgia within the framework of the EU project. David Zalkaliani highlighted Georgia’s foreign policy priorities paying special attention to the European and Euro-Atlantic integration process and the tangible results achived in this regard. Discussions focused on the strategic partnership between Georgia and the U.S. and the importance of The bi-partisan Georgia Support Act introduced by Co-Chairmen of the Georgia Caucus to the House of Representatives. Discussing the situation in Georgia’s occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, David Zalkaliani updated journalists on the Georgian government’s peace initiative “A step toward a better future”, which is directed towards the strengthening of stability in the region and ensuring that Georgia’s occupied regions also enjoy the benefits of integration with the EU. The meeting proceeded in a Q&A format. Journalists also held meetings with President Giorgi Margvelashvili, as well as with representatives of the non-governmental sector and mass media.
