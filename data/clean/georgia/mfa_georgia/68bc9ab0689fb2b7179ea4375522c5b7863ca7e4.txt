24 September 2025 Within the framework of the 80th session of the United Nations General Assembly, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, took part in the high-level meeting of the International Coalition for the Return of Ukrainian Children. The meeting was held under the theme: “Restoring Childhood and Humanity - Promoting Peace in Ukraine through the Return of Ukrainian Children.” In its statement, the Georgian side reaffirmed Georgia’s firm support for Ukraine’s sovereignty, territorial integrity, and the safe return of Ukrainian children to their homeland. “The return of Ukrainian children is not merely a legal obligation. It is a moral imperative, a humanitarian priority, and an essential condition for achieving a just and sustainable peace in Ukraine,” the statement emphasized. Attention was drawn to the efforts undertaken by Georgia, including the enrollment of more than 1,600 Ukrainian students in Georgian schools, the development of Ukrainian-language educational programs, and government-organized initiatives that have hosted approximately 600 Ukrainian children in Georgia. “Georgia, which continues to endure the trauma caused by Russian occupation and forced displacement, expresses its full solidarity with Ukraine. We strongly support the goals of the International Coalition for the Return of Ukrainian Children and highly commend the ‘Bring Back the Children’ initiative,” the statement underscored.
