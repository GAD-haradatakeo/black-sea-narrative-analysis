16 November 2021 On 16 November 2021, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia hosted a roundtable discussion “Geneva International Discussions and Women, Peace and Security” supported by the UN Women and the United Kingdom. The purpose of the meeting was to facilitate dialogue with civil society on the peaceful settlement of the conflict and promote the involvement of women in peace processes. The meeting was opened by the UN Women Country Representative to Georgia, Kaori Ishikawa, after which Tea Akhvlediani, State Minister for Reconciliation and Civic Equality, spoke about the importance of increasing women’s role in the peacebuilding processes and the steps taken in this direction. The roundtable dialogue was conducted in two sessions. Within the framework of the first thematic session, the First Deputy Foreign Minister Lasha Darsalia briefed the participants on the ongoing international negotiations in Geneva, the activities carried out by the Georgian authorities for the peaceful settlement of the conflict and the current challenges. Kate Ferron, the Deputy Head of the EUMM in Georgia, spoke about the activities of the EUMM, with a special focus on the "Women, Peace and Security" agenda. The second session was dedicated to the Humanitarian Working Group under the Geneva International Talks. UNHCR Representative in Georgia, Kemlin Furley spoke about the organization's activities in the occupied territories, as well as in the territory controlled by the Georgian government, humanitarian issues and the needs of women. Both thematic sessions continued in a question-and-answer mode. At the end of the meeting, the discussion was summed up by the moderator of the event, Ketevan Chumbadze, Deputy Director of the Political Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia.
