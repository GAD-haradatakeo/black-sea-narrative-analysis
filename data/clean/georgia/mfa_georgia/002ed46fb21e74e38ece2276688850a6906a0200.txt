23 March 2021 The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister Lasha Darsalia travelled to Bern, on 22 March, to hold meetings with the State Secretary at the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs of the Swiss Confederation, Livia Leu Agosti, the Director of the Eurasia Department, Anna Ifkovits Horner and the Director of the Peace and Human Rights Department, Simon Gisbuller. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of bilateral and multilateral cooperation, including the Swiss-led peace programs , the importance of operation of international monitoring mechanisms in Georgia’s occupied territories, results in the fight against the COVID-19 pandemic in the two countries. Lasha Darsalia thanked the Swiss side for its support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and highlighted the role of Switzerland as a mediator in relations with the Russian Federation. The sides discussed the security and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, putting special accent on Russia’s recent provocations and kidnappings of Georgian citizens. The Georgian Deputy Minister updated the Swiss side on the Geneva discussions, the government’s peace policy and the reconciliation process. The State Secretary, Livia Leu Agosti praised Georgia’s steps towards peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict and expressed the hope that the Swiss side will make its own positive contribution to this cause.
