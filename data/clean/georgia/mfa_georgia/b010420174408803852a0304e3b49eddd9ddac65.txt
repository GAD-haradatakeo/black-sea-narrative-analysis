4 July 2022 The First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, participated in the Helsinki Commission’s first ever high-level dialogue (The Black Sea Security Summit) hosted by Constanta, Romania. The event was chaired by the Helsinki Commission co-chairman, Senator Roger Wicker and the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Romania, Bogdan Aurescu. Along with Georgia, high-level representatives of Ukraine, Turkey, Bulgaria, NATO, the European Union, and the U.S. European Command attended the roundtable dialogue. Discussions focused on the security challenges facing the Black Sea region, which have been further exacerbated in the light of Russia's ongoing aggression in Ukraine. The participants of the summit emphasized the need for the international community to maintain unity and take effective steps to respond to the threats in the region. The event focused on the strategic importance of the Black Sea region for the U.S., NATO and European partners, both in terms of security and economic cooperation. In his address, the First Deputy Minister drew a special attention to the threats facing the region, emphasized the difficult security and humanitarian situation in the occupied territories. Lasha Darsalia thanked the U.S. and European partners for supporting the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia and emphasized the importance of their involvement in the peaceful resolution of the conflict. The First Deputy Foreign Minister brought to the attention of the participants the steps taken by Georgia to support Ukraine. Within the framework of the visit, Lasha Darsalia had a bilateral meeting with the U.S. Congress delegation led by Roger Wicker, the co-chairman of the Helsinki Commission. The meeting was also attended by the members of the U.S. House of Representatives, the co-chairman of the Helsinki Commission, Joe Wilson, John Garamendi, Robert Aderholt and August Flager.
