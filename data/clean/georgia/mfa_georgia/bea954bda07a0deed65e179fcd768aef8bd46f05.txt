16 November 2018 15.11.2018 – The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani held a meeting with the representatives of the EU Member States accredited to Georgia. The discussions focused on the recent developments in relations with the EU. According to the Minister, these relations are highly intensive to which attest the discussions held within the European Parliament and EU Council, as well as the meeting with the European Commission scheduled for 21 November. “We are heading to Brussels with the concrete proposals focused on the development of our future cooperation with the European Union,” – commented David Zalkaliani on the meeting of November 21. “This is a new format of cooperation which clearly demonstrates the dynamics of relationship between Georgia and the EU as well as the readiness of both sides to further deepen cooperation in concrete directions. This format will not only facilitate the implementation of the Association Agreement but it will also contribute to the intensification of cooperation with the EU that also envisages the identification of possibilities of sectoral cooperation with the EU beyond the Association Agreement. As you know, the sectoral integration is one of the main components of Georgia’s initiative – roadmap for EU integration successful realization of which will prepare Georgia for the next stages of integration within the EU. We are heading to Brussels with the concrete proposals focused on the development of our future cooperation with the European Union, we will discuss these proposals with the President of the European Commission and with the EU Commissioners and will agree on the next steps to be taken. The proposals concern the fields of cooperation which are already agreed with the European Union like trade and economy, communications, education, science, innovations and culture, penitentiary and internal security, including the civil defense,” – stated David Zalkaliani.
