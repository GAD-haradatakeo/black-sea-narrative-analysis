3 October 2018 3.10.2018, Tbilisi -​Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister David Dondua met with the U.S. representative to the Geneva International Discussions, Andrew Schofer. The sides discussed the progress made in the Geneval International Discussions and the agenda of the next round. It was noted that October marks 10 years since the launch of the Geneva format which makes the resolution of the security and humanitarian problems of the conflict-affected population all the more important. The Georgian side highlighted the need to achieve progress on such issues as Russia’s non-use of force pledge, creation of the international security arrangements on the ground and the return of refugees and IDPs. The need of Russia’s full compliance with its international commitments under the Ceasefire Agreement of 12 August 2008 was also underlined. Special attention was paid to destructive steps taken by Russia and the occupation regimes with respect to the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanisms (IPRM). David Dondua provided the U.S. representative with detailed information regarding the difficult security and human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. Talking points also included the Russian Federation’s intensified military build-up in Georgia’s occupied territories and steps taken towards actual annexation of Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. The Deputy Minister updated Andrew Schofer on the recent abduction of Maia Otinashvili from the Georgian-controlled territory highlighting the need to deliver justice on the murder cases of Georgian citizens Archil Tatunashvili, Giga Otkhozoria, and Davit Basharuli. For his part, Andrew Schofer highlighted the importance of strategic partnership between the two countries reaffirming the full support of the United States for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity.
