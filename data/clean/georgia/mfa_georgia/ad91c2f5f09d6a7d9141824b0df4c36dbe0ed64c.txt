29 May 2022 Within the framework of the Prime Minister Irakli Gharibashvili’s visit to the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili, met with the Deputy Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Jordan, Ayman Safadi. The sides discussed the bilateral cooperation between the two countries and the ways to further strengthen it in various fields. Particular attention was paid to the importance of intensifying economic relations between Georgia and Jordan, the growth of trade and tourism, as well as to the opportunities of creating joint ventures. Talks also focused on the next round of the Intergovernmental Economic Commission, as well as on the expansion of the bilateral legal framework between the two countries. In addition, the ministers spoke about the importance of visa-free regime between the two countries, which will help increase the number of both tourist and pilgrim tours and give greater impetus to raising awareness and rapprochement between the two nations. In this regard, the Ministers welcomed Georgia’s granting visa-free regime to Jordan. The Georgian Foreign Minister thanked his counterpart for his unwavering support for Georgia's territorial integrity and sovereignty, as well as for the transfer of a plot of land along the Jordan River, and invited him to visit Georgia.
