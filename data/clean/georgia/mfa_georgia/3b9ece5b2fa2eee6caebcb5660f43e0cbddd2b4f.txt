7 July 2020 On 7 July 2010, the First Deputy Foreign Minister, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili held a video-conference with Lawrence Meredith, Director for Neighbourhood East, DG Neighbourhood and Enlargement Negotiations (DG NEAR) of the European Commission and Richard Tibbels, the Head of the Eastern Partnership Division of the European External Action Service (EEAS). The sides discussed topical issues on the EU-Georgia cooperation agenda, including the recent EU decision to lift travel restrictions from Georgia, along with 14 other countries. This fact is an important signal and the recognition of Georgia’s successful efforts in effectively containing the spread of the virus. According to Makharoblishvili, Georgia continues bilateral consultations for taking practical steps to restore bilateral relations with the EU/Schengen countries. Taking stock of the results of the high-level meetings held in the format of video-conferencing on the future of Eastern Partnership, the sides agreed to continue cooperation for preparing the summit scheduled for March 2021. Discussions also focused on issues relating to the new Georgia-EU Association Agenda and the Single EU Support Framework.
