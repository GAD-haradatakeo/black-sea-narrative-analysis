28 February 2025 On 27 February 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Giorgi Zurabashvili, participated in an event dedicated to the birthday of His Majesty Emperor Naruhito of Japan and delivered a speech to the assembled audience. On behalf of the Government of Georgia and the Georgian people, the Deputy Minister extended his heartfelt congratulations to His Majesty and the people of Japan on this momentous occasion. In his address, Giorgi Zurabashvili highlighted the longstanding and successful cooperation and friendly relations between Georgia and Japan. "Japan was among the first countries to recognize Georgia’s independence in 1918, and since the establishment of diplomatic relations in 1992, it has remained a steadfast and strong supporter of our country’s sovereignty and territorial integrity," he stated. He further emphasized that over the years, Japan has significantly contributed to Georgia’s economic progress, technological advancement, and democratic development. In this regard, he underscored the vital role of the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) in supporting infrastructure and educational initiatives. Additionally, he noted the growing dynamism of bilateral relations between the two nations. In this context, he highlighted the establishment of the Japan-Georgia Chamber of Commerce and Industry in 2023 and the opening of an Honorary Consulate in Kagoshima Prefecture last year. He also pointed out the considerable increase in the number of Japanese visitors to Georgia in 2024. During his speech, the Deputy Minister emphasized the importance of exchanging high-level visits, strengthening inter-parliamentary ties, and enhancing economic cooperation. "We place particular emphasis on expanding economic collaboration and welcome Japanese investments in various sectors of Georgia’s economy. Through our joint efforts, I am confident that we will achieve many successes," he stated. In conclusion, the Deputy Minister expressed his gratitude to the Embassy of Japan in Georgia and, in particular, to Ambassador Ishizuka for their significant contributions to strengthening Japan-Georgia relations.
