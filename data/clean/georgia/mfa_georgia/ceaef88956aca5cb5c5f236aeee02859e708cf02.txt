19 September 2025 Within the framework of the OSCE Permanent Council, the 17th anniversary of the 2008 Russia-Georgia war was discussed. At the session, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, delivered a statement. He highlighted the severe situation in Georgia’s occupied territories with respect to security and human rights. He also drew attention to the peaceful resolution of the conflict and Georgia’s peace-oriented policies, which aim, on one hand, at the country’s de-occupation and, on the other, at restoring trust and promoting reconciliation among communities divided as a result of the war. During the Permanent Council meeting, a statement in support of Georgia was delivered by the Group of Friends of Georgia within the OSCE, consisting of the United States, Bulgaria, the United Kingdom, Denmark, Estonia, Ireland, Iceland, Canada, Latvia, Lithuania, Norway, Poland, Romania, Slovakia, Ukraine, Finland, Sweden, and the Czech Republic. The statement reaffirmed the Group’s strong and unwavering support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders. The statement noted that, seventeen years after the 2008 war, Georgia’s regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali remain under Russian occupation, while the Charter of the United Nations and the Helsinki Final Act of the OSCE clearly reaffirm respect for sovereign rights, political independence, and territorial integrity, as well as the inadmissibility of altering borders through the use of force. The OSCE Group of Friends of Georgia, in support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, called on the Russian Federation to fully comply with the obligations undertaken under the 12 August 2008 Ceasefire Agreement, withdraw its forces to the positions held prior to the outbreak of hostilities, and revoke its decision recognizing the so-called independence of the regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali. The OSCE Group of Friends of Georgia further condemned the military aggression carried out by Russia against Georgia in 2008 and expressed concern over the continued presence of Russian forces on Georgia’s territory, the unlawful actions of the occupation regime, and the difficult humanitarian and security situation on the ground. The OSCE Group of Friends of Georgia reaffirmed its support for the Geneva International Discussions as the only format dedicated to the implementation of the ceasefire agreement and the pursuit of a peaceful resolution of the conflict. The statement underscored the important role of the European Union Monitoring Mission (EUMM) in contributing to stability on the ground. The Group of Friends of Georgia at the OSCE further called upon the OSCE and the wider international community to remain actively engaged with regard to the occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali, with the aim of ensuring the peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict. In conclusion, the statement highlighted the importance of the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanisms (IPRMs) in Ergneti and Gali, stressing that their functioning in accordance with established procedures is vital for preventing escalation.
