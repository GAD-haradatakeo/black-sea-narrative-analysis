26 June 2020 On 26 June 2020, Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister Lasha Darsalia spoke before the ministerial conference organized by the Community of Democracies – a global intergovernmental coalition. The Conference was dedicated to the 20th anniversary of the adoption of the Warsaw Declaration and the founding of the community. Speaking before the Conference, the Deputy Foreign Minister emphasized the commitment of Georgia as one of the signatories of the Warsaw Declaration to the democratic principles enshrined in the Declaration. He highlighted that 75 years after the foundation of the UN, the main principles of the UN Charter, including the principles of sovereignty and territorial integrity and inviolability of borders are breached every day and multilateralism and international law-based order is under attack by the authoritative countries. Delivering his remarks, Lasha Darsalia paid special attention to the dire humanitarian situation in the Russian-occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, as well as to bans placed on providing healthcare and humanitarian aid. According to him, against such background, the Georgian Government continues proactive measures to contain the pandemic in Georgia’s occupied regions. Based on Georgia’s experience, Lasha Darsalia highlighted the importance of building effective, responsible and inclusive institutions and the need of youth engagement in the decision-making processes. On the occasion of the 20th anniversary of the Community of Democracies, the meeting adopted a joint declaration reflecting the democratic values and principles enshrined in the Warsaw Declaration.
