1 March 2023 On 1 March 2023, within the framework of his working visit to Berlin, Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia held a meeting with Matthias Lutenberg, the German Foreign Ministry's Special Representative for Eastern Europe, the South Caucasus and Central Asia. The sides highlighted that Georgia and Germany are reliable and valuable partners. The existing friendship between the two countries is based on common values and values, which, in turn, create a solid foundation for fruitful multidimensional cooperation in almost all fields, as well as within international organizations. The sides expressed their satisfaction with the exchange of high-level visits of both the legislative and executive authorities of the two countries. They talked about future plans, which will raise and strengthen the partnership relations between Georgia and Germany to a higher level. The Deputy Minister thanked his colleague for Germany’s political and practical support to Georgia over the years. The representative of the German Foreign Ministry, for his part, once again noted Berlin's support for Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration and the country's sovereignty and territorial integrity. He also confirmed the importance of strengthening close partnership with Georgia. Attention was focused on the importance of further deepening cooperation with Germany, as one of the leading trade and economic partners of Georgia, in the field of economy and in various fields. The special role of Georgia in the process of ensuring energy security was emphasized. The German side confirmed Germany's interest in helping Georgia in this direction. The sides especially welcomed Georgia’s growing interest in Germany and Georgia's participation as an honored guest in the Berlin international tourism exhibition "ITB Berlin 2023", which will be inaugurated in a few days.
