26 September 2019 On 26 September 2019, in Geneva, Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister Mikheil Ninua participated in the 42nd session of the UN Human Rights Council where the participants discussed the Resolution on Cooperation with Georgia prepared by the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights. The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister brought to the attention of the UN Member States the recent developments in Georgia’s occupied territories. Special attention was paid to the erection of barbed wire fences and other artificial barriers across the occupation line in the vicinity of the villages of Gugutiantkari and Chorchana, restrictions on free movement and other gross violations of fundamental human rights. Special accent was put on the increasing number of murders in Georgia’s occupied territories. It was highlighted that the death of illegally detained Irakli Kvaratskhelia in Russia’s illegal military base in occupied Abkhazia points to the alarming situation in the occupied territories. The importance of international support for the “Otkhozori-Tatunashvili List" adopted by the government was also highlighted in this context. According to Deputy Minister Ninua, the Russian Federation undermines the conflict resolution process and the Geneva International Discussions, as well as the work of the IPRM. He called on the Russian Federation to comply with the Ceasefire Agreement of 12 August 2008. In his remarks, Ninua strongly condemned the decision of the occupied regime that provides for the punishment by death. He underscored the necessity of providing the human rights monitoring organizations and the Office of the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights with an access to the occupied territories. Based on the HTC Resolution on Cooperation with Georgia, the Report of the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights provides a detailed review of the human rights situation in the occupied territories.
