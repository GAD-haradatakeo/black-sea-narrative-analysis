16 September 2021 The Vice Prime Minister/ Foreign Minister of Georgia, David Zalkaliani, held his first meeting with the newly appointed UN Women Country Representative to Georgia Kaori Ishikawa. The sides discussed the fruitful cooperation between the Government of Georgia and the United Nations Women in Georgia. The conversation focused on the tangible progress made by the Georgian government over the recent years in terms of gender equality, protection of women’s and girls' rights, including the elimination of violence against women and the economic empowerment of women. The need to strengthen the women, peace and security agenda in the process of peaceful resolution of the conflict was also stressed. Kaori Ishikawa welcomed Georgia’s progress in the protection of women’s and girls' rights and gender equality and noted the importance of sharing Georgia's successful experience with other countries. At the end of the meeting, Davit Zalkaliani wished success to the UN Women Country Representative to Georgia in her future activities and reaffirmed the Georgian Government’s support for the activities of the UN Women.
