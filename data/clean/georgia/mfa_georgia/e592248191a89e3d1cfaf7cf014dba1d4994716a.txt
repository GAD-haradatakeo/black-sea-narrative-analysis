1 September 2018 1.09.2018, Tbilisi - Johannes Hahn, European Commissioner for European Neighbourhood Policy and Enlargement Negotiations, will visit Georgia on 3 and 4 September. The European School for the Eastern Partnership will be officially launched during his two-day visit to Tbilisi. The European School for the Eastern Partnership is an unprecedented project. It is the first such school to be launched outside the EU Member State. It will offer scholarships to students from Eastern Partnership countries for an IB Diploma Programme. 30 students have already been selected for the 2018-2019 academic year. The European Commission and the Ministry of Education and Science of Georgia have selected the private New School as a partner where the Eastern Partnership School will be developed. The new building for the school will be constructed on land allocated by Tbilisi City Hall.
