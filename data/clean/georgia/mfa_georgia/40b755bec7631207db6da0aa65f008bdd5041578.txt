5 April 2019 05.04.2019, Washington – “The NATO Ministerial Meeting has a very important outcome”- Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said commenting on the NATO Ministerial held in Washington where new ways to ensure Black Sea security were agreed. According to the Minister, Georgia carried out extensive work prior to the Ministerial. “The NATO Ministerial Meeting has a very important outcome as the participants of the meeting approved a package of measures for improving security in the Black Sea region. It needs to be emphasized that Georgia did a good deal of work to achieve this result. We actively worked with our NATO partners prior to this meeting. The NATO Foreign Ministers spoke about the need to strengthen co-operation with Georgia and Ukraine in the context of Black Sea security, the visit of more sea vessels, the exchange of greater volume of information, training of personnel to meet NATO standards, as well as the need for NATO to qualitatively enhance its presence in the Black Sea Basin that will create greater security opportunities in the Black Sea region” – David Zalkaliani said. He also highlighted the importance of the declaration adopted at the Ministerial Meeting. The declaration, according to him, devotes special attention to the Open Door Policy. In the words of Zalkaliani, Georgia will continue to work with its partners to ensure that Georgia’s progress on NATO integration path is reflected in respective decisions of NATO.
