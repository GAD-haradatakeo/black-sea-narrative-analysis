11 October 2017 On 10th of October, 2017 on the first day of 41st round of Geneva International Discussions, bilateral consultations were held with Co-chairs and Co-moderators from EU, UN and OSCE, as well as with the representatives from the U.S. During the consultations with Co-chairs and representatives from the United States, the main topic of discussion was the continuation of ethnic discrimination in Georgia’s occupied regions. In this regard, Georgian representatives expressed concern over recent developments in Eredvi village, where houses of Georgian population have been massively demolished within the framework of Russian Investment Program. Particular focus has been made on severe social and humanitarian situation in Gali district, where ethnic Georgian residents are compelled to register as foreigners, and their fundamental rights, including right to property, freedom of movement, healthcare and right to get education in native language, are blatantly violated. It was underlined that those illegal actions may become ground for new wave of ethnic cleansing from Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions. In this regard particular emphasis was given to the establishment of international security mechanisms in the occupied territories of Georgia. The necessity of international human rights institutions’ access to occupied regions has also been highlighted.
