1 December 2021 The Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani and the Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade of Hungary, Péter Szijjártó signed a Memorandum of Understanding between the Levan Mikeladze Diplomatic Training and Research Institute and the Hungarian Diplomatic Academy within the framework of the NATO Meeting of Foreign Ministers. The memorandum envisages active cooperation between the two countries in the professional development of diplomats, as well as in the elaboration of joint training programs and exchange of experts, as well as close cooperation between diplomatic research institutions. The signing of the memorandum was followed by a meeting of the ministers where the Hungarian side reaffirmed its strong support for Georgia's sovereignty, territorial integrity and our country's Euro-Atlantic aspirations. The sides positively assessed the NATO Ministerial’s support for Georgia's integration goals, NATO's commitment to Open Door Policy and practical mechanisms for cooperation with Georgia.
