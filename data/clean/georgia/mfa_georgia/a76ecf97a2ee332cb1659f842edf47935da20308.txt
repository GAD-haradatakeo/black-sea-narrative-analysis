6 March 2019 6.03.2019 – The Ministry of Foreign Affairs responds to the Russian President, Vladimer Putin’s meeting with the leader of the Russian occupation regime in Tskhinvali, Anatoly Bibilov, which represents Moscow’s yet another provocative attempt aimed at legitimizing the illegal occupation of Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions and ethnic cleansing conducted there. The Russian Federation still continues to pursue its illegal policy directed against Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, in defiance of the fundamental principles of international relations. By taking such steps, the Russian Federation tries in vain to undermine the rules-based international order. The international community firmly supports Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and calls on Russia to respect its international commitments and stop the illegal occupation of Georgia’s integral regions. On our part, we call on the Russian Federation to reverse its illegal decision of 2008 on recognition of the so-called independence of Georgia’s occupied regions and to fully comply with the EU-brokered Ceasefire Agreement of 12 August 2008.
