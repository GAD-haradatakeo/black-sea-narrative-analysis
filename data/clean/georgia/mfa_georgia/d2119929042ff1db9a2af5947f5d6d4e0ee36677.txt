19 June 2018 19.06.2018, Tbilisi – Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili held a meeting with a group of ambassadors from OSCE-member states, who paid an official visit to Georgia from 19 to 21 June. Vakhtang Makharoblishvili thanked the ambassadors for their support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. He highlighted the importance of OSCE as the largest security organization in the region, in terms of counteracting the challenges facing the European security system. In this context, the Deputy Minister paid special attention to the necessity of peacefully resolving the Russian- Georgian conflict and keeping this conflict atop the OSCE agenda. The Georgian side updated the ambassadors on the Geneva International Discussions and the Georgian government’s peace initiatives. Discussions centred on the grave human rights and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions with special focus on the violent death of Archil Tatunashvili. It was emphasized that the international monitoring mechanisms have no access to the occupied territories.
