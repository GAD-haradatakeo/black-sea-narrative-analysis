29 October 2018 29.10.2018, Tbilisi – Within the framework of his visit to Turkey, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani held a meeting with his Turkish counterpart, Foreign Minister Foreign Minister Mevlüt Çavuşoğlu. Zalkaliani congratulated his colleague on the Day of the Republic and thanked Turkey for hosting the trilateral Azerbaijan-Turkey,-Georgia meeting. According to him, this format is of particular importance in terms of strengthening relations between the three countries in such areas as economy, energy, transport communications, etc. The Turkish Foreign Minister congratulated his Georgian counterpart on the conduction of the first tour of the presidential elections. According to him, Georgia once again demonstrated its commitment to democratic values and democratic way of development. Discussing the Georgia-Turkey bilateral relations, the sides reviewed various aspects of strategic co-operation and highlighted the support for Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic integration and sovereignty and territorial integrity. The sides reaffirmed the importance of the support of international partners, including Turkey, for Georgia’s peace policy and peaceful initiatives. The two Ministers highly appraised the Georgia-Turkey High-level Strategic Co-operation Council. High appraisal was given to Georgia-Turkey co-operation and the involvement of the two countries in the transport and energy projects of international importance. Special attention was paid to the importance of opening the Baku-Tbilisi-Kars (BTK) railway and theTrans-AnatolianNatural Gas Pipeline.The Ministers highlighted the Georgia-Turkey successful co-operation and discussed the possibilities of holding an inter-governmental economic commission. The Georgian Foreign Minister attends the trilateral Azerbaijan-Turkey-Georgia meeting in Istanbul. He will participate in the official ceremony of opening a new international airport in Istanbul.
