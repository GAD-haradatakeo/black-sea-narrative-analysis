24 March 2018 24.03.2018 -​ The United States supported the Resolution on Co-operation with Georgia at the United Nations (UN) Human Rights Council calling on all members of this Council to vote in favor of the resolution. According to the U.S. representative, the government of Georgia is cooperating closely with OHCHR to improve the human rights situation in their country. At the same time, de facto authorities in the Russian-occupied territories of Georgia continue to deny any access to OHCHR while there are reports of widespread abuses of rights in these territories. “The de facto authorities in the Russian-occupied territories of Georgia continue to deny any access to OHCHR while there are reports of widespread abuses of rights in these territories. We note that any right of return is limited to refugees however we consider that internally displaced person should also have the ability to return. The United States fully supports Georgia’s sovereignty, independence, and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders” – the U.S. representative said. Accoridng to him, the resolution calls for immediate OHCHR access to Abkhazia and South Ossetia and “in light of the current situation on the ground, it is essential that the Human Rights Council adopt the resolution on cooperation with Georgia before us now”.
