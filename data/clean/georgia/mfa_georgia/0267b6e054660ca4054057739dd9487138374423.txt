2 September 2021 On 2 September 2021, the delegation of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, led by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia, participated in the Bled Strategic Forum, dedicated to the ongoing discussions on the future of Europe in the European Union. The Deputy Foreign Minister addressed one of the sessions of the Forum ("EU's Challenging Eastern Partnership Summit 2021: Calibrating its Agenda"), which focused on the challenges and future goals of the Eastern Partnership. Teimuraz Janjalia spoke about Georgia's expectations for the Eastern Partnership Summit, the security challenges facing the country due to Russia's growing military presence in the occupied territories and its provocative policies, as well as the difficulties posed by the Covid Pandemic. According to the Deputy Foreign Minister, despite the challenges, Georgia is committed to its European choice and continues to pursue the country's European agenda, the ultimate goal of which is EU membership. Speaking about the future of the Eastern Partnership, the Georgian side stressed the need to strengthen the Eastern Partnership amid the challenges in the region. According to him, the summit scheduled for December is of crucial importance in this regard. Teimuraz Janjalia paid special attention to the importance of future-oriented agenda and ambitious goals based on the principle of differentiation. In this context, he welcomed the Eastern Partnership Economic and Investment Plan, one of the main focuses of which is to attract investments for the development of Black Sea connectivity between Georgia and the EU. In this regard, he stressed Georgia's interest in joining the Three Seas Initiative. On closing, the Deputy Foreign Minister answered questions from the audience. Documents Similar News
