17 October 2019 17.10.2019, Tbilisi – “I have been and am still confident that the meeting with the Russian Foreign Minister was needed, important and held at the right time” – the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani said at the parliamentary session “ministerial hour” when commenting on his meeting with his Russian counterpart, Sergey Lavrov. According to him, the recent escalation of the situation in the occupied regions was the main underlying factor leading to this meeting. “Amid the recent development of events and aggressive wave of borderization, Georgia continued to mobilize the international community raising the tension issue in all formats and with all international organisations working on conflict issues. However, the tension kept persisting and it was important to prevent further escalation” – Zalkaliani said. “We raised the issue at all meetings during the UN General Assembly session and our partners offered us to discuss the possibility of the meeting with Lavrov,” Zalkaliani said. He stated that the initiative was pushed forward by Switzerland and was discussed with allies, including foreign ministers of the EU member states and the US State Secretary Mike Pompeo. “All of them stated that the meeting was necessary in the situation and the statements made by them after the meeting confirmed that,” Zalkaliani said. He reiterated that he raised all the issues which were important for Georgia during his meeting with Lavrov, adding that they have not discussed the possibility of creating any new format of negotiations or a change in existing formats.
