12 September 2022 On 12 September 2022, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Lasha Darsalia held a meeting with Martti Ahtisaari Peace Foundation (CMI) Senior Advisor, Finnish diplomat and former UN Special Envoy to the Geneva International Negotiations, Antti Turunen. The sides discussed the security and human rights situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and the issues related to the peaceful settlement of the Russia-Georgia conflict, in the light of the situation in the region. The First Deputy Minister emphasized the necessity for Russia to implement the EU-brokered 12 August 2008 ceasefire agreement and the importance of the Geneva International Discussions format in the process of peaceful resolution of the conflict. Lasha Darsalia spoke in detail about the Georgian government’s peace policy aimed, on the one hand, at the de-occupation of the country, and on the other, at the restoration of trust and reconciliation between the communities divided as a result of the war.
