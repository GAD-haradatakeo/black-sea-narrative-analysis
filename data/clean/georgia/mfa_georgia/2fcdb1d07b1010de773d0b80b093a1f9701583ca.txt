31 January 2019 31.01.2019 – “The ruling of the ECHR is a victory Georgia has won” - the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani said. According to Zalkaliani, the Foreign Ministry was actively involved in the process and will continue to do so. “This is an essentially important ruling and a victory Georgia has won. I would like to express my gratitude to all people who have been involved in this process from the very outset. This is especially relevant to the Ministry of Justice. Very important evidence and materials have been collected to ensure that this result is achieved. The delivery of justice on this issue is of utmost priority. It is important to continue work to ensure that this judgment is enforced” – David Zalkaliani said. The Grand Chamber of the European Court of Human Rights (ECHR) s announced its judgment by sixteen votes to one in the case of Georgia v. Russia, regarding the arrest, detention and collective expulsion of Georgian nationals from Russia in the autumn of 2006. The European Court of Human Rights held by sixteen votes to one that Russia has to pay Georgia 10,000,000 euros for non-pecuniary damage suffered by a group of at least 1,500 Georgian nationals,” the court’s press office says.
