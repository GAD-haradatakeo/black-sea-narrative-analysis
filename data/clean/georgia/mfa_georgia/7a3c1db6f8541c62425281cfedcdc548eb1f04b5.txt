9 March 2022 On 9 March 2022, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani had a telephone conversation with his Swedish counterpart, Ann Linde. The sides discussed issues relating to Russia's aggression in Ukraine, European security and Georgia's application for EU membership. The sides reaffirmed Georgia’s and Sweden’s unwavering support for the territorial integrity and sovereignty of Ukraine. Special note was taken of the efforts of the Georgian and Swedish governments to provide humanitarian assistance to Ukraine. The Swedish side thanked Georgia for its support to Ukraine. The sides discussed the security environment and challenges facing Georgia in the light of the ongoing war in Ukraine. They exchanged views on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. Against this background, the need for greater involvement of European and international partners in the region was emphasized. Referring to Georgia’s application for EU membership, Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of obtaining the status of a candidate country. He stressed the irreversible integration of Georgia with the European Union, the progress already made on this path and future plans, including the full and consistent implementation of the obligations under the Association Agreement.
