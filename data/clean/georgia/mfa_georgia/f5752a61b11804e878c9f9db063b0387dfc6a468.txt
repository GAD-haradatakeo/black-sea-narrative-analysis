20 May 2021 The European Commission and the Georgia negotiated theupdated EU-Georgia Association Agenda (2021-2027) on 20 May 2021, by video conference. The First Deputy Foreign Minister, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili led the meeting from the Georgian side, while from the EU side - the meeting was chaired by the Head of the Eastern Partnership Division of the European External Action Service (EEAS),Richard Tibbels. Theupdated EU-Georgia Association Agenda (2021-2027) covers a longer period (7 years) than its previous counterparts. It includes short-term (3-4 years) and medium-term (4-7 years) priorities. The Association Agenda for 2021-2027 includes many of the priorities envisaged by the Association Agenda for 2017-2020 (the rule of law, human rights, security, legal and institutional harmonization). However, the updated version is more ambitious and reflects the goal of the Georgian government to continue the path of legal and institutional harmonization, as well as political, economic and sectoral integration.
