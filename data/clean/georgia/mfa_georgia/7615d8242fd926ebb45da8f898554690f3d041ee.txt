26 January 2026 Georgia’s National Report on the human rights situation in the country for the period 2021-2025 will be reviewed on 26 January 2026 in Geneva, within the framework of the fourth cycle of the United Nations Universal Periodic Review (UPR). The Georgian delegation, headed by Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Khatuna Totladze, presented information on the reforms implemented by the Government of Georgia during the reporting period with a view to improving the human rights situation in the country. In her address, the Head of the Delegation drew particular attention to the grave human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali. A total of 106 UN Member States participated in the review of Georgia’s report. During the session, more than 300 recommendations were made to Georgia. According to the Head of the Delegation the recommendations will be duly assessed with the involvement of all branches of government, and Georgia’s position on those recommendations which the State undertakes to implement over the next five years will be submitted to the United Nations within the established timeframe. The Georgian governmental delegation comprised representatives of executive authorities, as well as the Parliament of Georgia, the Central Election Commission of Georgia, the Prosecutor’s Office of Georgia, the High Council of Justice of Georgia, and the system of common courts of Georgia.
