6 September 2019 06.09.2019 – The OSCE Permanent Council discussed the situation in Georgia’s occupied regions. The Permanent Representative of Georgia updated members of the OSCE Permanent Council on the recent developments across the occupation line, including on the situation in the villages of Gugutiantkari and Chorchana. It was highlighted that on the 11th annivaersary of the Russia-Georgia war, Russia still continues annexation of Georgian territories and violation of the principles of international law. Representatives of the EU Member States and the United States made statements in support for Georgia. According to U.S. Ambassador James Gilmoreto the Permanent Council, the United States fully supports Georgia’s sovereignty, independence, and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders, as well as Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic aspirations. The American diplomat called on Russia “to suspend its occupation of 20 percent of Georgian territory; to withdraw its forces to pre-conflict positions; and to allow unfettered access for the delivery of humanitarian assistance in accordance with its commitment under the 2008 ceasefire agreement”. According to the Ambassador, the process of “borderization” unfairly affects Georgian civilians living in the area, separating them from their farmlands, family, livelihoods, and critical infrastructure. Borderization infringes on Georgia’s sovereignty and is contrary to the principles of the Helsinki Final Act. In the words of James Gilmore, “the United States continues to monitor recent reports of a military buildup by Russia near the Administrative Boundary Line of the Russian-occupied Georgian region of South Ossetia, and will expose violations of Georgia’s sovereignty”. https://osce.usmission.gov/on-the-situation-in-georgia/ Documents Similar News
