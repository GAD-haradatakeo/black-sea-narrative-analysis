22 July 2025 "This year, political dialogue between Georgia and Azerbaijan has been progressing dynamically and at the highest level. Numerous important high-level visits have taken place between our countries, and your visit to Georgia today is a continuation of this productive political dialogue. The relations between our countries are rich with outstanding examples of good neighborliness, friendship, and partnership. It is precisely on this foundation that we are discussing the prospects for deepening and strengthening our ties," stated Maka Botchorishvili at a joint press conference following her meeting with her Azerbaijani counterpart. According to her, the meeting with the Minister was productive, covering various areas of bilateral cooperation as well as regional and global developments. As Maka Botchorishvili emphasized, “The partnership between our countries has gone beyond bilateral and regional dimensions and has acquired important international significance.” The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia underscored the importance of peace and stability and reaffirmed Georgia’s consistent readiness to contribute to the development of regional cooperation. “We welcome the active steps and meetings aimed at normalizing relations in the South Caucasus. We hope that Azerbaijan and Armenia will soon sign a peace agreement, which will provide a solid foundation for ensuring long-term peace and development in the region,” the Georgian Foreign Minister stated. In this context, the ministers positively assessed the trilateral meeting held in Georgia on 17 April 2025, at the level of deputy foreign ministers of Georgia, Azerbaijan, and Armenia. In the context of regional cooperation, Maka Botchorishvili emphasized the importance of the existing trilateral format between Georgia, Azerbaijan, and Türkiye, describing it as a successful example of regional collaboration. The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia placed special emphasis on the ongoing international transit and energy projects involving both countries, highlighting in particular the vast potential of the Middle Corridor. “The major regional projects we have successfully implemented over the past decades are the result of exemplary bilateral cooperation between Georgia and Azerbaijan, and their significance continues to grow. The Baku-Tbilisi-Ceyhan oil pipeline, the Baku-Tbilisi-Erzurum gas pipeline, and the Baku-Tbilisi-Kars railway are just a few examples of these successful projects. The development of the Middle Corridor represents a significant opportunity afforded by the current geopolitical landscape. We possess enormous potential that we must work together to fully realize,” stated the Georgian Foreign Minister. In her remarks, the Minister once again underscored the robust trade and economic ties between the two countries, noting that Azerbaijan has ranked among Georgia’s top ten trading partners in recent years. “We have tremendous potential that calls for joint efforts to achieve the best possible outcomes in our economic cooperation, in line with the interests of both Georgia and Azerbaijan,” Bochorishvili remarked. The Ministers of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and Azerbaijan also reaffirmed their firm support for each other’s sovereignty and territorial integrity.
