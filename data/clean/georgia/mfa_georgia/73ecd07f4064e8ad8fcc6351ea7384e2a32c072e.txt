9 November 2021 On 9 November 2021, the 11th round of political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and Japan were held online, at the level of Deputy Ministers. The consultations were chaired by the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Alexander Khvtisiashvili from the Georgian side, and by the Special Representative of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for the Caucasus and GUAM, Ambassador Tokuda Shuichi - from the Japanese side. The sides discussed in detail the current issues on the agenda of Georgia-Japan cooperation both in bilateral and multilateral formats. Welcoming the recent intensification of relations, the sides stressed the need to further deepen political and economic cooperation between the two countries in all directions. The sides discussed in detail the prospects for future economic cooperation in regional formats, including in the Japan-Georgia-EU format. The need to further deepen the "connectivity” in the fields of energy, infrastructure and transport was also highlighted. The sides considered the possibility of holding joint cultural and public diplomacy events to mark the 30th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Japan. The Georgian side expressed its gratitude for the assistance Japan has been providing to Georgia in the fight against the pandemic, as well as for its financial support to Georgia over the years. The importance of the work done by the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) was also highlighted. Alexander Khvtisiashvili provided his Japanese counterpart with updated information on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and thanked him for Japan’s unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity.
