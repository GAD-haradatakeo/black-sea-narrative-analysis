3 July 2020 On 3 July 2020, the Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with the EU Special Representative for the South Caucasus and the Crisis in Georgia, Toivo Klaar. The sides discussed the difficult security and humanitarian situation in the Russian-occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions amid the COVID-19 pandemic. Special accent was put on the necessity of the EU’s active engagement in achieving the resolution of Russia-Georgia conflict and ensuring peace and security on the ground. The Minister paid special attention to an increasing number of Russia-orchestrated provocations, the so-called borderization process, closure of the occupation line, restrictions imposed on free movement and medical evacuation resulting in many fatalities in the Akhalgori district. The sides expressed their concern over Russia’s increased use of hybrid war instruments and disinformation campaigns targeting not only Georgia but also the EUMM. According to the Minister, such destructive steps further aggravate the situation of the conflict-affected situation. The sides also underlined the necessity of complying with the EU-brokered ceasefire agreement of 12 August 2008. Discussions also focused on the importance of making progress in addressing the security challenges and humanitarian problems resulting from the occupation within the Geneva International Discussions. Special mention was taken of the need to immediately resume the functioning of the Gali and Ergneti Incident Prevention and Response mechanisms. The EU Special Representative reaffirmed the EU’s full support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia.
