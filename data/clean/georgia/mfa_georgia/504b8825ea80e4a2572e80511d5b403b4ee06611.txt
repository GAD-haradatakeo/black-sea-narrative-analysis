12 February 2020 12.02.2019, Tbilisi - The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with the EU, UN and OSCE co-chairs of the Geneva International Discussions. The parties discussed the difficult security, human rights and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. Special attention was paid to the closure of two so-called crossing points and the need of ensuring free movement. It was highlighted that the Russia Federation and its occupation regimes continue to manipulate and politicize human rights and humanitarian issues. The Deputy Minister expressed his concern over the increasing number of provocations and serious escalation of tensions at the village of Chorchana. The parties identified steps needed to de-escalate the situation on the ground. Discussions focused on the need to effectively use the format of Geneva International Discussions for resolving security and humanitarian problems for the local population. Special accent was put on for achieving progress on the key items on the agenda, like the implementation of the international obligations under the 12 August 2008 Ceasefire Agreement, non-use of force, creation of international security arrangements and the safe and dignified return of refugees and internally displaced persons. Against the background of an increasing number of provocations, the parties underlined the need to restore fundamental principles of the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanism (IPRM) meetings in Gali and Ergneti.
