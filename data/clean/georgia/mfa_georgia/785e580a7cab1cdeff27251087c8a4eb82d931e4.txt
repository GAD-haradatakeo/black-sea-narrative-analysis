22 November 2017 22.11.2017, Tbilisi – Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze held a meeting with Kerstin Lundgren and Titus Corlăţean, co-rapporteurs of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe (PACE) for the monitoring of obligations and commitments by Georgia. The sides discussed the issues relating to relations between Georgia and the Council of Europe highlighting the importance of co-operation within the framework of both: the Council of Europe Committee of Ministers and the Parliamentary Assembly. The importance of keeping the Russian-Georgian conflict on the CoE agenda was also emphasized. Mikheil Janelidze provided the co-rapporteurs with information on the security, human rights and humanitarian situation in the occupied territories. Special attention was paid to the discrimination process on ethnic lines ongoing in the Abkhazian and Tskhinvali regions. Talking points also included the facts of destruction of Georgian houses in Eredvi that is a gross violation of the rights of IDPs. The need of creating CoE monitoring mechanisms and providing the organization with unhindered access to the occupied territories was also highlighted.
