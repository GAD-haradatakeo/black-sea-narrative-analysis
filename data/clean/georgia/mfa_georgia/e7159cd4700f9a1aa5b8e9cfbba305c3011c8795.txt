14 November 2019 In his remarks, the Deputy Foreign Minister spoke about the Russian Federation’s occupation of the Georgian regions and steps taken towards factual annexation, as well as about the difficult security, human rights and humanitarian situation on the ground. Expressing his concern over the infringement of the fundamental human right to life, Darsalia said that the failure to deliver justice for the killed Georgian citizens can only encourage discrimination on ethnic lines. Discussing the difficult human rights situation in the occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, Darsalia underscored that the closure of the so-called checkpoints entails dire consequences. The Deputy Minister also referred to the death of Margo Martiashvili as a clear proof of how insignificant human life is to the occupation regime. Lasha Darsalia focused on the illegal process of installation of barbed wire fences and other artificial barriers. He said that such acts impact on the daily life of the local population depriving them of such fundamental rights as the right to property, to free movement, native language education, etc. According to the Deputy Minister, on the 11th anniversary of Russia’s large-scale military aggression against Georgia, as a result of the erection of artificial barriers in the vicinity of the Gugutiantkari village, the houses of local residents and their land plots appeared locked in the occupied territories – yet another example of Russia’s illegal activities negatively impacting on the lives of the local population. Referring to the intense situation in the village of Chorchana, the Deputy Minister stressed that the number of provocations increased dramatically in 2019 due to the relocation of the occupation line deeper into the Georgian government-controlled territory. Darsalia also highlighted steps taken by the Government of Georgia towards peaceful resolution of the conflict and the importance of the Geneva International Discussions in this process.
