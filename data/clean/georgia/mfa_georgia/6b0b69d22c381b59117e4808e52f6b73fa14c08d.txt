11 May 2022 The Georgian and Spanish Foreign Ministers have reaffirmed their commitment to deepen relations The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili met with the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Kingdom of Spain, José Manuel Albares within the framework of the Ministerial Meeting of the Global Coalition against ISIS. Discussing Georgia-Spain partner relations and close contacts at high level, the Ministers welcomed the anniversary date of the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries, which provides a good opportunity to outline prospects for strengthening bilateral ties. Discussions focused on Georgia's European integration agenda and on Spain 's support in this regard, which is of particular importance in the light of Georgia' s application for EU membership and submission of self-assessment questionnaire. The Spanish Minister highly appraised Georgia’s efforts on the path of European and Euro-Atlantic integration and the tangible progress since the signing of the Association Agreement. The Ministers stressed the need for the EU to further support the aspirations of Georgia and the Associated Trio. Talking about regional security issues, the ministers reviewed the situation in the occupied regions of Georgia. They also expressed concern over the ongoing war in Ukraine and the negative impact of these processes on world security. Ilia Darchiashvili and José Manuel Albares discussed various areas of Georgia-Spain bilateral relations, including opportunities for strengthening cooperation in the fields of trade, economy and tourism, and the ministers agreed to intensify high-level contacts.
