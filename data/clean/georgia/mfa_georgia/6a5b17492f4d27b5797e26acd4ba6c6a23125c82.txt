29 December 2020 According to the statement of the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, European and Euro-Atlantic integration remains as a priority in terms of Georgia’s foreign policy. “Considering the global pandemic and the new configuration of the European Parliament and the Commission, as a result of our active efforts, we managed to establish close contacts with our European partners,” – noted Zalkaliani. According to the Minister, Georgia has managed to successfully fulfil its obligations taken within the framework of the association agreement, starting to work on the agenda of the association agreement outlined for the 2021-2027 period. “The primary priority must be to prepare to make the statement of accession into the EU by the year of 2024 as per the stated goal of the GoG (government of Georgia). From now on all efforts exerted by Georgia in terms of European integration must be diverted towards accomplishing this goal. Serious efforts are needed to achieve this goal; all instruments of bilateral relations must be put into action, work on effectively implementing the association agreement is also important,” – stated the Minister. The head of the Ministry also discussed the steps taken in terms of Euro-Atlantic integration, noting that significant passages were added to the NATO 2030 vision document, which clearly supports the open doors policy and further strengthening of cooperation with Georgia. “As an aspiring state, Georgia will continue to put to utmost use all available political and practical mechanisms in order to increase defence capabilities of Georgia, ensuring that membership of NATO comes sooner rather than later,” – noted David Zalkaliani.
