3 October 2022 On 3 October 2022, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, held a meeting with the delegation of the Parliament of Lithuania ( Seimas), led by the chairman of the National Security and Defense Committee, Laurynas Kasciunas.The meeting was also attended by Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Lithuania, Andrius Kalindra. During the conversation, special attention was paid to the issues of European and Euro-Atlantic integration. The Deputy Minister noted the importance of consolidating partners' support for Georgia's priorities, and also emphasized Georgia's fulfillment of its obligations under the association agenda and the recommendations of the European Commission on the way to European integration. The progress achieved by Georgia in terms of joining NATO was noted. The sides discussed the current security situation in the region, which has extremely deteriorated as a result of Russia's aggression against Ukraine. Special emphasis was placed on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and the importance of international negotiations in Geneva. The deputy minister noted that Georgia experienced Russian aggression and its severe consequences. It was emphasized that it is important to keep the issues related to the occupied regions of Georgia on the agenda of the European partners.
