30 January 2025 On 30 January 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Vladimer Konstantinidi, participated in the inaugural meeting of the Governing Board of the Global Diaspora Policy Alliance (GDPA), which was held in an online format under the auspices of the International Organization for Migration (IOM). Notably, Georgia currently co-chairs the Governing Board of the Alliance alongside Ireland and is actively engaged in the implementation of the Alliance’s action plan. In his address, the Deputy Minister underscored the significance and proactive role of the diaspora in addressing both national and global challenges. Furthermore, he emphasized Georgia’s commitment, as a co-chair of the Alliance, to intensifying efforts in the effective implementation of initiatives within the framework of the Global Diaspora Policy Alliance. In his video statement, the Deputy Minister reaffirmed Georgia’s readiness to host the next large-scale international conference of the Global Diaspora Policy Alliance in 2026. The meeting also featured discussions on the action plans of the Alliance’s working groups, covering key thematic areas such as climate change, healthcare, gender equality, and youth engagement. In light of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), participants highlighted the necessity of further enhancing the involvement of the diaspora in these domains. The Global Diaspora Policy Alliance (GDPA) serves as an international platform for cooperation, aiming to enhance diaspora engagement in sustainable development, deepen collaboration with compatriots residing abroad, and facilitate the exchange of best practices among participating states. As the first large-scale international initiative dedicated specifically to diaspora policy, the Alliance brings together not only governmental institutions but also international organizations, academia, civil society representatives, and, importantly, diaspora organizations.
