11 April 2022 On 11 April 2022, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, met with a delegation of the European Parliament’s Subcommittee on Security and Defense (SEDE). The delegation, which includes MEPs from different political groups, will pay an official visit to Georgia from 10 to 14 April 2022. During the visit, the members of the delegation will visit the occupation line and explore the situation on the ground. At the meeting, the First Deputy Minister emphasized the close and successful cooperation with the European Parliament. Lasha Darsalia thanked the MEPs for their active support of Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for the country's European integration, which was reflected in e numerous resolutions and statements of support adopted by the European Parliament. The strong support of the European Parliament is even more important today, especially when Georgia has applied for EU membership. The sides focused on the security situation and challenges in the region, the situation in Ukraine and its impact on the overall European security. The First Deputy Foreign Minister briefed MEPs on the deteriorating security, humanitarian and human rights situation in the Russian-occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali. The focus was on the extremely tense security situation, which is affecting human rights in the occupied regions and exacerbates the humanitarian crisis on the ground.
