25 August 2021 On 25 August 2021, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Alexander Khvtisiashvili, held a meeting with Albert Ionson, Head of OSCE/ODIHR Election Observation Mission for Self-Government Elections in Georgia scheduled for 2 October. The Head of the Observation Mission informed the Georgian side on the forthcoming visit of OSCE observers to Georgia. About 30 ODIHR long-term observers will arrive in Georgia in the coming days and will be deployed at the respective observation points. In carrying out its activities, the Mission will adhere to safery norms under the laws and regulations of Georgia to bring to minimum the risk of spread of COVID-19. Before officially starting monitoring activities in Georgia, the Mission will hold a press conference in the coming days, regarding the role of observers and their planned activities. The Deputy Foreign Minister expressed his gratitude to OSCE/ODIHR Election Observation Mission for carrying out its activities amid the difficult pandemic situation. He underlined Georgia’s commitment to involve the maximum number of international observers in the monitoring process. Alexander Khvtisiashvili briefed Albert Ionson on recent Covid-19 developments in the country and on efforts to ensure the arrival of international observers in Georgia and the full implementation of their mission.
