30 April 2022 Within the framework of his official visit to Armenia, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili met with his Armenian counterpart, Ararat Mirzoyan. The sides discussed issues of bilateral cooperation between Georgia and Armenia. The Ministers positively assessed the traditionally good-neighbourly relations between the two countries, which in turn facilitates the exchange of high-level meetings and visits. The Ministers welcomed the 30th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Armenia and underlined the importance of duly marking this anniversary date. While on the theme of the main areas of cooperation, special attention was paid to the trade- economic and transit potential of the region. While speaking about how important it is for both Georgia and Armenia to fully realize the economic potential of the two countries, the Ministers reaffirmed their commitment to make efforts, including by holding regular meetings of the bilateral economic commission, which deals with a wide range of trade and economic issues. Given the positive dynamics of tourism cooperation between the two countries over the past few years recent years, the ministers agreed to implement additional support mechanisms to deal with growing flows of tourism. The sides positively assessed Georgia-Armenia relations in the field of culture, education and people-to-people contacts. The Ministers agreed to maintain close bilateral contacts and to continue cooperation on all important issues for the countries. Following the meeting, the Foreign Ministers of the two countries made joint statements for the press.
