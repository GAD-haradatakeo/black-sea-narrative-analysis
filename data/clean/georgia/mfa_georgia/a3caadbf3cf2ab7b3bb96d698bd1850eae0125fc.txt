27 May 2022 Georgia has always been the leading reformer country in the Eastern Partnership region, and this is confirmed by official EU reports, as well as by the level of cooperation with the EU, according to the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ilia Darchiashvili. "The European Union considers Georgia to be a member of the European family," he said. According to him, the implemented reforms, as well as the progress made on fulfilling the obligations under the Association Agreement, including under the Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area Agreement, give Georgia all reasons to have optimistic expectations that "Georgia's future is European and Georgia's place is in the EU." In this regard, the Minister paid special attention to the Association Agenda agreed between Georgia and the European Union last week, which sets out priorities and steps to be taken in over the next seven years. "The agenda clearly reflects the very high level of cooperation between Georgia and the European Union, and it reflects all the issues that are crucial in the integration process," Ilia Darchiashvili said.
