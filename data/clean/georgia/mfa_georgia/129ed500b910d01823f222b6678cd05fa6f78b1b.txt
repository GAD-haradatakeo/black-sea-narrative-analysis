22 May 2017 22.05.2017, Tbilisi - The government delegation of the Republic of Turkey led by Prime Minister Binali Yıldırım paid an official visit to Georgia, on 22 May 2017. The official delegation was met by Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze at the Tbilisi Shota Rustaveli International Airport. The second meeting of the Georgia-Turkey High-level Strategic Cooperation Council will be held within the framework of this visit, on 23 May. “This is a visit of unprecedented level, in terms of the composition of the delegation. The Georgia-Turkey Strategic Cooperation Council will meet in its second session as part of this visit. The first such meeting was held in Ankara. It is interesting to note that the visit coincides with the 25th anniversary of establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Turkey. Over these past 25 years, Turkey and Georgia have succeeded to develop genuinely friendly relations. We are strategic partners, working on strategic projects which are essential to the development and stability of our region. We highly value Turkey’s firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its Euro-Atlantic aspirations. A series of agreements will be signed during the visit, which is a further proof that our relations are on a dynamic course of development and that our country is focused on the further development of these relations” – Mikheil Janelidze said. Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze will hold a bilateral meeting with his Turkish counterpart, Foreign Minister Mevlüt Çavuşoğlu, who arrived in Tbilisi together with other ministers.
