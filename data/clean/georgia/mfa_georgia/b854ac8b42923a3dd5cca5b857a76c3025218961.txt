25 April 2018 25.04.2018, New York - The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister, Mikheil Janelidze held a meeting with the Swedish Foreign Minister, Margot Wallström. Mikheil Janelidze and Margot Wallström reaffirmed the growing dynamics of co-operation between the two countries, appraising as particularly successful the co-operation between the law-enforcement agencies in terms of addressing the violations of asylum rules by certain Georgian citizens after Georgia was granted visa-free travel with the EU. The Swedish Foreign Minister highly appraised the level of co-operation between the law-enforcement structures of the two countries, including the decision to assign a Georgian police attaché to Sweden. The Ministers highlighted the importance of the visa-free travel and free trade regimes for the development of trade and business contacts between the two countries. The Swedish Foreign Minister reaffirmed the readiness to host the Georgian business delegation in Sweden. Discussions also focused on the recent developments in Georgia’s occupied territories, including human rights violations and killings. Mikheil Janelidze provided Margot Wallström with information regarding the violent death of Archil Tatunashvili and thanked the Swedish side for the support of Georgia on such issues as occupation of its territories and resulting consequences.
