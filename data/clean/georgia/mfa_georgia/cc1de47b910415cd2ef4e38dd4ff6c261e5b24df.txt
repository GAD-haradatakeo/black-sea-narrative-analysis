12 August 2021 The Georgian First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with the Head of the Delegation of the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) in Georgia, Severin Chappaz and the ICRC Envoy on Missing Persons in the Caucasus, Daniel Mac Sweeney, on 12 August 2021. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of cooperation issues between Georgia and the ICRC. Lasha Darsalia thanked the ICRC representatives for the Organization’s humanitarian efforts in Georgia, especially for its support to the population in Georgia’s occupied territories, including to the people affected by the Russia-Georgia conflict. Discussions also focused on the difficult human rights and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. The sides highlighted the need of close cooperation in finding and identifying missing persons, and the importance of further involvement of the International Committee of the Red Cross in this process. regions. Talking points also included the activities of the ICRC Envoy on Missing Persons in the Caucasus, Daniel Mac Sweeney, as well as to the ICRC’s activities in the three countries (Azerbaijan, Georgia, Armenia) of South Caucasus.
