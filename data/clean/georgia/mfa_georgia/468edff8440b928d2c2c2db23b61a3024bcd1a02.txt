21 November 2018 21.11.2018, Brussels, - On 21 November 2018, within the framework of the first high-level meeting between the Georgian Government and the European Commission, Georgia and the European Commission signed two agreements and one document within the framework of which Georgia will receive financial assistance worth €231 million. The agreements and the document were signed by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani from the Georgian side and Commissioner for European Neighbourhood Policy and Enlargement Negotiations, Johannes Hahn. The first Agreement (Skills Development and Matching for Labour Market Needs) represents a programming document under Single Support Framework – SSF,worth €49 million (48 850 000) will support capacity building and sustainable economic development in Georgia while the other (Economic and Business Development in Georgia) worth €48 million (47 650 000) aims to promote the social-economic development of Georgia and its regions. Besides, special attention will be paid to three key areas: providing speedy and fair dispute resolution mechanisms for commercial disputes, modernization of financial infrastructure, establishment of innovative business models and enhancement of business sophistication index. Commissioner Hahn and Georgian Foreign Minister Davit Zalkaliani also signedthe Annual Action Programme 2018 for Georgia, which is unprecedented in scale and envisages allocation to Georgia of €134 million in aid, of which €55 million will be directed, via the Neighbourhood Investment Facility, towards investment and infrastructure projects and €79 million will go towards the following priority areas: economic administration and fiscal policy (€18.6 million), security and the rule of law (€27 million), support for the implementation of the EU-Georgia Association Agreement (€33.5 million).
