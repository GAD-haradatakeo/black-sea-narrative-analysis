31 October 2020 By the information provided by the minister of foreign affairs, outside the Georgian borders in 38 countries, 52 election districts are open. Furthermore, because of the existing restrictions caused by the pandemic, every country has an existing plan, because of said restrictions, every country has its own set of regulations, along with the essential regulations provided by the country they are in. The minister noted that two countries, namely the Republic of Azerbaijan and the Republic of Greece (Thessaloniki), did not allow Georgia to open the voting districts. According to Zalkaliani, the city of Thessaloniki is a red zone, which remains under severe lockdown policies, which was the fundamental reason for the restriction of opening any voting districts in the area. “It is only natural that there are no political undertones to the reasons why the polling districts were not opened in the city of Thessaloniki, it is only because the local authorities did not permit for this. According to the international protocol, every time Georgia has elections that are being conducted in other countries, it has to ask permission from the local governments. In this instance, the Greek government did not issue an approval.” - Expressed Zalkaliani. Documents Similar News
