21 December 2021 The Georgian Parliament’s Foreign Relations Committee heard the First Deputy Foreign Minister and the Head of the Georgian Delegation to the Geneva International Discussions, Lasha Darsalia, on 21 December 2021. The First Deputy Foreign Minister spoke about the situation around the Russian-Georgian conflict and the ongoing international discussions in Geneva, Russia's intensified actions in the Black Sea region and growing security challenges. Lasha Darsalia provided the committee members with detailed information on the recent rounds of Geneva International Discussions and on the incident prevention and response mechanisms. He briefed the MPs on the difficult security, human rights and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied regions focusing on Moscow's steps towards the de facto annexation of the occupied territories of Georgia. The First Deputy Minister focused on the changing geopolitical context in the South Caucasus and the significant deterioration of the security environment in the Black Sea region. Lasha Darsalia spoke in detail about Russia's recent provocative steps and destructive attempts to restrict the sovereign choice of independent states as an attack on the rule-based international order posing a serious threat to peace and security in Europe. Following the speech of the First Deputy Minister, a productive discussion was held in a question-and-answer mode. The committee meeting attended by representatives of the ruling and opposition parties positively assessed the cooperation between the Parliament of Georgia and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and confirmed the readiness to continue coordination on the country's foreign policy priorities.
