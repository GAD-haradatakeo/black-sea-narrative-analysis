16 July 2025 On 15 July 2025, the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Georgia to the Republic of Austria and Permanent Representative of Georgia to the OSCE and international organizations based in Vienna, Aleksandre Maisuradze, held a meeting with the Director of the OSCE Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights (ODIHR), Maria Telalian. The meeting was held in response to the statement issued by ODIHR on 7 July regarding Georgia. During the meeting, the Ambassador reaffirmed Georgia’s strong commitment to the core values and obligations of the OSCE and expressed hope that cooperation based on mutual respect between the parties would continue. At the same time, the Ambassador conveyed concern over the statement made by Ms. Telalian, which, regrettably, was based on factual inaccuracies and misperceptions, and does not reflect the close and constructive cooperation that exists between Georgia and the OSCE. It was noted that the Director’s statement does not contribute to fostering a constructive pre-election environment in the country and casts a shadow over the legitimacy of national institutions. Furthermore, it was emphasized that the statement includes references to ongoing judicial proceedings in Georgia, which may be interpreted as interference in the functioning of an independent judiciary. Attention was also drawn to the OSCE/ODIHR election observation mission’s report concerning the October 2024 parliamentary elections in Georgia. The Georgian side noted with regret that the report became the subject of politically motivated attacks by certain members of the Council of Europe and the European Parliament. In particular, a resolution adopted by the European Parliament questioned the report’s content and methodology, thereby undermining the authority and credibility of the institution. The Georgian side also expressed disappointment that the leadership of ODIHR did not adequately respond to these attacks against the organization.
