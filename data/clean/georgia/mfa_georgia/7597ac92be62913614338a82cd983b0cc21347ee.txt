29 March 2019 29.03.2019, Tbilisi - At a meeting with the diplomatic corps accredited to Georgia, Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani and Minister of Labour, Health and Social AffairsDavid Sergeenko presented Nata Menabde as Georgia’s candidate to Regional Director of the World Health Organization (WHO). The sides discussed Nata Menabde’s many years of experience and achievements in the health sector. According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, it is highly important that Georgia increases its representation in leadership positions at international organizations. “Nata Menabde has valuable skills and extensive experience of working in this organization both at leadership and academic positions, and we believe that her proven track record will be decisive in the voting process. In the meantime we’ll work actively with all member states within Europe to ensure that the Georgian candidate receives due support” - David Zalkaliani said. According to the Minister of Labour, Health and Social AffairsDavid Sergeenko, Nata Menabde has an impressive international background and is a very strong candidate to WHO RegionalDirectorforEurope. Taking stock of her activities over the past few years, Nata Menabde highlighted the goals and objectives she plans to achieve in case of her election to the position of WHO RegionalDirectorforEurope. The WHO Regional Director will be nominated by secret ballot at the 69th meeting of the Regional Committee for Europe which will take place in Copenhagen from 16 to 19 September 2019.
