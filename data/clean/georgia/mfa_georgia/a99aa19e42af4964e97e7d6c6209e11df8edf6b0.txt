7 May 2018 7.05.2018, Tbilisi – “We are conducting the seminar entitled “Teacher – a guide to Europe” within the framework of the Days of Europe. I want to thank you for your active engagement and interest. You are assigned a special role in the European integration process. European integration opens up ample opportunities in the education sector. Special mention in this context should be made of the Erasmus + Programme. Georgia is among the top ten countries using this programme most actively and effectively. Over 800 students and 41 higher education institutions are currently involved in this programme and this is a very practical opportunity for our education sector” - The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze said when speaking before the particiapnts of the seminar “Teacher – a guide to Europe”. According to the Minister, up to 400 teachers have been trained and up to 380 projects – implemented within this programme. The seminar, which was conducted within the framework of the Days of Europe 2018, is attended by up to 100 teachers from the capital and the regions, including the regions of ethnic minorities and the villages across the occupation line. The participants of the event will receive information on Georgia’s European integration process, ongoing projects of co-operation and European values. The teachers presenting best projects will be awarded one-week training trips to Estonia, through the support of the Estonian Center of Eastern Partnership. Georgian Minister of Education and Science, Mikheil Chkhenkeli, Director of the Information Center on NATO and EU, Nino Bolkvadze, Director of the Teacher Professional Development Giorgi Machabeli, Director of Friedrich-Ebert Stiftung south caucasus office and Estonian Ambassador to Georgia Kai Kaarelson.
