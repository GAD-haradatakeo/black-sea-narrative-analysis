16 February 2026 “I am delighted to be in Bratislava on my first bilateral visit in the capacity of Minister for Foreign Affairs. Slovakia has long been, and continues to remain, a reliable partner of Georgia,” stated Maka Botchorishvili at a joint press conference held with the Minister of Foreign and European Affairs of the Slovak Republic, Juraj Blanár. The Minister for Foreign Affairs spoke about the security challenges facing the country and noted that the governments of Georgia and the Slovak Republic fully understand the importance of safeguarding sovereignty. “The commitment to upholding our countries’ sovereignty constitutes a shared priority that further unites us. Georgia is a country to which history has imparted a profound understanding of the true value of peace. In a context where our country is not covered by NATO’s security umbrella and where threats are growing, our government spares no effort to preserve peace and ensure the conditions for stable development. The pursuit of peace is what unites us today with the government of Slovakia,” Maka Botchorishvili said. The Minister for Foreign Affairs emphasized Georgia’s European integration agenda and expressed gratitude to the Slovak government for its support. “Once again, I would like to thank you for your support, as this is precisely how genuine backing for Georgia’s and the Georgian people’s European aspirations can be expressed. In a period full of challenges, cooperation and mutual support with our partners hold particular significance for us,” the Minister highlighted. Maka Botchorishvili also addressed the topic of sanctions. She stated that Georgia is fully committed to ensuring that sanctions are not circumvented through its territory. “On this matter, we cooperate very closely with our European partners. Nevertheless, we observe that, to a certain extent, certain steps are being taken without due consideration of the relevant information and without adequate communication. Such actions are detrimental to Georgia-European Union relations and undermine the potential that exists between the two sides to be utilized for their mutual benefit. In this regard as well, I would like to express my gratitude for your support”. Minister Botchorishvili further noted that during the meeting, the parties discussed the dynamics of political dialogue, the importance of high-level visits and trade, economic and sectoral cooperation. Special attention was given to the launch of direct flights between the countries, which is expected to enhance the flow of tourists.
