27 October 2017 27.10.2017, Tbilisi - The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze attended the reception dedicated to the 25th anniversary of restoration of diplomatic relations between Georgia and France held the Tbilisi National Conservatoire. Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze, French Ambassador to Georgia Pascal Meunier and the Tbilisi Conservatoire Rector Revaz Kiknadze spoke before the attending audience. Speaking about the traditionally friendly relations between the two countries, Mikheil Janelidze highlighted France’s role in recognizing the first Democratic Republic of Georgia. According to him one of the clear examples of France’s 25-year partnership with Georgia is the return of Le Ville estate to Georgia. The Minister thanked the French Government for its support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and for its European and Euro-Atlantic integration path. He paid special attention to close co-operation between the two countries in the areas of economy, trade, investments, defence, education, culture and agriculture. “On the occasion of the 25th anniversary of restoration of friendly and diplomatic relations between Georgia and France, the Bordeaux Center for Wine and Civilizations hosts the exhibition titled “Georgia – Cradle of Wine”, which has already been visited by over 33 000 visitors. Georgia is an honorary host of the Wine Civilization Museum that is essentially important in terms of promoting not only Georgian wine internationally but also for the success of the country” - Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze said.
