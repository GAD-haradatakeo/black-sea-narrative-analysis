24 January 2019 25.01.2019, Switzerland – The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani met with his Brazilian counterpart, Foreign Minister Ernesto Araujo, within the framework of the World Economic Forum Annual Meetingin Davos. During the meeting with the Foreign Minister of Brazil’s newly approved government, the sides discussed all aspects of bilateral relations between Georgia and Brazil. Highlighting the positive dynamic or trade and economic relations, the sides expressed their readiness for the further deepening of co-operation and positively appraised mutual support for key issues within the international organizations. Special attention was paid to the latest development of evets in Latin America, and in Venezuela, in particular. The Brazilian Foreign Minister highly appreciated Georgia’s clear and speedy decision to support the interim government and thanked the Georgian side for this. The Ministers highlighted the need for the international community to support the interim president of Venezuela to ensure that the country undergoes transformation within the margins of the constitution and that the Venezuelan people are allowed to express their free will in the fair and fee elections.
