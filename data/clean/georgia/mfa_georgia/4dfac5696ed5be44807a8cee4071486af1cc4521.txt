6 July 2018 06.07.2018, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani attended the reception to mark the 242nd anniversary of independence of the United States of America. President of Georgia, Giorgi Margvelashvili, Prime Minister Mamuka Bakhtadze and Acting U.S. Ambassador to Georgia Elizabeth Rood spoke before the attending audience. According to the President, since the adoption of the United States Declaration of Independence 242 years ago, freedom has become one of the major political values. “This day is a celebration not only for the American people, but also for all the people who consider freedom as the highest value. We celebrated the centennial of independence on May 26 and the American people shared our joy. Today we stand by our American friends because we share freedom as our highest value. Today we commemorate those who have sacrificed their lives for this value” – Giorgi Margvelashvili said. "United States Declaration of Independence was signed on July 4, 1776, which is a public holiday for all Americans. For me, this glorious day is a symbol of fight for freedom, unity and national values" stated Mamuka Bakhtadze, Prime Minister of Georgia in his well-wishing address to the US people at a festive reception held by the Embassy of USA in Tbilisi, Georgia. Prime Minister of the country noted that USA is a great country for the ideals and values that were never derailed. The reception dedicated to the National Day of the U.S. was attended by representatives of the executive and legislative branches of the government and members of the diplomatic corps accredited to Georgia and other invited guests.
