11 October 2022 On 11 October 2022, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili held a meeting with the President of the National Council of the Swiss Confederation, Irène Kälin. The Minister of Foreign Affairs noted with satisfaction the existing close and friendly relations between Georgia and Switzerland and focused on the great potential for the development of mutually beneficial cooperation in such areas as economic relations, professional education, climate protection, etc. In that context, the sides emphasized the importance of inter-parliamentary ties. The discussion further focused on the long-term involvement of Switzerland in the field of development, which contributed to the socio-economic transformation of the country and will continue actively in the future in the direction of implementing reforms in priority areas. The sides exchanged views on the challenges facing the region and the importance of the international community’s strong and united position to deal with the existing threats. The minister drew special attention to the security and human rights situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. He thanked the Swiss side for its firm support of Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its role as a mediator with the Russian Federation. The President of the Swiss National Council, for her part, emphasized the growing dynamics achieved over the past 30 years since the establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Switzerland. She also highlighted the impressive indicators of the country's economic growth. She welcomed Georgia’s peace initiative and its mediation efforts aiming to promote stability in the South Caucasus region.
