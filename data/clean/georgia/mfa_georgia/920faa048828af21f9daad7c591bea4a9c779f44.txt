30 October 2017 30.10.2017, Tbilisi - “The Baku-Tbilisi-Kars (BTK) railway project bears tremendous importance both to our country and the region as a whole. Europe, South Caucasus and Central Asia see this project as a new bridge connecting Europe and Asia. We make active efforts to ensure that Georgia’s role in this regard is highlighted to its maximum extent” - The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze said in connection with the official launch of the Baku-Tbilisi-Kars (BTK) railway project due to be held today, in Azerbaijan. According to Mikheil Janelidze, it is a tremendous success as the project is oriented towards the development of the countries in the region, towards their economic progress, new opportunities, peace and stability. The Baku-Tbilisi-Kars (BTK) railway officially open in Azerbaijan today. Georgia’s Prime Minister Giorgi Kvirikashvili will attend the event at the invitation of the President of Azerbaijan Ilham Aliyev.
