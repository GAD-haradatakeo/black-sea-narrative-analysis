10 November 2021 On 9 and 10 November 2021, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs hosted meetings between the NATO Assessment Team and representatives of government agencies involved in the implementation of the 2021 Annual National Programme of Georgia. The meetings focused on the reforms implemented to strengthen the country's defense capabilities and resilience. The sides also discussed the tangible progress made in NATO-Georgia political dialogue and practical cooperation. The sides also discussed the Parliament’s supervisory power over the security sector, as well as the ongoing reforms in the fields of security, democratic and economic development, including the 2021 municipal elections. During the meetings, representatives of the legislative, executive and judicial branches of the government provided the assessment teams with comprehensive information on the reforms and activities that have been implemented under the obligations under the 2021 document. The Annual National Programme is one of the most important tools for Georgia to join NATO, which reflects the ongoing democratic reforms in the country. The Alliance annually evaluates the implementation of the programme and discusses the results with the Georgian side. Over the years, Georgia has received positive feedback from the Alliance on the progress it has made on democratic reforms, consolidation of its defense institutions and integration with NATO.
