14 November 2024 Teimuraz Janjalia, held an introductory meeting with the United Kingdom’s Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to Georgia, Gareth Ward On 13 November 2024, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia, held an introductory meeting with the United Kingdom’s Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to Georgia, Gareth Ward. During the meeting, the parties exchanged views on key issues of bilateral relations, with particular emphasis on the strategic partnership framework between the two countries and the sectoral cooperation within this format. They discussed the successful cooperation between Georgia and the United Kingdom within international organizations. The discussion also covered the regional security environment and the impact of existing challenges on Georgia. The conversation touched upon the parliamentary elections held in Georgia. The Deputy Minister thanked the international observation missions for monitoring the election process and provided the Ambassador with information about the ongoing electoral procedures. Teimuraz Janjalia emphasized the irreversibility of Georgia’s European choice. In this context, he highlighted the importance of the international community’s unified and consistent stance. The Ambassador informed the Deputy Minister about the United Kingdom’s position regarding regional security challenges and reaffirmed London’s firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity.
