14 April 2020 Speaking before the Diaspora and Caucasus Issues Committee of the Parliamentary of Georgia, Deputy Foreign Minister Mikheil Ninua said that “A total of 5866 citizens of Georgia have returned home by air, sea and road. A total of 22 flights have been performed to date. According to Mikheil Ninua, Georgian Airways has already performed 13 flights (06.03. Teheran-Tbilisi; 10.03. Rime-Tbilisi; 10.03. Rome-Tbilisi; 23.03. Berlin-Batumi; 24.03. Athens-Tbilisi (Aegean Airlines); 26.03. Vienna-Tbilisi; 31.03. Amsterdam-Tbilisi; 03.04. Berlin-Tbilisi; 04.04. London-Batumi; 07.04. Athens-Tbilisi; 10.04. Larnaka-Tbilisi; 10.04. Doha-Tbilisi; 12.04. Berlin-Tbilisi), Polish Airways (LOT) has performed 5 flights (16.03. Warsaw-Tbilisi; 17.03. Warsaw-Tbilisi; 17.03. Warsaw-Tbilisi; 28.03. Warsaw-Tbilisi; 03.04. Warsaw-Tbilisi), Belarusian airways (Belavia) – 3 flights (25.03. Minsk-Yerevan; 29.03. Minsk-Yerevan; 05.04. Minsk-Yerevan) and MyWays Airline – 1 flight (Ürümqi-Tbilisi). He also singled out flights planned for the near future: 14.04. Amsterdam-Tbilisi; 16.04. Paris-Tbilisi; 18.04. Tel-Aviv – Tbilisi; 21.04. Rome-Tbilisi; 22.04. Erbil-Batumi; 24.04. Barselona-Tbilisi; 27.04. Warsaw-Tbilisi; 30.04. Madrid-Tbilisi;02.05.Prague-Tbilisi. According to the Deputy Minister, those wishing to return to Georgia need to fill in special e-applications. People with special needs get air tickets by order of priority. He said that the diplomatic representations help Georgian citizens stranded abroad to find alternate routes. Ninua also highlighted that owing to the pandemic-dictated restrictions, rules for managing dead bodies have also been tightened causing delays in the repatriation of the remains. “From 1 March to date 43 dead bodies have been repatriated to Georgia .
