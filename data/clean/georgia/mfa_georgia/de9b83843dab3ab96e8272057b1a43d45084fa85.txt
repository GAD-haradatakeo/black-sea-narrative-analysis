16 July 2022 "Today is a special day in Georgia. Tbilisi is hosting a very important dialogue earlier launched between our two neighboring countries. This process takes on a particular meaning as it gives us the opportunity, through our joint efforts, to ensure peace and security in our region" - said the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia while assessing the visit of his Azerbaijani and Armenian counterparts to Tbilisi. Ilia Darchiashvili highlighted the role of the Georgian Prime Minister, Irakli Gharibashvili in this process. Using the rostrum of the United Nations, the Prime Minister announced the Peaceful Neighborhood Initiative in the South Caucasus as early as 2021, and proposed the creation of a mediation platform in Tbilisi, the Minister said. Today’s visit is a logical follow-on to this process – he added. According to Ilia Darchiashvili, Georgia’s mediation initiative is being implemented successfully – through the efforts of Georgia as a mediator, the negotiation process with the the two countries has already led to concrete and tangible results. The Minister thanked his Azerbaijani and Armenian colleagues for their trust in the Georgian government and expressed the hope that Georgia-mediated dialogue will help achieve peace and stability in the region. "Tbilisi has been a place of making important decisions for centuries," the Minister said. Today's visit only renewed this ancient tradition - he added.
