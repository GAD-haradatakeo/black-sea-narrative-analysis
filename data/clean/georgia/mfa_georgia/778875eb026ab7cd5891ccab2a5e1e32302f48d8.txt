5 August 2025 On the sidelines of the ongoing United Nations Conference in Awaza, Turkmenistan, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, met with the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Turkmenistan, Rashid Meredov. The ministers reviewed the prospects for deepening cooperation between Georgia and Turkmenistan and discussed the dynamics of the political dialogue. Special emphasis was placed on the potential for strengthening trade and economic relations. The two sides underscored the importance of collaboration within multilateral formats, as well as the productive ties between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs and at the parliamentary level. Particular attention was devoted to the need for further development of the Middle Corridor and the Black Sea-Caspian Sea transport and transit initiative, which plays a significant role in enhancing regional connectivity. The frequency of high-level bilateral visits was also positively evaluated as a sign of deepening relations. The parties highlighted the importance of holding regular political consultations between the foreign ministries, noting that such dialogue serves as a vital platform for discussing pressing bilateral issues and reaching mutually beneficial decisions. Minister Botchorishvili extended an official invitation to her Turkmen counterpart to visit Tbilisi.
