2 October 2023 On 2 October 2023, the 12th round of regular political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and Japan was held in Tbilisi. The consultations were led by the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili from the Georgian side, and from the Japanese side - by the Assistant to the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ambassador Ikegami Masaki. Within the framework of the consultations, the sides discussed in detail the current issues on the Georgia-Japan agenda, prospects for the further development of bilateral relations, as well as cooperation opportunities within the international organizations. The sides noted with satisfaction the growing positive dynamics of relations between the two countries, especially in terms of high-level visits. Discussions focused on economic, energy, infrastructure and transport cooperation. Special attention was also paid to global and regional security issues. The Georgian side provided the Japanese delegation with updated information regarding the current situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. The head of the Japanese delegation once again confirmed the Japanese government’s unwavering position to support the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia.
