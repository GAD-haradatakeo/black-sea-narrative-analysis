27 February 2018 28.02.2018, Geneva – Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze held a meeting with UN High Commissioner for Refugees Filippo Grandi within the framework of the High-Level Segment of the of the UN Human Rights Council in Geneva. The sides noted with satisfaction Georgia’s successful co-operation with the UN High Commissioner for Refugees. While discussing the human rights situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and the status of refugees, Mikheil Janelidze provided the High Commissioner with detailed information on the death of Archil Tatunashvili and the detention of Georgia’s two citizens as a result of Russia’s criminal activities. The UN High Commissioner expressed his regret over the tragedy reaffirming that it is important to duly react to and discuss this issue in respective formats, including in the Geneva International Discussions. Positively appraising the government’s engagement policy directed towards confidence-building between the war-torn communities, the sides emphasized the importance of peaceful resolution of the conflict.
