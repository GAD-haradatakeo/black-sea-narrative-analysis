14 October 2025 On 14 October 2025, the OSCE Chairperson-in-Office and Minister for Foreign Affairs of Finland, Elina Valtonen, paid an official visit to Georgia. During the visit, the OSCE Chairperson held a meeting with the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili. The discussions focused on issues related to the Russia-Georgia conflict, the situation in the region, Georgia’s geostrategic location, and ongoing developments in the country. Minister Botchorishvili expressed her gratitude to Finland, as the country holding the OSCE Chairmanship, for its steadfast support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity within the framework of the Organization. The importance of the active engagement of the OSCE Co-Chairs in the Geneva International Discussions was particularly emphasized. Minister Botchorishvili briefed Elina Valtonen on the grave situation in Georgia’s Russian-occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali. The parties underlined the need for Russia to comply with the EU-mediated Ceasefire Agreement of 12 August 2008 and highlighted the importance of the safe and dignified return of internally displaced persons and refugees. The Minister also emphasized the legal significance of the decisions of international courts in ensuring accountability for Russia as an occupying power. It was noted that the Finnish Foreign Minister will also visit the occupation line to observe the situation on the ground. The two ministers also exchanged views on the current situation in the region and on Georgia’s role in promoting peace and stability. Minister Botchorishvili reaffirmed the importance for Georgia of maintaining a substantive and results-oriented dialogue with the European Union.
