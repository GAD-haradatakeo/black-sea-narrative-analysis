23 June 2017 23.06.2017, Belgrade – Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze paid a visit to Belgrade to participate in the ceremony of inauguration of the Serbian President Aleksandar Vucic. Mikheil Janelidze held meetings with Serbia’s newly-appointed President and the Acting Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs Ivica Dacic. Discussions focused on important issues relating to bilateral and multilateral co-operation. Both sides reaffirmed their firm commitment to the principles of international law and respect for the territorial integrity of each other’s countries. Special attention was paid to the need to activate trade-economic co-opeation and to exchange high-level visits. The sides outlined plans to sign a trade-economic co-operation agreement in the near future and expressed the intention to deepen a Georgia-Serbia political dialogue, inter-parliamentary co-operation, cultural and people-to-people contacts. The sides also spoke about the necessity of deepening co-operation in the area of tourism and of introducing direct flights between Tbilisi and Belgrade. The need of timely signing an agreement on the mutual lifting of visa requirements for holders of ordinary passports was also highlighted. The sides agreed to start work towards organizing Mikheil Janelidze’s first official visit to Serbia.
