5 April 2019 5.04.2019 - Congressman Gerry Connolly, Chairman of the U.S. Delegation to the NATO Parliamentary Assembly and Co-chair of the Georgia Caucus at the U.S. Congress, together with Congressman Michael Turner introduced a resolution celebrating 70th anniversary of NATO to the House of Representatives. The bipartisan resolution reaffirms its support for Georgia and Open Door Policy. “NATO has worked to deter Russian aggression, including responding to the illegal invasions and occupations of Georgia and Ukraine by the Russian Federation; NATO continues to support an open-door membership policy of admitting any European country that can contribute to the promotion and protection of freedom, democracy, stability, and peace throughout Europe” – reads the draft Resolution. Congressman Connolly highly appraised Georgia’s efforts towards ensuring international security. He highlighted the importance of Open Door Policy with respect to Georgia. According to him, NATO is about two directions. One concerns collective defence and the other shared values, such as democratic development, freedom of expression, and rule of law.
