27 January 2021 The heads of diplomatic services of Israel, China, Belarus, Estonia and Slovenia congratulate David Zalkaliani upon his appointment to the position of Vice-Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs. “Israel and Georgia shale excellent bilateral relations. There is 2 millennia of relations between our two peoples, cohabiting peacefully throughout history. I expect to work with you on further deepening said relations and strengthening potential prospects,” – stated the Israeli Foreign Minister, Gabi Ashkenazi, in his congratulatory letter. The head of the Chinese Foreign Ministry, Wang Yi, also expresses readiness to cooperate with the Georgian Foreign Ministry, noting that after the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries, Georgia and China have steadily and fruitfully developed said relations. In his address, the Foreign Minister of Belarus, Vladimir Makei, expresses hope that the existing friendly relations between our two countries will only continue to grow. The head of the Estonian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Urmas Reinsalu, once again, reconfirmed that Estonia will continue to support Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations, along with its sovereignty and territorial integrity. The Foreign Minister of Slovenia, Anže Logar, states in his address that Slovenia will support Georgia in terms of deepening dialogue within the framework of the Eastern Partnership Initiative, expressing hope that Georgia will continue its successful reforms aimed towards improving prosperity in terms of economy and social issues, strengthening ties with the EU.
