24 September 2021 In the framework of the 76th session of the UN General Assembly, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Czech Republic, Jakub Kulhanek. The sides discussed issues of bilateral cooperation between Georgia and the Czech Republic, as well as important aspects of economic relations. Special emphasis was placed on the Czech side's support for Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration. Zalkaliani thanked his Czech counterpart for the Czech Republic’s strong political and practical support for Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration. The sides also discussed the situation in the occupied territories. The Czech Republic, as a friend of Georgia, strongly supports Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. The sides highlighted that the Czech Republic also backs the policy of non-recognition and is committed to ensure that the issue of occupied territories is kept high on the international agenda. Zalkaliani thanked his Czech counterpart for his statements in support of Georgia within international organizations. Discussions also focused on prospects for deepening trade and economic cooperation and on holding the next round of the Intergovernmental Economic Commission in Prague in December. The sides discussed the successful projects implemented by the Czech Development Agency towards the economic development of Georgia. It was noted that Georgia is on the list of priority countries in the Agency strategy for 2018-2023.
