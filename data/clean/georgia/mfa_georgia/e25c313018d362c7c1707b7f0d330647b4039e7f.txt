28 January 2026 Another round of political consultations was held between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and Spain in Madrid, The Georgian delegation was headed by the First Deputy Minister, George Zurabashvili, while the Spanish delegation was led by State Secretary Diego Martínez Belío. Within the framework of the consultations, the parties reviewed key issues in bilateral relations between Georgia and the Kingdom of Spain, including trade and economic cooperation, humanitarian engagement and other areas of mutual importance, and discussed prospects for the further development of cooperation. George Zurabashvili briefed his Spanish counterpart on the grave humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and expressed gratitude for Spain’s support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. Particular attention was paid to ongoing developments in the region, as well as to issues related to European and Euro-Atlantic integration. At the conclusion of the meeting, the parties expressed their readiness to continue close cooperation. Within the framework of his visit to the Kingdom of Spain, George Zurabashvili also met with the Deputy Secretary-General of the Organization of Ibero-American States (OEI), Federico Ludovice. The meeting focused on prospects for strengthening cooperation in the fields of education, science, technology and culture.
