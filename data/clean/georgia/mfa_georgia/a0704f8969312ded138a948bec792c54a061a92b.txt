12 December 2022 The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili, met the European Commissioner for Neighborhood and Enlargement, Olivér Várhelyi, within the framework of the EU-Eastern Partnership ministerial meeting held in Brussels, on 12 December. At the meeting, the sides emphasized the successful relations between Georgia and the European Union and Georgia’s efforts on the path of EU integration, including the progress achieved in the implementation of the European Commission’s recommendations and the successful cooperation with the experts of the European Commission in this regard. At the meeting with the European Commissioner, special attention was paid to the importance of economic and sectoral rapprochement with the European Union and full integration into the internal market of the European Union, as well as the need for closer cooperation with the Eastern European Union for the effective implementation of flagship partnership projects. In this context, a positive assessment was given to the flagship projects of Georgia within the framework of the Eastern Partnership economic and investment plan, which will contribute to the strengthening of sustainability and economic and sectoral rapprochement. Discussions also focused on the format of the Eastern Partnership and the need to adapt it to the changes in the region. The sides also discussed the current security situation in the region.
