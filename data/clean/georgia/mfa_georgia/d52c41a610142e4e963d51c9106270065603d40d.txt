15 September 2020 On 15 September 2020, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, held a meeting with the co-chairs of the Geneva International Discussions, representing the UN and the OSCE, during their visit to Georgia. The Minister expressed concern over the rise in cases of provocation from the Russian side, threatening stability and peace in the entire region and, thus, infringing upon the fundamental rights of the local population. David Zalkaliani strongly condemned the illicit arrests and kidnappings of Georgian citizens, including against the background of the ongoing illegal process of ‘borderisation’ near the villages of Atotsi, Kvemo Nikozi and Khurvaleti. Special note was taken of Russia’s active use of hybrid warfare and misinformation campaigns targeted against Georgia and EUMM. According to the Minister, such destructive steps aim at destabilizing the situation in the country. Zalkaliani focused his attention on the difficult humanitarian situation in Gali and Akhalgori, the closure of the occupation line and restrictions on fee movement that weighs heavily on the local population. The parties the need to reach progress on the security and humanitarian issues caused by the Russian occupation, within the framework of the Geneva International Discussions. Special attention was paid to the necessity for the Russian Federation to comply with the 12 August 2008 Ceasefire Agreement, and the need to create international security arrangements in the occupied territories being an essential prerequisite for ensuring sustainable peace on the ground. David Zalkaliani urged the co-chairing international organizations to use all instruments available to put an end to provocations across the occupation line and ensure immediate release of Georgian citizens detained illegally.
