26 February 2019 26.02.2019, Geneva – Reforms aimed at strengthening intellectual property rights and successful co-operation with the World Intellectual Property Organizationwere discussed between the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani and WIPO's Director General, FrancisGurry. David Zalkaliani emphasized the priority importance of intellectual property field and thanked the Director General for his personal involvement in implementing the reforms in Georgia. The sides emphasized that intellectual property-related issues are included in the Free Trade Agreement with the EU. Special note was taken of successful co-operation with WIPO o issues relating to the National Intellectual Property Strategy, protection of Georgia’s geographical designations and implementation of various innovations in the field.
