23 February 2021 On 23 February a meeting was held between the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani and the Deputy Foreign Minister of the Republic of Turkey, Sedat Önal, who conducted a working visit to Georgia. Further perspectives of development of the strategic development between Georgia and Turkey were discussed. The successful and fruitful cooperation between the two states was noted in many dimensions, including political, trade, economy, transportation, energy sectors, etc. Additionally, the vast potential of deepening said cooperation was also underscored during the meeting. A particular emphasis was put on holding high-level visits. The issue of holding a session of the strategic cooperation council between Georgia and Turkey was also discussed. Additionally, the parties discussed the existing new realities of the region, challenges, threats and perspectives for cooperation. It was particularly underscored that Georgia views the bilateral economic projects as of having especial importance. During the meeting the Turkish party, once again, reconfirmed its staunch support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity as well as Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic aspirations.
