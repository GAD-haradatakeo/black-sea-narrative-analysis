16 September 2021 On 16 September, the Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia participated in a meeting of high-ranking Eastern Partnership officials, which was held in the format of video conference. The meeting was attended by representatives of the European External Action Service, the European Commission, the EU and EaP Member States and the Eastern Partnership Civil Society Forum. The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister stressed the importance of Eastern Partnership for Georgia and the region in general. The sides discussed the post-2020 agenda, which was developed in close coordination with the partners. The Deputy Foreign Minister spoke about the importance of the economic and investment plan developed within the framework of the partnership, as well as the flagship initiatives identified for Georgia. In terms of physical rapprochement with the EU, Teimuraz Janjalia stressed the importance of full connectivity, especially the full potential of the Black Sea. The meeting also focused on such priorities as strengthening the rule of law, green and digital transformation, strengthening the resilience of democratic and inclusive society. Talking points also included the Eastern Partnership summit scheduled for December 2021 in Brussels, which, along with a political declaration, will adopt future goals of the post-2020 partnership. The Deputy Foreign Minister stressed the importance of actively applying the principle of differentiation within the partnership and noted in this light the successful example of the Associated Trio as a platform established as a result of the policy of differentiation.
