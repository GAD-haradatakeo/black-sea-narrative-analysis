7 June 2018 On 7 June 2018, Georgia’s candidate - Dr. Lia Nadaraia - was elected as a member of the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) for the term 2019-2022. The elections were held during the 20th Meeting of the States parties to the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women in New York. Dr. Nadaraia is a current member of the CEDAW for the term 2015-2018. Together with Georgia, 16 other countries have nominated candidates for 12 vacant seats. Experts from following countries were elected: Georgia, Algeria, Azerbaijan, Bulgaria, Burkina Faso, Egypt, Japan, Mauritius, Peru, Saudi Arabia, Spain, Trinidad and Tobago. The CEDAW is a body comprising 23 independent experts, with competence in the relevant field of human rights, that supervises the implementation of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee also considers the periodic reports submitted by the States parties regarding the steps taken towards implementation of the Convention’s provisions. Recently, Georgia has stepped up its international efforts towards elimination of violence and discrimination against women. In April 2018, Georgia was elected as a member of the Executive Board of the United Nations Entity for Gender Equality and the Empowerment of Women (UN-Women), for the term 2019-2021 and in May 2018, candidature of Georgia Mr. Vladimer Mkervalishvili, was elected to the Group of Experts on Action against Violence against Women and Domestic Violence (GREVIO), for the term 2018-2022.
