1 February 2022 On 1 February 2021, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Lasha Darsalia, met with members of the Council of Europe delegation arriving in Georgia to prepare the 25th consolidated report "Conflict in Georgia" of the Secretary General of the Council of Europe. At the meeting, the Georgian side briefed the Council of Europe delegation on the deteriorating security, humanitarian and human rights situation in the Russian-occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali. The First Deputy Minister paid particular attention to the extremely tense security situation, which impacts human rights in the occupied regions and exacerbates the humanitarian crisis on the ground. Discussions focused on the de-facto annexation of the Russian-occupied territories of Georgia, increasing militarization, the so-called Borderization, closure of the occupation line in the Tskhinvali region, restriction of free movement, which isolates the population living in the occupied regions. The Georgian side spoke about the dynamics of the Geneva International Discussions, Russia's destructive steps, as well as Russia's non-fulfillment of the 2008 ceasefire agreement and the importance of the return of internally displaced persons and refugees. The participants of the meeting also stressed the importance of resuming the Incident Prevention and Response Mechanism (IPRM) meetings in Gali without any preconditions and the smooth functioning of the Ergneti IPRM. At the meeting, the Georgian side spoke about the Georgian government’s peace policy and the whole-of nation process aimed at de-occupation of Georgian territories and the peaceful settlement of the conflict. Lasha Darsalia stressed the need to keep the issues related to the Russian-occupied regions on the agenda of the international community.
