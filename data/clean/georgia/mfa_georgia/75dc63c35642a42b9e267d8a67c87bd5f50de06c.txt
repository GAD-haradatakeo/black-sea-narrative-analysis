21 October 2019 21.10.2019, London – Today marks an important date in the history of relations between Georgia and the UK” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said commenting on the signing of the UK-Georgia Strategic Partnership Agreement. According to the Minister, the signing of the strategic partnership agreement a few days before Brexit points to how important the relationship with Georgia is to Great Britain. “We did everything to ensure that no legislative vacuum is created in the relations between the two countries after Brexit. This Strategic Partnership Agreement provides the legislative framework for regulating relationship between the two countries in terms of foreign policy, economy, defence, security and people-to-people contacts” – Zalkaliani said. In the words of the Foreign Minister, this agreement will bring the strategic partnership between the two countries to a qualitatively higher level, and will also stimulate the Wardrop Strategic Dialogue and further deepen cooperation between the two countries.
