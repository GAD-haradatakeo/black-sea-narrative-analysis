18 January 2022 “We had a very productive meeting with the NATO Secretary General. This meeting has confirmed all my expectations. The Secretary General stated his position clearly that there will be no revising of NATO decisions, and that the main priority is Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as its Euro-Atlantic integration - that’s where the red line is drawn“ – said the Vice Prime Minister/Foreign Minister of Georgia, David Zalkaliani commenting on his meeting with the NATO Secretary General, Jens Stoltenberg. According to the Secretary General, NATO is committed to continuing the dialogue with the Russian Federation. There are issues that can be negotiated, but there will be no compromise on those red lines that are very clearly drawn. As the Foreign Minister said, Georgia’s NATO integration agenda will remain unchanged “We’ll do our utmost, in close coordination with our NATO partners, to ensure that this process is not damaged and all events we have been planning are conducted as per schedule.
