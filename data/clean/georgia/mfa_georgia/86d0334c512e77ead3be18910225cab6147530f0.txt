26 July 2018 26.07. 2018, Washington – “Georgia enjoys and will continue to enjoy the firm support of its strategic partner in terms of recognition of the country’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as of its European and Euro-Atlantic integration. The U.S. Deputy Secretary of State also reaffirmed his support for the Georgian government’s new plans for economic development” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said following his meeting with the U.S. Deputy Secretary of State John Sullivan on 26 July on the sidelines of a three-day Ministerial to Advance Religious Freedom in Washington DC. The sides discussed a wide range of bilateral and multilateral issues.“It was also noted that our economic cooperation should closely track our political relations with U.S., as well as the relations we have in defense and security,” Zalkaliani said. Discussions focused on Georgia-US strategic partnership and the further enhancement of relations, challenges in the region and the situation in the Georgian occupied regions, including the human rights violations cases in the Abkhazia and Tskhinvali occupied regions of Georgia.
