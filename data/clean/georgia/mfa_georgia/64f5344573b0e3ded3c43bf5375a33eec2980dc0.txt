8 May 2019 08.05.2019, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani participated in the workshop “Young European Ambassadors” at the Information Centre on NATO and EU of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia. Speaking before the participants of the meeting, the Minister highlighted the role of young people in raising public awareness about European and Euro-Atlantic integration. Zalkaliani also spoke extensively about Georgia’s successes on the path of EU integration, including Georgia’s unprecedented high-level formats of cooperation with the EU. According to him, it is important to develop sectoral so-operation with the EU in such areas as transport, energy and communications. The Minister said that membership of the EU is a guarantee of stability, security and sustainable development of the country, “as evidenced by the benefits of the European integration process benefitting the population of our country” – Zalkaliani said. The two-day seminar: “Young European Ambassadors” is conducted through cooperation with the European Union’s project EU NEIGHBOURS East. The event is part of the Europe Days and brings together last-year pupils and students from various regions of Georgia. The official opening ceremony of the seminar was attended by Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani, Ambassador of the European Union,Carl Hartzell, Deputy State Minister of Georgia for Reconciliation and Civic Equality Lia Gigauri and Mission Deputy Director for U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID)David Hoffman.
