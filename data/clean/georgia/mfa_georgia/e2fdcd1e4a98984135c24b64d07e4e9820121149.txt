10 March 2022 On 10 March 2022, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani had a telephone conversation with his Czech counterpart, Jan Lipavský. The sides discussed the security situation getting worse due to the ongoing war in Ukraine, exchanged information on political and humanitarian assistance provided by the two countries to Ukraine, and reaffirmed their strong support for Ukraine. The two ministers highlighted the need for international partners’ greater involvement in the region and the further consolidation of their support for Georgia, with due regard for the possible threats that the ongoing processes in the region may pose to Georgia. Particular attention was paid to the situation in the occupied regions. In this context, the Czech Minister stated that he is aware of the challenges Georgia is facing and expressed his support for our country. David Zalkaliani shared with his colleague the information on the progress made on Georgia's European integration, including in terms of fulfilling the obligations under the Association Agreement. Referring to Georgia’s EU membership application, he stressed that Georgia expects its partners’ support in this regard.
