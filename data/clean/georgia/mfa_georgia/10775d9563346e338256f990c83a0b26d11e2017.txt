10 July 2021 On 10 July, the Vice Prime Minister and Foreign Minister, David Zalkalianiheld a meeting with his Indiancounterpart, the Minister ofExternal Affairs, SubrahmanyamJaishankar. David Zalkaliani thanked SubrahmanyamJaishankar for this historical visit during which a holy relic of St Queen Ketevan will be donated to Georgia in permanent possession and the statute of Mahatma Gandhi will be unveiled in Tbilisi. It needs to be highlighted that it is the first time that the Indian Minister ofExternal Affairs visited independent Georgia. Minister Zalkaliani thanked the Indian Government and the Prime Minister, Narendra Modi, as well as all representatives of the Indian side, including the Ministry of External Affairs, the Ministry of Culture and the Indian Archaeological Agency, who, through close coordination with the Georgian Embassy to India, were involved in returning the holy relic of the great Georgian martyr to the Georgian nation. Zalkaliani also highlighted another important aspect of this visit: the unveiling of the statute of Mahatma Gandhi, the outstanding public figure and the leader of the Indian Non-Violent Independence Movement. The unveiling ceremony is dedicated to mark the 150thbirth anniversary of Mahatma Gandhi. The statue is produced by the artist Gautam Pal. India donated the statue as a gift to Georgia. The two ministers exchanged their views over the increasing dynamics of bilateral relations between Georgia and India, including in the political, economic, cultural, educational and legal areas. They also highly appraised cooperation in the sectors of transport, investments, tourism, agriculture, technologies, communications and innovations.
