29 December 2020 On 29 December, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani held an annual summarising press-conference concerning the activity of the institution. During the presentation of the annual report the Minister talked in detail regarding the crisis caused by the pandemic. “Notwithstanding the global pandemic the Georgian Foreign Ministry continued to provide support to our citizens residing abroad 24/7, protecting their rights and interests. I would like to use this opportunity and express my gratitude towards all diplomats, consular staff for the truly selfless and significant work that they did, as a result of which 23 296 Georgian citizens residing abroad received support, with 22 862 individuals being repatriated. 342 seamen received support. Particular attention was payed to students, as with the direct involvement of the Georgian PM 78 students were repatriated from the US, with an additional 73 from Italy and 10 from Egypt” – stated Zalkaliani. According to the statement of the head of the diplomatic institution, 74 flights were chartered from 27 countries world-wide regardless of the closure of state borders. The diplomatic missions were actively engaged in this process continuing their work to this day, mobilising medical raw materials, equipment and deficit products.
