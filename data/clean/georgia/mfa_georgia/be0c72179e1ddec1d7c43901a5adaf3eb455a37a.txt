3 September 2021 Political consultations were held between the Foreign Ministries of the two countries in the capital of Slovenia, Ljubljana, on 3 September 2021. The Georgian delegation was led by Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia, and the Slovenian delegation - by the Secretary of State, Stanislav Raščan. The agenda of the political consultations included a wide range of bilateral and multilateral relations, including prospects for deepening sectoral cooperation, as well as regional and global security issues. Speaking about the current developments in the region, the sides stressed the need to consolidate the efforts of international partners. The sides also highlighted the need to intensify relations and develop cooperation between the legislatures of the two countries. They discussed the progress made by Georgia on the path to European integration and, in this regard, the importance of Slovenia's support and the need to share Slovenia's experience. Ways to bring Georgia closer to the EU, the prospect of applying for full membership in the EU in 2024 and future plans regarding the "Associated Trio" were also highlighted. The sides exchanged views over the Eastern Partnership and its future. Teimuraz Janjalia informed the Slovenian side about Georgia’s expectations for the forthcoming summit scheduled for December. The Slovenian side was briefed on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia, the ongoing Geneva talks and the Georgian government's peace policy; special focus was put on gross human rights violations in the territories occupied by the Russian Federation; the need for the partners’ consolidated position on maintaining the relevance of the issue on the international agenda was also underlined.
