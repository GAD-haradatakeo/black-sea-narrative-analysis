17 February 2018 17.02.2018, Munich – On the sidelines of the 54th Munich Security Conference in Munich. Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidzemet Dan Brouillette, Deputy Secretary of the U.S. Department of Energy. According to Dan Brouillette, Georgia is an important partner of the U.S. and the United States appreciates the efforts of the Georgian government towards the improvement of economic and investment climate. The sides reaffirmed the importance of U.S.-Georgia co-operation in terms of reforming the energy sector, ensuring energy independence and Georgia’s involvement in the international energy projects. The U.S. side welcomed Georgia’s accession to the International Energy Charter. Special note was taken of Georgia’s important role in ensuring Europe’s energy security and diversification of energy sources. The Deputy Secretary of the U.S. Department of Energy pledged Georgia support in the development of its international transit function. The sides also spoke about the American companies’ interest in Georgia, highlighting in this context the importance of the large U.S. Consortium’s participation in the construction and development of the Anaklia Deep Sea Port.
