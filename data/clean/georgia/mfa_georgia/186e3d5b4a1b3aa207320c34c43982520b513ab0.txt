10 October 2018 10.10.2018, Germany - On the sidelines of his visit to Frankfurt, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with the Special Representative of the Federal Government of Germany for East Europe, Caucasus and Central Asia,Michael Zibert. Michael Zibert congratulated David Zalkaliani on Georgia’s participation as the Guest of Honour at the 2018 Frankfurt Book Fair that provides yet another proof of close friendship between Georgia and Germany. Highlighting the tremendous importance of this event not only in terms of Georgia-Germany bilateral relations but also in view of Georgia’s European integration goals,theForeign Minister said that Georgia’s participation in the Frankfurt Book Fair will raise awareness of Georgia and its culture in Germany. Discussions focused on issues on the agenda of bilateral relations between Georgia and Germany. High appraisal was given to the German Chancellor Angela Merkel’s visit to Georgia. It was highlighted that the tasks identified during this visit will bring the co-operation between the two countries up to an even higherlevel. Special attention was paid to the importance of creating formats for regular contacts between the business communities of the two countries. Zalkaliani updated the special representative on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. Expressing his gratitude for the support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, he emphasized the importance of Germany’s role in the peaceful resolution of the conflicts. Talking pints, among others, included Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration agenda. Georgia’s progress in the implementation of its commitments under the Association Agreement and the Georgian Government’s new initiative “Roadmap for EU Integration” was highly appraised.
