15 July 2021 On 15 July 2021, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia held a meeting the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of the Federal Republic of Germany to Georgia, Hubert Knirsch. The Ambassador congratulated Teimuraz Janjalaia on his appointment to the position of Deputy Foreign Minister and wished him success in his new role. Discussions focused on a broad spectrum of close and friendly relations between Georgia and Germany, including the possibilities of exchanging high-level visits. The Deputy Foreign Minister highlighted the importance of many years’ support of Germnay as one of Georgia’s largest donors and thanked the Ambassador for the assistance Germany has rendered to Geirgia in dealing with the pandemic and its consequeces. The sides stressed the need to further strengthen trade-economic cooperation through the use of various formats. They noted with satisfaction that Georgia’s trade turnover with Germany has increased, despite the pandemic. Janjalia expressed his gratitude for Germany’s significant role in the EUMM. The sides exchanged views over Georgia’s plans to apply for EU membership in 2024, including aspects of greater physical and sectoral rapprochement with the EU and future plans of the Associated Trio. The Ambassador highlighted the importance of the German side’s position in this regard. Referring to violent attacks on civil activists and journalists on 5 July, the sides expressed the hope that the incidents will be fully investigated.
