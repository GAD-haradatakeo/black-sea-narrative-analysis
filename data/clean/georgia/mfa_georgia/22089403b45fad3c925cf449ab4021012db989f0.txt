17 May 2021 The official visit of the Georgian Vice Prime Minister, David Zalkaliani, to Kiev has begun. Along with other members of the delegation, the minister laid wreath on the “Heavenly Hundred” (Небе́сна Со́тня) memorial and the “memorial wall of Ukraine’s fallen soldiers”, honouring those who lost their lives fighting for unity of Ukraine and during the Kiev Protests. Within the framework of his visit to Kiev, the head of the Georgian Foreign Ministry is to meet with the speaker of Rada. David Zalkaliani is also to participate in the – Georgia, Ukraine, Moldova – trilateral ministerial, which shall establish the “Associated Trio”, aiming to consolidate efforts on the path towards European integration. “This trio represents associated states of the EU. We have more ambitious plans in terms of accession into the EU. This memorandum also entails cooperation in terms of political dialogue, economic collaboration, cooperation within the framework of the DCFTA and sectoral harmonisation. I believe, that these dimensions are of utmost importance for us to be able to take consistent steps. This is a rather good start. I believe our EU partners will welcome and appreciate this, for our trio to take further consistent steps as to achieve our final goal of accession.” – stated David Zalkaliani. According to the statement of the Minister of Foreign Affairs, said visit is to support further strengthening of the close coordination between the strategic partners, in terms of Georgia’s and Ukraine’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration, protecting sovereignty and territorial integrity of both states.
