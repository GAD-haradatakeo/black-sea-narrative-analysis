24 September 2020 According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, the cultural dimension and people-to-people contacts are of utmost importance and represent the basis for positive dynamics created in the bilateral format between Georgia and Azerbaijan in terms of our strategic partnership, of which there are plenty examples. “We spoke about the work we have done up to this day and steps that need to be taken for further deepening relations in this regard. For example, a newly-built Georgian secondary school was launched in September 2019in the Alibeglo village of the Kakhi district of the Republic of Azerbaijan, and I want to highlight that this was done through of President Ilham Alyiev’s direct support. The school can accommodate 250 students. Currently over 160 students attend the school. And we view this fact as a clear example of our friendship” – David Zalkaliani said at a press briefing following the meeting with his Azerbaijani colleague. The Georgian Foreign Minister also spoke about the importance of the religious service officiated in the Trinity Church in the village of Kotoklo, Kakhi district. According to him, Azerbaijan pays tremendous attention to the development of cultural-humanitarian relations with Georgia. Georgia, for its part, is oriented towards the development and strengthening of Azeri culture, language and identity. Azeri schools, cultural and educational centres are up and running across Georgia. Active cooperation is underway between the Ministries of Culture and Education of our countries.
