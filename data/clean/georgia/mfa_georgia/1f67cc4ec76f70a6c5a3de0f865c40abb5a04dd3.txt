2 August 2025 “The summer camp is one of our most successful initiatives, which we organize annually to share our warmth, love and the beauty of our homeland with young representatives of the diaspora, while also fostering friendships among them,” stated Maka Botchorishvili during her meeting with Georgian teenagers living abroad who are visiting the Shaori Youth Camp. On 2 August 2025, the Prime Minister of Georgia, Irakli Kobakhidze, together with the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Maka Botchorishvili, the Minister of Education, Science and Youth, Givi Mikanadze, and the Chair of the Parliamentary Committee on Diaspora Affairs, Irakli Zarkua, visited the Shaori Youth Camp. Representatives of the Government of Georgia met with diaspora youth and spoke about the unique opportunity the camp offers - making new friends and becoming more closely acquainted with Georgia. Maka Botchorishvili emphasized that one of the key priorities of the Georgian government is to care for young compatriots abroad and to foster strong ties with them: “Our young compatriots living abroad, as well as the Georgian diaspora in general, are an inseparable part of our homeland. Georgia strives to implement a unified, sustainable policy for the development of the diaspora and to maintain strong connections with the country,” noted Botchorishvili. The Minister informed the young participants about the Ministry of Foreign Affairs’ programs, including the annual grant program aimed at promoting the Georgian language and culture abroad through the engagement of the diaspora, as well as initiatives supporting and strengthening Georgian weekend schools in various cities around the world. The project “Shaori Summer Camp for Young People Living Abroad” is implemented through the joint efforts of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, the Ministry of Education, Science and Youth of Georgia, and the Youth Agency.
