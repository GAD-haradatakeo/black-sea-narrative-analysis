15 December 2022 On 15 December 2022, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia held a meeting with the Deputy Secretary General for Common Security and Defence of the European External Action Service, Charles Fries, who visited Georgia on a two-day working visit. At the meeting, the sides discussed the security challenges facing the region. Special emphasis was placed on the involvement of the European Union in the process of promoting peace and stability in Georgia. The First Deputy Minister spoke about the difficult human rights, security and humanitarian situation in the occupied regions of Georgia. The EU side once again emphasized its strong support for the territorial integrity of Georgia within its internationally recognized borders and the readiness to continue its active engagement, including through the functioning of the EUMM, which is the only international mechanism on the ground. The sides also noted that the effectiveness of international discussions in Geneva is extremely important. Discussions focused on prospects for deepening cooperation between Georgia and the European Union in the field of defence and security. In this context, the sides highlighted the assistance provided by the European Union to Georgia within the framework of the European Peace Facility (EPF). The EEAS Deputy Secretary General Affairs confirmed to the Georgian side that the European Union considers Georgia as a future member and emphasized the importance of continuing the reform process in the country.
