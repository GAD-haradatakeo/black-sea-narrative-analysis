17 May 2021 Within the framework of his visit to Kiev, the Georgian Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani, held a meeting with the Speaker of Rada, Dmytro Razumko. During the meeting parties noted friendly relations and the strategic partnership between Georgia and Ukraine. Satisfaction was expressed over the active and close cooperation between Georgian and Ukrainian parliaments, which mirrors to spirit of said strategic partnership. The significance of interparliamentary cooperation was particularly underscored in terms of attaining our common goals – European and Euro-Atlantic integration. The establishment of the Associated Trio was also welcomed, which comes as a logical extension of the active efforts in terms of cooperation on the path of European integration. In this regard, the parties welcomed the establishment of the parliamentary assembly of Georgia, Moldova and Ukraine, which was first hosted by Georgia in 2018. The parties also underscored the significance of active cooperation in all existing international formats. The speaker of Rada expressed gratitude towards the Georgian party for supporting Ukraine’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, especially considering the escalation along Ukraine’s borders and in Crimea.
