24 April 2018 24.04.2018, Brussels – The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili held a meeting with the Director-General of the Directorate-General for Migration and Home Affairs of the European Commission, Paraskevi Michou. The sides positively appraised close co-operation between Georgia and the European Commission in various fields, including in terms of ensuring the smooth running of the visa-free travel regime with the European Union. The Deputy Minister highlighted that the visa-free travel became reality thanks to the joint efforts and years-long reforms of the state agencies and it is of paramount importance to the Georgian people both from the practical and political standpoint. Vakhtang Makharoblishvili brought to the attention of the EU representative the measures already taken or pending for reducing the number of asylum seekers and violators of visa-free travel rules, as well as for overcoming the persisting difficulties. The Deputy Minister also spoke about the third wave of the large-scale information campaign, legislative amendments and the increasing level of co-operation with the EU agencies and their member countries. Welcoming the Georgian government’s efforts to prevent the illegal use of visa-free travel regime, Paraskevi Michou positively appraised the fruitful co-operation between the EU structures and Georgia in this regard. She also identified the problems and challenges relating to illegal migration within the EU.
