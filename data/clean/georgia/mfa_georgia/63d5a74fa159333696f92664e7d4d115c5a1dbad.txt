23 September 2025 On 23 September 2025, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia participated in a ceremonial reception dedicated to the National Day of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. At the event, Alexandre Khvtisiashvili, on behalf of the Government and people of Georgia, congratulated the Saudi people on the 95th anniversary of the Kingdom’s unification and wished them peace, prosperity and well-being. The Deputy Minister noted that relations between Georgia and Saudi Arabia have been developing dynamically in recent years. He cited as examples the first official visit of the Georgian Minister of Foreign Affairs to Saudi Arabia in December 2024, as well as the establishment of an intergovernmental coordination council, whose inaugural session is scheduled to take place in the near future. “At the beginning of last year, a memorandum of understanding was signed on the creation of the Intergovernmental Coordination Council, laying the foundation for a vital mechanism that will expand and deepen bilateral cooperation between Georgia and Saudi Arabia,” stated Alexandre Khvtisiashvili. Particular attention was given to cultural and people-to-people ties, including direct air links and increasing tourist flows. The Deputy Minister also highlighted that the participation of the Saudi delegation in the upcoming “Silk Road Forum,” to be hosted by Georgia in October, will further strengthen the partnership between the two countries. Alexandre Khvtisiashvili expressed his gratitude to the Ambassador of Saudi Arabia, Salman Abdulrahman Al-Sheikh, and the entire embassy staff for their efforts in deepening friendly relations between the two countries and expressed hope that the partnership between Georgia and Saudi Arabia will continue to grow stronger in the future.
