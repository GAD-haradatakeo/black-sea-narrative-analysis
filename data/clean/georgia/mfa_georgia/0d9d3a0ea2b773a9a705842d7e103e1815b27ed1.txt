22 March 2018 22.03.2018, Tbilisi - On 22-24 March, the delegation of the Council of Europe Committee of Minister’s Rapporteur Group on Democracy (GR-DEM) is paying a visit to Georgia. The delegation is comprised of the Permanent Representatives of Belgium, Germany, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Norway, Poland, and Sweden to the Council of Europe as well as the Director of the Council of Europe Office of the Directorate General of Programmes and the member of the Secretariat. The objective of the visit is to gather first-hand information on the current state of implementation of the Council of Europe Action Plan for Georgia for 2016-2019, its results and the possible follow-up required. On March 22, the Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze held a working lunch with the delegation of the Council of Europe Committee of Minister’s Rapporteur Group on Democracy. During the meeting, particular attention was paid to the ongoing cooperation between Georgia and the Council of Europe in the areas related to democracy, human rights and rule of law, including, to the results achieved in the frames of the Action Plan projects. Parties also discussed the situation in the occupied regions of Georgia. Among other issues, Mikheil Janelidze provided the delegation with the update on difficult human rights situation in these areas and underlined the importance of access of the CoE monitoring bodies to these regions. Besides, the Georgian side touched upon the issue of the upcoming Georgian Chairmanship of the Council of Europe Committee of Ministers for 2019-2020. In the frames of the visit, the delegation will hold the meetings with the President and the Prime Minister of Georgia, as well as with the representatives of the State agencies and the civil society.
