27 July 2018 27.07.2018, Washington - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani spoke about the violation of religious rights of Georgian citizens in the country’s two occupied regions of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali at a ministerial in Washington DC yesterday. At the international event initiated and hosted by US Vice President Mike Pence and State Secretary Mike Pompeo with the participation of more than 40 foreign ministers worldwide, Zalkaliani condemned Russia’s illegal actions in Georgia. According to David Zalkaliani, the religious rights of Georgian citizens have been blatantly violated by Russia in Georgia’s occupied regions since the Russia-Georgia 2008 war. Representatives of the Georgian church are unable to conduct services in the churches which are now under the Russian control. Georgian citizens are illegally deprived of their rights to visit the cemeteries of their ancestors on religious. The Georgian Foreign Minister called upon the international community to adequately respond to the grave violations of human rights in Abkhazia and Tskhinvali by Russia. He stated that Georgia has always been distinguished by its tolerance to religious minorities in the country and that Ensuring religious freedom is one of the priorities of the Georgian government. Foreign Minister Zalkaliani visited Washington DC on the invitation of Secretary of State Mike Pompeo to participate in an international conference on freedom of religion attended by foreign ministers from more than 20 other countries.
