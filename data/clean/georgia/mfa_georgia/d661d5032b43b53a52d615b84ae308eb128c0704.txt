22 September 2021 On 22 September 2021, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani delivered a speech at the 10th Ministerial Conference of the Community of Democracies, which took place within the framework of 76th Session of the UN General Assembly. The Ministerial Conference was attended by the Secretary General of the Community of Democracies, the UN Under-Secretary-General for Political and Peacebuilding Affairs, the President of the 76th Session of the UN General Assembly, Cote d'Ivoire, Bangladesh, Bulgaria, Guatemala, Spain, Costa Rica, Cote d'Ivoire Ministers of Foreign Affairs of Malta, Morocco, Moldova, Montenegro, Mongolia, Panama, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Greece, Slovenia, Hungary, Uruguay, Sweden, Sri Lanka, Chile, Northern Macedonia, Croatia and Georgia; Also Albania, Argentina, USA, Belgium, United Kingdom, Ecuador, El Salvador, Estonia, Japan, India, Italy, Canada, Republic of Korea, Lithuania, Mexico, Namibia, Nepal, Nigeria, Norway, Senegal, Sierra Leone, -Representatives of Leicester Foreign Ministries and NGOs. Discussions focused on threats to democratic values, including on the COVID-19 pandemic, and the role of the Community of Democracies in preventing them. In his speech, the Vice Prime Minister/Foreign Minister of Georgia, Davit Zalkaliani stressed the special importance of the international community's joint efforts, multilateral cooperation and the main goals of the Community of Democracies. The participants of the Conference highlighted the need to ensure the protection of human rights, the rule of law, good governance and access to quality education in order to ensure the democratic development of the countries. Discussions also focused on the importance of civic engagement, participation of young people, women and girls in a decision-making process, transparency, generation of innovations as part of public service delivery, and the strengthening of partnerships. David Zalkaliani also spoke about the special role of digital technologies in accelerating the achievement of the 2030 Agenda for Human Rights, Democratic Development, Combating the Pandemic and Sustainable Development. Special attention was paid to the use of new technologies by the authoritarian states and the dangers posed in this regard.
