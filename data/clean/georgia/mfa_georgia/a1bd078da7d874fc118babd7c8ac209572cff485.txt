27 September 2018 28.09.2018, New York –within the framework of the 73rd session of the UN General Assembly, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with the President of the United Nations General Assembly MaríaFernanda Espinosa Garcés. The sides gave a positive appraisal to co-operation between Georgia and the U.N. and Georgia’s efforts in the implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals. Discussions focused on the priorities of the 73rd session of the UN General Assembly. Zalkaliani said that the main focus of the session – ensuring human rights and peaceful and stable development in the world – is fully in line with the Georgian Government’s priorities, specifically in the human rights areas where Georgia has tangible and impressive achievements for which the country was included into the interregional group created under the Good Human Rights Stories (GHRS)initiative. Special note was taken of Georgia’s successful co-chairmanship of the Open Government Partnership Initiative. According to MaríaFernanda Espinosa Garcés, Georgia may become a source of inspiration for many countries in terms of its reforms carried out towards attaining the Sustainable Development Goals. Discussions also focused on the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. Special attention was paid to the UN’s important role in the peaceful resolution of the conflicts and the importance of UN institutions in the prevention of human rights violations on the ground.
