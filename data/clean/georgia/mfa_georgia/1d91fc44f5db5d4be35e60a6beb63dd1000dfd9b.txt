7 November 2022 On 7 November 2022, within the framework of the 27th Climate Change Conference in Sharm El Sheikh, the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ilia Darchiashvili met with the Minister for Europe and Foreign Affairs of France, Catherine Colonna. Discussions focused on the close relationship and fruitful Georgia-France cooperation, which is supported by the Dimitri Amilakhvari political dialogue. Special emphasis was put on the great potential of the mentioned format in terms of development of bilateral relations. The Georgian side noted with satisfaction the importance of Georgia's participation in the meeting of the European Political Community launched by the French President. The sides talked about the current developments in the region and the security challenges. Ilia Darchiashvili provided Catherine Colonna with information regarding the difficult situation in the occupied territories of Georgia. Darchiashvili thanked his French counterpart for France's strong support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. It should be noted that the Prime Minister of Georgia, Irakli Garibashvili participates in the 27th UN Climate Change Conference, which is being held in Sharm el-Sheikh, the Arab Republic of Egypt.
