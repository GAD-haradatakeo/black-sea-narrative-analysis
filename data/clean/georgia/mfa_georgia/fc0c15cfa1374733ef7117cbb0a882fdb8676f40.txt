7 December 2017 7.12.2017, Vienna - Within the framework of the 24th OSCE Ministerial Council, Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze met with his Finnish counterpart Timo Soini . Mikheil Janelidze and his Finnish counterpart discussed bilateral relations between Georgia and Finland. Highly appraising the Finnish State Minister’s visit to Georgia in November, the sides reaffirmed that such high-level meetings will contribute to the further development of co-operation between the two countries. Georgia and Finland mark the 25th anniversary of diplomatic relations and effective co-operation having been shaped between the two countries over the past 25 years, including in the area of economy, to which attests the establishment of Georgia-Finland Business Association in April of this year and the business forum hosted by Tbilisi. The two ministers highlighted Georgia’s important role in the region and the function the country has acquired by carrying out economic, democratic and political reforms. Discussions also focused on Georgia’s transit potential both in the transport and energy areas. The Finnish Foreign Minister congratulated his Georgian counterpart on the successful conduction of One belt One road international forum.
