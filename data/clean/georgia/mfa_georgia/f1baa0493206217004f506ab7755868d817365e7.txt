24 February 2020 24.02.2020 – As a member of the group ofhuman rights-mindedcountries, Georgia presented effective mechanisms for early marriage prevention as its good human rights story. Speaking at the meeting of the Group of Good Human Rights Stories, Zalkaliani highlighted that advancing the human rights protection framework remains the top priority of my Government’s agenda. „In the spirit of the 25th anniversary of the Beijing Declaration this year, Georgia remains vocal in advancing gender equality and empowerment of women and girls. In this regard, we are pleased to have an opportunity of sharing our experience in fighting child early forced marriages within the Good Human Rights Stories Initiative event here in Geneva, today. Georgia attaches particular importance to the protection of human rights of women and girls affected by conflicts and in humanitarian settings and in this regard, we actively take part in relevant initiatives“ – Zalkaliani said. In his speech, the Minister focused his attention on the steps taken by the Government towards prevention of early marriage, including on legislative amendments that criminalize early marriage. In this context, the Georgian delegation recalled the personal story of Chinara Kojaeva, which highlights the effectiveness of all State-initiated mechanisms in this regard.
