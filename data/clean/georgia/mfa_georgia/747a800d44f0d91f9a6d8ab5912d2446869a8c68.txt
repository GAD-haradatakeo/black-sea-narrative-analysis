15 September 2022 The Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia, received the Ambassador-designate of the Kingdom of Denmark to Georgia, Anne Toft Sørensen. The Danish diplomat presented copies of her credentials to the Deputy Minister, after which the sides held their first meeting. The Georgian side welcomed Denmark’s decision regarding the opening of a diplomatic representation in Tbilisi, which will bring bilateral relations to a qualitatively newer level. Teimuraz Janjalia congratulated the first resident ambassador of the Kingdom of Denmark on her appointment and wished her success in her work. Within the framework of the meeting, the sides discussed the current agenda of relations between Georgia and Denmark. While identifying new areas of cooperation, attention was focused on the importance of high-level visits and the regular political dialogue between the two Ministries of Foreign Affairs. Speaking about the European integration of Georgia, the sides emphasized the irreversibility of the reforms implemented by the country. Teimuraz Janjalia informed the Danish diplomat about the current progress in terms of fulfilling the obligations stipulated by the association agenda and the recommendations of the European Commission. Anne Toft Sørensen emphasized Denmark's unwavering support for Georgia's territorial integrity and sovereignty, as well as for its European and Euro-Atlantic integration, and expressed her readiness to contribute to the further strengthening of bilateral relations.
