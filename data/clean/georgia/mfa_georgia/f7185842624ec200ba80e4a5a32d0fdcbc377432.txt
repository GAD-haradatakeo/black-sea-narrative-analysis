12 March 2019 “State authority belongs to the nation, the Parliament exercises the authority of the nation” – an extract from the Constitution of the Georgian Constituent Assembly (Parliament (1919-1921). Congratulations on the centenary anniversary of Georgian parliamentarism! The history of the Georgian Constituent Assembly dates back 100 years, when it convened its first session on 12 March 1919. With its composition and two-year activities, the Constituent Assembly went as a unique case on the record of Georgian and even world democracy. Being composed of six parties and represented by 5 women members (including one Muslim), with the Constitution that was ahead of its time with its legal principles and due to its efforts to ensure balance of power betweencentral and local government and separation of powers between the three branches of the government and many other factors, the Constituent Assembly of the first Democratic Republic of Georgia became a visible success story of Georgian parliamentarism. We consider it as the country’s national heritage and a pillar of Georgian democracy, on which the State of Georgia - a member of civilized nations is based! Documents Similar News
