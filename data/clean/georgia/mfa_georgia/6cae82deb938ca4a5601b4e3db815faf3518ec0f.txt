19 October 2022 On 18 October 2022, within the framework of his working visit to the Republic of Lithuania, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Teimuraz Janjalia met with the Senior Foreign Policy Adviser to the President of Lithuania on Foreign Affairs, AstaSkaisgirytė. The parties discussed current issues of cooperation between Georgia and Lithuania. Special attention was paid to the deepening of the political dialogue, the activation of high-level bilateral visits and the importance of Lithuania's support for Georgia's priorities. Discussions focused on Georgia’s European integration agenda. Teimuraz Janjalia spoke about Georgia's progress in the European integration process and the implementation of the European Union’s 12 recommendations. While on the theme of Euro-Atlantic integration, the sides referred to the NATO summit scheduled for the next year, in Vilnius. Teimuraz Janjalia spoke about the progress achieved by Georgia in terms of NATO integration. The deputy minister noted Georgia's expectations regarding the summit and emphasized the importance of support of Lithuania as the host country. The sides discussed in detail the current security situation in the region, which has extremely deteriorated as a result of Russia's aggression against Ukraine.
