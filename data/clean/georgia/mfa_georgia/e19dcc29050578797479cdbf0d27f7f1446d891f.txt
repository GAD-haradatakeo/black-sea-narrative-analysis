2 July 2024 On 2 July 2024, Georgia's Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, met with Ishizuka Hideki, the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Japan to Georgia. During the meeting, they discussed the current issues on the bilateral relations agenda between Georgia and Japan, the progress achieved, and future prospects. They focused on the recent activation of cooperation between the two countries, its positive dynamics, and the further deepening of political and economic ties. Special emphasis was placed on the future prospects of economic cooperation, particularly in the areas of trade, tourism, transport, energy, and logistics. The conversation also covered regional and joint projects, highlighting the importance of establishing direct flights between Georgia and Japan to enhance people-to-people relations.
