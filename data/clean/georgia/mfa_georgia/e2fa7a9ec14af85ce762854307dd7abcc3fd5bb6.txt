14 June 2022 On 14 June 2022, the Senate of Ireland passed a resolution in support of Georgia, calling on the European Union to grant Georgia the status of an EU candidate country. The Senate supports the enlargement of the European Union by integrating the countries that are guided by European norms and values, fulfill their commitments with the EU and deploy considerable efforts to advance their European integration. It also recognizes the rapid actions done by the Government of Georgia to answer and return the EU questionnaires to Brussels. The resolution recognizes the progress demonstrated by Georgia in terms of approximation with the European Union, and underlines that it is critical that the decisions taken on Trio countries are synchronized and the Associated Trio is not split by reacting differently on granting the candidate status with full realization of the geopolitical context. Otherwise leaving any of the country behind in this process will bear a devastating effect on the society’s pro-European feelings, while giving a “green light” to Russia for unpredictable destructing actions. The Senate recognizes that over the last decades the people of Georgia have persistently displayed an overwhelming support to European integration, which have also been reflected in the continuously pro-European policies of successive respectful governments. The Resolution singles out the areas in which the Government of Georgia has successfully implemented reforms, including towards consolidating democracy, ensuring human rights protection, rule of law, media freedom and good governance as well as strengthening government transparency and judicial independence.
