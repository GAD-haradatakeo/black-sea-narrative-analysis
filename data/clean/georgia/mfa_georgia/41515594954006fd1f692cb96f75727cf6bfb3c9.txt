3 February 2021 The Georgian First DeputyForeign Minister Vakhtang Makharoblishviliheld his first meeting with the Japanese Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary to Georgia, Akira Imamura. Congratulating the Japanese ambassador on his appointment to the new position, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili wished Akira Imamura success in his diplomatic activities. Special note was taken of the Pandemic-related challenges and the need of cooperation in dealing with them. The parties discussed Georgia-Japan bilateral relations based on friendship and partnership. Speaking about the need to further deepen cooperation, Vakhtang Makharoblishvili and Akira Imamura stressed the importance of infrastructural projects and energy efficiency. Noting the two recently signed intergovernmental contracts on the development of trade and business, the Georgian party expressed its interest in attracting Japanese investments to Georgia. The Japanese Ambassador reaffirmed his country’s firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for its Euro-Atlantic aspirations. Vakhtang Makharoblishvili briefed the Japanese Ambassador on the priorities of Georgia’s foreign policy.
