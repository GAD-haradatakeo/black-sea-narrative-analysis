26 July 2018 “It is highly important that the U.S. Secretary of State made references to Georgia in his speech. We believe that Georgia will further remain on the agenda of our strategic partners and we are determined to use every visit, every meeting to this end. The mere fact that Georgia continues to be discussed on the U.S. agenda provides a clear proof that we have active relations with our strategic partner” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said when commenting on the hearing of the U.S. Secretary of State Mike Pompeo at the Senate’s Foreign Relations Committee. When speaking about Russia’s destructive activities during the Senate hearing regarding the meeting between the U.S. and Russian Presidents, the Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman, Bob Corker made a mention of the occupation of Georgia’s territories. Georgia was cited as an example by the U.S. Secretary of State as well, who spoke about the concrete actions taken by the United States to protect its allies from the threats posed by Russia, including by providing Javelin anti-tank missiles to Georgia.
