26 February 2018 26.02.2018, Geneva – “The main goal of my visit is to mobilize to a maximum extent the international community, UN member states and institutions with regard to the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia, - Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze said when commenting on his participation and his meeting with the UN Human Rights Council session and the meeting with the UN Secretary General. According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, Georgia calls for active engagement of the international community on cases of violence in the occupied regions of Georgia and access of the Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights to the occupied territories. "I have provided the UN Secretary General and members of the UN Human Rights Council with information about the death of Archil Tatunashvili and detention of our citizens. We urge for active involvement and reaction of international institutions and community. We call for access of the High Commissioner for Human Rights to the occupied territories, as it is reflected in the resolution we will present at this session. We hope that this resolution will be supported by the Council," – Mikheil Janelidze said. Documents Similar News
