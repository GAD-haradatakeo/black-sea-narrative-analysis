9 June 2022 On 9 June 2022, Brussels hosted a meeting of the 5th thematic group of the EU-Georgia Association for Economic and Sectoral Cooperation Subcommittee in Brussels, where the EU side positively assessed Georgia's progress on fulfilling Georgia's commitments under the Association Agreement and the Association Agenda, Agriculture, Regional Development, Cross-Border Cooperation and Maritime Cooperation. At the meeting, the sides assessed the progress achieved on developing a strategy for "smart specialization" in the Imereti region and also underlined the successful introduction of sanitary and phytosanitary standards, the efforts made to promote the export of Georgian products to the EU market and the agreement reached last year to open a EU market for snails produced in Georgia. Special note was also made of the efforts to develop a fishing strategy in the Black Sea and the Georgian side’s readiness to host high-level Black Sea conference in 2023. At the meeting, the EU representatives expressed their gratitude to the Georgian side for filling in the EU Special Questionnaire in an unprecedentedly short time and submitting it to the European Commission. The meeting was chaired by Davit Bujiashvili, Director of the EU Assistance Coordination and Sectoral Integration Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, and Anna Strzaska, Head of the Green Transition Division of the EU External Action Service. Representatives of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Ministry of Regional Development and Infrastructure, the Ministry of Environment and Agriculture and the Maritime Transport Agency participated in the meeting from the Georgian side, and from the EU side – representatives of the European Commission, the European External Action Service and the EU Delegation to Georgia.
