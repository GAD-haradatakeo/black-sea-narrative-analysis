22 September 2025 Within the framework of the 80th session of the United Nations General Assembly, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, delivered a speech at the high-level meeting held to mark the 30th anniversary of the Fourth World Conference on Women. Minister Botchorishvili highlighted the importance of women’s empowerment and the protection of their rights, outlining the steps undertaken by the Government of Georgia in this regard. “For Georgia, advancing women’s rights is not only a moral imperative but also a cornerstone of democratic development,” stated Minister Botchorishvili. She emphasized the impact of conflicts on the rights of women and girls. Georgia, she noted, provides a clear example, as some of its regions remain occupied by the Russian Federation, where the rights of women living in these territories are grossly violated due to the absence of international monitoring mechanisms. Despite these challenges, the Minister affirmed Georgia’s readiness to implement the “Women, Peace, and Security Agenda” and to ensure the meaningful participation of women in decision-making processes. Minister Botchorishvili also stressed the significance of economic development, which creates the conditions necessary for the full realization of human rights. She underlined that Georgia has consistently ranked among the world’s leading countries for ten consecutive years in terms of doing business, attracting investments, and creating employment opportunities, while simultaneously taking concrete steps toward building a more equitable and inclusive society, including support for women entrepreneurs. “We firmly believe that empowered and educated women equipped with the knowledge, skills, and confidence, contribute not only to the well-being of families but also to the prosperity of future generations,” Minister Botchorishvili stated. Her speech placed particular emphasis on the problem of violence against women and girls. She noted that Georgia actively addresses this issue through legislative reforms, the strengthening of institutional capacities, and close international cooperation, including the implementation of national action plans aimed at preventing violence and protecting victims.
