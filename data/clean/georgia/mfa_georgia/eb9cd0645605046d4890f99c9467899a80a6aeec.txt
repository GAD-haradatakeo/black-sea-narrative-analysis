2 February 2021 The Foreign Ministers of Georgia, Moldova and Ukraine sent a joint letter to the EU High Representative for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy/Vice-President of the European Commission, Josep Borrell, European Commissioner for Neighbourhood and Enlargement Oliver Varhelyi and the Foreign Ministers of the EU Member States regarding closer cooperation with associated partners within the framework of the Eastern Partnership Initiative. The common message of the Associated Partners, according to the letter, is – the Eastern Partnership should open to the most interested and committed partners new cooperation horzons that would bring them much closer to the EU in terms of enhanced political dialogue, greater economic and sectoral integration. “In order to remain successful and relevant, the Eastern Partnership should be better adapted to the new realities and needs of all partner countries” – reads the letter. The joint letter focuses on the need of the three Associated Partners to enhance cooperation in such areas as transport, energy, digital transformation, green economy, justice and home affairs, strategic communications, healthcare. The three ministers underscore the importance of strengthening EU’s engagement in the peaceful conflict resolution. They also highlight the need of ensuring assistance to uphold complex reforms in the Associated Partners. According to the Ministers, their appeal “serves to building a new, strategic, long-term vision that would send encouraging signal to the Associated Partners, reinvigorate the Eastern Partnership and strengthen the EU transformative role in the region”.
