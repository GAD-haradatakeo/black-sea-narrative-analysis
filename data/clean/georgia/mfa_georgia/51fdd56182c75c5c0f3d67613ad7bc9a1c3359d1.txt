5 September 2018 05.09.2018, Brussels - A meeting of the NATO-Georgia Commission at the NATO headquarters of in Brussels, at the level of permanent representatives. The meeting was dedicated to the problems relating to the Russia-Georgia conflict. The meeting was chaired by NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg. The Georgian delegation was represented by Deputy Foreign Minister David Dondua and State Minister of Georgia for Reconciliation and Civic Equality Ketevan Tsikhelashvili, Prior to the NATO-Georgia Commission, the Georgian delegation held a meeting with Deputy Secretary General of NATO Rose Gottemoeller. Discussions focused on the Georgian Government’s new peace initiative: “A step toward a better future”, Geneva International Discussions and the Georgian Government’s successful policy towards peaceful resolution of the conflict. The Georgian representatives provided the Alliance with detailed information on the situation in the occupied territories 10 years after the Russian aggression, human rights violations on the ground, Russia’s intensified military build-up, military exercises and steps taken towards the annexation of Georgian territories. The Representatives of the NATO Member States reaffirmed their firm support for Georgia’s sovereignty, territorial integrity and Euro-Atlantic integration expressing their concern over the difficult humanitarian and security situation in Georgia’s occupied territories and condemning Russia’s aggressive actions. The North Atlantic Council was unanimous in expressing its support for Georgia peace policy, including the peace initiative.
