8 December 2022 On 8 December 2022, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Vladimer Konstantinidi, participated in the event dedicated to the International Day of Migrants and addressed the audience. Speaking before the audience, the deputy minister focused on the effective management of the migration processes as one of the Georgian government’s main priorities. He emphasized the importance of orderly, safe and regular migration, which calls for a consistent and comprehensive approach. According to Konstantinidi, this approach, which is incorporated into the policy of the Government of Georgia, takes on a particularly meaningful focus in the light of current challenges and contributes to the establishment of effective migration management mechanisms. "All state institutions of Georgia are ready to contribute to the protection of human rights, including the rights of migrants. We hope that we will have more achievements in this direction in the future," Konstantinidi said. “The ultimate determination of all the state institutions of Georgia is to contribute to the protection of Human Rights, encompassing migrants’ rights, and we hope that we will further carry on with joint achievement” – Konstantinidi said. According to the deputy minister, Georgia has achieved significant results in terms of protecting the rights and legal interests of migrants. He emphasized the importance of the support of international partners and organizations, including the European Union and the United Nations, and expressed his gratitude for the fruitful and close cooperation established between the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the International Organization for Migration (IOM). The evening dedicated to the International Day of Migrants was attended by representatives of the executive and legislative authorities, the International Organization for Migration (IOM) and non-governmental organizations.
