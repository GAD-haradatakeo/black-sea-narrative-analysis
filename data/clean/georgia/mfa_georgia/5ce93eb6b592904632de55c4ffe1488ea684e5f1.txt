26 February 2026 On 26 February 2026, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, George Zurabashvili, participated in an event organized by the Embassy of France, dedicated to commemorating the military support provided in 1921 by the French National Naval Forces to the army of the First Democratic Republic of Georgia in its struggle against Bolshevik Russia. To commemorate the historical event, a memorial plaque was ceremonially unveiled at the Batumi Sea Terminal, after which the audience was addressed by the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, George Zurabashvili, the Ambassador of the French Republic to Georgia, Olivier Courteaud and the Mayor of Batumi, Giorgi Tsintsadze. The First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs highlighted the historical significance of the assistance provided by the French Naval Forces to the army and government of the First Democratic Republic of Georgia 105 years ago, emphasizing that, in this context, the security of the Black Sea continues to represent a shared interest today. George Zurabashvili added that France remains one of Georgia’s most steadfast supporters of its sovereignty and territorial integrity. Following the inauguration of the memorial plaque, participants of the event paid tribute to the memory of Georgian and French officers and sailors by laying a wreath in the sea.
