7 November 2025 Today, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Khatuna Totladze, presented the draft of Georgia’s 2025 National Report on the State of Human Rights Protection within the framework of the fourth cycle of the United Nations Universal Periodic Review (UPR) at a session of the Parliamentary Committee on Foreign Relations. The report aims to provide the UN Human Rights Council with comprehensive information on the measures undertaken by the Government of Georgia during the 2021-2025 period to improve the protection of human rights in the country. The draft report was prepared under the coordination of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, with the participation of all relevant state institutions, involving representatives of the executive, legislative, and judicial branches of government. In her remarks, the Deputy Minister focused on key actions taken during the reporting period to promote and safeguard human rights, as well as on the important strategic documents adopted in this area - notably, the National Human Rights Strategy of Georgia for 2022-2030 and the Human Rights Action Plan for 2024-2026, developed to ensure its effective implementation. Ms. Totladze emphasized that the report pays particular attention to the grave human rights situation in the Russian-occupied regions of Georgia. She noted that the occupying power continues to commit serious violations of fundamental rights, including the right to life, freedom from torture and inhuman treatment, arbitrary detention, ethnically motivated discrimination, violations of the right to education in one’s native language, property rights, the right to health, and freedom of movement, among others. It was underscored that the Russian Federation bears full responsibility for these violations as the occupying power and the state exercising effective control over the occupied territories. Representatives of the state agencies involved in the preparation of the report also attended the committee hearing. Following the presentation, the Deputy Minister and the representatives of the relevant agencies answered questions raised by Members of Parliament. In conclusion, Khatuna Totladze noted that after parliamentary discussions, the document will be finalized and subsequently submitted by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia to the United Nations Human Rights Council.
