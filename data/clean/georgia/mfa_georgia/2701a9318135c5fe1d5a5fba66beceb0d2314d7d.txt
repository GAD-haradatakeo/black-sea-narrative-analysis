17 October 2019 17.10.2019, Tbilisi – Speaking at the parliamentary session, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani highlighted the Government’s efforts to bring the bilateral paretnership with the West European countries up to a strategic level, as well as the government’s balanced policy in the region and its steps towards deepening bilateral relations with the countries of the Middle east, Africa, Asia, Pacific, Latin America and the Caribbean. In this light, Zalkaliani singled out for special mention German President Frank-Walter Steinmeier’s visit to Georgia after a pause of 22 years; German Chancellor Angela Merkel’s official and the Italian President’s first official visits to Georgia; first trilateral political consultations between Georgia, France and Germany; Georgia-UK agreement on cooperation with Georgia following Brexit. According to him, the reporting period was significant in terms of enhanced cooperation with the Nordic countries as well. In particular, the minister held a tour of Finland, Denmark and Norway, after which Norway opened its embassy in Georgia. The Minister also spoke about the steps taken towards deepening strategic cooperation with Turkey and Azerbaijan, as well as towards establishing partner relations with Armenia. Zalkaliani also underscored the importance of the first visits of the Japanese and Chinese Foreign Ministers to Georgia. He took special note of the visit of the Saudi Arabian Foreign Minister and the first official visit of the Vanuatuan Foreign Minister to Georgia, as well as the Georgian Foreign Minister’s first official visit to the Republic of Uzbekistan, which is Georgia’s important economic partner.
