4 May 2022 On 4 May 2022, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Lasha Darsalia, met with the Director of the OSCE Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights (ODIHR), Matteo Mecacci. At the meeting, the sides discussed a wide range of issues relating to democracy cooperation between the ODIHR and Georgia. Lasha Darsalia thanked the ODIHR Director for monitoring the elections in Georgia over the years, as well as for the ODIHR’s significant assistance to Georgia in strengthening its democratic institutions, gender equality and the rule of law. The sides expressed their readiness to continue active cooperation in these areas. The Director of the ODIHR focused on the ongoing legal reforms in Georgia, the significant efforts made by Georgia towards tolerance and anti-discrimination, as well as on the ODIHR's commitment to continue its involvement in and support for the country's electoral and legislative processes. The sides spoke about Georgia’s strong support and solidarity with Ukraine in the face of Russia’s ongoing large-scale aggression against Ukraine. Special mention was made of the supportive actions taken by Georgia within the framework of international organizations, and it was highlighted that in this regard, Georgia has always been on the front line. Special attention was paid to the report the OSCE Mission of Expert, drafted through the support of 45 countries, including Georgia, on alleged war crimes and crimes against humanity committed in Ukraine. During the visit, the ODIHR Director held meetings with the Prime Minister of Georgia, representatives of the Georgian parliamentary majority and minority, as well as representatives of other government agencies and representatives of non-governmental organizations.
