21 June 2018 21.06.2018, Tbilisi – “It needs to be highlighted that theU.S. Senate’s defense authorization Act for the first time offers such an extensive passage on Georgia and this confirms the firm biipartisansupport of the US Congress for Georgia. And this also points to the fact that Georgia is atop the US political agenda. It is highly important that the Act views Georgia through the prism of European security, which means that Georgia’s security is directly tied to European security and vice versa” – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani said. On 18 June 2018, U.S. Senate passed its version of the Fiscal Year 2019 defense authorization Act, (NDAA). According to the Act, Georgia, along with the Black Sea and Baltic States is referred to as an ally and partner of the United States, in the context of European security. According to David Zalkaliani, the Act openly supports Georgia’s NATO integration in the context of the Open Door Policy. The Foreign Minister said that the Act brings the Georgia-US co-operation in the defence and security areas up to a qualitatively new level and this is confirmed by both executive and legislative branches of the government. “We expect that practical steps taken by Georgia towards the strengthening of its defence capabilities will further continue” – David Zalkaliani said.
