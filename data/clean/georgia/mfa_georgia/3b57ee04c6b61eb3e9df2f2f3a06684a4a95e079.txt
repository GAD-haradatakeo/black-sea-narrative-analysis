18 January 2021 Within the framework of his visit to Spain, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, held a meeting with the Spanish Interior Minister, Fernando Grande-Marlaska, who, simultaneously, acts as the head of the special, sanitary crisis-management coordination council. Therefore, during the meeting particular attention was payed to the global pandemic and the process of its management. The parties expressed satisfaction with the start of production of the COVID-19 vaccine en masse, noting the importance of availability of the drug. The Georgian Foreign Minister presented the Spanish Interior During the meeting it was also underscored that Georgia and Spain have also developed successful cooperation in terms of policing, as the institution of police attachés proved to be bilaterally effective. The Ministers also discussed visa-free travel regulations and the effective steps taken by the Georgian government in this regard, including the legislative amendments introduced to Georgian the legal code regarding the control of departures effective from 1st of January of 2021, which was also presented during the meeting. The parties expressed readiness to support the signing of the readmission agreement between Georgia and Spain. The acceptance of Georgian driving licences in Spain was also deliberated upon, which seems more plausible due to the reforms undertaken by the Georgian Interior Ministry, namely, in terms of implementation of EU standards, equating Georgian driving license standards to those of the EU.
