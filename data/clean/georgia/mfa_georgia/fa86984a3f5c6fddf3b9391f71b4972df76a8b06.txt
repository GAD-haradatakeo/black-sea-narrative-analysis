3 March 2019 3.03.2019, Bucharest – Within the framework of his visit to Romania, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani met with the Deputy Prime Minister, Foreign Minister of Turkmenistan, Rashid Meredov. The meeting was held on the sidelines of the quadrilateral meeting of the foreign ministers of Georgia, Azerbaijan, Turkmenistan, Romania. David Zalkaliani and Rashid Meredov highly appraised the increasing level of relations between Turkmenistan and Georgia as evidenced by the involvement of the two countries in various international projects. Special note was taken of the successful co-operation within the international organizations and the increasing dynamics of high-level meetings. The ministers signed the Co-operation Action Plan for 2019-2021 that will give an additional impetus to the relations between the two countries. The ministers highlighted the participation of the two countries in international transit and energy projects. High appraisal was given to the ongoing Ministerial meeting in Romania where Bucharest Declaration will be signed and will further contribute to the development of Black Sea and Caspian Transport Route While on the theme of trade-economic relations, the two ministers expressed their interest in bringing the country’s private sector and business communities closer to each other.
