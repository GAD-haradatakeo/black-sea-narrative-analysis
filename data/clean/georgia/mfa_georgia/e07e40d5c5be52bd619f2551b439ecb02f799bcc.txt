30 October 2017 30.10.2017, Tbilisi - “What sets Georgia apart, along with its European spirit, is its strong and active civil society making an important contribution to Georgia’s European integration process” - The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze said when speaking about the presentation titled “EU-Georgia Relations and Future Perspectives” held by Georgian Center for Security and Development. The Georgian Vice Prime Minister thanked the civil society for their active engagement in the Association Agenda development process. According to Mikheil Janelidze, it was the first clear example of the civil society’s comprehensive engagement in the European integration process and of co-operation between the government and civil society. As Mikheil Janelidze undelined, Georgia already makes concrete steps in many areas identified in the Policy Paper, including towards the development of transport links, harmonization with the EU Energy Market, reforms within the the Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area, participation in the EU programmes, security, defence, etc. According to Mikheil Janelidze, visions of the Government of Georgia and civil society coincide to a large extent that is the result of well-established co-operation between them.
