24 October 2018 24.10.2018, Poland – The Sejm of the Republic of Poland adopted Resolution “on the 10thAnniversary of the Russian Federation’s Aggression against Georgia”, which the Sejm Marshal, Marek Kuchcinski passed to the Georgian Foreign Minister, Davit Zalkaliani. The Resolution condemns the cases of murder, kidnapping, and the use of torture and other acts of violence towards Georgian citizens in the occupied regions of Georgia. “Being guided by the spirit of solidarity between the counties of Poland and Georgia on the 10th anniversary of the Russia’s armed aggression on the independent Georgian state, the Sejm of the Republic of Poland wishes to commemorate these tragic events. The Sejm of the Republic of Poland recalls the first military aggression in the post-war history of Europe of a neighbouring nation on a sovereign nation and the occupation of its territory, lasting to the present day, as well as the historic visit of the late Lech Kaczyński, President of the Republic of Poland in Tbilisi on 12 August 2008, which was an unprecedented expression of support for the independent Georgia. The Sejm of the Republic of Poland is opposed to any and all actions, the aim of which is to interfere in the sovereign right of the Tbilisi government to exercise rule over the territory of Georgia, and firmly condemns murder, kidnapping, the use torture and other acts of violence towards Georgian citizens on the occupied lands” – reads the Resolution. The Minister thanked the Marshal of the Sejm for Poland’s firm support of Georgia especially since the restoration of independence following the Soviet occupation. The sides highly appraised Georgia-Poland co-operation at the parliamentary level, and especially the agreement on establishment of the parliamentary assembly of the two countries which will be signed in November, during the Sejm Marshal’s visit to Georgia.
