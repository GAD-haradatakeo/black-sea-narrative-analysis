20 October 2022 On 20 October 2022, speaking before the Parliament as part of the Minister’s Hour, the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ilia Darchiashvili, spoke about the assistance provided by Georgia to Ukraine. According to the minister, since the beginning of Russia's full-scale military aggression in Ukraine, Georgia has consistently continued to support Ukraine in both political and diplomatic, as well as humanitarian areas. "Georgia expresses its solidarity with Ukraine not only though its practical support in a bilateral format, but also through its co-authorship or co-sponsorship of resolutions, or by joining statements within the framework of all international platforms. The number of statements, declarations and resolutions Georgia has made in support for Ukraine within the international organizations since the beginning of the war exceeds 400. In particular, Georgia has supported/sponsored 55 resolutions, decisions or declarations; made 184 national statements, acceded to 139 statements of the European Union, 42 other joint statements and 34 initiatives” said Darchiashvili. It should be noted that since the beginning of the war, Georgia has been providing significant humanitarian and financial aid to Ukraine, including to more than 28,000 Ukrainians who came to Georgia under various social programs.
