27 September 2022 The Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili held a meeting with the Deputy Prosecutor of the International Criminal Court,NazhatShameem Khan. The Minister once again confirmed Georgia’s commitment to continue fruitful cooperation with the International Criminal Court. For her part, the deputy prosecutor noted that Georgia is an exemplary country in terms of cooperation with the court. At the meeting, the sides spoke about the ongoing investigation into the 2008 Russia-Georgia war case. Emphasizing the importance of the investigation, the Minister welcomed the decision of the International Criminal Court made in June of this year to issue arrest warrants for high-ranking officials of the occupation regime. As the Minister noted, this process should continue, justice should be delivered and all persons accused of committing war crimes should be brought to the International Criminal Court. The Minister also spoke about the ruling of the European Court of Human Rights on 21 January 2021, which confirmed Russia's occupation of the territories of Georgia and held Russia, as the state exercising effective control, responsible for serious violations of human rights on the ground.
