12 September 2019 12.09.2019, Uzbekistan – Within the framework of his visit to Uzbekistan, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with the First Vice Premier and Minister of Transport, Achilbay Ramatov. On behalf of the President, the First Vice Premier expressed his support for Georgia and great interest in further deepening cooperation in various fields. Special attention was paid to the need to deepen historical friendship and people-to-people contacts between the Uzbek and Georgian nations. The sides expressed their interest in increasing the destinations of direct flights and expanding cooperation in the sector of tourism. The ways to further deepen cooperation in the area of transport were also highlighted during the meeting. The Uzbek side reaffirmed its close interest in exporting goods through the use of Georgia’s transit infrastructure. Discussing the existing level of trade-economic cooperation the sides underscored the necessity to intensify bilateral work in this regard. Both delegations pointed to the need to increase trade turnover between the two countries. The Uzbek side expressed its interest in conducting visits of business delegations and business forums.
