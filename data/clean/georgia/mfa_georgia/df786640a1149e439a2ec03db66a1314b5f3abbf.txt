30 June 2021 On 30 June 2021, the Georgian Vice Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani, participated in the 43rd meeting (held online) of Foreign Ministers of BSEC states. Speaking before the participants of the meeting, the Minister positively assessed the events conducted under the chairmanship of Albania, during the coronavirus pandemic. The Minister provided his colleagues with information on the steps and measures taken by the Georgian government in the fight against the pandemic, as well as on the results achieved in terms of economic recovery. David Zalkaliani shared with his colleagues the Georgian side’s visions on the Black Sea region as a bridge between Europe and Asia. He attached priority importance to strengthening security in the region as a prerequisite to ensuring the stabile and sustainable development of the BSEC countries. The Foreign Minister discussed the difficult situation in Georgia’s occupied territories. He also emphasized the importance of resolving the regional conflicts peacefully, in line with the principles of sovereignty and territorial integrity. Zalkaliani thanked Albania for the activities carried out during the Albanian chairmanship and expressed his commitment to fruitful cooperation with BSEC countries during the BSEC chairmanship.
