4 March 2022 On March 4, 2022, the Deputy Prime Minister, Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani, had a telephone conversation with a representative of the US Senate Foreign Relations Committee, the Chairman of the Helsinki Commission, and a member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, Senator Ben Cardin. The main topic of conversation was the ongoing war in Ukraine and the security situation in the region. At the meeting, the parties reaffirmed their strong support for Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity. It was noted that Russia's aggressive actions are directed not only against Ukraine, but also undermine the international order based on the rules and principles of international law and pose a threat to regional and European and Euro-Atlantic security. During the conversation, Davit Zalkaliani focused on the difficult humanitarian situation in the Russian-occupied regions of Georgia and the difficult security environment. The Minister thanked the Senator for the strong bipartisan support of Georgia in the US Senate. For his part, Senator Cardin reaffirmed the United States' unwavering support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as in its European and Euro-Atlantic integration process. Senator Cardin welcomed Georgia's application for EU membership. It was noted that the strong strategic partnership between the United States and Georgia is based on common values ​​and interests, and stressed the importance of further deepening this partnership.
