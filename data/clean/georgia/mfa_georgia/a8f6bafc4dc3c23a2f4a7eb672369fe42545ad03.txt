7 August 2018 The Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani and the Foreign Ministers of Latvia, Lithuania and Poland, and the Vice Prime Minister of Ukraine traveled to the occupation line near village Odzisi adjacent to Tskhinvali Region. David Zalkaliani spoke about the challenges facing the people living in the occupied territories as well as internally displaced persons. He also highlighted the necessity of creating the international security arrangements on the ground and of complying with the 2008 ceasefire agreement. “I would like to thank our European colleagues for their support and solidarity. We bring the foreign delegations visiting Georgia to the occupation line to let them see for themselves the dire consequences of the occupation, to see how our communities are torn apart and how they are denied access to their agricultural lands and to education in the native language. The international community should be unanimous in taking clear steps towards de-occupation of our territories” – David Zalkaliani said. Foreign Ministers of Latvia, Lithuania and Poland, and the Vice Prime Minister of Ukraine visited Georgia on August 6-7 to commemorate the tenth anniversary of the Russo-Georgian War and reaffirm their support to Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. They participated in a round table meeting organized by the Georgian Foreign Ministry – “For Lasting Peace and Security – 10 Years since the Russia-Georgia War.” The foreign officials also traveled to the occupation line near village Odzisi adjacent to Tskhinvali Region/South Ossetia, paid tribute to fallen soldiers and held meetings with President Giorgi Margvelashvili, Prime Minister Mamuka Bakhtadze and Parliamentary Speaker Irakli Kobakhidze.
