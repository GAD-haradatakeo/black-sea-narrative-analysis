23 December 2020 During the final working panels of the ongoing Ambassadors conference (21-23 December), the participants of the event discussed in detail the issues of Georgia’s positioning on the platforms of international organisations concerning Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. It was particularly underscored by Georgia’s representatives to international organisation that on the international arena the issues of the Russo-Georgian conflict and the occupied territories of Georgia remain high on the agenda. Therefore, there is a necessity to increase pressure on Russia from international actors. The attendees also deliberated on the issue of the humanitarian situation on the ground in the occupied regions, along with the engagement of the international community in this regard. Within the framework of the panel discussion the active work to achieve international support for the Otkhozoria-Tatunashvili list was also mentioned, along with the necessity to place sanctions on those implicated in said crimes. The discussion also touched upon the Geneva negotiations and the necessary steps that are to be taken in order to ensure effective use of the aforementioned platform. Additionally, the participants of the meeting discussed the future plans and priorities, noting that it is of utmost importance to strengthen Georgia’s positions on all platforms of international relevance, by increasing Georgia’s representation in all elective structures on the global arena and active cooperation with the international community on all new and relevant issues and challenges.
