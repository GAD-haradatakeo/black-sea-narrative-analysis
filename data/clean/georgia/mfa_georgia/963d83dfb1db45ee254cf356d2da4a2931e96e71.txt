27 August 2020 The selection competition for nominees under the state grant program “Support for Diaspora Initiatives” has concluded, resulting in 45 winners from 19 countries worldwide. The award ceremony for the winners, consisting of Georgian compatriots residing abroad, will take place on 27 August 2020. The event will be attended by the Chairperson of the Parliament of Georgia, Archil Talakvadze; the Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani; the Chairperson of the Parliament’s Committee on Diaspora and Caucasus Issues, Beka Odisharia; members of the Committee; and the diaspora representatives who won the competition. Invited guests will also include representatives of international organizations, Georgian governmental agencies, and the Administration of the President of Georgia. Given the global situation caused by the widespread outbreak of the novel coronavirus (COVID-19), priority within this year’s program was given to projects aimed at assisting Georgian citizens abroad in response to the pandemic-related crisis, as well as supporting the functioning of existing diaspora organizations and associations. From 87 project proposals submitted for the 2020 competition, 70 advanced to the second stage, of which 45 winners were selected by a special commission. The commission includes representatives from the Parliament of Georgia, the Administration of the President of Georgia, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and the Ministry of Education, Science, Culture, and Sport of Georgia. Launched last year, the “Support for Diaspora Initiatives” grant program aims to assist Georgian compatriots abroad in preserving national identity and cultural heritage, promoting public diplomacy, and strengthening ties with their homeland.
