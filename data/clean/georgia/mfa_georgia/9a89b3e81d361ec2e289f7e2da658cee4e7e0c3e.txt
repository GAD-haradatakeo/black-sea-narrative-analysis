28 September 2021 On 27 September 2021, in the framework of his working visit to the Republic of Lithuania, a meeting was held between the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Teimuraz Janjalia, and the Adviser to the Prime Minister of the Republic of Lithuania on Eastern Neighbourhood and Democratic Development, Galina Vaščenkaitė. Highly appraising the strong partnership between Georgia and Lithuania, the sides discussed the progress made by Georgia on the path to European integration and the importance of Lithuania's support in this regard. The ways to bring Georgia closer to the EU, Georgia’s plans to apply for full EU membership in 2024 and prospects for the Associated Trio were also highlighted. The sides also exchanged views over the Eastern Partnership and its future. Teimuraz Janjalia informed his Lithuanian counterpart about Georgia’s expectations regarding the EaP summit scheduled for December. The Deputy Foreign Minister briefed the Lithuanian side on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia, the ongoing Geneva International Discussions and the Georgian government's peace policy. Discussions focused on gross human rights violations and the current humanitarian situation in the territories occupied by the Russian Federation, as well as on the need for the partners’consolidated position to keep the aforesaid issues high on the international agenda. The Lithuanian side positively assessed Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic integration agenda and noted that Lithuania will continue to actively support Georgia's European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations in both bilateral and international formats.
