3 September 2018 3.09.2017, Tbilisi -​ Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with European Commissioner for European Neighbourhood Policy and Enlargement Negotiations Johannes Hahn. Discussing the priorities on the EU-Georgia co-operation agenda, the sides positively appraised the progress in the implementation of the Association Agreement. The Commissioner highly appreciated the Georgian Government’s speedy measures for ensuring the smooth functioning of the visa-free travel with the countries of the Schengen area. The Minister brought to the attention of the EU delegation steps already taken towards the implementation of the EU integration roadmap. The sides also focused on the upcoming high-level meeting between the Georgian Government and the EU Collegium and on the importance of the process of preparations. Special attention was paid to the peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict. Speaking about the difficult situation in the occupied territories resulting from Russia’s destructive policy, David Zalkaliani underlined the importance of the international community’s support for ensuring that the persons included in the “Otkhozoria-Tatunashvili list” are brought to justice. David Zalkaliani also highlighted the European Union’s special role in the peaceful resolution of the conflict and in ensuring the implementation of the Georgian Government’s peace initiative “A Step towards a Better Future”. The Georgian Foreign Minister thanked the European Commisssioner for his personal contribution to the opening the the European School for the Eastern Partnership. Accoridng to him, the Euroepan School is a materialized delivery of the goals that have been set at the Eastern Partnership Summit in Brussels in November 2017 and yet another proof of the close and fruitful co-operatin between Georgia and the EU.
