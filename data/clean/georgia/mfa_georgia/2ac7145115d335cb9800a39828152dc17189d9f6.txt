9 June 2020 The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani spoke over the phone with his Lithuanian counterpart, Linas Linkevičius, at the Lithuanian side’s initiative. Talking points included the dynamics of cooperation within the EU Eastern Partnership and its future priorities on which Foreign Ministers of the EU and EaP member states will hold a video conference on 11 June. Highlighting the strategic importance of Eastern Partnership the parties put special value on the joint efforts aimed at implementing ambitious goals of Eastern Partnership. The Lithuanian Foreign Minister highly appraised the Georgian Government’s active and coordinated policy in the fight against COVID-19. Speaking on Georgia’s success in dealing with the pandemic, Zalkaliani announced the country’s plans to open up its borders for foreign tourists from 1 July. Linas Linkevičius reaffirmed Lithuania’s readiness to cooperate with Georgia as the safe country of destination. The parties also spoke about Georgia’s progress in implementing the democratic reforms. Linas Linkevičius gave high assessment to the agreement reached on 8 March between Georgia’s political forces. David Zalkaliani assured his Lithuanian colleague that the parliamentary elections will be conducted in line with democratic standards.
