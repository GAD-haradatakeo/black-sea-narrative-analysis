11 March 2021 The EU-Georgia economic and sectoral cooperation subcommittee on “Employment, Social Policy and Equal Opportunities; Public Healthcare” convened its 6th online meeting on 11 March. Director of the EU Assistance Coordination and Sectoral Integration Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia chaired the meeting from the Georgian side, whereas from the EU side, the meeting was led by the Anna Strzaska, Head of Division “European Neighbourhood Strategy and Instruments” of the European External Action Service. The EU side positively appraised steps taken by Georgia towards achieving legislative and institutional harmonization with the EU. Efforts made in the areas of employment and vocational training won special approval. The sides highly assessed amendments to the Labour Code and the Law on Vocational Training, as well as the adoption of the Employment Service Law and the new Law on Labour Inspectorate. High value was placed on the steps taken at the institutional level, including on the launch of the Employment Promotion Agency and on the expansion of the mandate of the Labour Inspectorate, fully in line with EU regulations and directives.
