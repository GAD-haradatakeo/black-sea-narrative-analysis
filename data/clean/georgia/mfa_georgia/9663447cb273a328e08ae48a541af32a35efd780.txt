2 June 2017 2.06.2017, Tokyo – Within the framework of his visit to Japan, Georgian Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze visited the Japan External Trade Organization (JETRO) where he conducted a seminar on the opportunities of Japan-Georgia economic co-operation. The meeting was attended by representatives of over 50 companies, including such leading international corporations as TOYOTA, Toshiba, Mitsubishi, Nippon Electric Company, etc. Senior Managing Director Yasuyuki Murahashi of JETRO Istanbul spoke about Georgia’s investment opportunities highlighting Georgia’s leading position in international rankings. The Minister thanked the Japanese side for its interest in Georgia’s investment environment. According to him, business projects can play an important role in Georgia-Japan bilateral relations. The Minister highlighted the examples of the Japanese companies’ successful activities in Georgia reaffirming the Government’s interest in co-operation with the Japanese companies in the sectors of infrastructure, energy and agriculture. Mikheil Janelidze spoke extensively about Georgia’s liberal business and economic environment and Georgia’s transit potential. He also underlined the priority importance of trade relations with the Asian region. He singled out for special mention the signing of free trade agreement with China and the need to sign a similar agreement with India as well. The Minister paid special attention to the importance of establishing Japanese standards in the areas of agriculture, energy and infrastructure and the need to benefit from Japanese experience in the area of high technologies and innovations.
