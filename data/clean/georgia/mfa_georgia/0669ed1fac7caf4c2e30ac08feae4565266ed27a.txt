5 March 2021 The Community of Democracies Working Group on Democracy and Technologymetonline on 4 March. The meeting was chaired by Georgia. The meeting brought together officials of the Foreign Ministries of USA, India, Canada, Lithuania, Norway, Poland, Georgia (chair of the working group), as well as representatives of various NGOs and scientific-research institutes comprising the group. Nino Shekriladze, Head of the UN Division of the International Organizations Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs chaired the meeting. Participants of the meeting discussed the steps taken by the working group for supporting democratic governance, as well as for preventing the use of technologies to the detriment of democracy and human rights. They also exchanged information on events scheduled to take place during 2021. Currently the working group unites 7 states (USA, India, Canada, Lithuania, Poland, Norway, Georgia) as well as various NGOs and scientific-research institutes, including Atlantic Council, Cyber Policy Center - Stanford University, Fundacion Multitudes, International Center for Not-for-Profit Law, Innovation for Change Network (I4C), CIVICUS, Open Government Partnership (OGP), StrategEast, Latin America and Caribbean Network for Democracy (RedLad- Spanish acronym), National Democratic Institute, International Republican Institute.
