22 March 2024 "We heard a lot of lies and unfair assessments, as if certain persons were refused registration and consular registration. This is a complete lie that has no basis. This information is not based on any fact," said Ilia Darchiashvili in response to the questions posed by the opposition MPs in the Parliament. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs does not control the opening or closure of election constituencies or precincts; instead, it assists the Central Election Commission (CEC) in election administration. Consular registration is primarily for gathering statistical data about Georgian citizens residing overseas. This information is then provided to the CEC, which makes decisions about opening additional precincts for election purposes based on that data. "- the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ilia Darchiashvili, stated this during the hearing in the interpellation format in the Parliament, where he answered the questions asked by the opposition MPs about the participation of Georgian citizens living abroad in the elections. During the hearing, Minister Ilia Darchiashvili strongly denounced what he described as false claims and unfair assessments regarding the registration and consular registration of certain individuals. He stated unequivocally that these allegations were baseless and lacked factual support. Additionally, Darchiashvili refuted the notion that it was possible to establish a diplomatic mission in any city, calling it an attempt to mislead citizens with a groundless narrative that does not align with international practices. Despite the challenges posed by such accusations from the opposition, Darchiashvili highlighted the importance of the discussion for Georgian citizens residing abroad. He emphasized that the hearing served as a reminder of the significance of every citizen exercising their constitutional right to participate in elections. During his speech in Parliament, Ilia Darchiashvili highlighted the establishment of diplomatic representations abroad during the period of the "Georgian Dream" party being in power. He mentioned that approximately 15 new embassies or general consulates were opened during their tenure. Darchiashvili emphasized that according to established rules, conventions, and the main election code, there is an obligation to open polling stations in locations where there is a significant concentration of Georgian citizens based on substantiated information.
