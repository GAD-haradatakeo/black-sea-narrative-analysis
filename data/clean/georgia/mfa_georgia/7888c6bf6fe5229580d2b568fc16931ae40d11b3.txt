2 October 2024 On 2 October 2024, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, met with the President of Frankfurt University in Germany, Professor Enrico Schleif, the Head of the University's International Department, Johannes Müller, and Georgian linguist Professor Manana Tandashvili, who works at Frankfurt University. During the meeting, the Deputy Minister expressed satisfaction with Frankfurt University's successful collaboration with partner universities in Georgia. Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili and the members of the delegation had an extensive discussion on the opportunities for the development of Kartvelian Studies. Both sides expressed a desire to continue working together on the establishment and functioning of a Kartvelian Studies Research Center at Frankfurt University, aiming to further the scientific tradition that has been shaped over more than two decades of cooperation between German and Georgian scholars. It was noted that Frankfurt University is the only educational institution in Europe that offers accredited study programs at all three levels (Bachelor's, Master's, and Doctoral) and provides the opportunity for doctoral dissertation defense. It is noteworthy that since 2007, 11 doctoral dissertations have been defended at Frankfurt University in Caucasiology and Kartvelian Studies, and from 2002 to 2024, 22 international projects have been implemented in collaboration with Georgian colleagues.
