5 February 2020 06.02.2020, Washington – Within the framework of his visit to Washington, the Georgian Foreign Minister has met with U.S. Congressman Rick Larsen. Speaking about the strategic partnership between the two countries, the parties highlighted the bipartisan support of the U.S. Congress to Georgia. Positive appraisal was given to Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic integration agenda and the U.S. firm support in this regard. As a U.S. representative to the U.S., Congressman Larsen expressed his commitment to further support Georgia’s integration with NATO. Special note was taken of Georgia’s important role in ensuring Euro-Atlantic security. The American congressman highly appraised Georgian soldiers contribution to the NATO-led peacekeeping mission in Afghanistan. Special attention was focused on the security challenges in the region and the situation in the occupied territories. Zalkaliani highlighted the U.S. role in ensuring peaceful resolution of the conflict. Conversations included all aspects of strategic partnership, including defence and security. The congressman expressed his close interest in Georgia’s growing economic potential, free trade agreements with the regional and world markets and prospects for the further development of U.S.-Georgia economic cooperation.
