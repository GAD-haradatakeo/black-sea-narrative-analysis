13 July 2018 13.07.2018, Tbilisi –According to the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani, there is no alternative to the deepening of Georgia’s co-operation with its key strategic partner – the U.S., and intensive work continues actively in all areas under the Georgia-U.S. Strategic Partnership Charter David Zalkaliani spoke about the Georgia Support Act introduced to the U.S. House of Representatives by Co-Chairmen of the Georgia Caucus Congressmen Ted Poe and Gerald Connolly on June 26. According to him, the act contains important messages for Georgia and calls for imposing sanctions on Russian actors for the commission of serious human rights abuses in the occupied Georgian territories of Abkhazia and South Ossetia; pays special attention to security and defence co-operation between Georgia and the U.S.calls on the Trade Representative to make progress toward negotiations with the Government of Georgia to enter a bilateral free trade agreement with Georgia.
