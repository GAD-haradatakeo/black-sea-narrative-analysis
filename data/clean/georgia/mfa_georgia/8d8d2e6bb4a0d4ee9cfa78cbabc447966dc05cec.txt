20 January 2021 On 20 January, the Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, Head of the Georgian delegation in the Geneva International Discussions, Lasha Darsalia held his first online meeting with the newly appointed co-chair of the Geneva International Discussions and the special representative of OSCE in the South Caucasus region, Annika Söder. Congratulating her on the appointment to this new important position, the Deputy Minister expressed the hope that during Sweden’s chairmanship, the OSCE special representative will spare no efforts to achieve tangible progress in the Geneva International Discussions. Lasha Darsalia provided Annika Söder with updated information on Russia’s recent provocations and extremely difficult human rights, humanitarian and security situation in the occupied territories. In this regard, particular emphasis was put on the illicit arrests of Georgian citizens, the so called borderisation process and free movement restrictions, ethnic discrimination and banning of medical evacuations. Particular attention was paid to the increasing pace of annexation process by Russia, as well as to the so-called programme on creation of unified social-economic space. The Deputy Minister underscored that illegally arrested Georgian citizens must be immediately released. He highlighted the need to deliver justice on the murder cases of several Georgian citizens: Davit Basharuli, Giga Otkhozoria and Archil Tatunashvili. The sides discussed the need to unconditionally restore the Gali Incident prevention and response mechanism, as well as Ergneti meetings. The necessity to achieve progress in the Geneva International Discussions, as well as in the implementation by Russia of its obligations under the 12 August 2008 ceasefire agreement was also highlighted during the meeting.
