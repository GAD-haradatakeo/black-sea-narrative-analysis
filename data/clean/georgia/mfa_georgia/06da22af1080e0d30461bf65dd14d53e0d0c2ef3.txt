24 January 2019 24.012019, Switzerland – Commenting on the World Economic Forum Annual Meetingin Davos, Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani said that the geo-political role of Georgia, as well as its function as a transit country was broadly highlighted during the meeting. “The forum brought to the fore Georgia’s economic priorities and superiorities in the world. As far as you know the transit countries face a very stiff competition for their routes and I think Georgia figures very prominently in this competition. In this context, I’d like to refer to our European and Euro-Atlantic aspirations that are directed towards strengthening stability and security in the country. We are doing our utmost to bring to the minimum the risks and challenges our country is confronted with, in order to ensure stability because stability is a guarantee for development and economic prosperity of the country” – David Zalkaliani said.
