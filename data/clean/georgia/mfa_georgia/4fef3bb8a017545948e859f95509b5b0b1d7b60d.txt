26 April 2018 26.04.2018 – The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Tbilisi Ivane Javakhishvili State University will announce a student essay competition allowing students to demonstrate their thinking on issues relating to the foreign policy of the first democratic republic of Georgia (1918-1921). The competition opening ceremony will be held at the State University, on 27 April. The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister, Mikheil Janelidze and the Rector of the State University, Giorgi Sharvashidze will deliver opening remarks. The topic of the student essay competition is the foreign policy of democratic republic of Georgia and each essay should deal with a specific area of the foreign policy, a definite event or/and public figure. The competition opens submissions for students of all the three levels of universities accredited in Georgia. Essays must be 800-1000 words, font – Sylfaen, size – 11, spacing – 1.5. Applicants should indicate their names, surnames, contact details, university, department, level, specialty. The commission comprised of representatives of the Foreign Ministry and the Tbilisi Ivane Javakhishvili State University will shortlist three essays that will be published on the websites of the Foreign Ministry, the State University and partner media agencies. The authors of winning essays will be awarded various prizes. The award ceremony will be held on 24 May. Applicants will e-mail their essays to [email protected] . Deadline for submissions: 18 May 2018 included. Within the framework of the competition, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Tbilisi Ivane Javakhishvili State University will sign a memorandum of understanding aiming to facilitate co-operation between the parties in order to raise young people’s awareness about Georgia’s foreign policy during the period from 1918 to 1921.
