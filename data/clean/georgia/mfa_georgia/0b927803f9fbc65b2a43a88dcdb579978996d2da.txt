17 April 2024 On 17 April 2024, the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Republic of Rwanda conducted their first bilateral political consultations in Kigali. Leading the Georgian delegation was Zurab Aleksidze, the Director of the Middle East and Africa Department at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, while Clementine Mukeka, the Deputy Minister and Permanent Secretary, headed the Rwandan side. The discussions during the consultations encompassed various aspects of bilateral and multilateral cooperation between Georgia and Rwanda. Both sides emphasized the significance of reciprocal high-level visits, enhancing the legal framework, and collaborating within international organizations. Special focus was placed on deepening relations in trade, economics, and education. Rwanda's Ministry of Foreign Affairs reaffirmed its strong support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. As part of the political consultations, the foreign ministries of Georgia and Rwanda signed a memorandum of understanding on political consultations, as well as a memorandum of understanding on cooperation in diplomatic education. Additionally, the Georgian delegation met with Rwanda's Minister of Trade and Industry, Jean Chrysostome Ngabitsinze , discussing measures to strengthen trade relations and maximize export-import potential. Detailed information about Georgia's economic and transit opportunities was shared, paving the way for continued collaboration in mutually beneficial areas. The delegation also engaged with Faustin Karasira, the Deputy Executive Director of the Federation of Rwandan Businessmen, exploring possibilities for cooperation between organizations in both countries.
