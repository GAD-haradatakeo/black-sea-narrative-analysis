26 June 2021 The Georgian Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs, David Zalkaliani held an official meeting with his Austrian, Lithuanian and Romanian counterparts visiting Georgia. Organized under the aegis of the EU High Representative for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy, Josep Borrell, the visit of Alexander Schallenberg, Bogdan Aurescu and Gabrielius Landsbergis conveys an important political message that the EU is actively engaged in ensuring peace and stability in the region. Discussions focused on the complicated situation in Georgia’s occupied regions, Russia’s de-facto annexation process gaining momentum, the occupation forces’ provocations, the so-called borderization, illegal detentions and kidnappings. The sides called for the EU’s high-level political engagement for immediately release of Zaza Gakheladze and all other illegal detainees. The Ministers exchanged views over the need to ensure peace and security in the Black Sea region and the Eastern Partnership. The necessity of complying with the 12 August 2008 ceasefire agreement and of the withdrawal of Russian occupation troops from the Georgian territories was also highlighted. The Ministers discussed the security climate in the South Caucasus region, including Georgia’s initiative on cooperation within the trilateral format of South Caucasian countries. The EU Foreign Ministers welcomed Georgia’s mediation role in facilitating the talks between the two countries which resulted in the release of 15 Armenian detainees. , allowing our two neighbours to take steps towards agreement. This fact became a clear example of humanitarian cooperation aimed at further contributing to regional peace and stability. The three Foreign Ministers highlighted the EU’s commitment to engage more actively in the peaceful conflict resolution process and to further contribute to cooperation between the South Caucasus countries.
