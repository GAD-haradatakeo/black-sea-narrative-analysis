30 December 2020 “Environment and Human Rights” -one of the priorities of the Georgianchairmanship of the Committee of Ministers of the Council of Europe has been officially declared as one of the CoE’s priorities. On 26 December, the Directorate General Human Rights and Rule of Law of the Council of Europe posted a statement on its official website highlighting the impact of climate change and biodiversity decline on the human rights and the necessity of cooperation among the CoE member states in this regard, which, based on the statement, is further reaffirmed by the document adopted on 27 February 2020 during the Georgian chairmanship of the Committee of Ministers of the Council of Europe and the Joint Declaration adopted on 15 May 2020 by the current and two succeeding Chairs (Greece and Germany) of OSCE.
