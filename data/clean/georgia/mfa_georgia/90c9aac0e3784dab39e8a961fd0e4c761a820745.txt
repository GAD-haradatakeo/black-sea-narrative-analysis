23 April 2018 The Georgian Deputy Foreign Minister, David Dondua participated in the Rose Roth seminar of the NATO Parliamentary Assembly entitled: “Security and Euro-Atlantic Integration in the Black Sea Region”. He spoke about Georgia’s Euro-Atlantic integration process, the security situation in the Black Sea region and the practical co-operation between NATO and Georgia. David Dondua paid special attention to the preparations for the Brussels summit and related expectations “It is important to Georgia that the Summit takes a decision that is a clear step towards membership of the Alliance. We expect the Alliance to reaffirm political support for Georgia and recognize the irreversibility of its integration with NATO. We also expect that our practical co-operation will further continue and develop, including in the light of ensuring Black Sea security” – David Dondua said. The Turkish, Bulgarian and Ukrainian ambassadors to Georgia also participated in the panel. The participants highlighted Georgia’s contribution to ensuring Euro-Atlantic security and the progress our country has achieved in terms of consolidation of democracy.
