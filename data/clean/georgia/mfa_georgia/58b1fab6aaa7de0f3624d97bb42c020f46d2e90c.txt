25 September 2025 Within the framework of the 80th Session of the United Nations General Assembly in New York, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Maka Botchorishvili, held a meeting with the Secretary General of the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE), Feridun Sinirlioğlu. The discussion focused on cooperation between Georgia and the OSCE across various areas. The parties also reviewed the situation in the region, including security challenges, and underlined the necessity of upholding the principles of international law to achieve peace, sustainable stability, and further regional development. The parties also discussed the upcoming local self-government elections scheduled to take place in Georgia on 4 October 2025. In this context, the Minister expressed concern over the OSCE/ODIHR’s decision to decline the deployment of an election observation mission to Georgia. Particular attention was devoted to the situation in Georgia’s occupied regions, with both sides reaffirming their support for the state policy of peaceful conflict resolution.
