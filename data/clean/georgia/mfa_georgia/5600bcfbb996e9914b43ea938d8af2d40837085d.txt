21 September 2022 On 21 September 2022, the second round of political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Republic of Tajikistan was held in Dushanbe, at the level of deputy ministers. The Georgian delegation was led by the Deputy Minister, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, and the Tajik delegation – by the Deputy Minister, Sodiq Imomi. The sides discussed a wide range of bilateral and multilateral cooperation, including in the political, economic and cultural-humanitarian fields, the improvement of the bilateral legal framework and reforms in the fields of sharing Georgia's experience, as well as the issues relating to cooperation within the international organizations. The sides discussed in detail the development of cooperation in the fields of trade and economy, transport, logistics and tourism. The need to exchange bilateral visits of various business delegations was also noted. The Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, informed his colleague about the severe humanitarian situation in the occupied territories of Georgia and the actions taken by the Russian Federation along the occupation line. Within the framework of his visit to Tajikistan, Alexander Khvtisiashvili also held meetings with the Deputy Head of the Georgia-Tajikistan Friendship Group, Chairman of the Socialist Party of Tajikistan, Abdukhalim Gaforov and the Chairman of the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of the Republic of Tajikistan, Jamshed Jumakhonzoda, at the Majlisi Milli (lower house) of the Republic of Tajikistan.
