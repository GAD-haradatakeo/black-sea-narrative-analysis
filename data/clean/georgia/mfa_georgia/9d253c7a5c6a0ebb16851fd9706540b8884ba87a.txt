19 October 2021 On 19 October the Ministry of Foreign Affairs hosted a meeting with the European Commission Mission to discuss with the Georgian side the prospects for the implementation of the European Economic and Investment Plan and the five flagship projects for Georgia. From the Georgian side, the meeting was chaired by Deputy Foreign Minister, Teimuraz Janjalia. The Deputy Minister of Economy and Sustainable Development, Genadi Arveladze, the Deputy Minister of Finance, Ekaterine Guntsadze and the Deputy Minister of Environment and Agriculture, Solomon Pavliashvili also attended the meeting. From the EU side, the delegation was led by the Director-General for Neighbourhood and Enlargement Negotiations, Director for the Eastern Partnership, Lawrence Meredith, and from the European side – by representatives of the Directorate-General for Neighbourhood and Enlargement Negotiations (DG NEAR), the EU Delegation to Georgia, the European Investment Bank and the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development. Discussions focused on the goals and priorities set by the European Commission in its Joint Staff Working Document: Recovery, Resilience and Reform: post 2020 Eastern Partnership Priorities, as well as on prospects for the implementation of the European Economic and Investment Plan and its 5 flagships. The sides agreed that consultations on technical modalities and tools for the implementation of specific projects will continue in the future with the active involvement of the EU Delegation to Georgia.
