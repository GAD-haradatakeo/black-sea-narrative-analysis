12 November 2018 “This year marks the 10th anniversary of the Georgian conflict and I want to express Finland’s unwavering solidarity to Georgia. Georgian People have our full support for territorial integrity and sovereignty of your country. We condemn the recent illegal installation of artificial barriers in Kareli municipality and elsewhere in the Georgian territory” - The Finnish Foreign Minister Timo Soini declared at the joint press conference in Helsinki with the Georgian Foreign Minister. The Finnish Foreign Minister highlighted the importance of the EUMM’s activities on the ground and participation of Finland in this process. According to Minister Soini, Georgia is high on the EU agenda and the EU is unanimous in its support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. The Georgian Foreign Minister expressed gratitude to his Finnish counterpart for Finland’s firm support of Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, as well as for the Foreign Minister’s clearly demonstrated support related to 10 years after the Russian-Georgian war. David Zalkaliani highlighted Finland’s important contribution to the EUMM as the only international monitoring mechanism in Georgia entrusted with an important task of avoiding escalation of the conflict and providing the international community with reliable and objective information on the security and human rights situation on the ground. The Georgian Foreign Minister once again highlighted the Georgian Government’s peace initiative “A Step towards a Better Future” aiming at sharing the benefits of Georgia’s European integration with the communities living on the other side of the occupation line.
