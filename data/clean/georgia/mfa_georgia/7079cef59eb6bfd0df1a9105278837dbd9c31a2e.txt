11 November 2022 On 10 and 11 November 2022, the NATO Partnerships and Cooperative Security Committee (PCSC) for the first time visited Georgia for a working visit. Within the framework of the visit, the PCSC met with the representatives of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, the Ministry of Defense of Georgia, civil sector and parliamentary parties. The committee visited the joint NATO-Georgia Training and Assessment Center, which is one of the successful projects of cooperation between the Alliance and Georgia within the framework of the NATO-Georgia substantial package, and visited the occupation line in the village of Odzisi. Within the scope of the visit, the sides discussed the security environment, issues related to the security of the Black Sea and Georgia's accession to NATO. Special attention was paid to the development and implementation of theset oftailored supportmeasures forGeorgia,approved at the NATO Madrid Summit. Against the backdrop of the deteriorating regional security environment, the visit of the NATO Partnerships and Cooperative Security Committee (PCSC) once again emphasizes the growing dynamics of relations between Georgia and NATO, the readiness for closer cooperation on common challenges, and confirms the importance of Georgia to the security of the Euro-Atlantic space.
