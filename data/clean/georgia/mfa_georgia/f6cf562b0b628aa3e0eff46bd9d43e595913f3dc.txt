22 April 2018 22.04.2018, Tbilisi – Tbilisi will host political consultations in an extended format between Georgia and Canada, on 23 and 24 April 2018. The Canadian delegation led by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Stephanie Beck will arrive in Georgia for this end. This visit takes on a particularly meaningful importance given the fact that Canada has assumed the presidency of the Group of Seven in 2018. The consultations from the Georgian side will be chaired by the Deputy Foreign Minister, Khatuna Totladze. During the consultations, representatives of the foreign ministries of the two countries will discuss the bilateral relations between Georgia and Canada, the situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, co-operation in the defence sector, prospects for the development of bilateral trade-economic relations between Georgia and Canada. Along with representatives of various agencies of Georgia and departments of the foreign ministry, the consultations will be attended by the Georgian and Canadian ambassadors. Following the consultations, the sides will sign a joint declaration incorporating main aspects of co-operation between Georgia and Canada. Within the framework of the visit, the Canadian delegation will hold meetings within various government agencies and will visit the occupation line to receive the information on the ground.
