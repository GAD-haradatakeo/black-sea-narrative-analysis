28 May 2018 The Georgian Vice Prime Minister, Foreign Minister Mikheil Janelidze participated in the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development – Tbilisi Implementation Conference hosted by the Georgian Parliament. Mikheil Janelidze spoke before the participants of the Conference about Georgia’s steps taken towards the sustainable development. He said that Georgia is one of the first among the UN Member States to nationalize the Sustainable Development Goals and to make them the priority of the country. According to him, the Sustainable Development Goals are directly linked with the human rights. “Georgia’s success in terms of human rights protection is unanimously recognized by our partners and ternational organizations. The EU included Georgia, along with other countries, into the list developed within the EU Good Human Rights Stories initiative, which means that Georgia will share its human rights reforms and experience with partners”. Mikheil Janelidze highlighted that the UN Sustainable Development Goals are fully in line with the Government’s four-point plan, which envisages qualitative changes in the development of the country, rapid growth of economy and improved level of welfare. “The fact that Georgia assumed the chairmanship of the Open Government Partnership (OGP) in 2017 is the recognition of the Government’s successful reforms. One of the aims of Georgia as the chair will be the promotion of the Sustainable Development Goals in the member states of the Initiative. The focus will primarily be on Goal 16. I am glad to note that Georgia will host Open Government Partnership Summit from 17 to 19 June” – the Minister said. In his address Mikheil Janelidze also paid special attention to the importance of peace and stability in the Sustaiable Development Goals implementation process.
