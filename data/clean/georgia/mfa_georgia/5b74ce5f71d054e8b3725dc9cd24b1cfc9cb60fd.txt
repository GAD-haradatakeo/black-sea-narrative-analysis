2 August 2018 02.08.2018, Tbilisi – Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Davit Zalkaliani held a farewell meeting with Head of the European Union Delegation to Georgia Janos Herman. The Minister thanked Janos Herman for his personal contribution to the further strengthening of Georgia-EU relations wishing him success in his future endeavours. The sides highlighted the progress and success Georgia has achieved over the past few years on its path to European integration. Special attention was paid to the signature and enforcement of the EU-Georgia Association Agreement, the entry into force of visa-liberalization with the EU, the accession of Georgia to the EU Energy Community, the launch of High-level Strategic Dialogue on EU-Georgia, as well as to the importance of EU assistance to Georgia. The role of Georgia as the European Union’s key partner and the successful leader within the Eastern Partnership format was highlighted. The sides discussed steps taken towards European integration. Discussions also focused on the upcoming high-level meeting scheduled for 21 November between the European Commission and the Georgian Government.
