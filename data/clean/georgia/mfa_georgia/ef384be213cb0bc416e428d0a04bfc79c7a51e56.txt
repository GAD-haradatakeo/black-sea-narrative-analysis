3 June 2020 An intermediary round of the Georgia-UKWardropStrategic Dialogue was held between the Georgian Foreign Ministry and the UKForeign and Commonwealth Office on 3 June. From the Georgian side the meeting was headed by the Director of the Department of Europe, Nino Baratashvili and from the UK side - Additional Director of the Eastern Europe and Central Asia Directorate (EECAD) Bilal Zahid. The online meeting was conducted to prepare a plenary session, through active involvement of the Georgian Embassy to Great Britain and the UK Embassy in Georgia. The parties noted with satisfaction the dynamically developing strategic partnership between the two countries both in bilateral and multilateral formats and highlighted the special importance of the Annual Wardrop Strategic Dialogue successfully covers a wide range of issues of bilateral cooperation, focusing on political, defence and security policy spheres, economic, commercial, and people-to-people dimensions. Special mention was taken of the Strategic Partnership and Cooperation Agreement signed between Georgia and the UK on 21 October 2019. The participants of the meeting exchanged views over the issues within the four pillars of the Dialogue (Political Pillar; Defence Security and Foreign Policy Pillar; Trade and Commercial Pillar; People-to-People Pillar) and agreed on key items on the agenda of the 7th round of the Dialogue scheduled for September 2020. The British side highly appraised the Georgian Government’s efforts and measures against COVID-19. For its part, the Georgian side welcomed Great Britain’s leading role in developing the vaccine. Speaking on Georgia’s plans in the post-pandemic period, the Georgian side announced the intention to open up its borders for foreign tourists from 1 July based on international recommendations and through so-called safe corridors.
