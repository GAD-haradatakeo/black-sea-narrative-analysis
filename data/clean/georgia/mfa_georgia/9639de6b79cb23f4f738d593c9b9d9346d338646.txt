22 June 2018 22.06.2018, Tbilisi – The Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani met with the Brazilian Justice Minister, Torquato Jardim. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of bilateral and multilateral cooperation. The sides noted with satisfaction an increasing number of visits and meetings between the two countries, as well as work towards the improvement of the bilateral contractual framework. David Zalkaliani briefed the Brazilian Justice Minister on the priorities of Georgia’s domestic and foreign policy. Talking points also included the situation in Georgia’s occupied regions and Georgia’s peace policy. David Zalkaliani thanked the Brazilian government for the support of Georgia’s support and territorial integrity. The sides spoke about the successful co-operation within the UN. Special attention was paid to the need to fulfil sustainable development goals and to deepen co-operation within the framework of Open Government Partnership (OGP). David Zalkaliani highlighted Georgia’s priorities as a chair of the OGP and welcomed Brazil’s plans to participate in the OGP summit scheduled to be held in Tbilisi, in July Torquato Jardim paid particular attention to the importance of providing joint efforts in the fight against organised crime and cyber security issues.
