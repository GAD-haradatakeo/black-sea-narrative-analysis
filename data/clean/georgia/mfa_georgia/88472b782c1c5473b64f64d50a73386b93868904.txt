1 November 2023 On 1 November 2023, within the framework of his visit to Helsinki, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs Teimuraz Janjalia held a meeting with the Minister of Economic Affairs and Employment of the Republic of Finland, Wille Rydman. The sides welcomed the existing cooperation between Georgia and Finland in the areas of trade, economy and tourism. The importance of the Georgian-Finnish Business Forum was emphasized in order to strengthen business ties between the countries and encourage investments. Teimuraz Janjalia spoke about Georgia's attractive investment environment, the country's economic and tourism potential, the country's geostrategic role in terms of promoting the development of Black Sea connectivity and ensuring Europe's energy security. According to the Deputy Minister, joining the European Union is a geopolitical and civilizational choice of the Georgian people, and Georgia is making maximum efforts to achieve further economic and sectoral integration in the European Union by fully utilizing the potential of the Association Agreement, including the Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area. Teimuraz Janjalia highlighted the importance of strategic projects initiated by Georgia, such as the Black Sea submarine electric cable and optical fiber cable, as well as the activation of direct ferry services on the Black Sea. The Minister of Economic Affairs and Employment of Finland, for her part, expressed her willingness to deepen trade and economic cooperation with Georgia, including within the framework of the Agreement on Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area between Georgia and the European Union.
