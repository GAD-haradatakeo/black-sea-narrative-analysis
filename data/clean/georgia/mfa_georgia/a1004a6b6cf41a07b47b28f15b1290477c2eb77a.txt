12 December 2018 12.12.2018, Tbilisi - Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with NATO Secretary General’s Special Representative for the Caucasus and Central Asia James Appathurai. The sides summarized the NATO Foreign Ministerial Meeting in Brussels and defined the NATO-Georgia Joint Action Plan for 2019. “The visit of James Appathurai following the NATO Foreign Ministerial Meeting in Brussels is yet another proof of how high NATO places its relations with Georgia. The goal of the visit is to ensure that the issues posed at the recent ministerial meeting are kept on the future agenda as well. We discussed the plans for 2019. High appraisal was given to the progress Georgia has made in terms of implementing all practical tools for membership. We also highly appraised NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg’s statements with regard to Georgia’s integration prospects. His statement that NATO continues to help Georgia prepare for membership of the Alliance is an important message indeed” – Zalkaliani said. While on the theme of plans for 2019, the Minister noted that military co-operation and joint military exercises still continue. “I believe that the future agenda of co-operation with NATO will be focused on ensuring greater involvement of NATO in Georgia. We highlighted NATO-led joint exercises. Georgia continues to make its important contribution to the operations of Resolute Support Mission and this co-operation will continue in a consistent and intensive manner in 2019 as well” – the Minister said.
