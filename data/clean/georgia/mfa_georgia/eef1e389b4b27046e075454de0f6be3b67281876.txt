27 April 2022 Within the framework of his visit to Baku, the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Ilia Darchiashvili met with his Azerbaijani counterpart, Jeyhun Bayramov, who welcomed the Georgian Foreign Minister’s visit to Baku and expressed the hope that the high-level close ties will further contribute to strengthening the traditional friendly relations between Georgia and Azerbaijan. The Ministers exchanged views on all main areas of Georgia-Azerbaijan strategic partnership. They noted with satisfaction the 30th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries and expressed their commitment to work actively for the further deepening of cooperation. Discussing the processes ongoing in the region and across the globe and taking into consideration the global security environment and challenges, the sides stressed the importance of stability and sustainable development in the South Caucasus region. The Ministers highly appraised the economic, energy, trade and transport ties between the two countries, including the Baku-Tbilisi-Ceyhan Pipeline, Baku-Tbilisi-Erzurum Gas Pipeline, Baku-Tbilisi-Kars Railway, Southern Gas Corridor Projects of international importance, which increase the attractiveness of Georgia and Azerbaijan and have great potential in terms of ensuring energy security and energy independence. The ministers expressed satisfaction with the growing trade turnover between the two countries as part of the post-pandemic economic recovery and expressed the hope that successful sectoral cooperation and regular economic forums will improve trade figures. Discussions also focused on tourism as a priority for both countries and good opportunities for cooperation in this regard. The Ministers reviewed the cultural, educational and inter-ethnic ties between Azerbaijan and Georgia and welcomed the efforts made to further deepen these ties through various projects. Ilia Darchiashvili thanked his colleague for the warm hospitality and expressed the hope that the visit will give additional impetus to the strong friendship and people-to-people contacts between the two countries.
