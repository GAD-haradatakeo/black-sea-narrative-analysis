15 February 2022 On 15 February 2022, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani met with the European Union Special Representative for the South Caucasus and the Crisis in Georgia, the Co-Chair of the Geneva International Discussions, Toivo Klaar. The sides focused on the tense security environment in the region resulting from Russia's policy and Russia's steps against the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia and Ukraine. The sides discussed the difficult security, humanitarian and human rights situation in the occupied regions of Georgia. Attention was paid to Russia's illegal and provocative actions, including Moscow's steps towards de facto annexation of Abkhazia and Tskhinvali, military build-up, installation of barbed wire fences and artificial barriers across the occupation line and human rights abuses in the occupied territories. Emphasis was placed on restriction of free movement and illegal detentions, and the need for immediate release of illegally detained Georgian citizens. The sides focused on the ongoing Geneva Discussions and stressed the need for Russia to fulfill its international obligations, including under the 12 August 2008 ceasefire agreement. The sides exchanged views on the Georgian government’s policy and efforts towards peaceful settlement of the conflict and on the whole-of-the nation inclusive process aimed to develop a state strategy for peaceful settlement of the conflict. The Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia thanked the EU Special Representative for his contribution to peaceful settlement of the Russian-Georgian conflict and stressed the importance of the EU's active involvement in promoting peace and stability in the region.
