28 May 2019 28.05.2019, Tbilisi – Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a meeting with the U.S. Congress delegation consisting of representatives of the offices of the House of Representatives and the Senate. The delegation arrived in Georgia Mutual Educational and Cultural Exchange programme (MECEA). At the meeting the Minister expressed his gratitude for the support of the U.S. Congress, namely for the approval the bipartisan Georgia Support Act by the Foreign Affairs Committee of the House of Representatives on 22 May. Paying special attention to the priorities of Georgia-U.S. partnership, the sides highlighted the importance of the U.S. support for the strengthening of Georgia’s security and defence capabilities. The Minister spoke about the positive dynamics of co-operation between Georgia and the U.S. in the areas of economy, trade and investments. Zalkaliani provided the U.S. Congress delegation with detailed information on Georgia’s success on the European and Euro-Atlantic integration path, as well as on the security climate in Georgia and the Black Sea region. Conversations also included Georgia-Russia conflict, the human rights situation on the ground and the Georgian Government’s steps and initiatives for conflict resolution.
