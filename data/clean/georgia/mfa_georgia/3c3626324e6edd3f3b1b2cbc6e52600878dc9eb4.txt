18 April 2022 The Georgian First Deputy Foreign Minister, Lasha Darsalia met with Denise Duran, Deputy Regional Director for Europe and Central Asia at the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC), on 18 April 2022. The sides discussed a broad spectrum of cooperation issues between Georgia and the ICRC. Lasha Darsalia thanked the ICRC representatives for the Organization’s humanitarian efforts in Georgia, especially for its support to the population in Georgia’s Abkhazia and Tskhinvali regions, including to the people affected by the Russia-Georgia conflict. The First Deputy Foreign Minister focused on the difficult security, human rights and humanitarian situation in Georgia’s occupied territories, which is further aggravated by the Russian Federation’s provocative actions. In this light, Lasha Darsalia highlighted the necessity of ensuring unhindered access for the international humanitarian and human rights organizations to Georgia’s occupied territories. The sides highlighted the ICRC-Georgia effective cooperation in finding and identifying missing persons and the importance of the ICRC’s further engagement in this regard.
