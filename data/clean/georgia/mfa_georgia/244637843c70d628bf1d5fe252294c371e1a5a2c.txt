25 March 2019 25.03.2019, Tbilisi - Assessing NATO Secretary GeneralJens Stoltenberg’s visit to Georgia, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of the eventful NATO-Georgia agenda. “The NATO Secretary General’s visit was highly import both in terms of timing and messages. He positively appraised the ongoing reforms in the defence sector as well as in terms of political and economic development and the strengthening of democratic institutions. NATO’s partners show strong support for Georgia, which was farther reaffirmed by the NATO Secretary General saying that Georgia will become a member of NATO as it is indicated in the decisions of the latest NATO Summit. Discussions during the visit focused on Black Sea security, which will be one of the key items on the agenda of the NATO-Georgia Commission in Brussels. The Black Sea and Georgia as the key countries of the region are under the NATO spotlight” – the Minister said. Highlighting NATO’s Open Door Policy, David Zalkaliani said that the integration with NATO is a priority of Georgia’s foreign policy strategy due to be approved by the government in the near future. The Minister also paid special attention to the importance of joint military exercises ongoing in Georgia. “The exercise held under the aegis of theNATO-GeorgiaJoint Training and Evaluation Centreis the first of its kind and no such NATO-led exercise has been conducted in Georgia before. The Secretary General’s visit, as well as the eventful agenda of NATO-Georgia relations should be viewed through the prism of the upcoming NATO Ministerial Meeting. We are perfectly aware of the path we are following and each step is coordinated with our partners. Such high-level events are not organized overnight” – David Zalkaliani said. David Zalkaliani made this comment at the Tbilisi International Airport following the official farewell ceremony for the NATO Secretary General,Jens Stoltenberg. Documents Similar News
