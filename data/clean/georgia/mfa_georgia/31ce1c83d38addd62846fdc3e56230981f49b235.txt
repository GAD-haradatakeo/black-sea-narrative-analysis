21 April 2022 On 21 April 2022, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs hosted inter-agency consultations chaired by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ilia Darchiashvili, to discuss the progress in completingthe questionnaire delivered to Georgia by the European Commission. The purpose of the meeting was to coordinate, evaluate and outline further steps to complete the self-assessment questionnaire in time. As Ilia Darchiashvili stated at the meeting, the delivery of the Self-Assessment Questionnaire to Georgia is a historic step on the country's path to European integration, and it is important that, according to the Prime Minister of Georgia, government agencies are mobilized to fill in the questionnaire effectively and in a timely manner. The Minister thanked all government agencies and civil servants working on the document for the successful management of the process. The Government Commission for EU Integration chaired by the Prime Minister of Georgia was held on 15 April 2022 where all agencies were instructed to mobilize maximum resources to fill in the questionnaire in a timely manner. The process of filling in the questionnaire is coordinated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, a number of coordination meetings were held at the expert / technical level. The EU membership self-assessment questionnaire was handed over to Georgia on 11 April, on the basis of which the European Commission will prepare a report on granting the country EU candidate status.
