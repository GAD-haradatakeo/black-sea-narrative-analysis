20 February 2020 20.02.2020,Tbilisi - the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani participated in the event to mark the National Day of Japan - Birthday of Emperor Naruhito of Japan. Congratulating the Japanese people on their national holiday and wishing them peace and prosperity, Zalkaliani spoke extensively about close relations between Japan and Georgia based on such shared values as democracy, freedom human rights and the rule of law. “Being one of the first states to recognize Georgia’s independence back in 1918, Japan continues to be a strong supporter of sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia since the re-establishment of the Diplomatic relations in 1992” – the Minister said. According to him, Georgia deeply appreciates Japan’s development assistance that plays an important role in Georgia’s sustainable and comprehensive economic growth. Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of the fruitful visits of Prime Minister and President of Georgia to Japan, as well as of the first visit of the Minister for Foreign Affairs of Japan Mr. Taro Kono to Georgia in September 2018. “Let me express delight that Tbilisi has been gifted 100 Sakura trees by Japan’s Central Federation of Societies of Commerce and Industry as a token of friendship between Japan and Georgia. Just like our relationship, these trees will grow stronger and each year when they blossom, it will remind us the deep friendship between our two nations” – Zalkaliani said. On closing, Zalkaliani expressed his gratitude to the Embassy of Japan in Georgia and personally to Ambassador Uehara and his staff for their active contribution toward strengthening Georgia- Japan bilateral relations.
