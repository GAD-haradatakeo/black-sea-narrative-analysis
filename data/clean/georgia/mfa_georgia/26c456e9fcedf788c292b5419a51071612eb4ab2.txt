28 May 2020 To mark the completion of the Georgian Presidency of the Committee of Ministers of the Council of Europe, the Ukrainian Foreign Minister Dmytro Kuleba sent a letter to his Georgian counterpart, David Zalkaliani. According to the letter, the Georgian Presidency coincided in time with the COVID-19 pandemic that called for innovative approaches and formats in the activities of the Council of Europe. “The Georgian presidency successfully led the CoE activities that is a great achievement of Georgian diplomacy” – reads the letter. According to the letter, Ukraine particularly values the Georgian Foreign Minister’s personal attention to the infringement of Ukraine’s sovereignty and territorial integrity and the violation of the Ukrainian people’s rights and fundamental freedoms. Kiev expresses its gratitude and welcomes the statement David Zalkaliani as the President of the Committee of Ministers of the Council of Europe on the 6th anniversary of the illegal annexation of the autonomous Republic of Crimea and the city of Sevastopol by the Russian Federation. According to the letter, Ukraine highly appraises Georgia’s active role in coordinating Baltic Plus countries both at the government and parliamentary levels.
