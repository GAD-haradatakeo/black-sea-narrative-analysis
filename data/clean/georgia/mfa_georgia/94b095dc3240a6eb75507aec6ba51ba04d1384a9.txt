28 July 2021 On 27 July 2021, in the framework of his working visit to the Italian Republic, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Teimuraz Janjalia met Piero Fassino, Chairman of theCommitteeonForeign and European Affairsof theItalianChamber of Deputies (Lower House of the Italian Parliament). At the meeting, the sides discussed issues of bilateral and multilateral relations. Particular attention was paid to Georgia's progress towards EU and NATO integration. Discussions also focused on the intensification of relations and development of cooperation between the legislatures of the two countries, which becomes particularly important in the context of Georgia's closer rapprochement with the EU, its plans to apply for full membership in the EU in 2024 and Associated Trio-related prospects. The importance of Italy's support for activating NATO's open door policy was also noted. Teimuraz Janjalia provided detailed information on the situation in the occupied territories of Georgia, the ongoing Geneva Discussions and the Georgian government's peace policy. The sides focused on gross human rights violations and the current humanitarian situation in the territories occupied by the Russian Federation. The Italian side reaffirmed its unwavering support for Georgia's policy of sovereignty, territorial integrity and non-recognition. The sides also discussed current developments in the region and the need to consolidate international partners’ efforts to strengthen peace and security in the region.
