17 March 2021 The NATO Secretary General released an annual report on 16 march 2021. It covers NATO’s work and achievements throughout the year, including activities carried out within the framework of NATO-Georgia cooperation. The document makes its first reference to Georgia in NATO’s Open Door Chapter. It is highlighted that Allies remain committed to supporting eventual NATO membership of Georgia, in line with the 2008 Bucharest Summit decision, and subsequent Summits. The document confirms NATO’s own commitment to its Open Door Policy, which is a founding principle of the North Atlantic Treaty and one of the Alliance’s greatest and most tangible successes throughout the years. A separate chapter in the NATO Secretary General’s annual report is dedicated to cooperation with Georgia, which, according to the document, continued under the framework of the Substantial NATO-Georgia Package, designed to strengthen Georgia’s defence capacities, including to develop a cyber lab, establish secure communications between Georgia and NATO, train military engineers, and improve maritime and airspace situational awareness. The NATO Secretary General’s annual report gives an adequate coverage of threats emanating from Russia as one of the main Euro-Atlantic security challenges.
