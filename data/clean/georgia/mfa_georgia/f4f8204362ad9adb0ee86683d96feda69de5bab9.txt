20 March 2018 20.03.2018, France – Within the framework of his visit to the French Republic, Georgian First Deputy Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held meetings with representatives of the French Foreign Ministry, in particular, with the Director of Continental Europe Directorate; Director for United Nations, International Organizations, Human Rights and Francophonie; Deputy Director General for Political Affairs and Security; Counsellor to the Minister of Foreign Affairs. The sides discussed the issues relating to bilateral co-operation between Georgia and France. The sides discussed the ways to enhance political, economic, cultural and scientific co-operation. The representatives of the French Foreign Ministry highly appraised the Georgian Government’s reforms for the strengthening of state institutions and human rights. Discussions during each meeting focused on the ways for peaceful resolution of the Russia-Georgia conflict and the Geneva International Discussions. Providing the French colleagues with information on the tragic death of Archil Tatunashvili, David Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of engaging the partner countries and international organizations in the conflict settlement process and the need to create international security and human rights monitoring mechanisms in the occupied regions. The participants of the meetigns also spoke about the issues relating to regional security and the need to expand multilateral co-operation in the face of persisting challenges.
