20 December 2018 20.12.2018, Tbilisi – Chairman of the Parliament of Georgia Irakli Kobakhidze met with Georgian ambassadors, within the framework of the Ambassadors’ Conference – 2018. Discussions focused on the Parliament’s role in the implementation of foreign policy tasks. According to the Chairman of the Parliament, the Ambassadors’ Conference coincided in time with two important events – inauguration of the president and enforcement of the new Constitution. Irakli Kobakhidze highlighted the importance of institutional strengthening of the Georgian Parliament. He also spoke about the strategic agreements signed with the legislative bodies of the partner countries. Kobakhidze paid special attention to two resolution adopted unanimously by the Parliament on “Foreign Policy Priorities of Georgia”, which once again reaffirms Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic priorities, and on “Gross Violations of Human Rights in the Occupied Abkhazia and Tskhinvali Regions and Otkhozoria Tatunashvili List”. David Zalkaliani attached paramount importance to intense co-operation between the executive and legislative authorities of Georgia and coordination to achieve key foreign political goals.
