21 December 2018 Denmark positively appraises the fact that the number of asylum applications put forward by Georgian citizens has dropped – reads the letter of the Danish Minister for Immigration, Integration and Housing Inger Støjberg to the Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani. The Danish Minister for Immigration commends the Georgian authorities for the seriousness with which they have dealt with the challenges that have risen, since the visa liberalization deal entered into force. Denmark highly appraises “many measures you have implemented and are about to implement, such as your information campaigns, legislative changes and strengthened law enforcement cooperation. All with the purpose to limit and prevent the abuse of Georgian citizens’ visa freedom to the Schengen area” – reads the letter. Inger Støjberg is pleased by the fact that Georgian authorities greatly cooperate in the facilitation and fast return of Georgian citizens with no legal stay in Denmark. Documents Similar News
