7 November 2023 On 6 November 2023, the seventh round of political consultations between the Ministries of Foreign Affairs of Georgia and the Republic of India was held in New Delhi. The consultations were led by the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili, from the Georgian side, and by Shri Sanjay Verma, Secretary [West], Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India - from the Indian side. Within the framework of consultations, the sides discussed a wide range of topics of common interest between Georgia and India. Special emphasis was placed on the need for high-level exchange visits, as well as on deepening cooperation in the fields of trade and investment, energy, transport, tourism, innovation and information technology, agriculture, culture and education. The sides also emphasized the traditionally friendly ties and interaction between Georgia and India in both bilateral and multilateral formats. Once again, the historical importance of the visit of Indian Foreign Minister Subrahmanyam Jaishankar to Tbilisi in 2021 and the arrival of the holy part of the Great Martyr Queen Ketevan in Georgia was highlighted. The sides exchanged views on the current processes and security environment in different regions of the world. The Indian side expressed its strong support for the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia.
