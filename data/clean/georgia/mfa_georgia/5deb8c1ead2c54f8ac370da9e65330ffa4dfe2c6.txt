18 April 2024 On 18 April 2024, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, Vladimer Konstantinidi took part in the joint symposium hosted by the Shota Rustaveli National Science Foundation and the Francophonie University Agency titled "Scientific Diplomacy for Peace and Development." During his address to the audience, Konstantinidi emphasized the significant role of Georgian scientists residing abroad in bolstering international cooperation and internationalization efforts. He particularly highlighted the state grant programme initiated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to support diaspora-driven initiatives, which annually supports numerous projects. Konstantinidi focused on the vital contribution of the Georgian diaspora as a valuable human resource, especially concerning Georgia's European integration and the development of a Western-oriented state. He highlighted the primary concept behind the annual Diaspora Awards, noting its purpose to honor the achievements of compatriots who excel in their respective countries, thereby enhancing Georgia's image, culture, and sports globally. In conclusion, the Deputy Minister extended best wishes to the symposium attendees and expressed the Ministry of Foreign Affairs' full commitment to executing results-oriented initiatives.
