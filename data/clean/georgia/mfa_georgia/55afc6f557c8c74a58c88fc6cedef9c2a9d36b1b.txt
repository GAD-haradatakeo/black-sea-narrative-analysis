17 October 2019 17.10.2019, Tbilisi – According to David Zalkaliani, the reporting period was eventful in terms of bilateral meetings and visits and Georgia continued to effectively use all instrument of NATO integration. The Minister paid special attention to the NAC’s fifth visit to Batumi, which was highly important in terms of deepening both political and practical cooperation between Georgia and NATO. Besides, the NAC’s visit prior to the London Leaders’ Meeting scheduled for December is a clear indicator that Georgia as an aspirant country is high on the Alliance’s agenda and is a reliable and valuable partner of NATO. In the words of the Minister, the NATO Summit in Brussels where the NATO Heads of State held a meeting with Georgia and Ukraine. Following the meeting, the NATO-Georgia Commission adopted a declaration affirming NATO’s strong support for Georgia’s sovereignty, territorial integrity and democratic development. The Summit’s final document also highlights the Alliance’s political support for Georgia in terms of the Open Door Policy and reaffirms a decision to deepen NATO-Georgia practical cooperation towards strengthening Black Sea security.
