22 July 2022 On 22 July 2022, the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, Aleksandre Khvtisiashvili held a meeting with the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Japan to Georgia, Imamura Akira. Within the framework of the meeting, the sides reviewed current issues on the agenda of bilateral relations between Georgia and Japan. Attention was focused on the positive dynamics of cooperation and the need to further deepen it in all possible directions, especially on cooperation within the international organizations. The sides highlighted the importance of using the economic potential of the two countries to the maximum possible extent. The Ambassador reaffirmed Japan's steadfast support for Georgia's sovereignty and territorial integrity. Referring to the 30th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between Georgia and Japan, which is celebrated this year, the sides highlighted the importance of exchanging congratulatory letters and holding events dedicated to this date.
