7 August 2018 The Foreign Ministers of Latvia, Lithuania and Poland, and the Vice Prime Minister of Ukraine left Georgia. Attending the farewell ceremony at the airport, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani thanked Latvia’s Edgars Rinkēvičs, Lithuania’s Linas Linkevičius, Poland’s Jacek Czaputowicz and Ukraine’s Pavlo Rozenko for their support of Georgia. According to him, their visit ten years after the Russia-Georgia, as well as 10 years ago, is a clear demonstration of solidarity with Georgia. Foreign Ministers of Latvia, Lithuania and Poland, and the Vice Prime Minister of Ukraine visited Georgia on 6-7 Augustto commemorate the tenth anniversary of the Russo-Georgian War and reaffirm their support to Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. They participated in a round table meeting organized by the Georgian Foreign Ministry – “For Lasting Peace and Security – 10 Years since the Russia-Georgia War.” The members of the delegationalso traveled to the occupation line near village Odzisi adjacent to Tskhinvali Region/South Ossetia, paid tribute to fallen soldiers and held meetings with President Giorgi Margvelashvili, Prime Minister Mamuka Bakhtadze and Parliamentary Speaker Irakli Kobakhidze.
