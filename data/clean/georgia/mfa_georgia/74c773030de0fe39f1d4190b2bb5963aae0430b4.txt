15 October 2018 15.10.2018, Luxembourg - The Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani held a meeting with theState Secretaryof the Ministryof Foreign Affairs ofRomania, Dan Neculăescu, within the framework of the Eastern Partnership Ministerial Meeting. Speaking about the close partnership and dynamically developing relations between Georgia and Romania, the sides underlined the 2019 marks the 300th anniversary of relations between Georgia and Romania. Highlighting Romania’s particular support for Georgia’s sovereignty and territorial integrity, the Georgian Foreign Minister thanked Dan Neculăescu for Romania’s statements in support for Georgia 10 years after the Russia-Georgia war. Discussions focused on Romania’s upcoming Presidency of the EU Council (from January to June 2019). The sides pledged that Georgia and Eastern Partnership will remain on the priority list of the Eastern Partnership. The sides exchanged views over the increasing number of visits that facilitate not only political but also sectoral relations between Georgia and Romania. David Zalkaliani welcomed Vice Prime Minister Ana Birchall’s upcoming visit to Tbilisi to attend the Ambassadorial Meeting. Special attention during the meeting was paid to the vast potential of Georgia-Romania co-operation within the framework of the Black Sea region and the involvement of the two countries in international transport projects.
