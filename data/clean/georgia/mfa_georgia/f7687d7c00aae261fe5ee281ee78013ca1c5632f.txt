12 April 2024 On 12 April 2024, Lasha Darsalia, the First Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs, participated in an event commemorating NATO's 75th anniversary and delivered a speech. During his address, Darsalia highlighted NATO's crucial role in upholding the security and stability of its member states, underscoring the Alliance's remarkable unity in addressing escalating security challenges. He particularly emphasized NATO's "Open Door Policy" as instrumental in spreading freedom and democracy across Europe, viewing it as a significant driver for the modernization and progress of aspiring member countries. Darsalia extensively discussed Georgia's strides toward Euro-Atlantic integration, highlighting NATO's support in significantly enhancing Georgia's Defence Forces and its participation as the first non-member ally in the "Sea Guardian" operation. Towards the end of his speech, Darsalia stressed the significance of Georgia's EU candidate status, expressing optimism about its positive impact on the country's NATO integration.
