23 February 2021 On 22 February the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani spoke before the 46th UN Human Rights Council, in a high-level framework, which was held online. David Zalkaliani made particular emphasis in his speech over the dire humanitarian situation in Georgia’s regions occupied by Russia. The Minister underscored that despite the direct calls of the resolution of the Human Rights council and numerous efforts exerted on behalf of the high commissionaire, the apparatus of the high commissionaire still is unable to entre Georgia’s occupied regions, when there is a deteriorating humanitarian situation, with fundamental rights of ethnic Georgians coming under violation day after day. It was noted that considering the increased ethnic discrimination in both regions, along with the violations of such fundamental human rights as the right to life, freedom, property and health, and illicit arrests and kidnappings, with the prohibition of use of the mother tongue for ethnic Georgians remaining in said regions, the local population lacks basic, vital human rights. David Zalkaliani underscored that Russia’s occupational regime continues erecting barbed wire fences across Georgian territory, along with other barriers being constructed along the occupation line. So called check-points (passage points) have also been closed, which further escalated the violations of human rights to free movement, limiting access of ambulances. This has, in turn, further exacerbated the situation on the ground. Additionally, the occupational forces continue their destructive actions, not allowing the Government of Georgia to support its citizens residing across the occupation line, especially as there is a global pandemic taking place.
