4 September 2018 04.09.2018, Tbilisi - “Japan regards Georgia as an important partner, we share fundamental values such as freedom, democracy, human rights and the rule of law” - Minister for Foreign Affairs of Japan Taro Kono said at the joint press conference following his meeting with his Georgian counterpart, Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani. Taro Kono highly appraised the ongoing bilateral relations between the two countries. According to him, the two ministers discussed the wayshow Japan and Georgia can cooperate more closely to advance their bilateral relations. In this regard he made special mention of Japan’s Caucasus initiative under which important agreements have been reached on simplification of visa requirements for Georgian citizens to promote people-to-people contacts and deepen relations between the two countries; on assistance for human resource development for state building and support for infrastructure and business environment improvement. “Japan plans to cooperate with Georgia in fostering young diplomats, lawyers and other experts who are key players in state building through people to people exchanges. Japan has also decided to ease visa requirements for nationals of Georgia,” underlined Taro Kono and stressed that his first visit to Georgia will strengthen relations between the two countries. He also said that an agreement concerning a bilateral investment treaty between Japan and Georgia had been reached between Georgia and Japan. According to Taro Kono, the Government of Japan and the Government of Georgia signed an agreement on the East-West Highway Development Project (second phase). Based on the Agreement, Japan acting through the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) will allocate about $343 million USD (38.735.000.000 JPY) that will be spent on constructing the 14km-long Shorapani-Argveti section of the East-West Highway. .
