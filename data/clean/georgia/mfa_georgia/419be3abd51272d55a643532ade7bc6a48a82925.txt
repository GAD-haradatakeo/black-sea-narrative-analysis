7 May 2020 On 7 May, Georgian Foreign Minister David Zalkaliani held a telephone conversation with his Polish counterpart, JacekCzaputowicz, at the latter’s initiative. The Polish side highly appraised the Georgian Government’s effective measures against coronavirus. JacekCzaputowicz reaffirmed Poland’s firm support for Georgia’s European and Euro-Atlantic integration, as well as for the country’s sovereignty and territorial integrity within its internationally recognized borders. He condemned Russia’s destructive policy against Georgia and the fact that Russia continues installing barbed wire fences and dividing lines even amid the pandemic. While on the theme of relations with the EU, the sides reaffirmed that they remain unequivocally committed towards the Eastern Partnership, as the EU’s strategic and ambitious initiative. The two ministers highlighted the importance of the next Eastern Partnership Summit. The sides also focused on the need to deepen bilateral cooperation with special focus on the importance of supporting the pandemic-impacted economy and tourism sector. David Zalkaliani briefed his Polish counterpart on the Prime Minister’s anti-crisis plan on tourism. He thanked JacekCzaputowicz for the Polish Airlines’ [LOT] performed flight to bring Georgian citizens home. Zalkaliani expressed his readiness to open up its tourism relations with Poland again.
