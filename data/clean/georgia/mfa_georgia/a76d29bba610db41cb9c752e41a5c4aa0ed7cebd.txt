28 March 2022 On 28 March 2022, the Vice Prime Minister/Minister of Foreign Affairs of Georgia, David Zalkaliani and the Chairman of the Parliament of Georgia, Shalva Papuashvili participated in a working meeting organized by the Committee on Diaspora Affairs of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Parliament of Georgia. It was noted that the Georgian diplomatic representations and consular establishments in Ukraine, as well as in neighboring countries (Poland, Moldova, Romania, Slovakia, Hungary, Turkey) continue to work in an emergency mode to provide emergency assistance to Georgian citizens. According to the Minister, the Embassy of Georgia in Ukraine and the Consulate-General in Odessa made enormous efforts to repatriate and assist Georgian citizens in Ukraine. The Minister also noted the importance of the Georgian Government’s decision to admit Ukrainian children to Georgian schools and kindergartens free of charge. According to David Zalkaliani, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs continues to work actively on ensuring the safe return of Georgian citizens to their country. David Zalkaliani thanked the diaspora organizations and Georgian citizens living abroad for their cooperation and demonstration of high responsibility in the face of unprecedented challenges.
