12 November 2018 During his visit to Finland, the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani paid a visit in the European Centre of Excellence for Countering Hybrid Threats (Hybrid CoE). At the meeting the representatives of the Hybrid CoE introduced to the Minister the activities and structure of the Center and discussed the challenges of the modern world and the hybrid threats as one of the active and aggressive directions. The possibilities of cooperation in the field of analysis, prevention and sharing of hybrid threats were discussed. The Foreign Minister noted that in 2008 Georgia experienced large-scale hybrid attack from the Russian Federation and today Russia still continues anti-western propaganda in Georgia. Minister Zalkaliani highlighted the importance of co-operation with European and US partners in the fight against European and U.S. partners. According to Minister, Georgia attaches great importance to raising public awareness on Hybrid threats. In this respect, Minister Zalkaliani highlighted the activities of the Information Centre on NATO and EU and a good experience of co-operation with the United States. In this context, discussions focused on the ways of co-operation between the Hybrid CoE and the Information Centre on NATO and EU. It was noted that the Hybrid CoE is currently working on the elaboration of concept envisaging co-operation with non-member states of NATO and EU, including active relations with Georgia. The European Centre of Excellence for Countering Hybrid Threats (Hybrid CoE) was launched in October 2017, initiated by Finland. Hybrid CoE is an international hub for practitioners and experts.
