26 February 2019 26.02.2019,Geneva - The Director-General of the World Trade Organization,RobertoAzevêdoconsiders Georgia’s upgrading in Fitch rating as the recognition of the progress the country has made. “We started by congratulating Georgia with the recent developments, particularly with the upgrading of the rating of Fitch that is a recognition of the economic progress that has been achieved in Georgia where there is more economic stability, and the upgrading of the rating reflects all kinds of reforms, economic openness that we see. Georgia is the number sixth in the World Bank’s ranking - ease of doing business - that is the extraordinary achievement and that is also very important for the sustainable development we are all looking for” - RobertoAzevêdosaid follow his meeting with the Georgian Foreign Minister, David Zalkaliani. According to the WTO Director General, it is very important that Georgia enjoys favourable business environment. “In today’s integrated economy it is extremely important to have a global environment that is conducive to business, that is predictable, that is easy for companies to integrate and set up global productive chains” – he said. In the words of RobertoAzevêdo, he had a very good conversation with the Georgian Foreign Minister. “I am sure that we will be working even closer now discussed the ways where the WTO can help even more we the discussed the possibility of the outreach of activities of WTO in Georgia and also receiving experts and officials from Georgia in WTO and have even closer dialogue and cooperation” – he added.
