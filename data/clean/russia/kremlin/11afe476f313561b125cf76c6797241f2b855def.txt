Events Greetings on the opening of the Russia-Kazakhstan high-level conference, Sustainable Development and Security in the Eurasian Space Vladimir Putin sent greetings to the participants and guests of the Russia-Kazakhstan high-level conference titled Sustainable Development and Security in the Eurasian Space, which opened in Astana and is dedicated to the 80 th anniversary of the Moscow State Institute of International Relations (University) of the Russian Foreign Ministry. September 30, 2024 10:20 The message reads, in part: “We greatly value the initiative to host this conference, proposed by President of the Republic of Kazakhstan Kassym-Jomart Tokayev, an alumnus of MGIMO. Open and informal dialogue among representatives from government bodies, academia, business, and civil society in our two countries undoubtedly creates a valuable opportunity to discuss various aspects of the evolving situation on the Eurasian continent, as well as to explore different areas of multilateral cooperation and integration in this expansive region. The conference agenda covers important topics such as energy, transport, and logistics infrastructure development, alongside issues related to climate change and environmental protection. A dedicated session will focus on the prospects for expanding cooperation in education and cultural exchange, including enhancing collaboration between our countries’ universities. In this regard, addressing the planned opening of a MGIMO branch in Kazakhstan is particularly significant. I am confident that your discussions will be fruitful and constructive, and that the proposals and initiatives presented will be implemented effectively.” Publication status Published in section: News Publication date: September 30, 2024, 10:20 Direct link: en.kremlin.ru/d/75213 Text version
