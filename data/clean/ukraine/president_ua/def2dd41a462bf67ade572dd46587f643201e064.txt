Formula for heads of diplomatic missions accredited in Türkiye 1 September 2023 - 16:56 Head of the Office of the President of Ukraine Andriy Yermak took part in a video briefing on the implementation of President Volodymyr Zelenskyy's Peace Formula for heads of diplomatic missions accredited in Türkiye. The event was organized by the Embassy of Ukraine in the Republic of Türkiye. The briefing was attended by the heads of diplomatic missions of 30 countries, including the Group of Seven, the EU and the Global South. Andriy Yermak expressed gratitude to the partner countries for their solidarity with Ukraine and urged them to continue coordinated efforts to bring victory closer. The Head of the President's Office emphasized that Russia has not abandoned its aggressive plans to destroy Ukraine, international institutions and the world order. He informed the participants of the briefing about the levels of implementation of the Peace Formula, noting the important role of regular consultations at the level of ambassadors of partner countries accredited in Ukraine, as well as successful and constructive meetings in the format of national security advisors. "The coordinated work of international partners will bring the end of the war closer. We welcome the new partners joining the Peace Formula," said Andriy Yermak. The leadership role of Türkiye and the active participation of the Turkish leadership in ensuring food security were emphasized. "We understand better than anyone the importance of helping countries suffering from the consequences of the war unleashed by Russia. Ukraine is ready to continue supplying its products, but Russia continues to destroy the food infrastructure of one of the key regions for food security - the Black Sea. Joint efforts should be aimed at preventing Russia from using famine to achieve its own goals," the Head of the Presidential Office emphasized. It was emphasized that the Ukrainian Peace Formula is universal in nature and provides, among other things, mechanisms for ensuring stability and security in world food markets in the interests of the global community.
