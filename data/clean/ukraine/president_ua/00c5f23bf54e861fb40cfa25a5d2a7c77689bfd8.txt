The group is 100% composed of volunteers and interested parties, and has expanded into a large amount of related projects for saving online and digital history. History is littered with hundreds of conflicts over the future of a community, group, location or business that were "resolved" when one of the parties stepped ahead and destroyed what was there. With the original point of contention destroyed, the debates would fall to the wayside. Archive Team believes that by duplicated condemned data, the conversation and debate can continue, as well as the richness and insight gained by keeping the materials. Our projects have ranged in size from a single volunteer downloading the data to a small-but-critical site, to over 100 volunteers stepping forward to acquire terabytes of user-created data to save for future generations. The main site for Archive Team is at archiveteam.org and contains up to the date information on various projects, manifestos, plans and walkthroughs. This collection contains the output of many Archive Team projects, both ongoing and completed. Thanks to the generous providing of disk space by the Internet Archive, multi-terabyte datasets can be made available, as well as in use by the Wayback Machine , providing a path back to lost websites and work. Our collection has grown to the point of having sub-collections for the type of data we acquire. If you are seeking to browse the contents of these collections, the Wayback Machine is the best first stop. Otherwise, you are free to dig into the stacks to see what you may find. The Archive Team Panic Downloads are full pulldowns of currently extant websites, meant to serve as emergency backups for needed sites that are in danger of closing, or which will be missed dearly if suddenly lost due to hard drive crashes or server failures. a meeting chaired by the President 14 July 2020 - 13:57 Four additional hospitals are involved in working with COVID-19 patients in Zakarpattia to help with the admission of the infected. This was announced by Chief medical officer of Ukraine Viktor Lyashko in response to a question from the President of Ukraine at a traditional conference call chaired by Volodymyr Zelenskyy. "It is necessary to provide funding for these hospitals from the National Health Service of Ukraine. If there is already funding and provision of a sufficient number of beds for patients, then it is necessary to report not only to me - it is necessary to tell people in Zakarpattia about it, so that they do not worry that something is missing. The government must always be with the people," the Head of State stressed. In response, Viktor Lyashko informed that there are enough beds for patients with COVID-19 in Zakarpattia, the situation is stabilizing. Prime Minister of Ukraine Denys Shmyhal noted the stabilization of the situation with coronavirus infection in Ukraine and added that today there were more people who had recovered (651 people) than those who had fallen ill (638 people). In addition, out of 2,635 ventilators in first-line hospitals (247 institutions), only 153 units (5.8%) are involved. In 375 health care facilities, 28,732 beds were prepared for the treatment of patients with COVID-19.
