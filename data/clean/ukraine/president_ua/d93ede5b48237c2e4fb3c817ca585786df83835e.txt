The group is 100% composed of volunteers and interested parties, and has expanded into a large amount of related projects for saving online and digital history. History is littered with hundreds of conflicts over the future of a community, group, location or business that were "resolved" when one of the parties stepped ahead and destroyed what was there. With the original point of contention destroyed, the debates would fall to the wayside. Archive Team believes that by duplicated condemned data, the conversation and debate can continue, as well as the richness and insight gained by keeping the materials. Our projects have ranged in size from a single volunteer downloading the data to a small-but-critical site, to over 100 volunteers stepping forward to acquire terabytes of user-created data to save for future generations. The main site for Archive Team is at archiveteam.org and contains up to the date information on various projects, manifestos, plans and walkthroughs. This collection contains the output of many Archive Team projects, both ongoing and completed. Thanks to the generous providing of disk space by the Internet Archive, multi-terabyte datasets can be made available, as well as in use by the Wayback Machine , providing a path back to lost websites and work. Our collection has grown to the point of having sub-collections for the type of data we acquire. If you are seeking to browse the contents of these collections, the Wayback Machine is the best first stop. Otherwise, you are free to dig into the stacks to see what you may find. The Archive Team Panic Downloads are full pulldowns of currently extant websites, meant to serve as emergency backups for needed sites that are in danger of closing, or which will be missed dearly if suddenly lost due to hard drive crashes or server failures. At the invitation of Chief Foreign Policy Advisor to the President of Lithuania Asta Skaisgirytė, he took part in the annual meeting of advisors to the presidents of Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Poland and Ukraine, delivering a keynote speech on the topic: Supporting Ukraine – what is on the list for 2023 and beyond? During the meeting, the Deputy Head of the Office of the President expressed gratitude to the Baltic countries and Poland for the unwavering political support for Ukraine and the provision of significant practical assistance, and informed the participants in detail about the current security situation in view of the ongoing full-scale military invasion of the Russian Federation. He also emphasized the need for further military and technical assistance to our country, increased sanctions pressure on Russia, and prosecution of those involved in crimes of aggression. Separately, during a meeting of diplomatic advisers, preparations for the summit of the North Atlantic Alliance in Vilnius in 2023 were discussed in the context of the realization of Euro-Atlantic aspirations of Ukraine. Ihor Zhovkva was invited to participate in such a meeting for the second time, which once again confirms the importance of Ukraine's role in the regional and European context. In addition, during the negotiations with Vice-Ministers of Foreign Affairs of Lithuania Jonas Survila, Mantas Adomėnas and Jovita Neliupšienė, the issue of the practical implementation of the European integration and Euro-Atlantic strategy of Ukraine was discussed, as well as the possibility of using advisory and other practical assistance of Lithuania on the way of our country to membership in the EU and NATO. The Deputy Head of the President's Office emphasized that Ukraine is successfully implementing the seven recommendations of the European Commission, and this process should receive a proper political assessment from the EU leaders. At the meeting of Ihor Zhovkva with Head of the Seimas Delegation to the Assembly of the Seimas of the Republic of Lithuania and the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine Žygimantas Pavilionis, the discussion was about the importance for our country of the ten resolutions adopted by the Seimas of the Republic of Lithuania since the beginning of the full-scale invasion of Russia. The Deputy Head of the Office of the President also held a short meeting with President of Lithuania Gitanas Nausėda, during which he thanked for the multidimensional assistance, in particular to the temporarily displaced citizens of Ukraine who found refuge in a friendly country.
