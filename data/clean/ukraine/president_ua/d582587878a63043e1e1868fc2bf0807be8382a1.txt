The group is 100% composed of volunteers and interested parties, and has expanded into a large amount of related projects for saving online and digital history. History is littered with hundreds of conflicts over the future of a community, group, location or business that were "resolved" when one of the parties stepped ahead and destroyed what was there. With the original point of contention destroyed, the debates would fall to the wayside. Archive Team believes that by duplicated condemned data, the conversation and debate can continue, as well as the richness and insight gained by keeping the materials. Our projects have ranged in size from a single volunteer downloading the data to a small-but-critical site, to over 100 volunteers stepping forward to acquire terabytes of user-created data to save for future generations. The main site for Archive Team is at archiveteam.org and contains up to the date information on various projects, manifestos, plans and walkthroughs. This collection contains the output of many Archive Team projects, both ongoing and completed. Thanks to the generous providing of disk space by the Internet Archive, multi-terabyte datasets can be made available, as well as in use by the Wayback Machine , providing a path back to lost websites and work. Our collection has grown to the point of having sub-collections for the type of data we acquire. If you are seeking to browse the contents of these collections, the Wayback Machine is the best first stop. Otherwise, you are free to dig into the stacks to see what you may find. The Archive Team Panic Downloads are full pulldowns of currently extant websites, meant to serve as emergency backups for needed sites that are in danger of closing, or which will be missed dearly if suddenly lost due to hard drive crashes or server failures. Presidential Office Iryna Mudra: Implementation of Judicial Reform is a Key Component of Transparent Business Development 8 May 2024 - 20:19 The Deputy Head of the Office of the President of Ukraine Iryna Mudra met with the President of the American Chamber of Commerce in Ukraine Andy Hunder, Vice President Tetyana Prokopchuk, and Co-Chair of the Chamber Legal Committee Vitaliy Odzhykovskyi. During the meeting, the parties discussed the main aspects of the judicial reform focusing on the establishment of the High Administrative Court, staffing of courts, in particular of the Supreme Court, and holding competitions for the positions of judges of the High Anti-Corruption Court and the Constitutional Court. “The rule of law, effective judicial reform, fair justice and transparent selection of judges to courts of all instances are key components for the transparent business development in Ukraine,” emphasized Iryna Mudra. The Deputy Head of the Office of the President noted that the Ukrainian side is committed to maximum cooperation and successful implementation of the judicial reform. The representatives of the American Chamber of Commerce in Ukraine stated that the stability of the rule of law and state institutions is the basis for investing significant capital in our country.
