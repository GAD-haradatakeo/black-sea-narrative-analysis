The group is 100% composed of volunteers and interested parties, and has expanded into a large amount of related projects for saving online and digital history. History is littered with hundreds of conflicts over the future of a community, group, location or business that were "resolved" when one of the parties stepped ahead and destroyed what was there. With the original point of contention destroyed, the debates would fall to the wayside. Archive Team believes that by duplicated condemned data, the conversation and debate can continue, as well as the richness and insight gained by keeping the materials. Our projects have ranged in size from a single volunteer downloading the data to a small-but-critical site, to over 100 volunteers stepping forward to acquire terabytes of user-created data to save for future generations. The main site for Archive Team is at archiveteam.org and contains up to the date information on various projects, manifestos, plans and walkthroughs. This collection contains the output of many Archive Team projects, both ongoing and completed. Thanks to the generous providing of disk space by the Internet Archive, multi-terabyte datasets can be made available, as well as in use by the Wayback Machine , providing a path back to lost websites and work. Our collection has grown to the point of having sub-collections for the type of data we acquire. If you are seeking to browse the contents of these collections, the Wayback Machine is the best first stop. Otherwise, you are free to dig into the stacks to see what you may find. The Archive Team Panic Downloads are full pulldowns of currently extant websites, meant to serve as emergency backups for needed sites that are in danger of closing, or which will be missed dearly if suddenly lost due to hard drive crashes or server failures. 20:24 Deputy Head of the Office of the President of Ukraine Ihor Zhovkva had a phone conversation with Adviser to the President of Romania on European Affairs Leonard Orban. Ihor Zhovkva informed the interlocutor about the preparations for the inaugural summit of the Crimea Platform and its content. For his part, Leonard Orban reaffirmed Romania's continued support for Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity, and stressed that the Romanian side would be represented at this important event at a high level. Particular attention was paid to the implementation of effective efforts by our state to achieve the strategic goal of joining the European Union. There was an exchange of views on the importance of fixing Ukraine's European perspective and further support of this idea by Romania. Ihor Zhovkva and Leonard Orban discussed the elaboration of a number of bilateral projects, including in the field of infrastructure and cross-border cooperation, aimed at strengthening ties between Ukraine and Romania. The interlocutors praised mutual steps aimed to enhance bilateral cooperation in the areas of common interest. They also agreed on the importance of continuing to maintain the high dynamics of bilateral contacts in order to make progress in resolving certain sensitive issues on the Ukrainian-Romanian agenda.
